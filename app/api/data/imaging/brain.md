\## General Medical Necessity Framework

Advanced imaging is medically appropriate only when all core clinical
appropriateness elements are supported:

\- Diagnosis is confirmed or there is a reasonable pretest likelihood
based on:

\- History

\- Physical examination

\- Relevant laboratory studies when applicable

\- Prior diagnostic testing when applicable

\- Response to prior treatment when applicable

\- Expected benefit outweighs potential harm.

\- Current guidelines/literature/standards support the requested study.

\- The imaging is reasonably likely to change management or improve
outcome.

\### General Repeat/Utilization Rules

\- Multiple studies ordered at the same time may require additional
review or peer-to-peer discussion.

\- Repeat diagnostic testing of the same anatomic area is generally
limited to:

\- Post-intervention evaluation

\- Change in clinical status

\- Clarification of prior finding using a different technique/protocol

\- Repeat diagnostic testing may be subject to additional review when
due to:

\- Technical issues

\- Facility change/provider preference

\- Persistent symptoms without clinical change

\- Short-interval duplicate ordering by different providers

\- Repeat therapeutic intervention is appropriate only when:

\- Prior intervention was effective/beneficial, and

\- Expected duration of relief has lapsed

\- Repeat therapy before expected duration is not appropriate unless the
prior intervention was never actually performed.

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\## 1) Congenital and Developmental Conditions

\### Developmental Delay (Pediatric Only)

Advanced imaging is medically necessary in either of the following:

\- Cerebral palsy

\- Significant delay or loss of milestones in any 2 of the following
domains:

\- Activities of daily living

\- Cognition

\- Motor skills (gross/fine)

\- Social/personal

\- Speech/language

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- CT brain

\- MRI brain (preferred)

\### Congenital Anomalies

Includes:

\- Chiari malformation

\- Craniosynostosis

\- Macrocephaly

\- Microcephaly

\- Ataxia-telangiectasia

\- Fragile X syndrome

\- Congenital anomalies of the posterior fossa

Advanced imaging is medically necessary for diagnosis and management.

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- Ultrasound required first for macrocephaly in patients under 5 months

\- CT brain (preferred with 3D reconstruction for craniosynostosis)

\- MRI brain (preferred for congenital cerebral anomalies)

\### Sickle Cell Disease (Pediatric Only)

Advanced imaging is medically necessary for periodic screening and
surveillance for silent cerebral infarcts.

\*\*Imaging study\*\*

\- MRI brain

\## 2) Infectious Conditions

\### Infectious Conditions - Not Otherwise Specified

Advanced imaging is medically necessary for diagnosis and management.

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- CT brain

\- MRI brain (preferred)

\## 3) Inflammatory Conditions

\### Multiple Sclerosis and Other White Matter Diseases

Advanced imaging is medically necessary in any of the following:

\#### Diagnosis

\- Neurologic signs or symptoms of demyelinating disease

\#### Management

\- New or recurrent neurologic signs or symptoms

\- Recent or current use of natalizumab

\- New baseline before starting or changing therapy

\- After change in disease-modifying therapy:

\- Initial imaging at 3-6 months

\- Follow-up at 6-12 months

\- Periodic evaluation of white matter diseases other than multiple
sclerosis

\#### Surveillance

\- Clinically isolated syndrome (CIS) or radiologically isolated
syndrome (RIS):

\- 3-6 months after presentation

\- 6-12 months after presentation

\- Annually thereafter

\- Annual evaluation in stable multiple sclerosis patients with no
therapy change within past year

\*\*Imaging study\*\*

\- MRI brain

\### Inflammatory Conditions - Not Otherwise Specified

Advanced imaging is medically necessary for diagnosis and management.

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- CT brain

\- MRI brain (preferred)

\-\--

\## 4) Neurodegenerative Conditions

\### Movement Disorders (Adult Only)

Advanced imaging is medically necessary in any of the following:

\- Pre-procedural evaluation for MR-guided focused ultrasound for
essential tremor

\- Perioperative evaluation for deep brain stimulator placement

\- Initial evaluation of the following movement disorders to exclude
structural lesion:

\- Hemifacial spasm

\- Huntington's disease

\- Multiple system atrophy

\- Parkinson's disease with atypical features

\- Progressive supranuclear palsy

\- Secondary dystonia

\- Other focal or lateralizing movement disorder such as:

\- Hemiballismus

\- Athetosis

\- Chorea

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- CT brain

\- MRI brain (preferred)

\### Neurocognitive Disorders (Adult Only)

Includes:

\- Mild cognitive impairment

\- Dementia

\- Vascular dementia

\- Alzheimer's disease

\- Frontotemporal degeneration spectrum

\- Diffuse Lewy body disorders

Advanced imaging is medically necessary to direct management in any of
the following:

\- Initial evaluation of documented cognitive abnormality unexplained by
clinical evaluation

\- Rapidly progressive symptoms

\- Patients being treated with amyloid therapy (MRI brain only)

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- CT brain

\- MRI brain (preferred)

\#### PET Brain Criteria

PET brain is medically necessary to differentiate frontotemporal
dementia from Alzheimer's disease when substantial diagnostic
uncertainty remains after all of the following:

\- Neuropsychological testing

\- Evaluation by a physician experienced in neurodegenerative disease

\- Structural imaging with CT or MRI

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- FDG PET or PET/CT

\- Amyloid brain PET or PET/CT when amyloid therapy is being considered

\## 5) Trauma

\### Adult Trauma

Advanced imaging is medically necessary in either of the following:

\#### Acute Head Trauma with Any of the Following

\- Age 65 or older

\- Retrograde amnesia

\- At least 2 episodes of emesis

\- Open, depressed, or basilar skull fracture

\- Focal neurologic findings

\- Glasgow Coma Scale less than 15 or altered mental status

\- High-risk mechanism of injury

\- Seizure

\- Bleeding diathesis/coagulopathy

\- Intracranial shunt

\#### Subacute or Chronic Head Trauma with Either of the Following

\- Cognitive or focal neurologic deficits

\- Nonfocal neurologic signs or symptoms, including post-concussive
syndrome, refractory to therapy

\### Pediatric Trauma

Advanced imaging is medically necessary in either of the following:

\#### Acute Head Trauma with Any of the Following

\- Altered mental status

\- Change in behavior

\- Vomiting

\- Loss of consciousness

\- High-risk motor vehicle accident or other high-risk mechanism

\- Scalp hematoma when younger than age 2

\- Evidence of basilar skull fracture

\- Non-accidental injury

\#### Subacute or Chronic Head Trauma with Any of the Following

\- Follow-up study 3-6 weeks after head trauma in patients age 6 years
or younger when neurologic exam is stable or inconclusive

\- Cognitive or focal neurologic deficits

\- Nonfocal neurologic signs or symptoms, including post-concussive
syndrome, refractory to therapy

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- CT brain

\- MRI brain for non-acute trauma

\## 6) Tumor or Neoplasm

\### Acoustic Neuroma

Advanced imaging is medically necessary in any of the following:

\- Management of known acoustic neuroma

\- Signs, symptoms, or imaging findings suggesting recurrence or
progression

\- Surveillance:

\- Neurofibromatosis type 2

\- Following conservative treatment or incomplete resection:

\- Annually for 5 years

\- Then every 5 years thereafter

\- Following gross total resection:

\- Baseline within first year after surgery

\- Follow-up at 2 years, 5 years, and 10 years

\*\*Imaging study\*\*

\- MRI brain

\### Meningioma

Advanced imaging is medically necessary in either of the following:

\#### Management

\- Known meningioma with new or worsening symptoms

\#### Surveillance

\- Every 6 months if any of the following:

\- Vasogenic edema on prior MRI

\- Interval growth on prior imaging

\- Lesion in sphenoid wing, venous sinus, or skull base region

\- Atypical or malignant/anaplastic meningioma (WHO grade II or III)

\- Every 12 months if none of the above are present

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- MRI brain

\- CT brain when MRI cannot be performed

\### Pituitary Mass (Including Pituitary Adenoma/Incidentaloma)

Advanced imaging is medically necessary in any of the following:

\- Incidental pituitary lesion on CT or MRI that is at least 10 mm and
not a simple cyst

\- Suspected pituitary adenoma supported by signs/symptoms and
laboratory findings

\- Management, including perioperative evaluation, of known adenoma

\- Surveillance of clinically stable adenoma in either of the following:

\- Unresected adenoma

\- Macroadenoma greater than 10 mm

\- Microadenoma 10 mm or less: annual surveillance imaging

\- Resected adenoma

\- At least 3 months after resection

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- MRI brain

\- CT brain for management or surveillance of microadenoma when MRI
cannot be performed, or as an alternative for macroadenoma

\### Tumor - Not Otherwise Specified

Advanced imaging is medically necessary for diagnosis, management, and
surveillance when suggested by prior imaging.

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- CT brain

\- MRI brain

\*\*Exclusions\*\*

Routine surveillance is not indicated, in the absence of suspicious
features such as hemorrhage, contrast enhancement, or calcifications,
for:

\- Arachnoid cyst

\- Pineal cyst

\- Lipoma

\- Epidermoid

\## 7) Miscellaneous Conditions

\### Bell's Palsy

Advanced imaging is medically necessary in either of the following:

\- Additional neurologic findings suggesting intracranial pathology

\- Symptoms persisting beyond 6 weeks without additional neurologic
findings

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- MRI brain

\- CT brain when MRI cannot be performed

\### Hematoma or Hemorrhage - Intracranial or Extracranial

Advanced imaging is medically necessary for diagnosis and management.

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- CT brain

\- MRI brain

\### Horner's Syndrome

Advanced imaging is medically necessary for diagnosis and management.

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- MRI brain

\- CT brain when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\### Hydrocephalus / Ventricular Assessment

Advanced imaging is medically necessary in any of the following:

\- Signs or symptoms suggesting increased intracranial pressure or
hydrocephalus

\- Ultrasound required first in patients under 5 months

\- Diagnosis and management of congenital or secondary hydrocephalus

\- Diagnosis of idiopathic normal pressure hydrocephalus with:

\- Gait disturbance

\- Cognitive impairment and/or

\- Urinary incontinence

\- Management of established hydrocephalus and ventricular shunts

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- CT brain

\- MRI brain

\### Neurocutaneous Disorders

Includes:

\- Neurofibromatosis

\- Sturge-Weber syndrome

\- Tuberous sclerosis

\- Von Hippel-Lindau disease

Advanced imaging is medically necessary for diagnosis and management,
including perioperative evaluation, of CNS lesions associated with a
known neurocutaneous disorder.

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- CT brain

\- MRI brain (preferred)

\### Seizure Disorder and Epilepsy

\#### Adult

Advanced imaging is medically necessary in any of the following:

\- Initial evaluation of new or changing seizure to rule out structural
lesion

\- Seizures increasing in frequency or severity despite optimal medical
management

\- Before discontinuing anticonvulsant therapy if not previously imaged

\- Epilepsy refractory to optimal medical management in surgical
candidates

\#### Pediatric

Advanced imaging is medically necessary in any of the following:

\##### Neonatal/Infantile Seizure (Age 2 or Younger)

\- Initial evaluation of seizure not associated with fever

\- Periodic follow-up every 6 months up to 30 months if initial imaging
is nondiagnostic

\##### Childhood/Adolescent Seizure (Over Age 2)

\- Focal neurologic findings at time of seizure

\- Persistent neurologic deficit in postictal period

\- Idiopathic generalized epilepsy with atypical clinical course

\- Partial or absence seizures

\- Nondiagnostic EEG

\- Seizures increasing in frequency or severity despite optimal medical
management

\- Before discontinuing anticonvulsant therapy if not previously imaged

\- Epilepsy refractory to optimal medical management in surgical
candidates

\##### Complex Febrile Seizure (Age 6 Months to 5 Years)

\- More than one seizure during a febrile period

\- Seizure lasting more than 15 minutes

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- CT brain

\- MRI brain

\- Functional MRI for refractory epilepsy surgical candidates when
replacing Wada testing or direct electrical stimulation mapping

\- PET brain imaging in refractory epilepsy surgical candidates

\### Spontaneous Intracranial Hypotension

Imaging is medically necessary for diagnosis in the setting of
orthostatic headache.

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- MRI brain (preferred)

\- CT brain

\### Stroke or Transient Ischemic Attack

Advanced imaging is medically necessary for diagnosis and management.

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- CT brain preferred for acute hemorrhagic stroke

\- MRI brain preferred for subacute/chronic hemorrhage and ischemia

\- CT perfusion

\### Trigeminal Neuralgia and Persistent Idiopathic Facial Pain (Adult
Only)

Advanced imaging is medically necessary for diagnosis and management.

\*\*Imaging study\*\*

\- MRI brain

\## 8) Procedural Imaging

\### Lumbar Puncture Risk Assessment

Advanced imaging is medically necessary when at least one of the
following is present:

\- Papilledema

\- Absent venous pulsations on funduscopic exam

\- Altered mental status

\- Abnormal neurologic exam

\- Evidence of meningeal irritation

\*\*Imaging study\*\*

\- CT brain

\### Magnetoencephalography / Magnetic Source Imaging

Advanced imaging is medically necessary in any of the following:

\- Preoperative seizure localization for intractable epilepsy when MRI
is nondiagnostic

\- Preoperative mapping of eloquent cortex

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- MEG

\- MSI

\## 9) Signs and Symptoms

\### Abnormality on Neurologic Exam

Advanced imaging is medically necessary when all of the following apply:

\- Focal abnormality on neurologic evaluation

\- Abnormality has not been evaluated by advanced imaging, or has
progressed since prior imaging

\- Abnormality is concerning for intracranial pathology

\*\*Note\*\*

\- Does not apply to diffuse abnormalities such as generalized weakness

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- CT brain

\- MRI brain (preferred)

\### Ataxia

Advanced imaging is medically necessary for diagnosis and management.

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- CT brain

\- MRI brain (preferred)

\### Dizziness or Vertigo

Advanced imaging is medically necessary when dizziness is associated
with any of the following:

\- Abnormal neurologic exam, audiogram, or vestibular function testing
suggesting intracranial or vestibulocochlear mass lesion

\- Unilateral hearing loss or tinnitus

\- Tullio's phenomenon (noise-induced dizziness)

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- CT brain

\- MRI brain

\### Headache

Advanced imaging is medically necessary to evaluate headache not
previously imaged by MRI in any of the following:

\- Thunderclap or sentinel headache

\- Headache triggered by exertion or Valsalva, including cough,
exercise, or sexual activity

\- Positional or orthostatic headache

\- New headache onset after age 50

\- Change in headache pattern

\- Abnormal neurologic exam

\- Unexplained/unexpected increase in frequency and/or severity

\- Trigeminal autonomic cephalgia, including cluster headache

\- Comorbid conditions increasing likelihood of intracranial lesion,
including:

\- Malignancy

\- Immunosuppression

\- Sarcoidosis

\- Neurocutaneous disorders

\- Pregnancy

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- MRI brain (preferred)

\- CT brain

\### Hearing Loss

\#### Adult

Advanced imaging is medically necessary to detect a structural cause of
hearing loss in either of the following:

\- Conductive hearing loss

\- Sensorineural hearing loss with any of the following:

\- Idiopathic sudden onset

\- Gradual unilateral or asymmetric hearing loss on audiometric testing

\- 15 dB or greater at 2 consecutive frequencies between 0.5 and 8 kHz

\- Hearing loss with at least one neurologic sign or symptom increasing
likelihood of retrocochlear lesion

\#### Pediatric

Advanced imaging is medically necessary to evaluate structural cause of:

\- Sensorineural hearing loss

\- Conductive hearing loss

\- Mixed hearing loss

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- MRI brain for sensorineural hearing loss

\- CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa/outer-middle-inner ear for:

\- Pediatric sensorineural hearing loss

\- Adult sensorineural hearing loss when MRI cannot be performed or is
nondiagnostic

\- Conductive hearing loss

\- MRI brain or CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa/outer-middle-inner ear
for mixed hearing loss depending on clinical scenario

\### Papilledema

Advanced imaging is medically necessary for diagnosis and management.

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- CT brain

\- MRI brain

\### Syncope

Advanced imaging is medically necessary when any of the following are
present:

\- Documented abnormality on neurologic exam

\- At least one persistent neurologic symptom

\- Seizure activity at time of episode

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- CT brain

\- MRI brain

\### Tinnitus

Advanced imaging is medically necessary in either of the following:

\- Evaluation for vascular pathology when tinnitus is pulsatile

\- Evaluation for retrocochlear pathology when at least one of the
following is present:

\- Associated neurologic findings

\- Unilateral or asymmetric symptoms

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- MRI brain

\- CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa/outer-middle-inner ear when MRI cannot
be performed or is nondiagnostic

\### Visual Disturbance

Advanced imaging is medically necessary for visual symptoms when vision
testing or fundoscopic exam suggests central nervous system pathology.

Examples may include:

\- 3rd cranial nerve palsy

\- 4th cranial nerve palsy

\- 6th cranial nerve palsy

\- Nystagmus

\- Papilledema

\- Visual field deficits

\*\*Imaging studies\*\*

\- CT brain

\- MRI brain (preferred)

\## Required Documentation

\### Core Documentation for All Requests

\- Relevant history

\- Physical examination findings

\- Working diagnosis or pretest probability

\- Clinical indication for imaging

\- Relevant laboratory data when applicable

\- Prior diagnostic testing when applicable

\- Prior treatment and response when applicable

\- Ordering provider's clinical intent

\- Documentation reflecting the patient's condition at the time of
request

\### Additional Documentation Often Needed by Scenario

\- Differential diagnosis

\- Temporal course of symptoms

\- Comorbidities and risk factors

\- Age-specific considerations

\- Prior imaging results and whether symptoms progressed since prior
imaging

\- Details supporting need for repeat imaging

\- Documentation supporting why multiple studies are needed
simultaneously

\### Condition-Specific Examples

\- Multiple sclerosis:

\- New/recurrent neurologic symptoms

\- Therapy status/change

\- Natalizumab exposure

\- Prior interval imaging

\- Neurocognitive PET:

\- Neuropsychological testing

\- Specialist evaluation by clinician experienced in neurodegenerative
disease

\- Prior structural imaging (CT or MRI)

\- Pituitary adenoma:

\- Signs/symptoms

\- Laboratory findings

\- Surgical status if surveillance

\- Seizure disorder:

\- Seizure type

\- Frequency/severity change

\- EEG results when relevant

\- Medication response

\- Surgical candidacy when applicable

\- Hearing loss:

\- Audiometric testing

\- Unilateral/asymmetric findings

\- Associated neurologic findings

\- Visual disturbance:

\- Vision testing/fundoscopic findings suggestive of CNS pathology

\- Syncope:

\- Neurologic abnormality, persistent neurologic symptoms, or seizure
activity

\- Headache:

\- Red-flag features

\- Change in pattern/frequency/severity

\- Neurologic exam findings

\- Relevant comorbid conditions

\## Limitations and Exclusions

\### General

\- Any indication or modality not specifically addressed is not
medically necessary.

\- Imaging findings unrelated to the condition being evaluated should be
interpreted in context.

\- Nonspecific symptom-based imaging is subject to high-level clinical
review.

\### Not Routinely Indicated / Excluded

\- Typical migraine or tension headache without red flags and without
change in pattern

\- Simple febrile seizures

\- Typical Parkinson's disease

\- Primary dystonia

\- Essential tremor except pre-procedural imaging for focused ultrasound

\- Positional vertigo/dizziness clearly related to positional change

\- Routine neuroimaging for uncomplicated syncope without neurologic
features

\- Routine imaging for new-onset Bell's palsy unless atypical or
prolonged

\- Routine surveillance of the following lesions when no suspicious
features are present:

\- Arachnoid cyst

\- Pineal cyst

\- Lipoma

\- Epidermoid

\### Modality-Specific Limitations

\- MRI limitations may include:

\- Implanted devices

\- Claustrophobia

\- Need for sedation in pediatric patients

\- Gadolinium risk in advanced renal disease

\- CT limitations include:

\- Ionizing radiation exposure

\- Contrast-related allergy or renal risk

\- CT is not recommended for routine evaluation of demyelinating disease
because of lower sensitivity.

\- CT head/brain should not be the initial study for presumptive sudden
sensorineural hearing loss.

\### Repeat Imaging Limits

\- Repeat imaging of same site/same indication without change in status
may require additional review.

\- Ongoing services may depend on completion and outcome of previously
authorized services.

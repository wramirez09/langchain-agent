\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\### General Clinical Appropriateness Requirements

A request should be supported by all of the following:

\- Confirmed diagnosis or established pretest likelihood based on:

\- History

\- Physical examination

\- Relevant laboratory studies, when applicable

\- Prior diagnostic testing, when applicable

\- Response to prior therapeutic intervention, when applicable

\- Expected benefit outweighs potential harms, including harm from delay
or reduced access

\- Widely used treatment guidelines, current literature, or accepted
standards of care support the requested study as the best option among
alternatives

\- The study is reasonably likely to change management or improve
outcome

\### SPECT Imaging - General

\#### Preoperative evaluation

\- Medically necessary to further localize a lesion already identified
on planar scintigraphy or SPECT when:

\- Additional anatomic information is needed to direct surgery, and

\- That information has not already been provided by CT or MRI

\#### Selective internal radiation therapy (SIRT)

\- Medically necessary for:

\- Planning of SIRT

\- Evaluation of administered dose activity and distribution after
radioembolization

\### SPECT Bone Imaging

\#### Avascular necrosis

\- Medically necessary when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic,
and either applies:

\- Diagnosis after negative or inconclusive radiographs

\- Preoperative planning for osteonecrosis with femoral head collapse

\#### Fracture, including occult or stress fracture

\- Medically necessary in any of the following:

\- Suspected spinal fracture when radiographs, CT, or MRI are
nondiagnostic

\- Suspected skeletal injury in non-accidental trauma when MRI cannot be
performed or is nondiagnostic

\- Suspected fracture at a high-risk or weight-bearing site when MRI
cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic, including:

\- Femoral neck or proximal femur

\- Tibia (anterior, lateral, or plateau)

\- Great toe sesamoid

\- Patella

\- Scaphoid

\- Lunate

\- Talus

\- Navicular

\- Metatarsal base of the second or fifth digit

\#### Indeterminate bone lesions

\- Medically necessary for further characterization when MRI, CT, or
planar scintigraphy is equivocal

\#### Infection, not otherwise specified

\- Medically necessary in either of the following:

\- Diagnosis and management of osteomyelitis when MRI, CT, or planar
scintigraphy is nondiagnostic

\- Evaluation of sternal wound infection or dehiscence when CT chest is
nondiagnostic

\#### Osseous metastatic disease, not otherwise specified

\- Medically necessary in either of the following:

\- Diagnostic workup and management when both apply:

\- Patient has documented malignancy and signs or symptoms concerning
for bony metastatic disease

\- Suspicious findings on CT, MRI, or planar bone scintigraphy need
further clarification

\- Determination of bone invasion before surgical resection of head and
neck malignancy when CT, PET/CT, or MRI is nondiagnostic

\#### Postoperative joint or spine pain

\- Medically necessary when radiographs, CT, or MRI are nondiagnostic

\#### Spondylolysis (pars defect)

\- Medically necessary when other imaging studies are nondiagnostic

\### SPECT Brain

\#### Dopaminergic SPECT - Movement disorders (adult only)

\- Medically necessary to distinguish Parkinson's disease from essential
tremor when diagnostic uncertainty remains after all of the following:

\- Independent evaluation by at least one physician experienced in
movement disorders

\- Structural imaging with CT or MRI

\- Results will determine whether medication will be started, stopped,
or whether deep brain stimulation will be performed

\#### Dopaminergic SPECT - Neurocognitive disorders (adult only)

\- Includes mild cognitive impairment, dementia, and variants

\- Medically necessary as a one-time study to distinguish diffuse Lewy
body dementia from Alzheimer's disease when substantial diagnostic
uncertainty remains after all of the following:

\- Neuropsychological testing

\- Independent evaluation by at least one physician experienced in
neurodegenerative disease

\- Structural imaging with CT or MRI

\- Results will determine whether a sustained course of medication will
be started or stopped

\#### Perfusion scintigraphy

\- Medically necessary for stroke or transient ischemic attack to
evaluate non-acute ischemia and determine candidacy for vascular
intervention

\#### Thallium scintigraphy

\- Medically necessary for evaluation of an intracranial mass in an
HIV-positive patient

\### SPECT Gallium Scintigraphy

\#### Lymphoma

\- Medically necessary for diagnosis and management when:

\- PET imaging cannot be performed, and

\- Standard imaging with CT or MRI does not provide enough information
to guide management

\#### Osteomyelitis of skull base or calvarium

\- Medically necessary for diagnosis when CT or MRI cannot be performed
or is nondiagnostic

\#### Perioperative evaluation, including delayed hardware failure

\- May be used with or without bone marrow scintigraphy

\- Medically necessary for diagnosis and management of periprosthetic
infection

\#### Spinal infection

\- Medically necessary for diagnosis and management of spinal infection
when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic, including but not
limited to:

\- Epidural abscess

\- Arachnoiditis

\- Discitis

\- Osteomyelitis

\### SPECT Hepatic Scintigraphy

\#### Indeterminate liver lesion

\- Medically necessary for evaluation of an indeterminate liver lesion
larger than 1 cm to assess for:

\- Focal nodular hyperplasia

\- Hemangioma

\### SPECT Leukocyte Scintigraphy

\#### Fever of unknown origin

\- Applies when contrast-enhanced CT cannot be performed or is
nondiagnostic

\- Medically necessary in either of the following:

\- Fever lasting more than 3 weeks and unexplained after a standard
diagnostic evaluation to identify the source

\- Unexplained fever in an immunocompromised patient

\#### Inflammatory bowel disease, including Crohn's disease and
ulcerative colitis

\- Applies when CT or MRI is nondiagnostic

\- Medically necessary in either of the following:

\##### Diagnosis of suspected Crohn's disease after nondiagnostic
colonoscopy in any of the following scenarios

\- Patient meets criteria for irritable bowel syndrome, has a normal
colonoscopy, and has elevated:

\- Fecal calprotectin, or

\- C-reactive protein

\- Patient has concurrent upper GI signs or symptoms and a nondiagnostic
upper endoscopy

\- Patient does not meet irritable bowel syndrome criteria and does not
have concurrent upper GI signs or symptoms

\##### Management of new or worsening symptoms

\- To confirm exacerbation or evaluate for complications, including:

\- Stricture

\- Abscess

\- Toxic megacolon

\- Fistula

\#### Osteomyelitis and septic arthritis

\- May be used with or without bone marrow scintigraphy

\- Medically necessary in either of the following:

\- Diagnosis and management of osteomyelitis of the skull base or
calvarium when CT, MRI, or planar scintigraphy is equivocal

\- Diagnosis and management of osteomyelitis or septic arthritis at
other sites when:

\- Radiograph, ultrasound, or arthrocentesis is nondiagnostic or
insufficient to guide treatment, and

\- CT, MRI, or planar scintigraphy is equivocal

\#### Perioperative evaluation, including delayed hardware failure

\- May be used with or without bone marrow scintigraphy

\- Medically necessary in either of the following:

\- Diagnosis and management of periprosthetic infection

\- Evaluation of clinically suspected vascular graft or endograft
infection

\#### Infectious and inflammatory conditions not otherwise referenced

\- Medically necessary in either of the following:

\- Evaluation of sternal wound infection or dehiscence when CT chest is
nondiagnostic

\- Evaluation of sites of intra-abdominal infection suspected by CT or
MRI

\### SPECT Lymphoscintigraphy

\#### Sentinel node localization when clinical evaluation is negative
for nodal involvement

\- Medically necessary in the following scenarios:

\- Stage I-III invasive breast cancer

\- Ductal carcinoma in situ when mastectomy is planned

\- Cervical cancer stage IA1 with lymphovascular invasion, IA2, IB1, or
IIA1

\- Head and neck cancer when decisions are being made regarding
mandibular resection

\- Melanoma stage IA with adverse features, IB, stage II, in-transit, or
locally recurrent

\- Penile cancer

\- Uterine cancer confined to the uterus

\- Vulvar cancer T1 or T2

\### SPECT MIBG Scintigraphy

\#### Neuroendocrine cancer

\##### Diagnostic workup

\- Medically necessary for neuroblastoma or tumors of the autonomic
nervous system when any of the following apply:

\- Suspected metastatic disease

\- Suspected neuroblastoma, pheochromocytoma, paraganglioma, or
ganglioneuroma based on CT, MRI, or abnormal serum or urine metanephrine
levels

\- Pheochromocytoma or paraganglioma before planned I131 iobenguane
treatment

\##### Management

\- Medically necessary for pheochromocytoma or paraganglioma before
planned I131 iobenguane treatment

\### SPECT Parathyroid Imaging

\#### Parathyroid adenoma

\- Medically necessary for:

\- Identification of a parathyroid adenoma for surgical planning in
primary hyperparathyroidism

\- Localization of residual parathyroid tissue in recurrent or
persistent disease after parathyroidectomy

\### SPECT Pyrophosphate Imaging

\#### Amyloidosis

\- Medically necessary in any of the following:

\##### TTR gene mutation carrier

\- Baseline screening for cardiac amyloidosis in an asymptomatic carrier

\- Surveillance every 2 years in an asymptomatic carrier

\- New or worsening symptoms suggestive of amyloidosis, cardiac or
extracardiac

\##### New-onset symptomatic heart failure

\- African-American patient older than 60 years with either:

\- Heart failure of unknown cause

\- Mean left ventricular wall thickness greater than 12 mm on
echocardiography

\- Non-African-American patient older than 60 years with both:

\- Heart failure of unknown cause

\- Mean left ventricular wall thickness greater than 12 mm on
echocardiography

\- Known or suspected familial amyloidosis

\- Unexplained peripheral sensorimotor neuropathy

\- Patient older than 60 years with low-flow, low-gradient aortic
stenosis

\- Patient older than 60 years with unexplained biceps tendon rupture

\##### Other eligible scenarios

\- Established extracardiac transthyretin amyloidosis with new or
worsening cardiac symptoms

\- Bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome, with or without heart failure, in
patients older than 60 years

\- Other testing suggestive of cardiac amyloidosis, including either:

\- Echocardiogram consistent with amyloidosis

\- Cardiac MRI consistent with amyloidosis

\### SPECT Renal Scintigraphy (Pediatric Only)

\#### Renal scarring

\- Medically necessary to evaluate for renal scarring at least 4 months
after urinary tract infection in either of the following:

\- Age under 3 years with atypical urinary tract infection

\- Recurrent urinary tract infection

\### SPECT Somatostatin Receptor Imaging

\#### Well-differentiated neuroendocrine cancer

\- Medically necessary for diagnostic workup and management in either of
the following:

\##### Diagnostic workup

\- Biopsy-proven well-differentiated neuroendocrine tumor

\- Suspected well-differentiated neuroendocrine tumor based on:

\- Endoscopy

\- Conventional imaging

\- Biochemical markers

\- And not amenable to biopsy

\##### Management

\- Before planned peptide receptor radioligand therapy

\- When identification of more extensive disease will change management
and any of the following apply:

\- Equivocal disease progression on conventional imaging

\- Clinical or biochemical progression with negative conventional
imaging

\- Original disease was only detectable by somatostatin receptor-based
imaging

\### SPECT Thyroid Scintigraphy

\#### Documented thyroid cancer

\- Medically necessary for diagnostic workup, management, and
surveillance of documented differentiated thyroid cancer

\##### Diagnostic workup

\- Prior to planned definitive radioactive iodine therapy in low-risk
patients

\- After thyroidectomy when radioactive iodine therapy is planned,
except in low-risk patients

\- For known or suspected metastatic disease when radioactive iodine
therapy is planned

\##### Management

\- Immediately after radioactive iodine therapy

\- Evaluation of persistent disease found on post-radioactive iodine
therapy imaging

\- Evaluation of suspected recurrent thyroid cancer during surveillance,
such as:

\- Elevated thyroglobulin

\- Stable or rising anti-thyroglobulin antibodies

\- Abnormal surveillance ultrasound

\##### Surveillance

\- Intermediate-risk or high-risk differentiated thyroid cancer, 6 to 12
months after therapy completion

\##### Low-risk papillary thyroid cancer definition

\- Classic papillary thyroid cancer

\- Largest primary tumor less than 2 cm

\- Intrathyroidal

\- Unifocal or multifocal, with all foci 1 cm or smaller

\- No detectable anti-thyroglobulin antibodies

\- Postoperative unstimulated thyroglobulin less than 1 ng/mL

\##### Low-risk follicular and Hurthle cell thyroid cancer definition

\- Largest primary tumor less than 2 cm

\- Intrathyroidal

\- No vascular invasion

\- Clinical N0

\- Postoperative unstimulated thyroglobulin less than 1 ng/mL

\### SPECT VQ Scintigraphy

\#### Pulmonary embolism

\##### Adult

\- Applies in pregnancy or when CT/CTA cannot be performed or is
nondiagnostic

\- Medically necessary in any of the following:

\- Pulmonary embolism likely based on modified Wells criteria greater
than 4

\- Pulmonary embolism unlikely based on modified Wells criteria 4 or
less with a positive D-dimer

\- Suspected pulmonary embolism in pregnancy

\##### Pediatric

\- Medically necessary in either of the following:

\- Moderate or high clinical suspicion of pulmonary embolism

\- Concern for recurrent embolism despite adequate medical therapy

\## Required Documentation

\### General documentation

\- Clinical history and physical examination

\- Relevant laboratory results, when applicable

\- Prior diagnostic testing and results

\- Response to prior treatment, when applicable

\- Documentation that the requested study is expected to affect
management or outcome

\- Documentation must:

\- Accurately reflect the clinical situation at the time of request

\- Clearly show the ordering provider's clinical intent

\### Common indication-specific documentation to support a request

\- Current diagnosis, symptoms, or reason for suspicion

\- Prior imaging results and why they were:

\- Nondiagnostic

\- Equivocal

\- Insufficient to guide treatment

\- Not feasible to perform

\- Clinical scenario details such as:

\- Cancer type and stage

\- Evidence of recurrent, persistent, metastatic, or progressive disease

\- Pregnancy status when relevant

\- Wells score and D-dimer result for pulmonary embolism evaluation

\- Colonoscopy and upper endoscopy findings for inflammatory bowel
disease scenarios

\- CRP or fecal calprotectin results when relevant

\- Neuropsychological testing and specialist evaluation for
neurocognitive indications

\- Specialist movement disorder evaluation for Parkinson's disease
versus essential tremor

\- Thyroglobulin and anti-thyroglobulin antibody results for thyroid
cancer surveillance

\- Urine or serum metanephrine abnormalities for MIBG requests

\- Echocardiogram or cardiac MRI findings suggestive of amyloidosis

\- Age-based criteria where required

\- Timing after urinary tract infection for pediatric renal scarring
requests

\- Documentation of surgical planning need when localization is
requested

\### Additional documentation considerations

\- Requests for multiple diagnostic or therapeutic interventions at the
same time may require additional clinical justification

\- Repeat studies may require explanation of:

\- Clinical change

\- Prior intervention

\- Why repeat imaging is needed

\- Nonspecific or unlisted procedure coding may require additional
documentation and review

\## Limitations and Exclusions

\### General

\- Indications, diagnoses, or imaging modalities not specifically
addressed are considered not medically necessary

\- General SPECT indications apply only when the scenario is not
addressed elsewhere in the guideline

\- FDA approval of a drug, radiotracer, or device does not by itself
establish medical necessity

\- Repeated testing of the same anatomic area for the same indication is
generally limited to:

\- Follow-up after an intervention, or

\- A change in clinical status requiring additional evaluation

\### Repeat testing scenarios that may trigger added review

\- Repeated diagnostic testing at the same facility because of technical
issues

\- Repeated diagnostic testing at a different facility due to provider
preference or quality concerns

\- Repeated testing of the same anatomic area with persistent symptoms
but no clinical change, treatment, or intervention since the prior study

\- Repeated testing of the same anatomic area by different providers
over a short period

\### Repeat therapeutic intervention

\- Repeat therapy in the same anatomic area is generally appropriate
only when:

\- Prior intervention was effective or beneficial, and

\- Expected duration of benefit has lapsed

\- Repeat therapy before expected benefit duration has ended is not
appropriate unless the prior intervention was never administered

\### Modality-specific limitations and cautions

\- Dopaminergic SPECT for neurocognitive disorders is limited to a
one-time study

\- Leukocyte scintigraphy for inflammatory bowel disease is reserved for
cases where CT or MRI is nondiagnostic

\- Gallium scintigraphy for lymphoma is reserved for cases where PET
cannot be performed and CT/MRI is insufficient

\- Adult VQ scintigraphy is limited to pregnancy or cases where CT/CTA
cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\- Routine thyroid scintigraphy surveillance is not supported for
asymptomatic patients without evidence of disease

\- Pyrophosphate imaging can produce false-positive results in
light-chain amyloidosis; correlation with laboratory evaluation is
important

\- Pediatric renal scarring evaluation requires waiting at least 4
months after urinary tract infection

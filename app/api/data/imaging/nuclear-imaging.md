\## General Medical Necessity Framework

A request should generally show all of the following:

\- A confirmed diagnosis or a supported pretest likelihood based on:

\- History

\- Physical examination

\- Relevant laboratory results

\- Prior diagnostic testing

\- Response to prior treatment, if applicable

\- Expected benefit outweighs potential harm

\- Requested study is supported by accepted clinical practice or
published guidance

\- The result is reasonably expected to change management or improve
outcome

\## Required Documentation

For all requests, documentation should support:

\- Current signs, symptoms, and working diagnosis

\- Relevant history and physical exam findings

\- Prior imaging and whether it was nondiagnostic or insufficient, when
that is part of the criterion

\- Relevant laboratory or biomarker data, when applicable

\- Prior treatment and treatment response, when applicable

\- Cancer staging details, when applicable

\- Ordering rationale showing how the study will affect management

\- Any contraindication to preferred imaging, when applicable, such as:

\- MRI cannot be performed

\- CT/CTA cannot be performed

\- Ultrasound was nondiagnostic

\- For repeat studies:

\- Evidence of change in clinical status, interval treatment, or need
for reassessment after intervention

\- For simultaneous requests:

\- Justification for why multiple studies are needed at the same time

\# Modality-Specific Medical Necessity Criteria

\## Bone Marrow Scintigraphy

\*\*Codes:\*\* 78102, 78103, 78104

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Extramedullary hematopoiesis

\- For evaluation of an indeterminate mass seen on other imaging when
extramedullary hematopoiesis is suspected and the test is being used to
avoid biopsy

\- Osteomyelitis, when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\- Includes chronic recurrent multifocal osteomyelitis

\- Must be performed with leukocyte scintigraphy

\- Radiograph, ultrasound, or arthrocentesis must be nondiagnostic or
insufficient

\- Procedure-related imaging

\- With gallium or leukocyte scintigraphy for diagnosis and management
of periprosthetic infection

\- Sickle cell disease

\- With bone scintigraphy to distinguish osteomyelitis from avascular
necrosis when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\### Required Documentation

\- Prior imaging results

\- Why MRI cannot be performed or why MRI was nondiagnostic

\- Evidence that other initial tests were nondiagnostic or insufficient

\- Clinical suspicion for infection, marrow process, or avascular
necrosis

\### Limitations / Exclusions

\- Usually adjunctive rather than stand-alone

\- Periprosthetic infection workup often requires combined-study
rationale

\## Bone Scintigraphy

\*\*Codes:\*\* 78300, 78305, 78306, 78315

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Avascular necrosis, including sickle cell disease

\- MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\- Radiographs are nondiagnostic or insufficient

\- Either:

\- Diagnosis after negative or inconclusive radiographs

\- Preoperative planning for femoral head collapse

\- Occult fracture

\- MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\- Nondiagnostic radiographs

\- Any of:

\- Suspected spinal fracture

\- Suspected skeletal injury in non-accidental trauma

\- Suspected fracture at high-risk or weight-bearing sites:

\- Femoral neck / proximal femur

\- Tibia

\- Great toe sesamoid

\- Patella

\- Scaphoid

\- Lunate

\- Talus

\- Navicular

\- Second or fifth metatarsal base

\- Infection, including diabetic foot

\- Any of:

\- Osteomyelitis of skull base or calvarium

\- Sternal wound infection or dehiscence when chest CT is nondiagnostic

\- Spinal infection when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\- Chronic recurrent multifocal osteomyelitis

\- Osteomyelitis at other sites when radiograph, ultrasound, or
arthrocentesis is nondiagnostic or insufficient

\- Septic arthritis when radiograph, ultrasound, or arthrocentesis is
nondiagnostic or insufficient and MRI cannot be performed or is
nondiagnostic

\- Indeterminate bone lesion of an extremity

\- After nondiagnostic radiographs

\- Further characterization must be necessary to guide management

\- Limping child age 5 years or younger

\- Triple-phase study when MRI and radiographs are nondiagnostic

\- Osseous tumor

\- Diagnosis and management

\- Osteoid osteoma

\- After negative or inconclusive hip radiographs

\- Paget\'s disease

\- Any of:

\- Determine extent of disease when radiographs suggest it

\- Monitor response to therapy when baseline bone turnover markers are
normal

\- Evaluate possible malignant transformation

\- Pelvic fracture

\- Includes sacral insufficiency, stress, or traumatic fracture

\- Nondiagnostic pelvic or sacral radiographs

\- For diagnosis or management

\- Perioperative evaluation, including delayed hardware failure

\- Clinically suspected periprosthetic infection or aseptic loosening

\- Tumor evaluation

\- Skull base or calvarial lesion when CT is nondiagnostic

\### Malignancy-Related Bone Scintigraphy Criteria

\- Invasive bladder, renal pelvis, or ureter cancer

\- Diagnostic workup:

\- High-risk urothelial cancer features, or

\- Clinical, radiographic, or biochemical evidence suggesting bone
metastases

\- Management:

\- Known or suspected bone involvement

\- Breast cancer

\- Diagnostic workup:

\- Stage III or higher invasive disease, or

\- Clinical, radiographic, or biochemical evidence suggesting bone
metastases

\- Management:

\- Known or suspected bone involvement

\- Colorectal cancer

\- Clinical, radiographic, or biochemical evidence suggesting bone
involvement

\- Melanoma

\- Diagnostic workup:

\- Stage III or higher cutaneous melanoma, or

\- Evidence suggesting bone metastases

\- Management:

\- Known or suspected bone involvement

\- Prostate cancer

\- Diagnostic workup if any of:

\- Unfavorable intermediate risk with T2 disease and PSA greater than 10

\- High-risk disease

\- Very high-risk disease

\- Regional nodal involvement

\- Metastatic disease

\- Clinical, radiographic, or biochemical evidence suggesting bone
metastases

\- Management:

\- Known or suspected bone involvement

\- Risk definitions:

\- Unfavorable intermediate risk: one or more of:

\- 2 or 3 intermediate-risk factors

\- Grade group 3

\- 50% or more biopsy cores positive

\- High risk: one or more of:

\- T3a

\- Grade group 4 or 5

\- PSA greater than 20

\- Very high risk: one or more of:

\- T3b to T4

\- Primary Gleason pattern 5

\- 2 or 3 high-risk features

\- 4 cores with grade group 4 or 5

\- Bone or soft tissue sarcoma

\- Clinical, radiographic, or biochemical evidence suggesting bone
cancer or metastases

\- Suspected metastases, not otherwise specified

\- Documented malignancy with symptoms or findings concerning for bone
metastases

\### Required Documentation

\- Prior radiographs and MRI status

\- Anatomic site

\- Cancer type and stage, when applicable

\- Symptoms or imaging/lab findings supporting metastatic concern

\- For infection:

\- Prior radiograph, ultrasound, arthrocentesis, CT, or MRI results

\### Limitations / Exclusions

\- Not indicated when concurrent PET is already diagnostic for bone
metastases

\- Routine surveillance after breast cancer treatment is not medically
necessary

\- Low to favorable intermediate-risk asymptomatic prostate cancer is
not supported

\- Often add-on testing when MRI is preferred but unavailable or
nondiagnostic

\## Gallium Scintigraphy

\*\*Codes:\*\* 78800, 78801, 78802, 78804

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Lymphoma

\- Diagnosis or management when PET cannot be performed and CT or MRI is
insufficient

\- Osteomyelitis of skull base or calvarium

\- When CT or MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\- Periprosthetic infection

\- With or without bone marrow scintigraphy

\- Spinal infection

\- Including epidural abscess, arachnoiditis, discitis, or osteomyelitis

\- When MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\### Required Documentation

\- Why PET, CT, or MRI is not adequate

\- Site of infection or tumor concern

\- Prior structural imaging results

\### Limitations / Exclusions

\- Used mainly when preferred structural or PET imaging is unavailable
or insufficient

\- Axial skeletal infection workup may favor gallium/bone over leukocyte
imaging

\## Gastric Emptying / Gut Transit Scintigraphy

\*\*Codes:\*\* 78264, 78265, 78266

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Gastrointestinal motility disorders, including:

\- Delayed gastric emptying

\- Dumping syndrome

\- Severe constipation not responding to conservative treatment

\- Specific uses:

\- Gastric emptying study for delayed emptying or dumping syndrome

\- Whole gut transit for severe refractory constipation

\- Small bowel transit for severe refractory constipation

\### Required Documentation

\- GI symptoms

\- Prior conservative management

\- Why transit results will change management

\### Limitations / Exclusions

\- Reserved for cases where objective motility measurement affects
treatment planning

\## Hepatic Scintigraphy

\*\*Codes:\*\* 78201, 78202, 78215, 78216

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Diffuse liver disease

\- Includes chronic hepatitis, cirrhosis, glycogen storage disease,
hemochromatosis, Wilson disease

\- Suspected liver disease based on clinical findings or abnormal liver
tests

\- Ultrasound nondiagnostic

\- Indeterminate liver lesion

\- Lesion greater than 1 cm

\- CT or MRI nondiagnostic

\- For evaluation of focal nodular hyperplasia or hemangioma

\- Periprocedural imaging

\- Evaluation related to radioembolization procedures such as Y-90

\### Required Documentation

\- Liver-related symptoms, exam findings, or abnormal LFTs

\- Prior ultrasound, CT, or MRI results

\- Lesion size when lesion characterization is requested

\- Procedure planning details for radioembolization

\### Limitations / Exclusions

\- Historical/secondary technique for many focal liver lesions

\- Usually reserved when CT/MRI is nondiagnostic or unavailable

\## Hepatobiliary Scintigraphy

\*\*Codes:\*\* 78226, 78227

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Suspected bile leak or biloma

\- Congenital/developmental disorders

\- Adult:

\- Choledochocele after nondiagnostic ultrasound, CT, or MRI

\- Pediatric:

\- Neonatal jaundice, biliary atresia, or choledochocele when ultrasound
is nondiagnostic

\- Cholecystitis

\- Acute or chronic cholecystitis, including chronic acalculous
cholecystitis, after nondiagnostic ultrasound

\- Complications of acute cholecystitis or cholecystectomy including:

\- Perforation

\- Abscess

\- Gangrenous cholecystitis

\- Hemorrhagic cholecystitis

\- Gallstone ileus

\- Mirizzi syndrome

\- Bile leak

\### Required Documentation

\- Right upper quadrant symptoms or hepatobiliary concern

\- Prior ultrasound result

\- Postoperative context if complication is suspected

\### Limitations / Exclusions

\- Ultrasound is generally preferred first-line

\- Usually an add-on test after nondiagnostic ultrasound

\## Leukocyte Scintigraphy

\*\*Codes:\*\* 78800, 78801, 78802, 78804

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Fever of unknown origin when contrast CT cannot be performed or is
nondiagnostic

\- Fever more than 3 weeks unexplained after standard evaluation

\- Unexplained fever in an immunocompromised patient

\- Inflammatory bowel disease when CT or MRI is nondiagnostic

\- Suspected Crohn disease after nondiagnostic colonoscopy in any of:

\- IBS criteria plus normal colonoscopy and elevated fecal calprotectin
or CRP

\- Upper GI signs/symptoms with nondiagnostic upper endoscopy

\- Does not meet IBS criteria and no upper GI symptoms

\- Management of new/worsening symptoms to confirm flare or assess
complications:

\- Stricture

\- Abscess

\- Toxic megacolon

\- Fistula

\- Osteomyelitis and septic arthritis

\- Skull base or calvarium when CT or MRI cannot be performed or is
nondiagnostic

\- Other sites, with or without bone marrow scintigraphy, when
radiograph, ultrasound, or arthrocentesis is nondiagnostic or
insufficient and MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\- Periprosthetic infection

\- With or without bone marrow scintigraphy

\- Other infectious/inflammatory conditions

\- Sternal wound infection or dehiscence when chest CT is nondiagnostic

\- Suspected intra-abdominal infection sites based on CT or MRI

\### Required Documentation

\- Prior CT/MRI/colonoscopy/arthrocentesis results as applicable

\- Duration of fever for FUO

\- Immune status if relevant

\- GI symptoms and inflammatory markers for IBD

\- Evidence that MRI or CT is unavailable, nondiagnostic, or
contraindicated

\### Limitations / Exclusions

\- Often reserved as add-on imaging

\- Lower spatial resolution than CT/MRI

\- Higher radiation exposure than some alternatives

\- Usually not first-line for osteomyelitis

\## Lymphoscintigraphy

\*\*Code:\*\* 78195

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Lymphedema

\- Diffuse swelling unexplained by venous ultrasound

\- Breast cancer

\- Sentinel node localization for:

\- Stage I-III invasive breast cancer with clinically negative nodes

\- DCIS when mastectomy is planned

\- Must have no metastatic disease on conventional imaging and no
regional lymphadenopathy at the time of request

\- Cervical cancer

\- Sentinel node localization for stage IA1 with lymphovascular
invasion, IA2, IB1, or IIA1 with clinically negative nodes

\- Head and neck cancer

\- Sentinel node localization for T1-T2 oral cavity squamous cell
carcinoma with clinically negative nodes

\- Melanoma

\- Sentinel node localization for stage IA with adverse features, stage
IB, stage II, in-transit, or locally recurrent melanoma with clinically
negative nodes

\- Merkel cell carcinoma

\- Sentinel node localization with clinically negative nodes

\- Penile cancer

\- Sentinel node localization with clinically negative nodes

\- Vulvar cancer

\- Sentinel node localization for T1 or T2 disease with clinically
negative nodes

\- Uterine cancer

\- Sentinel node localization when cancer is confined to the uterus and
nodes are clinically negative

\### Required Documentation

\- Cancer diagnosis and stage

\- Nodal status

\- Conventional imaging/staging results

\- Planned surgical management

\### Limitations / Exclusions

\- Primarily used for sentinel node mapping in select cancers

\- Higher-stage disease may require more extensive nodal surgery instead

\## Meckel\'s Scan

\*\*Codes:\*\* 78261, 78290

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Diagnosis of Meckel diverticulum

\- Characterization of a lesion suspected to contain gastric mucosa

\- Requires history of unexplained GI bleeding or anemia

\### Required Documentation

\- Bleeding history and anemia information

\- Why ectopic gastric mucosa is suspected

\### Limitations / Exclusions

\- Most useful when bleeding suggests ectopic gastric mucosa

\- Lower value in presentations without likely ectopic gastric mucosa

\## MIBG Scintigraphy

\*\*Codes:\*\* 78800, 78801, 78802, 78804

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Documented neuroendocrine cancer

\- Diagnostic workup for neuroblastoma or autonomic nervous system
tumors such as:

\- Pheochromocytoma

\- Paraganglioma

\- Ganglioneuroma

\- Diagnostic scenarios include:

\- Suspected metastatic disease

\- Suspicion based on CT, MRI, or abnormal serum/urine metanephrines

\- Before planned I-131 iobenguane treatment

\- Management

\- Prior to planned I-131 iobenguane treatment

\### Required Documentation

\- Tumor type

\- Prior imaging or biochemical evidence

\- Metastatic concern

\- Treatment planning for iobenguane if applicable

\### Limitations / Exclusions

\- Not described as routine for all pheochromocytoma/paraganglioma cases

\- Used selectively, especially for metastatic or treatment-planning
scenarios

\## Parathyroid Scintigraphy

\*\*Code:\*\* 78070

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Parathyroid adenoma when ultrasound is normal or nondiagnostic

\- Either:

\- Adenoma localization for surgical planning in primary
hyperparathyroidism

\- Localization of residual parathyroid tissue after parathyroidectomy
for recurrent or persistent disease

\### Required Documentation

\- Hyperparathyroidism diagnosis/history

\- Ultrasound result

\- Surgical planning need or recurrent/persistent disease history

\### Limitations / Exclusions

\- Typically follows ultrasound rather than replacing it

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Refractory seizure

\- Ictal perfusion study for preoperative focus localization

\- Stroke or transient ischemic attack

\- Evaluation of non-acute ischemia to determine candidacy for vascular

\## Pyrophosphate Scintigraphy

\*\*Codes:\*\* 78800, 78801, 78802, 78804\*\*

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Evaluation of cardiac amyloidosis in any of the following:

\- TTR mutation carrier:

\- Baseline screening if asymptomatic

\- Surveillance every 2 years if asymptomatic

\- New or worsening cardiac or extracardiac symptoms

\- New symptomatic heart failure with any of:

\- African-American patient older than 60 with heart failure of unknown
cause or mean LV wall thickness greater than 12 mm

\- Non-African-American patient older than 60 with both heart failure of
unknown cause and mean LV wall thickness greater than 12 mm

\- Known or suspected familial amyloidosis

\- Unexplained peripheral sensorimotor neuropathy

\- Age over 60 with low-flow, low-gradient aortic stenosis

\- Age over 60 with unexplained biceps tendon rupture

\- Established extracardiac ATTR with new or worsening cardiac symptoms

\- Bilateral carpal tunnel syndrome, with or without heart failure, in
patients older than 60

\- Other testing suggestive of cardiac amyloidosis:

\- Echocardiogram consistent with amyloidosis

\- Cardiac MRI consistent with amyloidosis

\### Required Documentation

\- Cardiac symptoms and heart failure details

\- Echo or cardiac MRI findings

\- Genetic, neurologic, tendon, valvular, or carpal tunnel history when
relevant

\- Lab workup excluding AL amyloidosis when used for ATTR diagnosis

\### Limitations / Exclusions

\- False positives can occur with AL amyloidosis

\- Interpretation should consider serum/urine immunofixation and
light-chain evaluation

\## Radionuclide Cisternography

\*\*Codes:\*\* 78630, 78635, 78645, 78650

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Leptomeningeal metastases

\- Symptoms or conventional imaging suggest CSF flow blockage and
intrathecal chemotherapy is planned

\- After treatment if prior CSF flow scan showed abnormalities

\- CSF leak of the skull base

\- Suspected CSF leak with either:

\- Beta-2 transferrin-positive rhinorrhea

\- History of skull base trauma or surgery

\- Hydrocephalus or shunt malfunction

\- When MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\- Spontaneous intracranial hypotension

\- When MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\### Required Documentation

\- Symptoms and prior imaging

\- CSF leak lab confirmation if available

\- History of trauma, surgery, or shunt

\- Why MRI is not available or not diagnostic

\### Limitations / Exclusions

\- Not routinely recommended if MRI is adequate

\- Invasive and lower resolution than MRI

\## Radionuclide Cystography

\*\*Codes:\*\* 78730, 78740

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Pediatric only

\- Alternative to VCUG for vesicoureteral reflux evaluation in any of:

\- Ultrasound suggests high-grade reflux

\- First febrile UTI that is atypical

\- Recurrent febrile UTI

\### Required Documentation

\- Pediatric age

\- UTI history and fever history

\- Ultrasound findings

\- Why radionuclide cystography is being used instead of VCUG

\### Limitations / Exclusions

\- Not recommended routinely after a first uncomplicated febrile UTI

\- Alternative study with lower spatial resolution than VCUG

\## Renal Scintigraphy

\*\*Codes:\*\* 78700, 78701, 78707, 78708, 78709, 78725

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Congenital/developmental conditions

\- Known or suspected ureteropelvic junction obstruction, megaureter, or
multicystic dysplastic kidney when ultrasound is nondiagnostic

\- Split renal function evaluation in congenital renal anomalies

\- Hydronephrosis

\- After nondiagnostic ultrasound, CT, or MRI to distinguish obstructive
from nonobstructive hydronephrosis

\- Kidney cancer / renal cell carcinoma

\- Diagnostic workup and management of documented kidney cancer

\- Perioperative evaluation, including transplant

\- Split renal function before nephrectomy

\- Renal transplant viability

\- Renal artery stenosis / renovascular hypertension

\- Assess split renal function in established renal artery stenosis

\- Trauma

\- Characterize suspected urinoma

\- Pediatric urinary tract infection

\- Renal scarring evaluation at least 4 months after infection in
either:

\- Age under 3 years with atypical UTI

\- Recurrent UTI

\### Required Documentation

\- Prior ultrasound, CT, or MRI

\- Indication for split function measurement

\- Transplant or nephrectomy planning details

\- Timing relative to UTI for renal scarring study

\### Limitations / Exclusions

\- Not routine in initial UTI evaluation

\- Typically problem-focused functional assessment

\## Somatostatin Receptor Scintigraphy

\*\*Codes:\*\* 78800, 78801, 78802, 78804

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Well-differentiated neuroendocrine cancer

\- Diagnostic workup:

\- Biopsy-proven well-differentiated neuroendocrine tumor

\- Suspected well-differentiated neuroendocrine tumor based on
endoscopy, conventional imaging, or biochemical markers when biopsy is
not feasible

\- Management:

\- Prior to planned peptide receptor radionuclide therapy

\- When finding more extensive disease would change management and one
of:

\- Equivocal progression on conventional imaging

\- Clinical or biochemical progression with negative conventional
imaging

\- Original disease detectable only on somatostatin receptor-based
imaging

\### Required Documentation

\- Tumor differentiation status

\- Prior pathology, imaging, endoscopy, or biomarker evidence

\- Treatment planning for PRRT

\- Explanation of how results will change management

\### Limitations / Exclusions

\- Other modalities, especially newer receptor PET imaging, may be
preferred in practice

\- Use should be management-directed

\## Thyroid Scintigraphy / Radioactive Iodine Uptake

\*\*Codes:\*\* 78012, 78013, 78014, 78015, 78016, 78018, 78020

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Thyroid cancer

\- Diagnostic workup for differentiated thyroid cancer if any of:

\- Prior to definitive radioactive iodine therapy in low-risk patients

\- After thyroidectomy when radioactive iodine therapy is planned,
except low-risk patients

\- Known or suspected metastatic disease when radioactive iodine therapy
is planned

\- Management if any of:

\- Immediately after radioactive iodine therapy

\- Persistent disease on post-therapy imaging

\- Suspected recurrence during surveillance, such as:

\- Elevated thyroglobulin

\- Stable or rising anti-thyroglobulin antibodies

\- Abnormal surveillance ultrasound

\- Surveillance:

\- Intermediate- or high-risk differentiated thyroid cancer, 6 to 12
months after treatment completion

\- Low-risk thyroid cancer definitions noted in the guideline:

\- Low-risk papillary:

\- Classic type

\- Primary tumor under 2 cm

\- Intrathyroidal

\- Unifocal or multifocal with all foci 1 cm or less

\- No detectable anti-thyroglobulin antibodies

\- Post-op unstimulated thyroglobulin under 1 ng/mL

\- Low-risk follicular/Hurthle cell:

\- Primary tumor under 2 cm

\- Intrathyroidal

\- No vascular invasion

\- Clinical N0

\- Post-op unstimulated thyroglobulin under 1 ng/mL

\- Thyroid nodule or goiter after nondiagnostic ultrasound

\- Any of:

\- Characterization of nodules in low TSH / thyrotoxicosis

\- Identification of ectopic thyroid tissue in unexplained subclinical
hyperthyroidism or when suspected on other imaging

\- Confirmation of retrosternal goiter

\- Thyrotoxicosis

\- Diagnosis:

\- RAIU and/or thyroid scintigraphy to determine cause

\- Management:

\- RAIU before radioactive iodine therapy

\### Required Documentation

\- Cancer risk category or stage

\- Thyroglobulin/antibody trends if recurrence or surveillance is
requested

\- Ultrasound result

\- TSH/thyrotoxicosis status

\- Planned radioactive iodine therapy details if applicable

\### Limitations / Exclusions

\- Not supported for asymptomatic ongoing surveillance without evidence
of disease

\- Limited role for routine thyroid nodule workup unless ultrasound is
nondiagnostic and specific criteria are met

\## VQ Scintigraphy

\*\*Codes:\*\* 78579, 78580, 78582, 78597, 78598

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Congenital heart disease

\- Evaluate cardiac shunts

\- Pediatric congenital pulmonary airway malformation

\- Congenital lobar emphysema

\- Congenital cystic adenomatoid malformation

\- Congenital thoracic anomalies

\- Diagnosis and management

\- Lung cancer

\- Diagnostic workup and management of documented small cell or
non-small cell lung cancer

\- Malignant pleural mesothelioma

\- Surgical evaluation when FEV1 is less than 80%

\- Perioperative evaluation, including pre-lung transplant

\- Quantify pulmonary function before pneumonectomy or lobectomy

\- Evaluate for lung volume reduction procedures

\- Evaluate for radioembolization procedures

\- Pulmonary hypertension

\- Diagnose chronic thromboembolic pulmonary hypertension

\- Define disease extent for surgery planning when CTA cannot be
performed or is nondiagnostic

\- Pulmonary embolism

\- Adults:

\- Pregnant patient, or CT/CTA cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\- Any of:

\- Modified Wells score greater than 4

\- Wells score 4 or less with positive D-dimer

\- Suspected PE in pregnancy

\- Pediatric:

\- Moderate or high clinical suspicion of PE

\- Concern for recurrent embolism despite adequate treatment

\### Required Documentation

\- PE risk assessment

\- Pregnancy status when relevant

\- D-dimer when applicable

\- Why CT/CTA cannot be performed or was nondiagnostic

\- Pulmonary function data and surgical plan when used preoperatively

\### Limitations / Exclusions

\- Not for routine advanced imaging in asymptomatic patients after
venous thromboembolism

\- CTA may be preferred for anatomic extent in established chronic
thromboembolic disease when feasible

\# Global Limitations and Exclusions

\- Any indication or modality not specifically addressed is not
medically necessary

\- The guideline does not determine benefit coverage; plan rules may
still apply

\- Repeated imaging of the same site for the same indication should
generally be limited to:

\- Post-intervention assessment

\- A meaningful clinical change

\- Clarification with a different protocol/technique

\- Repeated same-site testing may trigger additional review when due to:

\- Technical problems

\- Facility changes based on preference

\- Persistent symptoms without clinical change or interval treatment

\- Multiple providers ordering similar studies over a short time

\- Repeat therapeutic intervention in the same area is generally
appropriate only if:

\- Prior intervention worked, and

\- Expected duration of benefit has ended

\- Simultaneous ordering of multiple studies may require added
justification

\- Nonspecific or unlisted codes may require additional review and
supporting records

\# Practical Documentation Checklist

\- Clinical indication and specific question to be answered

\- Symptoms and relevant exam findings

\- Prior imaging reports

\- Why a preferred study was not possible or was nondiagnostic

\- Key labs/biomarkers

\- Stage/risk category for cancer cases

\- Procedure or treatment plan if pre-op or treatment-related

\- How the requested study will change management

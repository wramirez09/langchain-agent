\## Core documentation required for any request

\- Complete history and physical examination

\- Relevant laboratory studies, when applicable

\- Prior diagnostic testing results

\- Response to prior therapeutic interventions, when applicable

\- Clear documentation of the current clinical situation at the time of
request

\- Clear statement of the ordering clinician's intent

\- Evidence that the requested test is expected to change management or
improve outcomes

\- Evidence that expected benefit outweighs potential harm

\- Support from accepted guidelines, current literature, or standard
practice

\## Situations that may require added review

\- Multiple tests ordered at the same time

\- Repeated testing of the same anatomic area for the same indication

\- Repeated testing without clinical change

\- Repeated testing at a different facility for preference or quality
concerns

\- Repeated testing due to technical issues

\## Common modality-specific documentation elements

\- Recent EKG, usually within 30 days, for stress-based cardiac imaging
and CCTA

\- Symptom description:

\- Chest pain

\- Dyspnea

\- Palpitations

\- Syncope or presyncope

\- Heart failure symptoms

\- Pretest probability of CAD where required

\- ASCVD risk information where required

\- Prior imaging dates and results

\- Whether prior imaging was technically limited, suboptimal,
inconclusive, indeterminate, or equivocal

\- Clinical stability versus new or worsening findings

\- Dates of prior interventions such as CABG, PCI, transplant, valve
procedure, congenital repair, or cancer therapy

\- If maximal medical therapy is relevant, documentation that symptoms
persist despite therapy or that therapy is contraindicated

\-\--

\# Global Limitations and Exclusions

\- Acute coronary syndrome workup performed in the emergency room or
inpatient setting is generally outside the scope of several elective
imaging sections

\- Repeated testing without change in clinical status is generally not
appropriate

\- Nonspecific or miscellaneous procedure codes may trigger extra
documentation review

\- Low-likelihood CAD patients have higher false-positive risk on
noninvasive testing

\- Selection of imaging should avoid unnecessary layering of tests

\- Some tests have safety constraints related to radiation, contrast,
MRI safety, sedation, obesity, arrhythmia, pregnancy, or inability to
cooperate

\# 1) Cardiac CT With Quantitative Evaluation of Coronary Calcification

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

Coronary artery calcium testing is medically necessary only when ALL
apply:

\- No known atheromatous vascular disease

\- Not diabetic

\- Age 40 to 75 years

\- LDL cholesterol 70 to 190 mg/dL

\- 10-year ASCVD risk 5% to 20%

\- Patient does NOT have any of the following risk enhancers:

\- Family history of premature atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease

\- Persistently elevated LDL 160 mg/dL or higher

\- Persistently elevated triglycerides over 175 mg/dL

\- Metabolic syndrome

\- Chronic kidney disease

\- Chronic inflammatory condition

\- Menopause before age 40

\- History of preeclampsia

\- High-risk race or ethnicity

\- If measured, any of:

\- High-sensitivity CRP 2.0 mg/L or higher

\- Lipoprotein(a) over 50 mg/dL

\- Apolipoprotein B over 130 mg/dL

\- Ankle-brachial index under 0.9

\## Required Documentation

\- Age

\- Diabetes status

\- Known vascular disease status

\- LDL level

\- ASCVD 10-year risk

\- Documentation that listed risk enhancers are absent

\## Limitations and Exclusions

\- Not intended for symptomatic CAD evaluation

\- Often not covered as a screening study

\- Uses ionizing radiation

\- Not the same as CCTA

\- Not the same as cardiac CT for structure/function

\## Relevant Codes

\- CPT/HCPCS:

\- 75571

\- S8092

\- ICD-10:

\- Not listed

\# 2) Cardiac CT for Structure and Morphology

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\### Congenital heart disease

Any of the following:

\- Suspected or established congenital heart disease with technically
limited or nondiagnostic echocardiogram

\- Further evaluation when echocardiogram suggests new complex
congenital heart disease

\- Complex congenital heart disease within 1 year after surgical
correction

\- Complex congenital heart disease with new or worsening symptoms or
exam change

\- Surgical planning for complex congenital heart disease

\- Surveillance in asymptomatic complex congenital heart disease when no
cardiac MRI or cardiac CT was done within the prior year

\### Cardiomyopathy

Any of the following:

\- Suspected arrhythmogenic right ventricular dysplasia with any of:

\- Severe right ventricular dysfunction on another imaging study

\- Precordial T-wave inversion not associated with RBBB

\- First-degree relative with established disease or unexplained sudden
death before age 35

\- Ventricular tachycardia or frequent PVCs

\- Need to assess LV function in suspected or established cardiomyopathy
when other noninvasive imaging is not feasible or is technically
suboptimal

\- Need to assess RV function when other noninvasive imaging is not
feasible or is technically suboptimal

\### Valvular heart disease

Either of the following:

\- Suspected dysfunction of native or prosthetic valves when other
cardiac imaging is not feasible or is technically suboptimal

\- Established dysfunction of native or prosthetic valves when other
cardiac imaging is not feasible or is technically suboptimal

\### Established CAD

\- Noninvasive localization of bypass grafts or potential grafts and/or
retrosternal anatomy before repeat surgical revascularization

\### Intracardiac or paracardiac masses

Any of the following:

\- Suspected mass suggested by other imaging and no cardiac CT/MRI
within 60 days

\- Established mass and clinically unstable

\- Established mass, clinically stable, and no cardiac CT/MRI within 1
year

\- Established mass after recent treatment and no cardiac CT/MRI within
60 days

\### Left atrial appendage closure device

Either of the following:

\- Pre-implant anatomy evaluation

\- One post-placement study as an alternative to TEE to assess for
intracardiac thrombus

\### Cardiac aneurysm or pseudoaneurysm

\- Evaluation is medically necessary

\## Required Documentation

\- Why echocardiography or MRI is insufficient, infeasible, or
technically limited

\- Congenital heart disease details and prior surgical history

\- Mass/tumor status and prior treatment dates

\- Symptoms or exam change

\- Prior CT/MRI dates

\## Limitations and Exclusions

\- Uses iodinated contrast and ionizing radiation

\- Quality limitations:

\- Arrhythmia

\- Uncontrolled heart rate

\- Motion

\- Breath-hold inability

\- Claustrophobia

\- Use caution in pregnancy and children

\- Not applicable to CAC scoring

\- Not applicable to CCTA

\- Follow-up CT generally should only be done with clinical change or
required surveillance

\- If both cardiac CT and MRI are ordered, rationale must be specific

\## Relevant Codes

\- CPT/HCPCS:

\- 75572

\- 75573

\- ICD-10:

\- Not listed

\# 3) Coronary CT Angiography (CCTA) and FFR-CT

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\### Suspected CAD in symptomatic patients without evaluation in the
prior 60 days

Any of the following:

\- Chest pain with or without other ischemic symptoms and pretest
probability over 15%

\- Dyspnea as predominant symptom and pretest probability over 15%

\- Cardiac symptoms with CAD-associated conditions such as:

\- Abdominal aortic aneurysm

\- Established symptomatic peripheral vascular disease

\- Prior stroke, TIA, carotid endarterectomy, or high-grade carotid
stenosis

\- Chronic kidney disease

\### Established flow-limiting CAD with new or worsening symptoms

\- Symptoms persist despite maximal anti-ischemic medical therapy or
therapy is contraindicated

\### Established or suspected CAD

Any of the following:

\#### Post-transplant

\- New or worsening cardiac symptoms

\- New or worsening physical examination abnormalities

\- Clinically stable patient with no CAD evaluation in the prior year

\#### New arrhythmia

\- Sustained or nonsustained ventricular tachycardia

\- Atrial fibrillation/flutter with intermediate or high CAD risk

\- Atrial fibrillation/flutter with established CAD

\- Frequent PVCs greater than 30 per hour

\#### New onset CHF or recently recognized LV systolic dysfunction

\- No CAD evaluation since onset

\#### Abnormal resting EKG

\- Newly recognized and not previously evaluated:

\- LBBB

\- ST depression 1 mm or greater

\- LVH with repolarization abnormality

\#### Resting EKG that makes exercise EKG uninterpretable

\- LBBB

\- Ventricular paced rhythm

\- LVH with repolarization abnormality

\- Digoxin effect

\- ST depression 1 mm or greater on recent EKG

\- Pre-excitation syndromes

\### Preoperative and miscellaneous CCTA indications

\- Preoperative evaluation for cardiac valve surgery

\- Preoperative non-cardiac surgery or solid organ transplant evaluation
in selected patients

\- Inability to perform exercise EKG for non-obesity reasons

\- Established Kawasaki disease surveillance:

\- Up to 1 year after diagnosis if prior imaging shows coronary
abnormalities, LV dysfunction, pericardial effusion, significant
valvular regurgitation, or aortic dilation

\- Annual if small or medium coronary aneurysms

\- Every 6 months if large or giant aneurysms or coronary obstruction

\- Suspected congenital coronary artery anomaly with:

\- Exertional syncope

\- First-degree relative history

\- Inadequate coronary angiographic definition

\- Abnormal coronary ostial location on echo

\### FFR-CT

ALL of the following:

\- Symptoms consistent with myocardial ischemia

\- Symptoms persist despite maximal guideline-directed medical therapy

\- CCTA performed within 90 days

\- At least one 40% to 90% stenosis in proximal or mid segment of a
major native coronary artery or named branch

\## Required Documentation

\- Recent EKG

\- Symptom profile and pretest probability

\- ASCVD risk when used in criteria

\- Prior CAD evaluation date

\- CAD severity and current therapy

\- Arrhythmia type/frequency

\- CHF/LV dysfunction onset

\- Inability to exercise, if relevant

\- Kawasaki disease details

\- Coronary stenosis location and severity for FFR-CT

\## Limitations and Exclusions

\- Elective outpatient guideline; not for inpatient/emergency ACS workup

\- Uses contrast and radiation

\- Lower image quality with obesity, heavy calcium burden, and small
stents

\- Beta blockers often needed

\- Claustrophobia may interfere

\- Not clinically indicated for infrequent premature atrial or
ventricular depolarizations

\## Relevant Codes

\- CPT/HCPCS:

\- 75574

\- 75580

\- ICD-10:

\- Not listed

\# 4) Cardiac MRI

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\## Stress Cardiac MRI

\### Appropriate use framework

\- Used when pharmacologic stress is preferable

\- Similar CAD indications to other stress imaging modalities

\### Suspected CAD in symptomatic patients without prior evaluation in
60 days

Any of the following:

\- Chest pain or ischemic symptoms with pretest probability over 15%

\- Dyspnea with pretest probability over 15%

\- Cardiac symptoms with CAD-associated conditions:

\- Abdominal aortic aneurysm

\- Symptomatic peripheral vascular disease

\- Prior stroke/TIA/CEA/high-grade carotid stenosis

\- Chronic kidney disease

\### Established flow-limiting CAD in asymptomatic patients

Any of the following:

\- Stable patient after CABG more than 5 years prior and no CAD
evaluation in 2 years

\- Incomplete revascularization: eligible 3 years after procedure and
every 2 years thereafter

\- ACS within prior 90 days without coronary angiography at the time and
no evaluation since

\- Myocardial viability evaluation when candidate for revascularization
and LV ejection fraction under 55%

\### Established flow-limiting CAD with new/worsening symptoms

Either of the following:

\- Persistent symptoms despite maximal anti-ischemic therapy or
contraindication

\- Myocardial viability evaluation when candidate for revascularization
and LV ejection fraction under 55%

\### Established or suspected CAD

Includes:

\- Cardiac transplant surveillance or symptom-driven evaluation

\- New arrhythmia

\- CHF or new LV systolic dysfunction

\- Abnormal resting EKG

\- Abnormal or inconclusive exercise EKG

\- Recent technically limited, equivocal, or inconclusive stress imaging

\- Selected preoperative non-cardiac surgery cases

\- Inability to perform exercise treadmill testing

\- Kawasaki disease

\- Prior to initiation of Interleukin-2

\## Resting Cardiac MRI

\### Myocarditis

Either of the following:

\- Suspected myocarditis with ALL:

\- Symptoms such as chest pain, dyspnea, or palpitations

\- Recent negative CAD evaluation or low pretest probability

\- Evidence of myocardial injury by any of:

\- Elevated troponin

\- Ventricular dysfunction

\- EKG changes

\- Follow-up LV function in established myocarditis when transthoracic
echo is technically suboptimal

\### Cardiomyopathy

Any of the following:

\- Assess LV function in symptomatic suspected or established
cardiomyopathy when other studies are discordant or technically
suboptimal

\- Annual surveillance when BOTH:

\- Chronic progressive disease likely to cause cardiomyopathy, excluding
CAD

\- Cardiomyopathy not evident on recent non-MRI imaging within the prior
year

\- Annual quantification of cardiac iron load in chronically transfused
patients

\- Suspected arrhythmogenic right ventricular dysplasia with any of:

\- Severe RV dysfunction on another study

\- Precordial T-wave inversion not associated with RBBB

\- First-degree relative with established disease or unexplained sudden
death before age 35

\- Ventricular tachycardia or frequent PVCs

\- Evaluation/management of Fabry disease

\### Cardiac aneurysm or pseudoaneurysm

\- Evaluation is medically necessary

\### Congenital heart disease

Includes:

\- Suspected congenital coronary anomaly

\- Echo suggesting new complex congenital heart disease

\- Less than 1 year after surgical correction

\- New or worsening symptoms or exam findings

\- Surgical planning

\- Surveillance if no cardiac MRI or CT within the prior year

\### Valvular heart disease

Either of the following:

\- Inconclusive echo or echo not feasible

\- Moderate or severe disease needing further definition likely to
affect management, including:

\- Regurgitant volume

\- Regurgitant fraction

\- Ejection fraction

\- Ventricular volumes

\- Timing of surgery

\### Intracardiac or paracardiac mass

Any of the following:

\- Suspected mass and no cardiac MRI/CT within 60 days

\- Established mass and clinically unstable

\- Established mass, stable, and no MRI/CT within 1 year

\- Established mass after recent treatment and no MRI/CT within 60 days

\### Cardiac venous anatomy

Either:

\- Pulmonary vein localization before atrial fibrillation/flutter
ablation

\- Coronary venous localization before biventricular pacemaker
implantation

\### Pericardial conditions

Any of the following:

\- Suspected constriction

\- Suspected congenital pericardial disease

\- Suspected pericardial effusion with technically suboptimal
echocardiography

\- Complex effusion on echocardiogram

\### Thoracic aorta

Any of the following:

\- Suspected thoracic aortic aneurysm/dilation with no CT/MRI in prior
60 days

\- Confirmed aneurysm/dilation with new or worsening symptoms

\- Stable aneurysm/dilation without thoracic imaging in prior 6 months

\- Suspected dissection

\- Confirmed dissection with new or worsening symptoms

\- Confirmed dissection when surgery is anticipated

\- Stable dissection without thoracic imaging in prior year

\- Post-repair dissection or aneurysm within prior year without thoracic
imaging in 6 months

\- Blunt chest trauma

\## Required Documentation

\- MRI safety screening

\- Recent EKG for stress MRI

\- Reason pharmacologic testing is preferable, when applicable

\- Prior imaging dates and results

\- Symptom severity and stability

\- Evidence of myocarditis, cardiomyopathy, mass, pericardial,
congenital, or aortic disease

\- If echo was insufficient, documentation of technical limitations

\## Limitations and Exclusions

\- Elective outpatient guideline; not for inpatient/emergency ACS workup

\- MRI contraindications may apply:

\- Noncompatible pacemaker/ICD

\- Certain clips or implants

\- Unsafe external equipment

\- Image quality may be limited by irregular rhythm

\- Contrast use is discretionary

\- Resting MRI is not first-line in many scenarios when echo is adequate

\## Relevant Codes

\- CPT/HCPCS:

\- 75557

\- 75559

\- 75561

\- 75563

\- 75565

\- ICD-10:

\- Not listed

\# 5) PET Myocardial Imaging

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\## Perfusion PET

Appropriate as initial noninvasive stress imaging when either applies:

\- At least one relative contraindication to conventional nuclear
imaging

\- Pharmacologic testing is preferable

\### Suspected CAD in symptomatic patients without evaluation in prior
60 days

Any of the following:

\- Chest pain or ischemic symptoms with pretest probability over 15%

\- Dyspnea with pretest probability over 15%

\- Cardiac symptoms plus:

\- Abdominal aortic aneurysm

\- Symptomatic peripheral vascular disease

\- Prior stroke/TIA/CEA/high-grade carotid stenosis

\- Chronic kidney disease

\### Established flow-limiting CAD in asymptomatic patients

Any of the following:

\- Stable post-CABG patient beyond 5 years with no CAD evaluation in
past 2 years

\- Incomplete revascularization: 3 years after procedure and every 2
years thereafter

\- ACS within 90 days without coronary angiography and no evaluation
since

\- Viability assessment before revascularization if LV ejection fraction
under 55%

\### Established flow-limiting CAD with new/worsening symptoms

Either of the following:

\- Persistent symptoms despite maximal anti-ischemic therapy or
contraindication

\- Viability assessment before revascularization if LV ejection fraction
under 55%

\### Established or suspected CAD

Includes:

\- Cardiac transplant evaluation

\- New arrhythmia:

\- Sustained or nonsustained VT

\- AF/flutter with intermediate or high CAD risk

\- AF/flutter with established CAD

\- Frequent PVCs over 30 per hour

\- New CHF or newly recognized LV systolic dysfunction without CAD
evaluation since onset

\- Abnormal resting EKG

\- Abnormal or inconclusive exercise treadmill test

\- Recent technically limited or equivocal stress imaging

\- Selected preoperative non-cardiac surgery cases

\- Inability to perform exercise EKG

\- Kawasaki disease

\- Prior to initiation of Interleukin-2

\### PET perfusion with metabolic PET

\- Suspected or established cardiac sarcoidosis

\## Metabolic PET

\### Myocardial viability

ALL of the following:

\- Established CAD

\- LV systolic dysfunction

\- Viability not defined by other testing

\- Revascularization is under consideration

\### Cardiac sarcoidosis

\- Medically necessary with or without perfusion imaging

\## Required Documentation

\- Why PET is preferred over conventional nuclear imaging or
exercise-based testing

\- Pretest probability and symptom profile

\- Recent EKG

\- Prior CAD evaluation dates

\- LV function for viability requests

\- Sarcoidosis diagnosis or suspicion details

\- Prior imaging limitations if applicable

\## Limitations and Exclusions

\- Outpatient elective guideline; not for inpatient/emergency ACS workup

\- Not clinically indicated for infrequent premature atrial or
ventricular depolarizations

\- Stress imaging after recent normal or definitively abnormal stress
imaging is generally not appropriate

\- Pharmacologic stress is generally used when exercise is not suitable

\## Relevant Codes

\- CPT/HCPCS:

\- 78429

\- 78430

\- 78431

\- 78432

\- 78433

\- 78459

\- 78491

\- 78492

\- S8085

\- ICD-10:

\- Not listed

\# 6) Myocardial Perfusion Imaging (MPI)

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\### Suspected CAD in symptomatic patients without evaluation in prior
60 days

Any of the following:

\- Chest pain or ischemic symptoms with pretest probability over 15%

\- Dyspnea with pretest probability over 15%

\- Cardiac symptoms plus:

\- Abdominal aortic aneurysm

\- Symptomatic peripheral vascular disease

\- Prior stroke/TIA/CEA/high-grade carotid stenosis

\- Chronic kidney disease

\### Established flow-limiting CAD in asymptomatic patients

Any of the following:

\- Stable patient after CABG more than 5 years earlier with no CAD
evaluation in prior 2 years

\- Incomplete revascularization: eligible 3 years after procedure and
every 2 years thereafter

\- ACS within prior 90 days without coronary angiography and no
evaluation since

\- Viability assessment before revascularization if LV ejection fraction
under 55%

\### Established flow-limiting CAD with new/worsening symptoms

Either of the following:

\- Persistent symptoms despite maximal anti-ischemic therapy or
contraindication

\- Viability assessment before revascularization if LV ejection fraction
under 55%

\### Established or suspected CAD / additional categories

Includes:

\- Cardiac transplant

\- New arrhythmia

\- CHF or new LV systolic dysfunction

\- Abnormal resting EKG

\- Abnormal exercise EKG

\- Abnormal stress imaging

\- Preoperative non-cardiac surgery evaluation

\- Inability to perform exercise EKG

\- Kawasaki disease

\- Prior to initiation of Interleukin-2

\## Required Documentation

\- Recent EKG within 30 days

\- Symptom profile and pretest probability

\- Prior revascularization and CAD evaluation dates

\- Whether exercise is possible

\- If recent radioactive imaging was done, enough time for tracer
clearance

\- Whether pregnancy or breastfeeding is present

\## Limitations and Exclusions

\- Contraindications include:

\- Pregnancy or breastfeeding

\- Inability to remain still or follow instructions

\- Morbid obesity may reduce image quality

\- Exercise is preferred when feasible

\- Pharmacologic testing preferred when resting EKG makes exercise
interpretation difficult

\# 7) Infarct Imaging

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

Any of the following:

\- Suspected acute myocardial infarction likely within the last 7 days,
especially when:

\- Cardiac enzymes are negative after expected peak

\- Baseline ECG is abnormal due to prior MI

\- Left bundle branch block is present

\- Differentiate subendocardial infarction from ischemia

\- Post-cardioversion

\- Chest pain after significant chest trauma or major surgery

\## Required Documentation

\- Timing from suspected infarction

\- Relevant ECG findings

\- Enzyme results

\- Trauma, surgery, or cardioversion history

\- Reason standard evaluation is inadequate

\## Limitations and Exclusions

\- Best performed 48 to 72 hours after the event

\- False positives can occur with:

\- Amyloidosis

\- Valvular or pericardial calcification

\- Cardiomyopathy

\- Doxorubicin treatment

\- Myocarditis or pericarditis

\- Persistently positive prior MI

\- Radiation therapy

\- Ventricular aneurysm

\## Relevant Codes

\- CPT/HCPCS:

\- 78466

\- 78468

\- 78469

\- ICD-10:

\- Not listed

\# 8) Cardiac Blood Pool Imaging (MUGA / First Pass)

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\### Evaluation of left ventricular function

Any of the following:

\- Initial evaluation of known or suspected heart failure

\- Reevaluation of known LV dysfunction with deterioration in clinical
status

\- Resting EKG abnormalities

\- Evaluation prompted by cardiotoxic therapy:

\- Baseline before therapy

\- Serial during therapy or within 6 months after completion

\- Annual surveillance after that

\- Screening every 2 years in clinically stable first-degree relatives
of patients with inherited cardiomyopathy

\- Suspected restrictive, infiltrative, or genetic cardiomyopathy

\- Diagnosed or suspected myocarditis

\- Cardiomyopathy being evaluated for CRT, ICD, or VAD

\- Initial CRT optimization after implantation

\- New or persistent heart failure symptoms during CRT for device
optimization

\- VAD device setting optimization

\- LV dysfunction suggested by chest imaging or elevated BNP when not
otherwise assessed

\- Significant discrepancy between two other imaging modalities

\- Pre-cardiac transplant evaluation

\- Post-cardiac transplant:

\- New/worsening signs, symptoms, or EKG abnormalities

\- Stable surveillance:

\- Within first 6 months

\- Every 3 months from 6 to 24 months

\- Every 6 months after 24 months

\### Evaluation of right ventricular function

Any of the following:

\- Suspected RV dysfunction

\- Established RV dysfunction with change in status

\- Pulmonary hypertension

\- Diseases known to affect RV function

\- MI with suspected RV involvement

\- Cardiac or lung transplant evaluation/follow-up

\### Established CAD

Any of the following:

\- Recent ACS under 3 weeks for initial LV function assessment

\- Prior ACS for recovery-phase reevaluation up to 6 months

\- Prior ACS beyond 6 months with new heart failure symptoms

\- Prior MI when being considered for ICD or CRT

\### Congenital heart disease

Either:

\- Detection/localization of shunts

\- Assessment of RV and/or LV function in established complex congenital
heart disease

\### Valvular heart disease

Either:

\- Established valvular disease with new or worsening signs/symptoms

\- Severe asymptomatic aortic regurgitation to help time valve
replacement

\## Required Documentation

\- Why echo was not recently sufficient

\- Heart failure or ventricular dysfunction status

\- Arrhythmia history if image quality may be affected

\- Cardiotoxic treatment dates

\- Transplant dates and surveillance interval

\- Device type and optimization reason

\- BNP/chest imaging findings if used as trigger

\## Limitations and Exclusions

\- If LV function was recently assessed by echocardiography, repeat
blood pool imaging is generally not needed

\- Irregular rhythms may reduce image quality or prolong acquisition

\- Some medications and disease states interfere with red blood cell
labeling

\## Relevant Codes

\- CPT/HCPCS:

\- 78414

\- 78428

\- 78472

\- 78473

\- 78481

\- 78483

\- 78494

\- 78496

\- ICD-10:

\- Not listed

\# 9) Resting Transthoracic Echocardiography (TTE)

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\### Suspected valvular heart disease

Any of the following:

\- Evaluation of murmur when valvular disease is not yet established

\- Initial evaluation of mitral valve prolapse with signs or symptoms

\- Initial evaluation of bicuspid aortic valve with first-degree
relative history

\### Established native valvular stenosis

Any of the following:

\- Changing signs or symptoms

\- Annual evaluation if moderate or severe

\- Every 3 years if mild

\- Assessment during pregnancy in known aortic stenosis

\### Established native valvular regurgitation

Any of the following:

\- Changing signs or symptoms

\- Annual evaluation if moderate or severe

\- Every 3 years if mild

\### Established bicuspid aortic valve

Any of the following:

\- New or worsening symptoms suggesting dysfunction

\- Annual if prior dilated aortic root

\- Every 3 years if prior normal aortic root

\### Established mitral valve prolapse

\- Changing signs or symptoms

\### Post valve intervention

Any of the following:

\- Baseline study within about 3 months

\- Signs/symptoms of repaired or replaced valve dysfunction

\- Annual if prior moderate or severe dysfunction

\- Every 3 years if prior mild dysfunction

\- Stable surgical bioprosthetic valve at 5 and 10 years after
implantation and annually thereafter

\- Annual after transcatheter bioprosthetic valve placement

\- Surgical mitral valve repair at 1 year and every 2 years thereafter

\- Annual after transcatheter mitral valve repair

\### Congenital heart disease

Includes:

\- Suspected congenital heart disease based on symptoms, exam, EKG, or
chest x-ray

\- Associated chromosomal or major extracardiac abnormalities

\- Surveillance in selected unrepaired or repaired congenital defects

\- Postoperative outpatient follow-up within 60 days

\- Staged surgical correction

\- Procedural planning

\### Ventricular function / heart failure / cardiomyopathy

Any of the following:

\- Resting EKG abnormalities suggesting structural disease

\- LV dysfunction suggested by chest imaging or elevated BNP

\- Discrepancy between other imaging tests

\- Initial evaluation of hypertension

\- Initial evaluation of known or suspected heart failure

\- Reevaluation of heart failure or LV dysfunction with worsening status

\- Stable pediatric LV systolic dysfunction at 6-month intervals

\- Every 2 years in stable first-degree relatives of inherited
cardiomyopathy

\- Suspected restrictive, infiltrative, or genetic cardiomyopathy

\- Suspected HOCM

\- Known or suspected myocarditis

\- Evaluation of RV function in disease affecting RV

\- Cardiotoxic therapy monitoring:

\- Baseline

\- Serial during or within 6 months after treatment

\- Annual surveillance

\- Post-transplant:

\- New/worsening symptoms, signs, or EKG abnormalities

\- Stable surveillance at defined intervals

\### Arrhythmias

Any of the following:

\- Sustained or nonsustained ventricular tachycardia

\- Sustained or nonsustained supraventricular tachycardia

\- Frequent PVCs over 30 per hour

\- Persistent frequent PVCs to exclude arrhythmia-induced LV dysfunction

\- Persistent uncontrolled AF/flutter to exclude arrhythmia-induced LV
dysfunction

\### Infective endocarditis

Any of the following:

\- Suspected endocarditis with positive blood cultures and/or new murmur

\- Reevaluation of established endocarditis with:

\- Virulent organism

\- Severe hemodynamic lesion

\- Aortic involvement

\- Persistent bacteremia

\- Clinical deterioration

\### Suspected CAD

Either:

\- Chest pain when a nonischemic structural explanation is being
evaluated

\- Suspected anomalous coronary origins or coronary fistula

\### Known CAD

Any of the following:

\- Recent ACS with hemodynamic instability or suspected complication

\- Recent ACS for initial LV function assessment

\- Prior ACS during recovery phase

\- Prior ACS after recovery if new symptoms/signs of heart failure

\- Prior MI before ICD or CRT consideration

\### Kawasaki disease

Any of the following:

\- Suspected disease

\- Established disease at 2 to 4 weeks and again at 6 to 8 weeks

\- Surveillance up to 1 year with persistent fever

\- Surveillance up to 1 year if prior echo showed coronary
abnormalities, LV dysfunction, pericardial effusion, significant
regurgitation, or aortic dilation

\- Annual if small or medium aneurysms

\- Every 6 months if large or giant aneurysms or coronary obstruction

\### Signs, symptoms, or abnormal testing

Any of the following:

\- Newly recognized dyspnea, syncope, reduced capacity, orthopnea, PND,
TIA, stroke

\- Lightheadedness or palpitations with other symptoms/signs/EKG
findings suggesting structural disease

\- Chest pain not thought due to ischemia/infarction

\- New signs of structural disease

\- Hemodynamic instability or unexplained hypotension

\- Abnormal chest imaging, EKG, or labs suggesting cardiac disease

\- Respiratory failure of unknown cause

\- Annual in syndromes predisposing to myocardial or aortic disease

\- Suspected acute rheumatic fever

\### Pulmonary embolus

Any of the following:

\- Known acute pulmonary embolus for initial management decisions

\- Follow-up after pulmonary embolus to evaluate RV function or
pulmonary pressures

\### Pulmonary hypertension

Any of the following:

\- Suspected pulmonary hypertension

\- Follow-up for treatment response

\- Annual in stable established disease

\- New or worsening symptoms

\### Aortic disease

Any of the following:

\- One-time evaluation for suspected ascending
aneurysm/dilation/dissection

\- Annual if connective tissue/genetic disease predisposes to aortic
pathology

\- Evaluation in suspected connective tissue/genetic syndrome

\- Annual for established ascending aneurysm/dissection

\- New symptoms or signs in established aneurysm/dissection

\- One-time screening of first-degree relatives of thoracic aortic
aneurysm patients

\### Pericardial disease

Either:

\- Suspected pericardial disease

\- Established moderate/large pericardial disease

\### Cardiac mass or embolic source

Either:

\- Possible cardiac source of systemic embolus

\- Pre- and post-treatment evaluation of cardiac mass

\## Required Documentation

\- Symptom and exam findings

\- Severity and stability of valve disease

\- Prior valve procedure type and timing

\- Congenital diagnosis and repair history

\- LV/RV function concern and prior imaging

\- Rhythm findings and Holter data if applicable

\- Cardiotoxic therapy timing

\- Endocarditis evidence

\- Aortic risk syndrome or family history

\- Kawasaki disease status

\- Pulmonary embolus or pulmonary hypertension details

\## Limitations and Exclusions

\- Not appropriate outpatient test when active myocardial
ischemia/infarction is the likely cause of chest pain

\- Routine surveillance of small stable pericardial effusions without
change is not appropriate

\- Not clinically indicated for infrequent atrial or ventricular
premature depolarizations

\- If another single modality recently evaluated ventricular function,
repeat echo may be unnecessary

\# 10) Transesophageal Echocardiography (TEE)

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\### When TTE is or is likely to be suboptimal

Either:

\- TTE image quality was inadequate to answer the clinical question

\- TTE likely to be suboptimal due to:

\- Prior suboptimal TTE

\- Severe thoracic contour abnormality

\- Recent thoracic surgery

\- Severe chest trauma

\- Extensive thoracic burns

\- Need for reevaluation of a diagnosis previously made by TEE if
results will change therapy

\### When TEE is preferable to TTE

Any of the following:

\- Suspected acute aortic pathology

\- Valvular structure/function assessment for planning surgical or
catheter-based intervention

\- Endocarditis with intermediate or high pretest probability

\- Endocarditis involving prosthetic valve

\- Persistent fever with intracardiac device to exclude endocarditis

\- Atrial fibrillation/flutter for decisions about anticoagulation,
cardioversion, or ablation

\- Post-surgical complex congenital heart disease to exclude
intracardiac thrombus

\- Evaluation for cardiac source of embolus when no noncardiac source
has been identified

\## Required Documentation

\- Why TTE is inadequate or expected to be inadequate

\- Specific reason TEE is expected to change management

\- Sedation suitability

\- Absence of contraindicating esophageal/oropharyngeal pathology

\## Limitations and Exclusions

\- Requires conscious sedation and monitoring capability

\- Not suitable with contraindicating oropharyngeal or esophageal
pathology

\- Not required in atrial fibrillation/flutter if the plan is
anticoagulation without cardioversion

\# 11) Stress Echocardiography (SE)

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\### Suspected CAD in symptomatic patients without evaluation in prior
60 days

Any of the following:

\- Chest pain or ischemic symptoms with pretest probability over 15%

\- Dyspnea with pretest probability over 15%

\- Cardiac symptoms plus:

\- Abdominal aortic aneurysm

\- Symptomatic peripheral vascular disease

\- Prior stroke/TIA/CEA/high-grade carotid stenosis

\- Chronic kidney disease

\### Established flow-limiting CAD in asymptomatic patients

Includes:

\- Surveillance after CABG

\- Follow-up after ACS when angiography was not performed

\- Viability assessment in selected patients

\### Established flow-limiting CAD with new/worsening symptoms

\- Persistent symptoms despite maximal anti-ischemic therapy or
contraindication

\- Viability assessment in selected patients

\### Established or suspected CAD

Includes:

\- Cardiac transplant

\- New arrhythmia

\- CHF or recently recognized LV systolic dysfunction

\- Abnormal resting EKG

\- Abnormal or inconclusive exercise treadmill test

\- Recent technically limited, indeterminate, or equivocal stress
imaging

\- Selected preoperative non-cardiac surgery cases

\### Additional non-CAD uses

\- Valvular heart disease

\- Pulmonary hypertension

\- Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy

\- Inability to perform exercise EKG

\- Kawasaki disease

\- Prior to initiation of Interleukin-2

\### Selected detailed criteria from retrieved text

\- New arrhythmias:

\- Sustained or nonsustained VT

\- AF/flutter with intermediate or high CAD risk

\- AF/flutter with established CAD

\- Frequent PVCs over 30 per hour

\- Abnormal resting EKG:

\- LBBB

\- ST depression 1 mm or greater

\- LVH with repolarization abnormality

\- Resting EKG making exercise EKG uninterpretable:

\- LBBB

\- Ventricular paced rhythm

\- LVH with repolarization abnormality

\- Digoxin effect

\- Recent ST depression 1 mm or greater

\- Pre-excitation syndrome

\- Recent abnormal or inconclusive treadmill test:

\- Chest pain

\- ST change

\- Abnormal blood pressure response

\- Complex ventricular arrhythmias

\- Or failure to reach target heart rate / incomplete protocol without
true positivity

\- Recent stress imaging:

\- Only if technically suboptimal, limited, inconclusive, indeterminate,
or equivocal so ischemia cannot be excluded

\### Selected preoperative criteria

SE is medically necessary when ALL apply:

\- Non-emergency non-cardiac surgery setting

\- At least two perioperative risk factors, such as:

\- Age 75 or older

\- History of heart disease

\- Angina or dyspnea

\- Low hemoglobin

\- Vascular surgery

\- Poor or unknown functional capacity

\- Additional qualifying surgical risk context

\## Required Documentation

\- Recent EKG within 30 days

\- Whether exercise is possible

\- Symptom profile and pretest probability

\- Prior CAD evaluation date

\- Prior stress testing results

\- Functional capacity and perioperative risk factors for pre-op
requests

\- Image quality issues if repeat/alternate stress testing is requested

\## Limitations and Exclusions

\- Elective outpatient guideline; not for inpatient/emergency ACS workup

\- Morbid obesity and advanced lung disease may reduce image quality

\- If recent stress imaging was normal or clearly abnormal, repeat SE is
generally not appropriate

\- Not clinically indicated for infrequent premature atrial or
ventricular depolarizations

\# Bottom Line

\## What must generally be shown

\- The patient's current symptoms, findings, and prior workup support
the specific imaging request

\- The test is expected to affect treatment decisions

\- Prior studies are either absent, outdated, inconclusive, or
technically inadequate when required

\- Timing rules, surveillance intervals, and severity thresholds are met

\## General Medical Necessity Framework

Advanced imaging is generally appropriate only when all of the following
are supported:

\- A confirmed diagnosis or a reasonable pretest likelihood based on:

\- history

\- physical exam

\- relevant laboratory studies when applicable

\- prior diagnostic testing when applicable

\- response to prior treatment when applicable

\- Expected clinical benefit outweighs potential risk or harm.

\- The requested study is supported by accepted practice standards or
current evidence.

\- The study is likely to change management or improve outcome.

\### General Documentation Expectations

Requests should document:

\- Current clinical presentation

\- Relevant history and physical findings

\- Diagnostic question being asked

\- Prior imaging or testing results, if any

\- Prior treatment and response, when relevant

\- Ordering provider's clinical intent

\- Why the requested modality will affect management

\## Rules Affecting Medical Necessity

\### Simultaneous Ordering of Multiple Studies

\- Ordering multiple diagnostic or therapeutic interventions at the same
time may require additional review.

\- Multiple studies are less likely to be supported when:

\- one study should reasonably come first

\- the outcome of the first study would determine whether the second is
needed

\- one requested study is more appropriate than the others

\### Repeat Diagnostic Imaging

Repeat imaging of the same area for the same indication is generally
limited to:

\- post-intervention evaluation

\- a meaningful change in clinical status

\- clarification of a prior result using a different technique or
protocol

Repeat imaging may trigger additional review when:

\- repeated at the same facility due to technical issues

\- repeated at a different facility due to provider preference or
quality concerns

\- repeated with persistent symptoms but no clinical change, treatment,
or intervention

\- repeated by different providers over a short time interval

\### Repeat Therapeutic Intervention

Repeat therapy in the same area is generally appropriate only when:

\- the prior intervention was beneficial, and

\- the expected duration of benefit has lapsed

It is generally not appropriate when:

\- repeat treatment is requested too early unless the prior intervention
was never actually performed

\# Medical Necessity Criteria by Indication

\## 1. Congenital and Developmental Conditions

Advanced imaging is medically necessary for diagnosis and management.

\### Imaging options

\- CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa and outer, middle, or inner ear

\- MRI orbit/face/neck

\- CT paranasal sinuses/maxillofacial area

\- CT soft tissue neck

\## 2. Sinusitis / Rhinosinusitis

\### Screening

A single study is medically necessary for:

\- immunosuppressed patients before chemotherapy

\- immunosuppressed patients before bone marrow transplant

\- immunosuppressed patients before stem cell transplant

\### Diagnosis

Imaging is medically necessary for:

\- complications of sinusitis, including:

\- orbital complications

\- intracranial complications

\- vascular complications

\- complications related to invasive fungal sinusitis

\- initial evaluation of:

\- acute recurrent rhinosinusitis

\- chronic rhinosinusitis

\- barosinusitis

when not responsive to at least 3 weeks of acceptable medical therapy
including either:

\- nasal saline irrigation plus intranasal steroids, or

\- nasal saline irrigation or intranasal steroids plus at least two
other forms of sinonasal medical therapy

\### Management

Imaging is medically necessary for:

\- repeat imaging when acute recurrent or chronic sinusitis is not
responsive to acceptable medical therapy and:

\- prior imaging is insufficient to direct management, or

\- signs or symptoms worsen

\- preoperative planning for functional endoscopic sinus surgery when
any of the following are present:

\- revision sinus surgery

\- sinonasal polyposis

\- complex or distorted sinonasal anatomy

\- disease abutting the skull base

\- disease predominant in the frontal, posterior ethmoid, or sphenoid
sinuses

\- evaluation of postoperative complications

\### Definitions used by the guideline

\- Acute sinusitis: symptoms less than 4 weeks

\- Recurrent acute rhinosinusitis: 4 or more episodes per year without
symptoms between episodes

\- Subacute sinusitis: symptoms more than 4 weeks but less than 12 weeks

\- Chronic sinusitis: at least 12 weeks with 2 or more of:

\- mucopurulent drainage

\- nasal obstruction

\- facial pain, pressure, or fullness

\- decreased sense of smell

\### Imaging option

\- CT paranasal sinuses/maxillofacial area

\## 3. Infectious Conditions, Not Otherwise Specified

Applies when the condition is not otherwise specifically addressed.

\### Medical necessity

\- Diagnosis and management

\### Imaging options

\- CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa and ear structures

\- MRI orbit/face/neck

\- CT paranasal sinuses/maxillofacial area

\- CT soft tissue neck

\- MRI temporomandibular joint

\## 4. Inflammatory Conditions, Not Otherwise Specified

Examples include granulomatosis with polyangiitis and neuromyelitis
optica spectrum disorders.

\### Medical necessity

\- Diagnosis and management

\### Imaging options

\- CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa and ear structures

\- MRI orbit/face/neck

\- CT paranasal sinuses/maxillofacial area

\- CT soft tissue neck

\- MRI temporomandibular joint for suspected inflammatory arthritis,
including juvenile idiopathic arthritis, after initial radiographs

\## 5. Trauma

\### Medical necessity

\- Diagnosis and management

\### Imaging options

\- Radiographs are required first for suspected mandibular fracture

\- CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa and ear structures

\- MRI orbit/face/neck

\- CT paranasal sinuses/maxillofacial area

\- CT soft tissue neck

\- MRI temporomandibular joint for suspected internal derangement when
surgery is being considered

\## 6. Acoustic Neuroma

\### Medical necessity

Management and surveillance of known acoustic neuroma in:

\- patients with neurofibromatosis type 2, or

\- any of the following:

\- symptoms or imaging findings suggesting recurrence or progression

\- surveillance after conservative treatment or incomplete resection:

\- annually for 5 years

\- then every 5 years

\- follow-up after gross total resection:

\- within the first year

\- at 2 years

\- at 5 years

\- at 10 years

\### Imaging option

\- CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa and ear structures when MRI cannot be
performed

\## 7. Cholesteatoma

\### Medical necessity

\- Diagnosis and management

\### Imaging option

\- CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa and ear structures

\## 8. Neck Mass Including Lymphadenopathy

\### Adult

Imaging is medically necessary for either diagnosis or management.

\#### Diagnosis

Any of the following:

\- firm mass greater than 1.5 cm

\- fixed mass of any size

\- ulceration

\- present longer than 2 weeks or increasing in size

\- suspicious history or physical exam findings

\- ultrasound or laryngoscopy suspicious for malignancy

\#### Management

\- benign or benign-appearing mass incompletely characterized on
ultrasound or laryngoscopy

\- persistent unexplained established lymphadenopathy

\### Pediatric

Imaging is medically necessary for either diagnosis or management.

\#### Diagnosis

\- incompletely characterized by ultrasound or laryngoscopy

\#### Management

\- benign or benign-appearing mass incompletely characterized on
ultrasound or laryngoscopy

\- established lymphadenopathy with any of the following:

\- ultrasound findings suggestive of nodal malignancy

\- nondiagnostic ultrasound and failure to resolve after 6 weeks of
empiric therapy

\- nondiagnostic ultrasound and any of the following:

\- absence of pain or tenderness

\- constitutional symptoms

\- firm or immobile mass larger than 3 cm

\- persistent enlargement on exam for more than 2 weeks

\- ulceration

\- supraclavicular or posterior triangle location

\### Imaging options

\- MRI orbit/face/neck

\- CT soft tissue neck

\## 9. Parathyroid Adenoma

\### Medical necessity

Imaging is medically necessary in either of the following settings:

\#### A. To localize an adenoma for surgical planning in patients with
any of:

\- symptomatic hyperparathyroidism

\- serum calcium more than 1 mg/dL above normal

\- primary hyperparathyroidism with imaging showing:

\- osteoporosis

\- fragility fracture

\- vertebral compression fracture

\- diagnosis at age 50 or younger

\- clinical or biochemical evidence suggesting parathyroid cancer

\- unwillingness or inability to comply with observation protocols

\- neurocognitive or neuropsychiatric symptoms attributed to
hyperparathyroidism

\#### B. Residual tissue localization

\- recurrent or persistent disease after parathyroidectomy

\### Imaging options

\- CT soft tissue neck when ultrasound and parathyroid scintigraphy are
nondiagnostic or normal but suspicion remains high

\- CT soft tissue neck as an alternative to parathyroid SPECT or
SPECT-CT when requested by an experienced treating provider

\## 10. Thyroid Nodule or Thyromegaly (Goiter)

\### Medical necessity

Imaging is medically necessary in either of the following:

\#### Diagnosis

\- to confirm retrosternal goiter suspected on ultrasound

\#### Management

\- mass effect on upper airway or esophagus

\- preoperative evaluation

\### Imaging options

\- CT soft tissue neck

\- MRI orbit/face/neck

\## 11. Tumor, Not Otherwise Specified

\### Medical necessity

\- Diagnosis and management

\### Imaging options

\- CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa and ear structures

\- MRI orbit/face/neck

\- CT paranasal sinuses/maxillofacial area

\- CT soft tissue neck

\## 12. Nasal Indications

Imaging is medically necessary for any of the following:

\- anosmia unexplained by sinusitis

\- foreign body

\- mucocele of the paranasal sinus

\- nasal airway obstruction refractory to medical therapy

\- nasal or sinus polyposis

\- recurrent epistaxis

\### Imaging options

\- CT paranasal sinuses/maxillofacial area

\- MRI orbit/face/neck for:

\- anosmia

\- recurrent epistaxis

\- nasal airway obstruction refractory to medical therapy

\- polyposis refractory to medical therapy

\## 13. Orbital Indications

Imaging is medically necessary for any of the following:

\- absence of red reflex in pediatric patients

\- dysconjugate gaze

\- exophthalmos or proptosis

\- extraocular muscle weakness

\- nystagmus

\- optic neuritis

\- orbital pseudotumor

\- papilledema

\- strabismus

\- thyroid ophthalmopathy

\### Imaging options

\- MRI orbit/face/neck

\- CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa and ear structures

\## 14. Temporomandibular Joint Dysfunction

\### Medical necessity

Both of the following are required:

\- mechanical symptoms such as locking, popping, or clicking that have
not improved after 6 weeks of conservative treatment including:

\- NSAID or acetaminophen therapy

\- short-term soft diet and proper chewing technique

\- oral appliance such as a bite block

\- surgical intervention is being considered

\### Imaging options

\- CT paranasal sinuses/maxillofacial area preferred for:

\- intra-articular loose bodies

\- temporomandibular joint osteoarthritis

\- MRI temporomandibular joint preferred for:

\- internal derangement

\- disc displacement

\## 15. Cerebrospinal Fluid Leak of the Skull Base

\### Medical necessity

Suspected CSF leak plus any of the following:

\- CSF rhinorrhea with beta-2 transferrin positive fluid

\- history of skull base trauma or surgery

\- known CSF leak with new or worsening symptoms

\### Imaging option

\- CT paranasal sinuses/maxillofacial area with cisternography technique

\## 16. Foreign Body Evaluation

\### Medical necessity

\- radiographs are nondiagnostic

\### Imaging options

\- CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa for foreign body in the ear canal or
orbit

\- CT soft tissue neck for foreign body in the aerodigestive tract

\## 17. Laryngeal Edema

\### Medical necessity

\- diagnosis and management, including perioperative evaluation

\### Imaging options

\- CT soft tissue neck

\- MRI orbit/face/neck

\## 18. Osseous Lesions

Examples include:

\- fibrous dysplasia

\- Paget disease

\- otosclerosis

\### Medical necessity

\- diagnosis and management, including perioperative evaluation, of
lesions in:

\- temporal bones

\- sella turcica

\- orbit

\- posterior fossa

\### Imaging option

\- CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa and ear structures

\## 19. Osteonecrosis of the Jaw

\### Medical necessity

\- radiographs or Panorex have already been performed, and

\- additional imaging is needed to direct management

\### Imaging options

\- MRI orbit/face/neck

\- CT paranasal sinuses/maxillofacial area

\- CT soft tissue neck

\## 20. Salivary Gland Ductal Calculi

\### Medical necessity

\- diagnosis and management

\### Imaging option

\- CT soft tissue neck

\## 21. Torticollis (Pediatric Only)

\### Congenital muscular torticollis

\- infants age 8 months or younger

\- ultrasound of neck and cervical spine radiographs are nondiagnostic

\- no improvement after 4 weeks of conservative treatment

\### Childhood acquired torticollis

\- evaluation for secondary causes such as:

\- infection

\- neoplasm

\- trauma

when clinically indicated

\### Imaging options

\- CT soft tissue neck

\- MRI orbit/face/neck

\## 22. Tracheal Stenosis or Upper Airway Obstruction

\### Medical necessity

\- diagnosis and management, including perioperative evaluation

\### Imaging options

\- CT soft tissue neck

\- MRI orbit/face/neck

\## 23. Perioperative Imaging

\### Cochlear Implant

Imaging is medically necessary for:

\- perioperative evaluation related to cochlear implant placement

\### Imaging option

\- CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa and ear structures

\### Perioperative Imaging, Not Otherwise Specified

Only for indications not listed elsewhere in the document.

\#### Medical necessity

\- preoperative planning for orthognathic surgery

\- preoperative planning for facial feminization surgery

\#### Imaging options

\- CT paranasal sinuses/maxillofacial area

\- CT soft tissue neck

\## 24. Dizziness or Vertigo

\### Medical necessity

Any of the following:

\- additional signs or symptoms suggesting a central nervous system
lesion

\- Tullio phenomenon

\- symptoms plus abnormal audiogram or vestibular function testing
suggesting an intracranial or vestibulocochlear mass lesion

\### Imaging option

\- CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa

\## 25. Hearing Loss

\### Adult

Imaging is medically necessary to detect a structural cause in either of
the following:

\- conductive hearing loss

\- sensorineural hearing loss with any of:

\- idiopathic sudden onset hearing loss

\- gradual unilateral or asymmetric hearing loss on audiometry

\- 15 dB or greater at 2 consecutive frequencies between 0.5 and 8 kHz

\- at least one neurologic sign or symptom that raises concern for
retrocochlear pathology

\### Pediatric

Imaging is medically necessary for structural evaluation of:

\- sensorineural hearing loss

\- conductive hearing loss

\- mixed hearing loss

\### Imaging options

\- MRI brain for sensorineural hearing loss

\- CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa for:

\- pediatric sensorineural hearing loss

\- adult sensorineural hearing loss when MRI cannot be performed or is
nondiagnostic

\- conductive hearing loss

\- MRI brain or CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa for mixed hearing loss,
depending on clinical scenario

\## 26. Hoarseness, Dysphonia, Vocal Cord Weakness or Paralysis

\### Medical necessity

Either of the following:

\- after laryngoscopy, findings suggest recurrent laryngeal nerve
dysfunction or a suspicious lesion

\- symptoms lasting longer than 1 month and unexplained by laryngoscopy

\### Imaging options

\- CT soft tissue neck

\- MRI orbit/face/neck

\## 27. Horner Syndrome

\### Medical necessity

\- diagnosis and management

\### Imaging options

\- CT soft tissue neck

\- MRI orbit/face/neck

\## 28. Localized Facial Pain, Including Trigeminal Neuralgia

\### Medical necessity

\- persistent and unexplained localized facial pain

\### Imaging options

\- CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa and ear structures

\- MRI orbit/face/neck

\## 29. Stridor

\### Medical necessity

Either of the following:

\- acute stridor

\- subacute or chronic stridor after nondiagnostic radiograph and
laryngoscopy

\### Imaging options

\- CT soft tissue neck

\- MRI orbit/face/neck

\## 30. Tinnitus

\### Medical necessity

Either of the following:

\- evaluation for vascular pathology when tinnitus is pulsatile

\- evaluation for retrocochlear pathology when at least one of the
following is present:

\- associated neurologic findings

\- unilateral or asymmetric symptoms

\### Imaging options

\- MRI brain

\- CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa and ear structures when MRI cannot be
performed or is nondiagnostic

\## 31. Visual Disturbance or Visual Field Defect

\### Medical necessity

\- findings on ophthalmologic examination suggest orbital or optic nerve
pathology

\### Imaging options

\- MRI orbit/face/neck

\- CT orbit/sella/posterior fossa and ear structures

\# Required Documentation

\## Core Documentation for Most Requests

\- Current signs and symptoms

\- Relevant duration of symptoms

\- Pertinent history and physical exam findings

\- Suspected diagnosis or reason for concern

\- Requested study and anatomic target

\- Prior imaging and results

\- Prior treatments and response

\- How the imaging will change management

\## Frequently Required Condition-Specific Documentation

\- Sinusitis/rhinosinusitis:

\- symptom duration

\- type of sinusitis

\- trial of medical therapy

\- failure of conservative treatment

\- whether complications are suspected

\- whether surgery is being planned

\- Trauma:

\- mechanism of injury

\- radiographs for suspected mandibular fracture

\- surgical consideration for TMJ internal derangement

\- Neck mass:

\- ultrasound or laryngoscopy results

\- size, fixation, ulceration, duration

\- features suspicious for malignancy

\- persistence or enlargement

\- Parathyroid adenoma:

\- biochemical diagnosis

\- calcium level

\- symptoms

\- evidence of osteoporosis, fracture, or cancer concern

\- ultrasound/parathyroid scintigraphy results

\- post-parathyroidectomy recurrence or persistence if applicable

\- Thyroid/goiter:

\- ultrasound findings

\- retrosternal extension concern

\- compressive symptoms

\- need for preoperative planning

\- TMJ dysfunction:

\- mechanical symptoms

\- completed 6-week conservative therapy

\- plan for surgery

\- CSF leak:

\- beta-2 transferrin result if rhinorrhea present

\- prior trauma or skull base surgery

\- worsening symptoms in known leak

\- Foreign body:

\- nondiagnostic radiographs

\- anatomic location

\- Osteonecrosis of jaw:

\- prior radiographs or Panorex

\- reason additional imaging is needed

\- Torticollis:

\- age

\- prior ultrasound and cervical spine radiographs

\- response to conservative treatment

\- concern for secondary causes

\- Dizziness/vertigo:

\- neurologic findings

\- vestibular testing or audiogram abnormalities

\- evidence suggesting central lesion

\- Hearing loss:

\- audiometric findings

\- laterality

\- type of hearing loss

\- sudden vs gradual onset

\- associated neurologic signs or symptoms

\- Hoarseness/dysphonia:

\- laryngoscopy findings

\- duration of symptoms

\- Tinnitus:

\- pulsatile vs non-pulsatile

\- unilateral or asymmetric symptoms

\- associated neurologic findings

\- Visual symptoms:

\- ophthalmologic exam findings suggesting orbital or optic nerve
disease

\# Limitations and Exclusions

\## General Exclusions

\- Indications, diagnoses, or imaging modalities not specifically
addressed are considered not medically necessary.

\- Imaging findings unrelated to the presenting problem should be
interpreted in clinical context before ordering more imaging.

\## Condition-Specific Limitations

\- Vertigo or dizziness clearly related to positional change does not
require advanced imaging.

\- Hoarseness should not be worked up with CT or MRI before the larynx
is examined.

\- Pediatric imaging should not be used to distinguish acute bacterial
sinusitis from viral upper respiratory infection.

\- For uncomplicated chronic rhinosinusitis, repeat CT within 90 days is
generally not appropriate if the first study was adequate.

\- TMJ imaging is generally deferred until nonoperative management has
failed and surgery is being considered.

\- Osteonecrosis of the jaw requires prior radiographs or Panorex before
advanced imaging.

\- Foreign body evaluation requires nondiagnostic radiographs first.

\- Suspected mandibular fracture requires initial radiographs.

\- Torticollis criteria in this document apply only to pediatric
patients.

\- Perioperative imaging not otherwise specified applies only when the
indication is not covered elsewhere in the guideline.

\## Referral / Out-of-Scope Situations

\- Documented malignancy should be handled under oncologic imaging
guidance.

\- Some conditions refer to other imaging guidance for full workup,
especially:

\- brain-related indications

\- chest-related indications

\- oncologic indications

\## Repeat Imaging Limits

Repeat imaging may be denied or require further review when:

\- the same study is repeated without clinical change

\- a repeat study is requested due only to provider preference

\- several providers order similar studies over a short interval

\- multiple studies are ordered at once without showing why all are
needed

\## Modality Limitations

\### CT

\- exposes the patient to ionizing radiation

\- contrast use may pose allergy or renal risk

\### MRI

\- may be limited by:

\- pacemakers or defibrillators

\- claustrophobia

\- need for sedation, especially in pediatric patients

\- gadolinium risk in advanced renal disease

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\### 1. General Clinical Framework

Advanced imaging is generally medically necessary only when all of the
following are supported:

\- Diagnosis is confirmed or there is an established reasonable pretest
likelihood based on:

\- History

\- Physical examination

\- Relevant laboratory studies when applicable

\- Prior diagnostic testing when applicable

\- Response to prior treatment when applicable

\- Expected benefit outweighs potential harm

\- Clinical literature, practice standards, or guidelines support the
test

\- Imaging is reasonably likely to change management or improve outcome

\### 2. General Prerequisites for Extremity Imaging

General prerequisites commonly include:

\- Conservative management

\- Clinical reevaluation

\- Prior radiographs in most cases

\#### Conservative management

Requires:

\- Physical therapy AND

\- At least one complementary conservative treatment

\##### Physical therapy requirement includes ANY of the following:

\- Physical therapy by a qualified provider

\- Supervised home treatment program that includes ALL of the following:

\- Patient-specific or tailored program

\- Initial active instruction by physician or allied health provider

\- Redemonstration of ability to perform exercises

\- Compliance documented or attested on follow-up

\- Exception when clearly documented unusual circumstances make physical
therapy not possible

\##### Complementary conservative treatment includes ANY of the
following:

\- Prescription-strength anti-inflammatory medications

\- Analgesics

\- Adjunctive medications such as nerve membrane stabilizers or muscle
relaxants

\- Intra-articular corticosteroid injection(s)

\- Alternative therapies when appropriate, such as:

\- Acupuncture

\- Chiropractic manipulation

\- Massage therapy

\- Activity modification

\- Trial period of rest

\##### Exception to duration of conservative management

May be allowed when clearly documented due to:

\- Worsening symptoms during treatment

\- Serial evaluation supporting the shortened duration

\#### Clinical reevaluation

\- Usually includes physical examination

\- Telephone or electronic follow-up may substitute when an office visit
is not feasible

\#### Failure of conservative management requires ALL of the following:

\- Completion of a full course of conservative management for the
current episode

\- Worsening or no significant improvement on reevaluation

\- Consideration of more invasive treatment

\## Condition-Specific Medical Necessity Criteria

\### A. Congenital and Developmental Conditions

\#### Congenital or developmental anomalies of the extremity (pediatric
only)

Medically necessary when:

\- Radiographs are nondiagnostic or insufficient to guide treatment

Additional requirement:

\- For acetabular dysplasia or developmental dysplasia of the hip in
infants age 4 months corrected age or less:

\- Initial hip ultrasound is required

Imaging:

\- CT upper extremity

\- CT lower extremity

\- MRI upper extremity (joint or non-joint)

\- MRI lower extremity

\#### Tarsal coalition

Medically necessary when:

\- Radiographs are nondiagnostic or insufficient to guide treatment

Imaging:

\- CT lower extremity

\- MRI lower extremity

\-\--

\### B. Infection

\#### Soft tissue infection

Medically necessary in ANY of the following:

\- Localization of known or suspected abscess to direct surgery

\- Known or suspected fasciitis

\- Other soft tissue infection not responding to treatment

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity (joint or non-joint)

\- MRI lower extremity

\- CT upper or lower extremity as an alternative to MRI for abscess

\- CT upper or lower extremity when MRI cannot be performed for other
indications

\#### Osteomyelitis

Medically necessary when:

\- Radiographs are nondiagnostic or insufficient to guide treatment

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity (joint or non-joint)

\- MRI lower extremity

\- CT upper or lower extremity when MRI cannot be performed

\- FDG-PET for chronic osteomyelitis

\#### Septic arthritis

Medically necessary when:

\- Radiograph, ultrasound, or arthrocentesis is nondiagnostic or
insufficient to guide treatment

Imaging:

\- MRI upper or lower extremity joint preferred

\- CT upper or lower extremity when MRI cannot be performed

\-\--

\### C. Inflammatory Conditions

\#### Capitellar osteochondritis

Medically necessary when:

\- Radiographs are nondiagnostic

\- Imaging is essential to establish diagnosis or direct management

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity joint

\- CT upper extremity when MRI cannot be performed

\#### Juvenile idiopathic arthritis (pediatric only)

Medically necessary for management of established disease when:

\- Radiographs are insufficient to determine treatment course,
especially intra-articular therapy

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity joint

\- MRI lower extremity

\- CT upper or lower extremity when MRI cannot be performed or is
nondiagnostic

\#### Myositis

Medically necessary in EITHER of the following:

\- Clinically suspected myositis for confirmation or biopsy localization

\- Monitoring response to therapy

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity non-joint

\- MRI lower extremity

\- CT upper or lower extremity when MRI cannot be performed

Special note:

\- Bilateral imaging may be appropriate for generalized symptoms when
selecting biopsy site

\#### Plantar fasciitis and plantar fibromatosis

Medically necessary in EITHER of the following:

\- Plantar fasciitis after failure of at least 6 months of treatment
including at least two of:

\- Mechanical de-weighting

\- Foot orthosis

\- Night splints

\- Taping

\- Manual therapy

\- Plantar fibromatosis when ultrasound cannot be performed or is
nondiagnostic and after failure of at least 3 months of conservative
management including corticosteroid injections

Imaging:

\- MRI lower extremity

\- CT lower extremity when MRI cannot be performed

\-\--

\### D. Trauma

\#### Acute traumatic injuries, not otherwise specified

Applies to traumatic injuries not otherwise addressed

Medically necessary when:

\- Radiographs are nondiagnostic or insufficient to establish diagnosis
and direct management

Imaging:

\- CT upper extremity

\- CT lower extremity

\- MRI upper extremity (joint or non-joint)

\- MRI lower extremity

\#### Fracture

Medically necessary in ANY of the following:

\- Occult fracture suspected after nondiagnostic radiographs at
high-risk or weight-bearing sites:

\- Upper extremity:

\- Scaphoid

\- Lunate

\- Lower extremity:

\- Femoral neck or proximal femur

\- Tibia:

\- Anterior tibial cortex

\- Tibial plateau

\- Medial malleolus

\- Patella

\- Talus

\- Navicular

\- Metatarsal base of second or fifth digits

\- Great toe sesamoid

\- Calcaneus when imaging will direct timing of return to vigorous
athletic activity

\- Radiographs show:

\- Supracondylar fracture

\- Intra-articular fracture

\- Salter-Harris fracture

\- Tibial plateau fracture

\- Assessment of delayed union or nonunion when radiographs are
nondiagnostic

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity (joint or non-joint)

\- MRI lower extremity

\- CT upper or lower extremity for supracondylar, intra-articular, or
Salter-Harris fractures

\- CT upper or lower extremity for occult fracture when MRI cannot be
performed

\- CT upper extremity for scaphoid delayed union or nonunion as an
alternative to MRI

\### E. Tumor / Neoplasm

\#### Brachial plexus mass

Medically necessary when:

\- Mass is identified on exam or prior imaging

\- Or suspected by electromyography

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity non-joint

\- CT upper extremity when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\#### Indeterminate bone lesion

Medically necessary when:

\- Further characterization is needed after nondiagnostic radiographs

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity (joint or non-joint)

\- MRI lower extremity preferred

\- CT upper extremity

\- CT lower extremity

\#### Morton's neuroma

Medically necessary for presurgical planning when ALL of the following
apply:

\- Ultrasound cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\- Failure of at least 6 weeks of conservative management

\- Conservative management included:

\- Focused steroid injection

\- Foot orthoses

Imaging:

\- MRI lower extremity

\- CT lower extremity when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\#### Pigmented villonodular synovitis

Medically necessary when:

\- Imaging results are essential to establish diagnosis or direct
management

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity joint

\- MRI lower extremity

\- CT upper or lower extremity when MRI cannot be performed or is
nondiagnostic

\#### Soft tissue mass, not otherwise specified

Medically necessary in ANY of the following:

\- Superficial or palpable non-popliteal mass after nondiagnostic
radiograph or ultrasound

\- Superficial or palpable popliteal mass after nondiagnostic
radiographs and ultrasound

\- Soft tissue evaluation when radiograph shows prominent or unexplained
calcifications

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity (joint or non-joint)

\- MRI lower extremity

\- CT upper or lower extremity when MRI cannot be performed or is
nondiagnostic

\### F. Conditions of the Upper Extremity

\#### Adhesive capsulitis (adult only)

Medically necessary when BOTH of the following are met:

\- Radiographs are nondiagnostic

\- Manipulation under anesthesia or arthroscopic lysis of adhesions is
planned

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity joint

\- CT upper extremity

\#### Biceps or triceps tendon rupture

Medically necessary when:

\- Imaging is essential to establish diagnosis or direct management

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity joint

\#### Brachial plexopathy

Medically necessary for diagnosis and management

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity non-joint

\- CT upper extremity when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\#### Entrapment neuropathy (excluding carpal and cubital tunnel)

Medically necessary when ALL of the following are met:

\- Diagnosis confirmed by electromyography

\- Failure of conservative management, unless there is objective
weakness on examination

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity joint

\- CT upper extremity when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\#### Labral tear - shoulder

Medically necessary for suspected labral tear in ANY of the following:

\- History of shoulder dislocation or recurrent subluxation with
persistent pain and/or instability

\- Acute trauma with:

\- Evidence of suprascapular nerve entrapment, or

\- Radiographic suspicion of bony Bankart lesion

\- Pain with at least one physical exam finding of SLAP tear, plus:

\- Nondiagnostic radiograph

\- Failure of at least 6 weeks of conservative management

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity joint

\- CT upper extremity when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\#### Ligament and tendon injuries - wrist

Medically necessary after nondiagnostic radiographs in ANY of the
following:

\- Suspected scapholunate ligament tear

\- Acute triangular fibrocartilage complex tear

\- Chronic triangular fibrocartilage complex tear with failure of at
least 6 weeks of conservative management

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity joint

\- CT when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\#### Rotator cuff tear

Medically necessary for new or recurrent tear when ALL of the following
apply:

\- Radiographs or ultrasound are nondiagnostic

\- At least one positive sign supports rotator cuff tear

\- EITHER:

\- At least one positive sign of complete tear, or

\- Failure of at least 6 weeks of conservative management

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity joint

\- CT upper extremity when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\#### Ulnar collateral ligament tear (elbow or thumb)

Medically necessary for diagnosis and management

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity joint

\- MRI upper extremity non-joint

\-\--

\### G. Conditions of the Lower Extremity

\#### Labral tear and femoroacetabular impingement - hip

Medically necessary when ALL of the following are met:

\- Hip pain or positive impingement on exam

\- Radiographs are nondiagnostic without severe osteoarthritis, or
normal

\- Failure of at least 6 weeks of conservative management

Imaging:

\- MRI lower extremity

\- CT lower extremity when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\#### Legg-Calve-Perthes disease (pediatric only)

Medically necessary when:

\- Radiographs are nondiagnostic

Imaging:

\- MRI lower extremity

\- CT lower extremity when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\#### Ligament tear - knee

Medically necessary after nondiagnostic radiographs or ultrasound in ANY
of the following:

\- Failure of at least 4 weeks of conservative management

\- Preoperative evaluation when ANY of the following exam tests are
positive:

\- Anterior drawer

\- Posterior drawer

\- Lachman

\- Medial or lateral stress suggesting grade 3 sprain

\- Pivot shift

\- Postoperative evaluation after ligament or tendon repair when new
symptoms are present

Imaging:

\- MRI lower extremity

\- CT lower extremity when MRI cannot be performed

\#### Ligament and tendon injury or rupture - foot and ankle

Medically necessary in EITHER of the following:

\- Suspected full-thickness ligament or tendon rupture

\- Radiographs or ultrasound are nondiagnostic and patient completed at
least 4 weeks of:

\- Protected weight-bearing, or

\- Immobilization, or

\- Strapping

Imaging:

\- MRI lower extremity

\#### Lisfranc injury

Medically necessary when:

\- Radiographs are nondiagnostic

\- Imaging is essential to establish diagnosis or direct management

Imaging:

\- MRI lower extremity

\- CT lower extremity

\#### Meniscal tear or injury

Medically necessary after nondiagnostic radiographs in EITHER of the
following:

\- Knee pain with locking, catching, or instability AND at least TWO of:

\- Joint swelling or effusion

\- Positive McMurray or Apley test

\- Joint line tenderness

\- Reduced range of motion

\- Knee pain with at least ONE physical exam finding of meniscal tear
and failure of at least 6 weeks of conservative management

Imaging:

\- MRI lower extremity

\- CT lower extremity when MRI cannot be performed

\#### Slipped capital femoral epiphysis (pediatric only)

Medically necessary when:

\- Radiographs are nondiagnostic

Imaging:

\- MRI lower extremity

\- CT lower extremity when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\#### Tarsal tunnel

Medically necessary when BOTH of the following are met:

\- Diagnosis confirmed by electromyography and nerve conduction study

\- Failure of at least 4 weeks of conservative management

Imaging:

\- MRI lower extremity

\- CT lower extremity when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\### I. Pain, Unspecified

\#### Non-specific hip pain, including limping child (pediatric only)

Medically necessary in EITHER of the following:

\- Patient under age 5 with nondiagnostic radiographs and ultrasound

\- Patient age 5 or older with nondiagnostic radiographs and failure of
conservative management

Imaging:

\- MRI lower extremity

\- CT lower extremity when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\#### Lower extremity pain, not otherwise specified

Medically necessary when BOTH of the following are met:

\- Radiographs are nondiagnostic without severe osteoarthritis, or
normal

\- Failure of at least 6 weeks of conservative management

Imaging:

\- MRI lower extremity

\- CT lower extremity when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\#### Upper extremity pain, not otherwise specified

Medically necessary when BOTH of the following are met:

\- Radiographs are nondiagnostic without severe osteoarthritis, or
normal

\- Failure of at least 6 weeks of conservative management

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity joint or non-joint

\- CT upper extremity when MRI cannot be performed or is nondiagnostic

\### J. Procedural Imaging

\#### Shoulder arthroplasty, presurgical planning

Not indicated for:

\- Robotic-assisted shoulder arthroplasty

Medically necessary in ANY of the following:

\- Preoperative assessment of bone stock and bone version before
shoulder arthroplasty

\- Assessment of rotator cuff status to determine surgical approach

Imaging:

\- MRI upper extremity joint for rotator cuff status

\- CT upper extremity joint for bone stock and bone version

\#### Knee arthroplasty, presurgical planning

Not indicated for:

\- Conventional total knee arthroplasty

May be medically necessary for patient-specific instrumentation or
robotic-assisted navigation when BOTH of the following are met:

\- Existing hardware prevents use of medullary guides

\- Radiographs show femoral or tibial diaphyseal deformity preventing
use of an external guide

Imaging:

\- CT lower extremity

\- MRI lower extremity

\#### Perioperative imaging, including delayed hardware failure, not
otherwise specified

Not indicated for:

\- Conventional hip arthroplasty

\- Robotic-assisted hip arthroplasty

Medically necessary when:

\- Radiographs or ultrasound are nondiagnostic

Imaging:

\- CT upper or lower extremity

\- MRI upper extremity (joint or non-joint)

\- MRI lower extremity

\## Required Documentation

\### Core documentation expected for most requests

\- Relevant history

\- Physical examination

\- Working diagnosis or clinical suspicion

\- Pretest likelihood / medical rationale

\- Relevant laboratory studies when applicable

\- Prior imaging and diagnostic test results

\- Response to prior treatment or intervention

\- Clinical intent of the ordering provider

\### Documentation specific to conservative management

\- Type and duration of physical therapy

\- Home exercise details if used

\- Confirmation of patient participation and compliance

\- Complementary conservative treatments tried

\- Dates and duration of treatment

\- Reevaluation findings showing lack of improvement or worsening

\- Reason more invasive treatment is being considered

\### Additional documentation when applicable

\- Radiograph results

\- Ultrasound results

\- Arthrocentesis results

\- Electromyography results

\- Nerve conduction study results

\- Ultrasound failure or inability to perform

\- Biopsy need or biopsy localization need

\- Surgical planning indication

\- New postoperative symptoms

\- Mechanical symptoms

\- Positive physical exam findings listed in condition-specific criteria

\- Contraindication to MRI when CT is requested

\- Contraindication to arthrocentesis when applicable

\- Reason an initially preferred imaging modality cannot be performed

\## Limitations and Exclusions

\### General exclusions and limits

\- Indications, diagnoses, or imaging modalities not specifically
addressed are considered not medically necessary

\- Imaging findings unrelated to the clinical question must be
interpreted in clinical context

\- Conventional radiographs should generally be obtained before advanced
imaging

\- Documentation of compliance with therapy may be required

\- Exceptions are case-by-case and must be clearly documented

\### Situations that often trigger extra review or peer discussion

\- Multiple imaging or therapeutic requests submitted at the same time

\- Repeated testing of the same area for the same indication without
clinical change

\- Repeated testing due to technical issues

\- Repeated testing at different facilities due to preference or quality
concerns

\- Repeated testing by different providers over a short period

\- Repeated therapeutic intervention before expected duration of relief
has lapsed

\### Specific non-indications

\- Robotic-assisted shoulder arthroplasty: advanced imaging not
indicated

\- Conventional total knee arthroplasty: advanced imaging not indicated

\- Conventional hip arthroplasty: advanced imaging not indicated in
perioperative section

\- Robotic-assisted hip arthroplasty: advanced imaging not indicated in
perioperative section

\### Modality limitations

\- CT limitations:

\- Ionizing radiation

\- Iodinated contrast risks

\- MRI limitations:

\- Implanted devices may prevent imaging

\- Claustrophobia

\- Sedation issues in some pediatric patients

\- Gadolinium may be unsuitable in advanced renal disease

\- Hardware artifact may limit imaging quality

\## Key Practical Summary

\- Most extremity advanced imaging requests require prior radiographs,
conservative care, and clinical reevaluation

\- MRI is generally preferred for soft tissue, marrow, infection,
internal derangement, and many occult processes

\- CT is often used when MRI is contraindicated, nondiagnostic, or when
bony detail is critical

\- PET use is very limited and specifically referenced for chronic
osteomyelitis

\- The document provides extensive CPT/HCPCS imaging codes but no
specific ICD-10 list

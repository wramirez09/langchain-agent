Medical Necessity Criteria General clinical review requirements in the
document include the following baseline elements for any requested
diagnostic or therapeutic intervention:

-   The diagnosis must be confirmed, or the pretest likelihood
    > established, through a complete clinical evaluation.

    > Evaluation should include:

    -   History

        > Physical examination

        > Relevant laboratory review, when applicable

        > Prior diagnostic testing review, when applicable

        > Response to prior therapy, when applicable

    > Expected benefit should outweigh potential harm.

    > Current practice guidelines, literature, or standards of care
    > should support the requested intervention.

    > There should be a reasonable likelihood that the intervention will
    > change management or improve outcome.

Specific policy determination for this document:

-   Polygenic risk scores

    -   Not medically necessary for all indications.

    > Polygenic expression prognostic testing

    -   Not medically necessary for all indications unless another
        > genetic testing guideline specifically indicates otherwise.

    > Multivariable prognostic genetic testing

    -   Not medically necessary for all indications.

Important supporting limitations underlying the non-medical-necessity
determination include:

-   Polygenic risk score results are predictive/statistical, not
    > diagnostic.

    > A low polygenic risk score does not exclude meaningful disease
    > risk.

    > Predictive value may be poor when derived from populations unlike
    > the tested patient.

    > Polygenic risk scores are not appropriate when a monogenic
    > disorder is suspected.

    > Evidence is currently insufficient to support use of polygenic
    > risk scores to guide medical management.

    > Large prospective clinical trials are still needed to establish
    > clinical utility for many of these tests.

Required Documentation The document does not give a long test-specific
checklist, but it does state that clinical documentation may be required
and must meet these standards:

-   Documentation must accurately reflect the patient's clinical status
    > at the time of the request.

    > Documentation must clearly support the ordering clinician's
    > intent.

    > Clinical records should support:

    -   The diagnosis or pretest likelihood

        > Relevant history and physical findings

        > Pertinent laboratory and diagnostic results

        > Prior treatment and response, when applicable

        > Why the requested service is expected to affect management or
        > outcome

Limitations and Exclusions Core exclusions in this policy are explicit:

-   Polygenic risk scores are excluded as not medically necessary for
    > all uses.

    > Polygenic expression prognostic tests are excluded as not
    > medically necessary for all uses unless separately addressed in
    > another applicable genetic testing guideline.

    > Multivariable prognostic genetic tests are excluded as not
    > medically necessary for all uses.

    > Genetic tests not specifically mentioned in the guideline are
    > considered not medically necessary.

Additional limitations noted in the document include:

-   Multiple tests ordered at the same time may require additional
    > review or peer-to-peer discussion.

    > Repeat testing of the same anatomic location for the same
    > indication is generally limited to:

    -   Post-intervention reassessment

        > A meaningful change in clinical status

        > Clarification of an earlier finding using a different
        > technique or protocol

    > Repeat testing may face additional review when:

    -   The repeat is due to technical problems

        > A different facility is requested because of provider
        > preference or quality concerns

        > Symptoms persist without clinical change, treatment, or
        > intervention since prior testing

        > Multiple providers request repeat testing in a short time
        > frame

Evidence-related limitations highlighted by condition/test type include:

-   Hematuria/bladder cancer:

    -   Urine-based tumor markers are not recommended for initial
        > microhematuria evaluation.

        > Available study data are limited by missing data and follow-up
        > issues.

    > Melanoma:

    -   Some classifiers show diagnostic performance, but clinical
        > utility has not been established.

    > Lung cancer:

    -   Biomarker approaches have inadequate predictive performance at
        > this time.

    > Prostate cancer:

    -   Existing assays lack strong prospective outcome data,
        > standardized cutoffs, and routine endorsement in national
        > guidelines.

    > Multivariable prognostic genetic testing:

    -   Clinical utility for prognosis has not been proven.

Concise Summary This document is a non-coverage style guideline for
predictive and prognostic polygenic testing. The main takeaway is
straightforward: the three major categories of testing addressed here
are considered not medically necessary for all indications, except where
another separate genetic testing guideline expressly allows a subset of
polygenic expression testing. The document requires standard clinical
documentation supporting diagnosis, clinical context, and intended use,
but it does not provide any positive test-specific approval pathway
within this policy. It also emphasizes limitations around simultaneous
ordering, repeat testing, and the current lack of proven clinical
utility for many of these assays.

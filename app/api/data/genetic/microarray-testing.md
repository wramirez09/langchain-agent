Medical Necessity Criteria

-   General clinical appropriateness requirements

    -   Before any intervention, the clinician should confirm the
        > diagnosis or establish the pretest likelihood through a
        > complete patient evaluation.

        > Evaluation should include:

        -   History

            > Physical examination

            > Relevant laboratory studies, when applicable

            > Relevant diagnostic testing, when applicable

            > Response to prior therapeutic intervention, when
            > applicable

        > The anticipated benefit of the requested intervention should
        > outweigh potential harms.

        > Current clinical literature, accepted practice standards, or
        > treatment guidelines should support the intervention.

        > There should be a reasonable likelihood that the intervention
        > will:

        -   Change management, and/or

            > Improve patient outcomes

    > Genetic counseling recommendations

    -   Counseling is encouraged before chromosomal microarray analysis.

        > Counseling should include all of the following:

        -   Interpretation of personal and family medical history for
            > disease risk assessment

            > Education on inheritance patterns

            > Education on genetic testing

            > Education on disease management

            > Education on prevention and risk reduction

            > Education on available resources

            > Counseling that supports informed decision-making

            > Counseling addressing adaptation to the risk or presence
            > of a genetic condition

            > Counseling on the psychological aspects of testing

        > Counseling should also address:

        -   Testing limitations

            > That a negative result does not mean hereditary risk is
            > zero or low

            > The possibility of incidental findings

            > The possibility of inconclusive findings, including
            > variants of uncertain significance

            > That variant interpretation may change over time and
            > results may later be reclassified

        > Post-test counseling should be performed for any genetic test
        > result

    > General requirements for chromosomal microarray analysis

    -   Diagnostic testing using chromosomal microarray analysis should
        > occur only once per fetus per pregnancy.

    > Prenatal evaluation: medically necessary when ANY of the following
    > are present

    -   Structural fetal anomaly noted on ultrasound

        > Fetal demise (stillbirth)

        > History of 2 or more miscarriages

        > Individual undergoing invasive diagnostic testing due to:

        -   Advanced maternal age, or

            > Positive findings on other screening tests

    > Postnatal/pediatric evaluation: medically necessary as a
    > first-line test in the initial postnatal evaluation when ANY of
    > the following are present

    -   Multiple congenital anomalies without an established diagnosis

        > Congenital or early onset epilepsy before age 3 years without
        > suspected environmental causes

        > Autism spectrum disorder with no identifiable cause

        > Developmental delay with no identifiable cause

        > Intellectual disability with no identifiable cause

        > Early neonatal death up to 7 days after birth

Required Documentation

-   Clinical documentation may be required to support the request.

    > Documentation should:

    -   Accurately reflect the clinical situation at the time of the
        > requested service

        > Clearly document the ordering provider's clinical intent

    > The submitted record should support the general clinical
    > appropriateness requirements, including:

    -   Relevant history

        > Physical examination findings

        > Relevant laboratory results, when applicable

        > Relevant prior diagnostic testing, when applicable

        > Response to prior treatment or intervention, when applicable

        > Reason the test is expected to affect management or improve
        > outcomes

    > Indication-specific documentation should support the qualifying
    > clinical scenario, such as:

    -   Prenatal structural anomaly on ultrasound

        > Fetal demise or history of recurrent miscarriage

        > Advanced maternal age or abnormal screening prompting invasive
        > testing

        > Multiple congenital anomalies

        > Early onset epilepsy without suspected environmental cause

        > Autism spectrum disorder without identifiable cause

        > Developmental delay or intellectual disability without
        > identifiable cause

        > Early neonatal death within 7 days of birth

    > Counseling documentation is not explicitly mandated as a separate
    > submission item, but the policy encourages documentation of
    > pre-test counseling and states that post-test counseling should
    > occur for any result.

Limitations and Exclusions

-   Chromosomal microarray analysis should occur only once per fetus per
    > pregnancy.

    > If chromosomal microarray analysis was already performed
    > prenatally, repeating it postnatally is not medically necessary.

    > Genetic tests not specifically mentioned in the guideline are
    > considered not medically necessary.

    > Optical genome mapping is considered not medically necessary for
    > both prenatal and postnatal evaluation.

    > Repeated testing may be subject to additional review or
    > peer-to-peer discussion in situations such as:

    -   Repeated testing at the same facility due to technical issues

        > Repeated testing at a different facility due to provider
        > preference or quality concerns

        > Repeated testing for persistent symptoms without clinical
        > change, treatment, or intervention

        > Repeated testing of the same area by different providers over
        > a short period

    > Requests for multiple diagnostic or therapeutic interventions at
    > the same time may require additional review or peer-to-peer
    > discussion.

    > Chromosomal microarray analysis has technical limitations,
    > including:

    -   It will not detect all pathogenic variants associated with a
        > syndrome

        > It will not detect genetic events that do not affect relative
        > copy number

        > It cannot detect molecularly balanced chromosomal
        > rearrangements

        > It may not fully clarify the chromosomal mechanism of
        > imbalance

Concise Summary

-   Chromosomal microarray analysis is medically necessary for specific
    > prenatal and postnatal/pediatric indications when clinical
    > criteria are met.

    > Prenatal indications include structural fetal anomalies, fetal
    > demise, recurrent miscarriage history, and invasive diagnostic
    > testing prompted by advanced maternal age or abnormal screening.

    > Postnatal/pediatric indications include multiple congenital
    > anomalies, early onset epilepsy, idiopathic autism spectrum
    > disorder, idiopathic developmental delay or intellectual
    > disability, and early neonatal death.

    > The test is generally limited to one prenatal test per fetus per
    > pregnancy, and repeat postnatal testing is not medically necessary
    > if prenatal testing was already completed.

    > The policy emphasizes complete clinical evaluation,
    > indication-specific support, and adequate provider documentation.

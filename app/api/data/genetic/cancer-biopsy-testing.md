MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

-   General clinical appropriateness requirements

    -   Diagnosis or pretest likelihood must be established from a
        > complete patient evaluation.

        > Evaluation should include:

        -   History and physical exam

            > Relevant laboratory studies

            > Relevant diagnostic testing

            > Response to prior therapy, when applicable

        > Expected benefit must outweigh potential harm.

        > Current literature, accepted guidelines, or standards of care
        > must support the test.

        > The test must be reasonably likely to change management or
        > improve outcome.

    > General requirements for genetic liquid biopsy

    -   Testing must have established analytical validity.

        > Testing must have established clinical validity.

        > Testing must be performed in an appropriately certified
        > laboratory.

        > Repeat testing of the same person for the same indication
        > should generally be limited to:

        -   Evaluation after an intervention, or

            > A change in clinical status requiring new management
            > decisions, or

            > Clarification of a prior result using a different
            > technique/protocol

        > Repeat testing may trigger added review or peer discussion if:

        -   The same tumor site is retested without clinical change,
            > treatment, or intervention

            > The same tumor is retested by different providers over a
            > short period

    > General medical necessity criteria for liquid biopsy

    -   Liquid biopsy may be medically necessary for an individual with
        > invasive malignancy when needed for treatment selection and
        > all of the following are met:

        -   A specific cancer treatment is being considered that matches
            > an FDA companion diagnostic indication

            > There is not enough tumor tissue for NGS-based somatic
            > profiling, or tissue biopsy is unsafe or infeasible

            > The patient has not already had prior testing for the
            > targeted gene(s) in the relevant clinical scenario

            > Other tumor testing results or clinical criteria do not
            > already support the therapy being considered

            > Clinical decision-making uses the known or predicted
            > effect of the genomic alteration on protein
            > function/expression and supporting clinical evidence

            > The test is reasonably targeted in scope

            > The test has clinical utility, meaning the result is
            > expected to meaningfully affect care and improve net
            > health outcomes

            > In addition, at least one of the following must apply:

            -   Biomarker-linked therapy is FDA-approved or supported by
                > NCCN category 2A for that cancer scenario and is being
                > considered soon

                > A therapy is being considered that has biomarker-based
                > contraindications or exclusions

                > The health plan has a drug-specific policy requiring
                > focused biomarker testing

    > Site-specific criteria

    -   Lung carcinoma

        -   For pathologically confirmed locally advanced stage IIIb,
            > recurrent, or metastatic non-small cell lung cancer

            > All of the following must be met:

            -   Insufficient tissue for NGS-based somatic profiling, or
                > tissue biopsy is unsafe or infeasible

                > No prior NGS-based somatic profiling for that NSCLC
                > diagnosis

                > Testing is being used to identify currently actionable
                > variants to guide management at diagnosis or at
                > progression on/after chemotherapy or immunotherapy

        > Biliary tract carcinoma

        -   For pathologically confirmed locally advanced, recurrent, or
            > metastatic biliary tract carcinoma

            > All of the following must be met:

            -   Insufficient tissue for NGS-based somatic profiling, or
                > tissue biopsy is unsafe or infeasible

                > No prior NGS-based somatic profiling for that biliary
                > tract cancer diagnosis

                > Testing is being used to identify currently actionable
                > variants to guide management at diagnosis or at
                > progression on/after chemotherapy or immunotherapy

        > Breast carcinoma

        -   For metastatic breast cancer when testing may identify
            > candidates for pathway-targeted treatment

            > Includes somatic testing for PIK3CA, AKT1, PTEN, and/or
            > ESR1

            > All of the following must be met:

            -   ER-positive and HER2-negative metastatic breast cancer

                > Candidate for near-term drug treatment aligned with
                > FDA label or NCCN 2A

                > No prior testing for the targeted gene(s) in the
                > metastatic setting

                > Insufficient tissue for NGS-based profiling, or tissue
                > biopsy is unsafe or infeasible

        > Prostate carcinoma

        -   For metastatic adenocarcinoma of the prostate when testing
            > may identify candidates for a PARP inhibitor or PD-1
            > inhibitor

            > All of the following must be met:

            -   Biopsy-proven adenocarcinoma of the prostate

                > No prior NGS testing in the metastatic setting

                > Candidate for one of the following:

                -   FDA-approved PARP inhibitor, or

                    > FDA-approved PD-1 inhibitor

                > Insufficient tissue for NGS-based profiling, or tissue
                > biopsy is unsafe or infeasible

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

-   Clinical records supporting the request should show:

    -   Confirmed diagnosis or strong pretest clinical suspicion

        > History and physical examination

        > Relevant laboratory results

        > Relevant prior diagnostic testing

        > Prior treatment history and response, if applicable

        > Current clinical status and reason the test is being requested

        > Why the result will affect treatment selection or management

        > Why tissue is insufficient, unavailable, unsafe to obtain, or
        > infeasible to obtain

        > Whether prior NGS or prior targeted gene testing has already
        > been performed

        > For site-specific requests, pathology confirmation where
        > required:

        -   NSCLC

            > Biliary tract carcinoma

            > Prostate adenocarcinoma

        > Evidence that the patient is a candidate for the proposed
        > targeted therapy when required

        > Ordering provider's clinical intent

    > Documentation submitted must:

    -   Accurately reflect the clinical situation at the time of request

        > Sufficiently document why the requested service is intended

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

-   Not medically necessary

    -   Genetic tests not specifically addressed in the guideline

        > Liquid biopsy for individuals without invasive malignancy when
        > used for:

        -   Early initial cancer diagnosis

            > Cancer screening

            > Multi-cancer early detection screening

        > Liquid biopsy for minimal residual disease assessment in
        > invasive solid tumors during or after treatment

    > Repeat testing limitations

    -   Repeated testing of the same indication is generally limited to
        > post-intervention reassessment, change in clinical status, or
        > clarification of prior findings

        > Added review or peer discussion may be needed for:

        -   Repeat testing without clinical change

            > Repeat testing by different providers over a short time

            > Repeat testing at the same facility due to technical
            > issues

            > Repeat testing at a different facility due to provider
            > preference or quality concerns

            > Persistent symptoms without interval change, treatment, or
            > intervention

    > Simultaneous ordering limitations

    -   Multiple diagnostic or therapeutic interventions ordered at the
        > same time may require added review or peer discussion

        > This is especially true if the need for later testing depends
        > on the result of the first test

    > Additional practical limitations noted in the policy discussion

    -   Tissue testing remains important when available, especially when
        > liquid biopsy is negative in some settings

        > ctDNA assays may have reduced sensitivity in some tumors or
        > low-shedding settings

        > False interpretation may occur due to clonal hematopoiesis or
        > non-target sources of DNA

        > Negative liquid biopsy results may not exclude actionable
        > findings if tissue is available

BOTTOM LINE

-   Covered use is limited mainly to treatment-selection scenarios in
    > invasive cancer when tissue is insufficient or unsafe/infeasible
    > and when results are expected to directly affect therapy.

    > The strongest policy support is for selected lung, biliary tract,
    > metastatic breast, and metastatic prostate cancer scenarios.

    > Screening use and MRD use are excluded.

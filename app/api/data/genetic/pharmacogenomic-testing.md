1.  MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

General clinical appropriateness requirements

-   Before any intervention, the clinician should confirm the diagnosis
    > or establish pretest likelihood through a complete evaluation.

    > The evaluation should include:

    -   History and physical examination

        > Review of relevant laboratory studies, when applicable

        > Review of relevant diagnostic testing, when applicable

        > Review of response to prior therapeutic intervention, when
        > applicable

    > The expected benefit of the intervention should outweigh potential
    > harms.

    > Current treatment guidelines, clinical literature, and standards
    > of care should support the intervention.

    > The intervention should be reasonably likely to change management
    > or improve outcomes.

Specific pharmacogenetic testing criteria Genotyping is considered
medically necessary only when all of the following are met:

-   The medication being evaluated is the most appropriate treatment for
    > the patient's underlying condition.

    > The pharmacogenetic test has established analytical validity,
    > clinical validity, and clinical utility for that patient.

    > Testing is focused on specific polymorphisms relevant to the
    > patient's condition and expected treatment.

    > The test is referenced in the applicable FDA drug labeling or in
    > NCCN guidelines.

3\. REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

The policy specifically indicates that supporting clinical documentation
may be required. Required documentation includes:

-   Documentation that accurately reflects the clinical situation at the
    > time of the request

    > Documentation that clearly shows the ordering provider's clinical
    > intent

    > Complete clinical evaluation information, including:

    -   History and physical examination

        > Relevant lab results

        > Relevant diagnostic testing

        > Prior treatment response, if applicable

    > Additional documentation may be required when nonspecific or
    > unlisted procedure codes are used.

4\. LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

General limitations

-   Pharmacogenetic testing is generally not medically necessary unless
    > specifically identified otherwise in the policy.

    > Genetic tests not specifically mentioned in the guideline are
    > considered not medically necessary.

    > The code list is not all-inclusive.

    > Authorization requirements may vary by health plan.

    > Use of a specific code does not by itself establish medical
    > necessity.

Simultaneous ordering limitations

-   Requests for multiple diagnostic or therapeutic interventions at the
    > same time may require additional review or peer-to-peer
    > discussion.

    > This is especially relevant when the need for one intervention
    > depends on the results of another.

    > Additional review may occur when one requested intervention
    > appears more appropriate than the others.

Repeat diagnostic testing limitations

-   Repeat testing of the same area for the same indication should
    > generally be limited to:

    -   Evaluation after an intervention, or

        > A change in clinical status requiring further testing

    > Repeat testing may require additional review or peer-to-peer
    > discussion when:

    -   The repeat is due to technical issues at the same facility

        > The repeat is requested at a different facility due to
        > provider preference or quality concerns

        > Symptoms persist but there has been no clinical change,
        > treatment, or intervention since the prior study

        > Different providers order repeated testing for the same member
        > over a short time period

Repeat therapeutic intervention limitations

-   Repeat therapeutic intervention in the same area is appropriate only
    > when the prior intervention was beneficial and the expected
    > duration of benefit has ended.

    > A repeat intervention before the expected duration of benefit ends
    > is not appropriate unless it can be confirmed that the prior
    > intervention was never administered.

    > Ongoing services may depend on completion of previously authorized
    > services when patient response is relevant to medical necessity.

Condition-specific exclusions or cautions noted in the rationale

-   Psychiatric pharmacogenetic panel testing is described as lacking
    > established clinical utility in most settings and is therefore not
    > medically necessary unless specifically provided for in the
    > policy.

    > APOE ε4 testing is not recommended for routine evaluation of
    > cognitive impairment; in this policy context, it is used to help
    > inform whether to initiate certain Alzheimer's therapies, not for
    > routine monitoring.

    > For DPYD testing:

    -   Discussion with patients is encouraged before fluoropyrimidine
        > therapy

        > Testing may help reduce toxicity risk

        > Dose reduction based on genotype may reduce toxicity, but
        > effect on efficacy remains uncertain

        > No specific test is endorsed in the policy text as universally
        > preferred

5\. PRACTICAL BOTTOM LINE

-   Covered pharmacogenetic testing under this policy is narrowly
    > limited to specific biomarker and drug pairs.

    > Approval depends on both general clinical appropriateness
    > requirements and the policy's drug-specific testing criteria.

    > The policy does not provide specific ICD-10 diagnosis codes.

    > Most broad multigene pharmacogenomic panels and many
    > psychiatry-related pharmacogenetic tests are listed as not
    > medically necessary.

    > Clear clinical documentation and alignment with the listed
    > biomarker-drug indications are central to medical necessity
    > review.

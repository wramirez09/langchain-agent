MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

General clinical appropriateness requirements

-   Before testing, the clinician should confirm the diagnosis or
    > pretest likelihood based on a complete evaluation, including
    > history, physical exam, and when relevant, laboratory studies,
    > prior diagnostic testing, and response to prior intervention.

    > The expected benefit of testing should outweigh potential harms.

    > Current literature, accepted practice standards, or treatment
    > guidelines should support the requested intervention.

    > The intervention should be reasonably likely to change management
    > or improve outcome.

General recommendations

-   Shared decision-making is expected.

    > Counseling is encouraged before reproductive carrier screening and
    > should address:

    -   Family and medical history-based risk assessment

        > Inheritance patterns

        > Severity of screened conditions

        > Potential need for prenatal diagnostic confirmation if both
        > partners are carriers

        > Informed choice and psychosocial implications

        > That positive carrier results are common and usually do not
        > affect the individual's own health

        > That partner testing is recommended if one partner is a
        > carrier for an autosomal recessive condition

        > That incidental findings may occur

        > That a negative result reduces but does not eliminate risk

    > Post-test counseling should be performed for at-risk individuals
    > or couples.

Clinical indications

1.  Repeat carrier screening

-   Limited to adults

    > Allowed only once per lifetime for a given condition

2\. Standard carrier screening: cystic fibrosis and spinal muscular
atrophy

-   Medically necessary for:

    -   All pregnant individuals

        > Any individual considering reproduction

3\. Standard carrier screening: hemoglobinopathies

-   Medically necessary for:

    -   All pregnant individuals

        > Any individual considering reproduction

    > Additional condition:

    -   No prior hemoglobin electrophoresis and/or HBA1/HBA2 and HBB
        > testing results are available for interpretation

4\. Expanded carrier screening: multigene or single-gene screening

-   Medically necessary only when all of the following are met:

    -   At least one of these applies:

        -   One or both individuals have ancestry associated with
            > increased risk for certain conditions other than cystic
            > fibrosis, spinal muscular atrophy, and hemoglobinopathies

            > One or both individuals lack access to biological family
            > history, such as due to adoption or use of a reproductive
            > donor, and this is documented in the medical record

            > The individual and reproductive partner are known or
            > suspected to be consanguineous, and this is documented in
            > the medical record

        > The condition or conditions on the test generally have at
        > least a 1 in 100 carrier frequency

        > The disorder or disorders have established gene-disease
        > clinical validity

        > Pathogenic variants in the tested gene or genes are associated
        > with significant morbidity and/or mortality

        > The test has adequate sensitivity and specificity for clinical
        > decision-making

        > Results may be used for pregnancy management, management of an
        > affected fetus or child, or family planning

    > Special note:

    -   For consanguineous partnerships, conditions on multigene panels
        > may have carrier frequencies below 1 in 100.

5\. Condition-specific carrier testing based on family history

-   Medically necessary when any one of the following is met:

    -   The individual has a previously affected child with the
        > condition being evaluated

        > Either partner has a first-, second-, or third-degree relative
        > who is affected with, or is a documented carrier of, the
        > condition being evaluated

        > The reproductive partner of the individual being tested has a
        > pathogenic or likely pathogenic variant in the relevant gene

6\. Fragile X premutation carrier testing

-   Medically necessary in either of the following:

    -   Individual assigned female sex at birth with a family history of
        > fragile X-related disorders or intellectual disability
        > suggestive of fragile X syndrome, and is pregnant or
        > considering pregnancy

        > Individual assigned female sex at birth with unexplained
        > ovarian insufficiency or ovarian failure, or elevated FSH
        > before age 40

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

Core documentation

-   Clinical documentation supporting the request must:

    -   Accurately reflect the clinical situation at the time of service

        > Clearly document the ordering clinician's intent

Situation-specific documentation typically needed to support medical
necessity

-   Pregnancy status or documentation that the individual is considering
    > reproduction

    > Prior test history for hemoglobinopathy screening, specifically
    > whether hemoglobin electrophoresis and/or HBA1/HBA2 and HBB
    > results are available for interpretation

    > Documented ancestry associated with increased carrier risk, when
    > used to justify expanded screening

    > Documentation that biological family history is unavailable due to
    > adoption or reproductive donor use, when used to justify expanded
    > screening

    > Documentation of known or suspected consanguinity, when used to
    > justify expanded screening

    > Family history showing a previously affected child or an
    > affected/documented carrier relative in a first-, second-, or
    > third-degree relationship, when used to justify condition-specific
    > testing

    > Documentation that the reproductive partner has a pathogenic or
    > likely pathogenic variant, when used to justify targeted carrier
    > testing

    > For Fragile X testing, documentation of:

    -   Relevant family history of fragile X-related disorders or
        > suggestive intellectual disability, or

        > Unexplained ovarian insufficiency/failure, or elevated FSH
        > before age 40

Additional review triggers

-   Requests for multiple tests at the same time may require
    > peer-to-peer review.

    > Repeated testing using the same or similar technology may require
    > additional review in certain scenarios.

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

General limitations

-   Carrier screening is limited to adults and generally once per
    > lifetime per condition.

    > Genetic tests not specifically addressed in the guideline are
    > considered not medically necessary.

    > Multiple simultaneous diagnostic requests may require peer-to-peer
    > review.

    > Repeat testing may be subject to review if requested:

    -   At the same facility because of technical issues

        > At a different facility due to provider preference or quality
        > concerns

        > For the same indication without clinical change, treatment, or
        > intervention

        > By different providers over a short period of time

Specific exclusions

-   Carrier screening for autosomal dominant conditions

    > Carrier screening for adult-onset conditions

    > Cell-free DNA screening for single-gene disorders, microdeletions,
    > or other unspecified indications

    > Testing for high-frequency, low-penetrance variants such as MTHFR

    > Whole exome or whole genome assays for carrier screening

    > Molecular screening where nonmolecular screening is sufficient,
    > such as hereditary hemochromatosis

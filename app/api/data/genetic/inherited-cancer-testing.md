MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

General clinical requirements

-   Genetic testing is medically necessary only when all of the
    > following are met:

    -   The person is at significant risk for a genetic disorder, is
        > suspected of having a known genetic condition, or was
        > previously tested inadequately.

        > The relevant gene or genes are well established in the
        > literature as being associated with the condition.

        > The test has clinical utility and is expected to affect
        > management in a way that improves outcomes.

    > Confirmatory germline testing is medically necessary when the
    > above general standards are met and a pathogenic or likely
    > pathogenic variant was first identified through:

    -   an FDA-authorized direct-to-consumer result, or

        > an IRB-approved research study.

    > Testing for a known familial pathogenic or likely pathogenic
    > variant is medically necessary for first-, second-, or
    > third-degree relatives when the result has established clinical
    > utility.

Genetic counseling

-   Strongly recommended before hereditary cancer testing.

    > Recommended components:

    -   Family and medical history review with risk assessment

        > Education on inheritance, testing, disease management,
        > prevention, and risk reduction

        > Counseling to support informed decision-making

        > Discussion of testing limitations

        > Discussion that a negative result does not eliminate inherited
        > risk

        > Discussion of variants of uncertain significance

        > Discussion that variant interpretation may change over time

        > Psychological counseling related to testing

    > Post-test counseling is recommended for any diagnostic genetic
    > test result.

Condition-specific criteria

Adenomatous polyp syndromes

-   Germline testing of APC and/or MUTYH is medically necessary when any
    > of the following apply:

    -   More than 10 cumulative colorectal adenomas

        > Multifocal or bilateral congenital hypertrophy of the retinal
        > pigment epithelium

        > First- or second-degree relative with a known APC or MUTYH
        > pathogenic variant

        > First-, second-, or third-degree relative with findings
        > suggestive of inherited polyposis syndrome

Hamartomatous polyposis syndromes

-   Juvenile polyposis syndrome:

    -   Testing of SMAD4 and BMPR1A is medically necessary when any of
        > the following are present:

        -   Five or more juvenile colon polyps

            > Multiple juvenile polyps elsewhere in the GI tract

            > Any juvenile polyps plus known family history of juvenile
            > polyps

            > First- or second-degree relative of someone suspected or
            > diagnosed with juvenile polyposis syndrome

    > Peutz-Jeghers syndrome:

    -   STK11 testing is medically necessary when any of the following
        > are present:

        -   Two or more histologically confirmed Peutz-Jeghers polyps in
            > the small intestine

            > Typical mucocutaneous pigmentation

            > Family history of Peutz-Jeghers syndrome

    > Cowden syndrome:

    -   PTEN testing is medically necessary when both of the following
        > are met:

        -   At least one pathognomonic feature:

            -   Adult Lhermitte-Duclos disease, or

                > Multiple mucocutaneous lesions such as multiple
                > trichilemmomas, acral keratoses, mucocutaneous
                > neuromas, or oral papillomas

            > Three or more associated features such as:

            -   Breast cancer

                > Fibrocystic breast disease

                > Non-medullary thyroid cancer

                > Thyroid adenoma or multinodular goiter

                > Endometrial cancer

                > Renal cell carcinoma

                > Colorectal cancer

                > Genitourinary malformations or testicular lipomatosis

                > Lipomas

                > Uterine fibroids

                > GI hamartomas or ganglioneuromas

                > Autism spectrum disorder

                > Intellectual disability

                > Biopsy-proven trichilemmoma

                > Multiple palmoplantar keratoses

                > Multifocal facial papules

                > Macular pigmentation of glans penis

                > Vascular anomalies

                > Macrocephaly

Serrated polyposis syndrome

-   Genetic testing is not medically necessary for any indication.

Hereditary mixed polyposis syndrome

-   Testing for GREM1 or any other gene for this indication is not
    > medically necessary.

Lynch syndrome

-   Germline testing of MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2, or EPCAM is medically
    > necessary in any of the following:

    -   Known Lynch variant in a first- or second-degree relative

        > Personal history of a tumor with mismatch repair deficiency on
        > PCR, NGS, or IHC

        > IHC loss pattern suggesting Lynch syndrome, including:

        -   loss of MSH2 or MSH6, or both

            > loss of PMS2

            > loss of MLH1 and PMS2 without BRAF V600E or MLH1 promoter
            > methylation

        > MSI-high tumor with MLH1/PMS2 loss and no BRAF V600E or MLH1
        > promoter methylation

        > Model-based Lynch syndrome risk of 5% or greater

        > Personal history of colorectal, endometrial, or another
        > Lynch-related cancer with:

        -   diagnosis at age 49 or younger, or

            > synchronous or metachronous colorectal cancer, or

            > another Lynch-related cancer

        > Family history including:

        -   first-degree relative with colorectal or endometrial cancer
            > before age 50

            > first-degree relative with colorectal or endometrial
            > cancer plus another Lynch-related cancer

            > two or more first- or second-degree relatives on one side
            > of the family with Lynch-related cancers, with at least
            > one before age 50

            > three or more first- or second-degree relatives on one
            > side of the family with Lynch-related cancers

Li-Fraumeni syndrome

-   TP53 testing is medically necessary when any of the following apply:

    -   Breast cancer at age 30 or younger

        > Breast cancer at age 45 or younger plus:

        -   a first- or second-degree relative with a Li-Fraumeni
            > spectrum tumor before age 56, or

            > a first- or second-degree relative with multiple primary
            > cancers

        > Another Li-Fraumeni spectrum tumor at age 45 or younger plus:

        -   a first- or second-degree relative with a Li-Fraumeni
            > spectrum tumor before age 56, or

            > a first- or second-degree relative with multiple primary
            > cancers

        > Multiple tumors, with two in the Li-Fraumeni spectrum and at
        > least one before age 45

        > Personal history of adrenocortical carcinoma, choroid plexus
        > carcinoma, embryonal anaplastic rhabdomyosarcoma, or pediatric
        > hypodiploid acute lymphoblastic leukemia

        > A first-, second-, or third-degree relative with a documented
        > TP53 germline pathogenic variant and that affected relative
        > meets one of the syndrome criteria

        > TP53 pathogenic or likely pathogenic variant found on somatic
        > testing plus:

        -   the person meets clinical criteria, or

            > any cancer diagnosed at age 29 or younger

Hereditary breast, ovarian, and pancreatic cancer

Breast cancer

-   New invasive breast cancer diagnosed at age 65 or younger:

    -   Multigene panel including BRCA1 and BRCA2 is medically
        > necessary.

    > New invasive breast cancer diagnosed after age 65:

    -   Medically necessary if any of the following apply:

        -   assigned male sex at birth

            > triple-negative disease

            > multiple primary breast cancers

            > lobular breast cancer with personal or family history of
            > hereditary diffuse gastric cancer

            > Ashkenazi Jewish ancestry

            > candidate for PARP inhibitor therapy

    > No personal history of breast cancer:

    -   Medically necessary if personal or family history suggests a
        > pathogenic variant, including:

        -   personal history of epithelial ovarian cancer or pancreatic
            > adenocarcinoma

            > BRCA1/2 model risk at least 5%

            > close blood relative with early breast cancer

            > close blood relative with ovarian, fallopian tube, or
            > primary peritoneal cancer

            > close blood relative with multiple primary breast cancers

            > male breast cancer in a blood relative

            > metastatic or high-risk prostate cancer in a blood
            > relative

            > three or more blood relatives on one side with breast
            > and/or prostate cancer

            > first-degree relative with pancreatic cancer

            > Ashkenazi Jewish ancestry with qualifying breast/ovarian
            > family history

            > need for confirmatory testing after direct-to-consumer,
            > somatic, or research findings

Epithelial ovarian cancer

-   Personal history of invasive epithelial ovarian, fallopian tube, or
    > primary peritoneal cancer at any age:

    -   Multigene panel including BRCA1/2 is medically necessary.

    > No personal history of ovarian cancer:

    -   Medically necessary if:

        -   a first-, second-, or third-degree blood relative has
            > epithelial ovarian, fallopian tube, or primary peritoneal
            > cancer, or

            > BRCA1/2 model risk is at least 5%

Pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma

-   Personal history of invasive exocrine pancreatic cancer:

    -   Multigene panel including BRCA1/2 is medically necessary at any
        > age.

    > No personal history of pancreatic cancer:

    -   Medically necessary if:

        -   first-degree relative has exocrine pancreatic cancer, or

            > BRCA1/2 model risk is at least 5%

HBOP multigene panel standards

-   Additional genes beyond BRCA1/2 are medically necessary when:

    -   the panel is targeted to the person's personal and family
        > history, and

        > included genes have established pathogenic relevance and
        > management implications.

    > Examples of condition-linked genes in the document:

    -   Breast risk: BRCA1, BRCA2, CDH1, PALB2, PTEN, STK11, TP53

        > Ovarian risk: ATM, BRCA1, BRCA2, BRIP1, MLH1, MSH2, MSH6,
        > PMS2, EPCAM, PALB2, RAD51C, RAD51D

        > Pancreatic risk: ATM, BRCA1, BRCA2, CDKN2A/CDK4, MLH1, MSH2,
        > MSH6, PMS2, EPCAM, PALB2, STK11, TP53

    > Prior limited negative testing does not have to be repeated before
    > broader appropriate panel testing.

Melanoma

-   Focused testing for genes such as CDKN2A, BAP1, and CDK4 is
    > medically necessary when any of the following apply:

    -   Personal history of three or more melanomas

        > Melanoma plus exocrine pancreatic cancer

        > Melanoma plus personal or family history in two or more
        > first-degree relatives with mesothelioma, clear cell renal
        > carcinoma, or basal cell carcinoma

        > Melanoma plus astrocytoma

        > Three or more first- or second-degree relatives with melanoma
        > or pancreatic cancer

        > Invasive cutaneous melanoma plus first-degree relative with
        > pancreatic cancer

        > Melanoma and astrocytoma in two or more first-degree relatives

Nevoid basal cell carcinoma syndrome

-   Focused testing including PTCH-related genes is medically necessary
    > if any of the following thresholds are met:

    -   two major criteria, or

        > one major plus two minor criteria, or

        > three minor criteria

    > Major criteria:

    -   Multiple basal cell carcinomas out of proportion to sun exposure
        > or one before age 30, excluding post-radiotherapy lesions

        > Lamellar calcification of falx cerebri

        > Odontogenic keratocyst

        > Palmar or plantar pits

        > First-degree relative with the syndrome

    > Minor criteria:

    -   Childhood medulloblastoma

        > Lymphomesenteric or pleural cysts

        > Macrocephaly

        > Cleft lip or palate

        > Vertebral or rib anomalies on x-ray

        > Preaxial or postaxial polydactyly

        > Ovarian or cardiac fibromas

        > Ocular anomalies

Endocrine neoplasms

-   Testing of a single gene or focused panel is medically necessary
    > with personal history of any of the following:

    -   Adrenocortical carcinoma

        > Paraganglioma or pheochromocytoma

        > Duodenal or pancreatic neuroendocrine tumor

        > Type 2 gastric neuroendocrine tumor

        > Gastrointestinal stromal tumor before age 30

        > Medullary thyroid cancer

        > Parathyroid adenoma, diffuse hyperplasia, or primary
        > hyperparathyroidism before age 30

        > Multiple parathyroid adenomas or recurrent primary
        > hyperparathyroidism

        > MEN2-type clinical features such as mucosal neuromas,
        > thickened lips, medullated corneal nerve fibers, marfanoid
        > habitus

        > Family history of neuroendocrine tumors or related conditions
        > in a first-, second-, or third-degree relative plus suspicious
        > clinical features

    > Gene groups noted in the document:

    -   Pheochromocytoma/paraganglioma: FH, MAX, RET, SDHA, SDHB, SDHC,
        > SDHD, TMEM127, NF1, VHL

        > GIST: KIT, PDGFRA, NF1, SDHA, SDHAF2, SDHB, SDHC, SDHD in
        > appropriate settings

        > Medullary thyroid cancer: RET

        > Adrenocortical tumors: APC, EPCAM, MEN1, MLH1, MSH2, MSH6,
        > PMS2, TP53

Kidney cancer

-   Single-gene or targeted panel testing is medically necessary for
    > hereditary kidney cancer syndromes when any of the following are
    > present:

    -   Renal cell carcinoma at age 46 or younger

        > Renal cell carcinoma plus first- or second-degree relative
        > with renal cell carcinoma

        > Bilateral or multifocal renal tumors

        > Specific tumor features such as:

        -   multifocal papillary histology

            > Birt-Hogg-Dubé-type histology

            > hereditary leiomyomatosis-associated RCC

            > fumarate hydratase-deficient or succinate
            > dehydrogenase-deficient RCC

            > renal angiomyolipomas plus another tuberous sclerosis
            > criterion

        > Unaffected person with renal cell carcinoma in two or more
        > first- or second-degree relatives

    > Example panel genes: BAP1, FH, FLCN, MET, SDHA, SDHAF2, SDHB,
    > SDHC, SDHD, PTEN, VHL

Prostate cancer

-   Focused panel testing is medically necessary when there is a history
    > of any of the following:

    -   Metastatic, locally advanced, or high/very-high risk localized
        > prostate cancer

        > Prostate cancer before age 60 plus first-degree relative with
        > prostate cancer before age 60

        > Low- or intermediate-risk localized prostate cancer plus any
        > of:

        -   personal history of breast, pancreatic, gastric, brain,
            > melanoma, intestinal, or upper tract urothelial cancer

            > family history of early female breast cancer

            > family history of pancreatic, gastric, brain, melanoma,
            > intestinal, or endometrial cancer before age 50

            > family history of upper tract urothelial cancer

            > Ashkenazi Jewish ancestry

            > intraductal or cribriform histology

        > Family history suggesting hereditary risk, including:

        -   two or more first-degree relatives with prostate cancer

            > relative with prostate cancer before age 60 or death from
            > prostate cancer

            > BRCA1/2 model risk at least 5%

            > qualifying family history of breast, ovarian, pancreatic,
            > or prostate cancer

            > need for confirmatory testing after direct-to-consumer,
            > somatic, or research findings involving BRCA2, BRCA1,
            > CHEK2, ATM, PALB2, HOXB13, MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2, or
            > EPCAM

    > Example genes: BRCA2, BRCA1, ATM, HOXB13, MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2,
    > EPCAM

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

-   The document explicitly requires submitted clinical documentation
    > to:

    -   accurately reflect the clinical situation at the time of
        > request, and

        > clearly document the ordering clinician's intent.

    > Based on the criteria throughout the policy, the supporting record
    > set should typically include:

    -   History and physical exam

        > Relevant laboratory and diagnostic test results

        > Prior treatment/intervention history and response when
        > applicable

        > Detailed personal cancer history, including age at diagnosis
        > and multiple primary cancers

        > Detailed family history, including degree of relation, cancer
        > type, side of family, and age at diagnosis

        > Prior genetic testing reports, including prior limited testing
        > if broader panel testing is requested

        > Somatic tumor testing reports when used to justify germline
        > testing

        > Lynch workup results when applicable:

        -   IHC

            > MSI

            > BRAF V600E

            > MLH1 promoter methylation

        > Pathology reports documenting:

        -   number and type of polyps

            > tumor histology

            > multifocal or bilateral tumors

            > syndrome-specific tumor features

        > Risk model output when criteria rely on a predicted mutation
        > probability of 5% or greater

        > Evidence of a known familial pathogenic or likely pathogenic
        > variant

        > For therapy-linked criteria, documentation showing candidacy
        > for relevant targeted treatment, such as PARP inhibitor
        > therapy

        > For counseling-sensitive indications, documentation that
        > pre-test counseling and informed decision-making occurred

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

-   Genetic tests not specifically addressed in the guideline are
    > considered not medically necessary.

    > Simultaneous requests for multiple tests or interventions may
    > require additional review or peer-to-peer discussion.

    > Repeat testing of the same indication is generally limited to:

    -   post-intervention reassessment, or

        > a meaningful change in clinical status.

    > Repeat testing may be challenged when due only to:

    -   technical repeat at same facility

        > different facility preference

        > persistent symptoms without change in status

        > duplicate short-interval ordering by different providers

    > Repeat therapeutic intervention is not appropriate before expected
    > duration of benefit has elapsed unless the prior intervention was
    > never actually given.

    > FDA approval alone does not establish medical necessity or
    > guarantee reimbursement.

    > The guideline does not address plan benefits or coverage mandates
    > directly.

    > Nonspecific or miscellaneous procedure codes may require extra
    > documentation and review.

    > Condition-specific exclusions:

    -   Serrated polyposis syndrome: testing not medically necessary.

        > Hereditary mixed polyposis syndrome / GREM1-associated mixed
        > polyposis: testing not medically necessary.

        > Li-Fraumeni testing is not supported for isolated colorectal,
        > gastric, or prostate cancer without other core Li-Fraumeni
        > spectrum findings.

Medical Necessity Criteria

-   Prenatal screening using cell-free DNA (cfDNA) should occur only
    > once per fetus per pregnancy.

    > cfDNA prenatal screening is medically necessary when all of the
    > following are met:

    -   Pregnancy is a viable singleton or twin pregnancy.

        > Gestational age is 9 weeks or later.

        > Screening is for fetal aneuploidies involving:

        -   Chromosome 13

            > Chromosome 18

            > Chromosome 21

            > Chromosome X

            > Chromosome Y

    > Included medically necessary indications:

    -   Follow-up to abnormal maternal serum screening results when
        > diagnostic testing is declined.

        > Pregnancy with multiple anomalies when diagnostic testing is
        > not possible.

    > General clinical appropriateness requirements:

    -   Diagnosis or pretest likelihood must be established through a
        > complete evaluation.

        > Evaluation should include history and physical examination.

        > Where applicable, evaluation should include review of relevant
        > laboratory studies, prior diagnostic testing, and response to
        > prior therapeutic intervention.

        > Anticipated benefit of testing should outweigh potential
        > harms.

        > Current literature, accepted treatment guidelines, or
        > standards of medical practice should support the intervention.

        > There should be a reasonable likelihood that the test will
        > change management or improve outcome.

    > Genetic counseling recommendations before screening:

    -   Explain the difference between screening and diagnostic prenatal
        > genetic testing.

        > Provide risk assessment and education about aneuploidies.

        > Counsel to support informed choices and adaptation to genetic
        > risk or condition.

        > Address psychological aspects of genetic testing.

    > Post-test counseling:

    -   Should be performed for any positive cfDNA result.

        > Should be performed for any non-reportable cfDNA result.

Required Documentation

-   Clinical documentation supporting the request may be required.

    > Documentation must:

    -   Accurately reflect the patient's clinical situation at the time
        > of the request.

        > Sufficiently document the ordering provider's clinical intent.

    > Supporting documentation should include, when applicable:

    -   Relevant history and physical examination.

        > Pertinent laboratory studies.

        > Prior diagnostic testing results.

        > Response to prior interventions, if applicable.

        > Confirmation of pregnancy type:

        -   Singleton or twin

            > Viable pregnancy

        > Gestational age confirming 9 weeks or later.

        > Intended scope of screening, limited to the covered chromosome
        > targets.

        > Clinical indication for testing, such as:

        -   Abnormal maternal serum screen with diagnostic testing
            > declined

            > Multiple anomalies with diagnostic testing not possible

        > Documentation of counseling/informed decision-making.

        > Post-test counseling documentation for positive or
        > non-reportable results, when applicable.

    > Additional review or peer-to-peer discussion may be needed if
    > documentation does not establish appropriateness.

    > Nonspecific or unlisted procedure codes may require added
    > documentation and review.

Limitations and Exclusions

-   Not medically necessary clinical scenarios include:

    -   Higher-order gestations with 3 or more fetuses

        > Fetal demise

        > Co-twin demise / vanishing twin

        > Multiple fetal anomalies

        > Concurrent screening with other maternal serum biomarkers

        > Testing before 9 weeks gestation

    > Not medically necessary screening purposes include:

    -   Sex-only screening without family history of an X-linked
        > disorder

        > Single-gene screening, including examples such as CFTR, HBB,
        > SMN1, RhD, or other fetal red blood cell antigens

        > Microdeletion screening

        > Twin zygosity testing

        > Genome-wide copy number variant screening

        > Screening for aneuploidies of autosomal chromosomes other than
        > 13, 18, and 21

        > Polygenic risk assessment

    > Other policy limitations:

    -   Genetic tests not specifically mentioned in the guideline are
        > considered not medically necessary.

        > Screening tests identify increased risk only and do not
        > diagnose, confirm, or exclude disease.

        > Positive screening results should be confirmed with diagnostic
        > testing before intervention.

        > Simultaneous ordering of multiple tests may trigger additional
        > review or peer-to-peer discussion.

        > Repeat testing of the same indication using the same or
        > similar technology may be subject to additional review,
        > especially when:

        -   Repeated because of technical issues

            > Repeated at a different facility due to provider
            > preference or quality concerns

            > Repeated for persistent symptoms without clinical change

            > Repeated by different providers over a short period

    > Note in policy:

    -   Some excluded tests may have a role in care under certain
        > circumstances, but should not be routinely offered.

Concise Summary

-   Covered use is limited to once-per-pregnancy, once-per-fetus cfDNA
    > screening for viable singleton or twin pregnancies at 9 weeks
    > gestation or later for chromosomes 13, 18, 21, X, and Y.

    > Covered indications include abnormal maternal serum screen
    > follow-up when diagnostic testing is declined and certain
    > pregnancies with multiple anomalies when diagnostic testing is not
    > possible.

    > No specific ICD-10 list is provided; the policy defers to the
    > ICD-10-CM manual.

    > Covered CPT/HCPCS codes are 81403, 81420, 81479, 81507, 81599, and
    > 0327U.

    > Noncovered CPT/HCPCS codes are 81422, 0060U, 0341U, 0488U, 0489U,
    > 0494U, and 0536U.

    > Required documentation centers on complete clinical evaluation,
    > clear indication, gestational age, pregnancy viability/type,
    > provider intent, and counseling/informed decision-making.

    > Major exclusions include higher-order gestations, fetal demise,
    > vanishing twin, testing before 9 weeks, concurrent maternal serum
    > screening, sex-only testing without X-linked risk, single-gene
    > tests, microdeletions, twin zygosity, genome-wide CNV testing,
    > other autosomal aneuploidies, and polygenic risk testing.

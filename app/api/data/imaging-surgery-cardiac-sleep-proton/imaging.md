\# Brain Functional Mapping MRI

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- \*\*Pre-operative / procedural evaluation\*\*

\- Mapping of a focal brain lesion in relation to eloquent cortex for
presurgical planning

\- Tumor

\- Vascular malformation

\- Pre-operative evaluation for epilepsy surgery

\- Brain tumor radiation treatment planning

\- \*\*Post-operative / procedural evaluation\*\*

\- Therapeutic follow-up only when there is a documented medical reason
explaining need for follow-up

\- Resting-state functional MRI may also be used for the above
indications

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*CPT:\*\* 70554, 70555

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* Not listed

\### Required Documentation

\- Standard documentation listed above

\- Clinical indication for presurgical mapping, epilepsy surgery
planning, or radiation planning

\- For follow-up: specific documented medical reason

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Not covered for diagnosis of primary degenerative dementia or mild
cognitive impairment

\## Brain PET

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- \*\*Known brain tumor or cancer\*\*

\- After brain MRI is insufficient or indeterminate to:

\- Distinguish radiation necrosis or post-treatment change from
residual/recurrent tumor

\- Distinguish low-grade from high-grade glioma

\- Evaluate primary brain lymphoma

\- Guide intervention or biopsy

\- \*\*Refractory seizures\*\*

\- To determine operability

\- \*\*Post-treatment / procedural evaluation\*\*

\- Follow-up only when additional imaging is medically necessary and
clearly justified

\- \*\*Mild cognitive impairment or dementia\*\*

\- FDG PET for:

\- Early Alzheimer disease detection, or

\- Differentiation among Alzheimer disease, dementia with Lewy bodies,
and frontotemporal degeneration

\- \*\*All of the following must be met\*\*

\- Brain MRI is insufficient or indeterminate

\- Objective impairment is documented

\- MMSE or MoCA under 26, or

\- Mild cognitive impairment on neuropsychological testing

\- Full lab evaluation completed

\- Thyroid function

\- CBC

\- CMP including liver tests

\- B12

\- Any abnormal labs have been treated and cognitive difficulty persists

\- Medication side effects and medical causes have been excluded

\- Brain CT is acceptable if MRI is contraindicated

\- \*\*Further evaluation of indeterminate findings\*\*

\- Initial clarification of an inconclusive prior imaging finding

\- One follow-up exam for a prior indeterminate MR/CT finding if needed
to confirm stability

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*CPT:\*\* 78608

\- \*\*Related note:\*\* amyloid or SSTR brain tracer PET is redirected
to oncologic PET coding

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* Not listed

\### Required Documentation

\- Standard documentation listed above

\- Prior brain MRI results and why MRI is insufficient, if applicable

\- For cognitive evaluation:

\- Objective cognitive testing

\- Neuropsychological testing if used

\- Full laboratory evaluation

\- Exclusion of medication and medical causes

\- For post-treatment follow-up:

\- Specific reason additional imaging is needed

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Functional neuroimaging for primary degenerative dementia or mild
cognitive impairment is non-covered in the cited state-specific section

\- No ongoing surveillance for indeterminate findings unless change or
high suspicion is documented

\## Brain MR Spectroscopy

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Evaluation of recurrent or residual brain tumor versus post-treatment
change

\- Example: radiation necrosis

\- Further evaluation of a brain lesion to distinguish tumor from
non-tumor causes

\- Example: abscess

\- Infectious process

\- Inflammatory process

\- Suspected inherited metabolic disorders

\- Canavan disease

\- Primary mitochondrial disorders

\- May be combined with brain MRI if not previously performed

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*CPT:\*\* 76390, +0698T

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* Not listed

\### Required Documentation

\- Standard documentation listed above

\- Prior conventional imaging and unresolved diagnostic question

\- Correlation with:

\- Clinical history

\- Physical exam

\- Laboratory testing

\- Diagnostic imaging

\- For inherited metabolic disease:

\- Family history

\- Condition-specific laboratory workup

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Adjunctive study; not a replacement for histopathology

\- Glioma grading use remains experimental/investigational

\- Most inherited metabolic condition use remains
experimental/investigational except select roles in Canavan disease and
primary mitochondrial disorders

\- Utility in other neurologic diseases is not well established

\## Breast MRI

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- \*\*General screening use\*\*

\- Screening in increased-risk patients

\- Screening may be performed at routine intervals in lactating
high-risk patients

\- \*\*No history of known breast cancer\*\*

\- \*\*Dense breast tissue on mammography\*\*

\- Inconclusive screening mammogram with category 0 due to breast
characteristics limiting mammographic sensitivity

\- Extremely dense breasts

\- Heterogeneously dense breasts

\- Implants obscuring tissue

\- \*\*High-risk screening\*\*

\- Lifetime breast cancer risk \*\*20% or greater\*\* by validated risk
model

\- Annual approval beginning 10 years before youngest affected family
member's diagnosis or age 40, whichever comes first

\- Not before age 25

\- Lifetime risk \*\*20% or greater\*\* due to LCIS/ALH or ADH

\- Annual approval beginning at diagnosis

\- Not before age 25

\- Intermediate lifetime risk \*\*15% to 20%\*\* due to LCIS/ALH or ADH
\*\*and\*\* dense breast tissue

\- Annual approval beginning at diagnosis

\- Not before age 25

\- History of extensive chest irradiation between ages 10 and 30

\- Begin 8 years after radiation

\- Not before age 25

\- Known BRCA1/2 mutation

\- Annual approval starting at age 25

\- First-degree relative with BRCA mutation, patient not yet tested

\- Annual approval starting at age 25

\- Personal history of high-risk germline mutations with age-based
screening starts

\- TP53

\- PTEN / BRRS

\- ATM

\- BARD1

\- CDH1

\- CHEK2

\- NF1

\- PALB2

\- STK11

\- RAD51C

\- RAD51D

\- \*\*Evaluation of lesion, mass, or abnormality\*\*

\- Suspected breast cancer when mammography, ultrasound, and exam are
inconclusive and biopsy cannot be performed

\- Includes suspected inflammatory breast cancer skin changes after
negative conventional imaging and skin biopsy

\- Suspicious mass/lesion/distortion in patient with history of breast
cancer when other imaging is inconclusive

\- New nipple inversion with inconclusive mammogram/ultrasound and
biopsy cannot be performed

\- Biopsy-proven LCIS/ALH or ADH

\- Spontaneous unilateral serous or bloody nipple discharge with BI-RADS
1-3 imaging and no palpable mass

\- Paget disease of the nipple with BI-RADS 1-3 imaging and no palpable
mass

\- Selected phyllodes tumor cases to assess extent/resectability

\- Follow-up of BI-RADS 3 lesion seen only on prior MRI when prior
mammogram and ultrasound were negative

\- \*\*History of known breast cancer\*\*

\- \*\*Yearly surveillance\*\*

\- Personal history of breast cancer and dense breast tissue

\- Personal history diagnosed before age 50

\- Personal history plus genetic or other high-risk features

\- Mammographically occult primary cancer

\- \*\*Initial staging\*\*

\- Conventional imaging indeterminate for:

\- Extent of disease

\- Multifocal disease

\- Multicentric disease

\- Contralateral disease

\- Tumor size discrepancy between exam and imaging

\- Invasive lobular carcinoma poorly defined on other modalities

\- Search for primary cancer in axillary nodal adenocarcinoma with
unknown primary

\- Baseline prior to planned neoadjuvant chemotherapy

\- During or after treatment to:

\- Evaluate response

\- Select candidates for breast-conserving therapy

\- Assess preoperative neoadjuvant therapy response

\- \*\*Silicone implants\*\*

\- Confirmation of suspected asymptomatic silicone implant rupture after
abnormal or indeterminate mammogram or ultrasound

\- Symptomatic silicone implant rupture evaluation; prior imaging not
required

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*CPT:\*\* 77046, 77047, 77048, 77049, +0698T

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* Not listed

\### Required Documentation

\- Standard documentation listed above

\- Risk assessment model result, if used

\- Mammography findings, including density or category 0 designation

\- Genetic mutation status or relevant family history

\- Prior imaging results and why they are inconclusive

\- Biopsy results or reason biopsy cannot be performed

\- Treatment history and purpose of MRI

\- Implant type and rupture symptoms/imaging findings, if relevant

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Contrast-enhanced MRI is not recommended during pregnancy

\- MRI is not indicated for saline implant complications

\- Supplemental MRI screening for dense breasts in non-high-risk
populations is non-covered in the cited state-specific section

\- High-risk screening in the cited state-specific section requires at
least 11 months between screenings

\## Fetal MRI

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- To better define or confirm:

\- Known or suspected fetal abnormality after ultrasound

\- Planned fetal surgery

\- Information needed to guide therapy, delivery, or prognosis
counseling

\- \*\*Combination imaging\*\*

\- Pelvis MRI and fetal MRI may both be used when each independently
meets medical necessity

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*CPT:\*\* 74712, +74713

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* Not listed

\### Required Documentation

\- Standard documentation listed above

\- Prior ultrasound findings

\- Clinical reason fetal assessment is needed

\- Planned fetal intervention, therapy decision, delivery planning, or
prognosis counseling need

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Should only be used for a valid medical reason after consideration of
sonographic findings or relevant family history

\- Before 18 weeks gestation, MRI may not add meaningful information

\- If only placenta or maternal pelvis is being assessed and fetal
assessment is not needed, use pelvic MRI instead

\## Pelvic CT Angiography

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- \*\*Venous disease\*\*

\- Suspected pelvic vascular disease or pelvic congestion syndrome when
ultrasound is indeterminate

\- Unexplained unilateral or asymmetric lower extremity edema with
negative or inconclusive abdomen/pelvis CT

\- Evaluation of inferior vena cava thrombosis

\- Venous thrombosis when prior studies such as ultrasound are not
diagnostic

\- Suspected May-Thurner syndrome when abdomen imaging is not needed

\- \*\*Other vascular abnormalities limited to the pelvis\*\*

\- Inconclusive vascular findings on prior imaging

\- Evaluation or monitoring of pelvic vascular disease when ultrasound
is inconclusive

\- Aneurysm

\- Dissection

\- AVM

\- Vascular fistula

\- Intramural hematoma

\- Compression syndromes

\- Vasculitis

\- Suspected complication of known aneurysm with new pelvic pain

\- \*\*Tumor\*\*

\- Clarification of vascular invasion from tumor

\- Imaging of deep inferior epigastric arteries before breast
reconstruction when abdomen imaging is not needed

\- \*\*Pre-operative evaluation\*\*

\- Planning before endovascular aneurysm repair

\- Patency/restenosis evaluation related to:

\- Atherosclerosis

\- Thromboembolism

\- Intimal hyperplasia

\- Deep inferior epigastric artery imaging when only pelvic imaging is
needed

\- Prior to uterine artery embolization for fibroids

\- Evaluation of vascular anatomy before solid organ transplantation

\- \*\*Post-operative / post-procedural evaluation\*\*

\- Follow-up of treatment or intervention

\- Luminal patency versus restenosis

\- Post-operative complications such as pseudoaneurysm involving grafts,
stents, or stent-grafts

\- Renal transplant allograft complications

\- \*\*Combination studies\*\*

\- Pelvic angiographic imaging plus pelvic CT when clarifying tumor
vascular invasion, including suspected renal vein thrombosis

\- Prior to uterine artery embolization

\- \*\*Further evaluation of indeterminate findings\*\*

\- Initial clarification of inconclusive prior imaging

\- One follow-up exam for prior indeterminate MR/CT finding

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*CPT:\*\* 72191

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* Not listed

\### Required Documentation

\- Standard documentation listed above

\- Ultrasound or CT findings and why they are inconclusive

\- Symptoms such as edema or pelvic pain

\- Reason abdomen imaging is not needed, if applicable

\- Planned procedure/transplant details, if applicable

\- Specific need for post-operative or post-procedural imaging

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Not appropriate as a screening tool for asymptomatic patients without
prior diagnosis

\- If both abdomen and pelvis are needed, combined abdomen/pelvis
angiographic imaging should be requested instead

\- If runoff imaging of aorta and legs is desired, a single runoff study
should be used instead

\- For uterine artery embolization planning, MRA is preferred

\- Iliac aneurysm ultrasound interval guidance:

\- 2.0 to 2.9 cm: every 3 years

\- 3.0 to 3.4 cm: annually

\- More than 3.5 cm: every 6 months

\## Diagnostic CT Colonography

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- \*\*Diagnostic evaluation\*\*

\- Symptomatic patient when conventional colonoscopy is contraindicated
or incomplete

\- Failed or incomplete colonoscopy

\- Obstructing colorectal cancer

\- Colonoscopy medically contraindicated or not possible

\- Unable to undergo sedation

\- Recent myocardial infarction

\- Recent colonic surgery

\- Bleeding disorder

\- Severe lung disease

\- Severe heart disease

\- \*\*Follow-up\*\*

\- 3-year follow-up when at least one polyp measuring 6 mm or more is
found on CT colonography and patient does not undergo or cannot undergo
colonoscopy/polypectomy

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*CPT:\*\* 74261, 74262

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* Not listed

\### Required Documentation

\- Standard documentation listed above

\- Symptoms supporting diagnostic need

\- Failed/incomplete colonoscopy details or contraindication reason

\- If follow-up: polyp size and reason colonoscopy/polypectomy was not
done

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Not indicated for routine follow-up of:

\- Inflammatory bowel disease

\- Hereditary polyposis syndromes

\- Non-polyposis cancer syndromes

\- Anal disease

\- Not indicated in pregnancy or possible pregnancy

\## Cardiac MRI

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- \*\*Cardiomyopathy and heart failure\*\*

\- Assessment of systolic and diastolic function in newly diagnosed
cardiomyopathy

\- Suspected infiltrative disease if PET has not been performed

\- Amyloidosis

\- Sarcoidosis

\- Hemochromatosis

\- Endomyocardial fibrosis

\- Suspected inherited or acquired cardiomyopathy

\- Suspected acute myocarditis with new unexplained findings such as:

\- Troponin rise not clearly due to myocardial infarction

\- ECG change suggesting acute myocardial injury or pericarditis without
evident infarction

\- Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy assessment

\- \*\*Aortic root / aortic and connective tissue surveillance\*\*

\- Biannual follow-up of enlarged aortic root or growth rate at least
0.5 cm/year

\- Screening of first-degree relative with bicuspid aortic valve

\- Re-evaluation in under 1 year for bicuspid aortic valve with
ascending aorta over 4 cm plus:

\- Diameter over 4.5 cm, or

\- Rapid change, or

\- First-degree family history of dissection

\- Turner syndrome

\- Annually if abnormality exists

\- Every 5 to 10 years if initial study is normal

\- Known or suspected connective tissue/genetic disorder predisposing to
aneurysm or dissection

\- At diagnosis

\- Again at 6 months

\- Then annually

\- More frequent if larger size or faster growth

\- \*\*Congenital heart disease\*\*

\- All lesions:

\- Evaluation before planned repair

\- Evaluation for change in clinical status or new concerning symptoms

\- Patent ductus arteriosus:

\- Routine surveillance every 1 to 2 years with postprocedural aortic
obstruction

\- Suspected anomalous pulmonary venous drainage or pulmonary outflow
tract obstruction when prior imaging has not documented congenital
disease

\- Eisenmenger syndrome / pulmonary hypertension associated with
congenital heart disease

\- Change in pulmonary hypertension-targeted therapy

\- Initial evaluation after congenital heart disease surgery when
pulmonary hypertension is suspected

\- Aortic stenosis or regurgitation with aortic sinus/ascending aortic
dilation

\- 6 to 12 month surveillance if increasing

\- 2 to 3 year surveillance if stable

\- Aortic coarctation / interrupted aortic arch

\- Suspected coarctation

\- 3 to 5 year surveillance for mild coarctation

\- 3 to 5 year post-procedure surveillance for aneurysm, in-stent
stenosis, stent fracture, or endoleak

\- Coronary anomalies

\- Tetralogy of Fallot

\- 2 to 3 year surveillance depending on pulmonary regurgitation,
ventricular function, or sequelae

\- Double outlet right ventricle

\- 3 to 5 year surveillance in asymptomatic patients with no or mild
sequelae

\- Postoperative transposition physiology

\- Surveillance interval depends on severity of findings

\- \*\*Stress CMR / CAD rules\*\*

\- Stress CMR generally requires diversion first to:

\- Exercise stress echocardiography

\- Then exercise myocardial perfusion imaging

\- Exemptions:

\- Marked obesity/body habitus interfering with other imaging

\- Younger patients with documented complex CAD expected to need
frequent radiation-free follow-up

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*CPT:\*\* 75557, 75559, 75561, 75563, +75565, +0698T

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* Not listed

\### Required Documentation

\- Standard documentation listed above

\- Relevant cardiac symptoms, risk factors, and diagnosis under
evaluation

\- Prior echo/TEE or other noninvasive testing and why insufficient, if
applicable

\- Aortic measurements and interval growth when surveillance is
requested

\- Congenital lesion type and surveillance reason

\- Prior testing and inability to tolerate/safely undergo alternatives
when relevant

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Cardiac stress MRI is specifically noted as out of scope/not reviewed
in one state-specific section

\- CMRA is not covered in CABG patients without CAD symptoms or for
cardiac lead placement unless vascular anomaly is suspected

\- Stress CMR is generally diverted to other stress imaging first unless
a listed exemption applies

\## Cardiac PET

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- \*\*General PET selection rule\*\*

\- Use when myocardial perfusion imaging criteria are met \*\*and\*\*
PET is expected to avoid equivocal imaging because of:

\- High BMI

\- Large breasts or implants

\- Mastectomy

\- Chest wall deformity

\- Pleural or pericardial effusion

\- Prior thoracic surgery

\- Prior equivocal MPI result

\- \*\*Suspected CAD when SE or MPI is not optimal\*\*

\- Symptomatic patients without known CAD

\- Low/intermediate pretest probability and unable to exercise

\- High pretest probability

\- New/worsening symptoms after negative result at least 1 year earlier

\- Asymptomatic patients without known CAD

\- Unevaluated ischemic ECG changes

\- Unevaluated pathologic Q waves

\- Unevaluated complete left bundle branch block

\- \*\*Abnormal calcium score\*\*

\- Stable symptoms with CAC over 100 and no MPI in prior 12 months

\- Asymptomatic high global risk with CAC over 100 and no MPI in prior
12 months

\- Asymptomatic with CAC over 400 and no MPI in prior 12 months

\- \*\*Inconclusive CAD evaluation / ongoing concern for obstructive
CAD\*\*

\- Low-risk Duke treadmill score but current symptoms suggest
intermediate/high pretest probability

\- Intermediate Duke treadmill score

\- Borderline or inconclusive CCTA or SPECT

\- Ischemic cardiomyopathy viability assessment after inconclusive SPECT

\- \*\*Special diagnostic conditions requiring coronary evaluation\*\*

\- Prior acute coronary syndrome without coronary evaluation in prior 12
months

\- Wall motion abnormality on other imaging when ischemia assessment is
needed

\- Newly diagnosed systolic or diastolic heart failure with suspicion
for ischemia

\- Reduced LVEF requiring myocardial viability assessment before
revascularization

\- Persistent stable anginal chest pain with suspected ischemia and
nonobstructive CAD

\- Ventricular arrhythmias

\- Sustained VT

\- VF

\- Exercise-induced VT

\- Nonsustained VT or frequent PVCs when exercise ECG cannot be done

\- Before and during class IC antiarrhythmic drug therapy in
intermediate/high-risk patients

\- Coronary anomaly or aneurysm hemodynamic significance

\- Anomalous coronary arteries

\- Muscle bridging

\- Coronary aneurysm

\- After prior chest radiation

\- Cardiac sarcoidosis

\- Therapy monitoring

\- High clinical suspicion after nondiagnostic CMR

\- Evaluation of probable disease to identify active inflammation

\- Infective endocarditis when TTE/TEE are inconclusive and probability
is moderate/high

\- Aortitis diagnosis or surveillance

\- \*\*Pre-operative non-cardiac surgery\*\*

\- Intermediate/high-risk surgery

\- At least one cardiac risk factor

\- Inability to walk or under 4 METs

\- No imaging stress test within 1 year

\- \*\*Transplant planning\*\*

\- Organ or stem cell transplant planning if no conclusive cardiac
evaluation in past year

\- \*\*Post-cardiac transplant\*\*

\- Annual evaluation for first 5 years if invasive coronary
arteriography is not being performed

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*CPT:\*\* 78459, 78491, 78492, +78434

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* Not listed

\### Required Documentation

\- Standard documentation listed above

\- Why SE/MPI is not technically feasible or clinically appropriate

\- Pretest probability, ECG findings, CAC score, Duke score, or prior
imaging result as applicable

\- LVEF when viability assessment is requested

\- Arrhythmia type and supporting rhythm data

\- Echo, ECG, CMR, TTE, or TEE findings for sarcoid/endocarditis where
relevant

\- Surgical risk level, inability to walk/METs, and risk factors for
pre-op requests

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- PET/MR has no specific CPT code and requires plan review

\- One state-specific section notes out-of-scope areas for that state
determination:

\- Asymptomatic individuals

\- Follow-up of prior abnormal cardiac imaging

\- Myocardial viability

\- Preoperative evaluation

\- Other non-CAD cardiac pathologies

\- State-specific section notes PET coverage under CAD conditions when
SPECT is not technically feasible or clinically appropriate

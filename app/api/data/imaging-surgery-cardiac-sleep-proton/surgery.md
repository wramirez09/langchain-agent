\## Cervical Procedures

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- \*\*Anterior fusion, single level\*\*

\- Medically necessary when either:

\- Progressive myelopathy or progressive neurologic deficit with
imaging-confirmed cord/root compression; or

\- All of the following:

\- Cervical radiculopathy or myelopathy due to ruptured disc,
spondylosis, instability, or deformity

\- Failure of conservative treatment for at least 6 weeks within the
last 6 months

\- Imaging confirms single-level spinal cord or nerve root compression

\- First-line surgery may be allowed without conservative care for:

\- Myelopathy/progressive neurologic deficit

\- Tumor, infection, or trauma with significant cord/root compression

\- Fracture/instability meeting radiographic thresholds

\- \*\*Anterior fusion, multiple levels\*\*

\- Same urgent neurologic exceptions as above

\- Otherwise requires:

\- Cervical radiculopathy or myelopathy from ruptured disc, spondylosis,
instability, or deformity

\- Failure of conservative treatment for at least 6 weeks within 6
months

\- Imaging confirms multilevel cord/root compression

\- \*\*Posterior decompression with fusion, single or multiple
levels\*\*

\- Same urgent neurologic indications as above

\- Otherwise requires:

\- Cervical radiculopathy or myelopathy

\- Failure of conservative treatment for at least 6 weeks within 6
months

\- Imaging confirms compression at the symptomatic level(s)

\- \*\*Fusion for non-radicular axial neck pain\*\*

\- All of the following:

\- Symptoms failed to improve or plateaued after 6-12 consecutive months
of active treatment

\- All pain generators are adequately defined and treated

\- Physical medicine/manual therapy interventions completed

\- X-ray, MRI, or CT demonstrates disc pathology or spinal instability

\- Pathology limited to 1-2 levels unless complicating factors exist

\- Psychosocial issues addressed

\- Effectiveness of fusion at 3 or more levels for non-radicular pain is
not established

\- \*\*Posterior decompression without fusion\*\*

\- Medically necessary for:

\- Progressive myelopathy or neurologic deficit with imaging-confirmed
compression; or

\- Cervical radiculopathy with:

\- Failure of conservative treatment for at least 6 weeks within 6
months

\- Imaging-confirmed compression

\- First-line surgery may be allowed for tumor, infection, or trauma

\- \*\*Artificial disc replacement\*\*

\- All of the following:

\- Skeletally mature

\- Intractable radiculopathy from 1-2 level disease at C3-C7

\- Failure of conservative treatment for at least 6 weeks within 6
months

\- Imaging confirms level-correlated compression

\- FDA-approved prosthetic disc used

\- \*\*Fusion without decompression / anterior decompression without
fusion\*\*

\- Reviewed case-by-case

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* None listed

\- \*\*CPT/HCPCS:\*\*

\- Anterior fusion, single level: 22548, 22551, 22554

\- Anterior fusion, multiple levels: +22552, +22585

\- Posterior decompression with fusion, single level: 22590, 22595,
22600

\- Posterior decompression with fusion, multiple levels: 22595, +22614

\- Artificial disc replacement, single level: 22856, 22861, 22864

\- Artificial disc replacement, two levels: +22858, +0095T, +0098T

\- Posterior decompression without fusion: 63001, 63015, 63020, +63035,
63040, +63043, 63045, +63048, 63050, 63051

\- Anterior decompression without fusion: 63075, +63076

\### Required Documentation

\- Neurologic exam findings

\- MRI preferred; CT with/without myelography if MRI contraindicated or
discordant

\- Conservative treatment details and failure

\- If axial pain fusion requested:

\- Documentation of all treated pain generators

\- Psychosocial evaluation/issues addressed

\- If fracture/instability:

\- Radiographic measurements

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Not recommended for:

\- Asymptomatic or mildly symptomatic cervical stenosis

\- Neck pain alone without neurologic deficits and without significant
cord/root compression

\- Artificial disc contraindications include:

\- Disease at 3 or more levels

\- Infection

\- Osteoporosis/osteopenia

\- Instability

\- Severe spondylosis

\- Severe facet arthropathy

\- Ankylosing spondylitis

\- Rheumatoid arthritis

\- Prior fracture deformity

\- OPLL

\- Active malignancy

\- Smoking/nicotine abstinence is required before fusion surgery

\- BMI should be \< 40 for certain posterior procedures unless
case-by-case review supports otherwise

\## Lumbar Procedures

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- \*\*Microdiscectomy\*\*

\- All of the following:

\- Primary radicular symptoms significantly impair daily activities

\- Failure of conservative treatment for at least 6 weeks within the
last 6 months

\- Imaging shows disc herniation exactly correlating with symptoms/signs

\- First-line surgery allowed for:

\- Significant acute motor deficit

\- Cauda equina syndrome

\- \*\*Decompression\*\*

\- All of the following:

\- Neurogenic claudication and/or radicular leg pain impairing daily
activities

\- Failure of conservative treatment for at least 6 weeks within 6
months

\- Imaging shows moderate to severe stenosis consistent with symptoms

\- First-line surgery allowed for:

\- Significant acute motor deficit

\- Cauda equina syndrome

\- Stenosis due to tumor, infection, or trauma

\- \*\*Fusion, single level\*\*

\- All of the following:

\- Lumbar back pain, claudication, and/or radicular leg pain without
major sensory/motor deficit for at least 6 months

\- Failure of conservative treatment for at least 6 weeks within 6
months

\- Imaging corresponds with clinical findings

\- At least one of:

\- Spondylolisthesis

\- Segmental instability

\- Revision for pseudoarthrosis at same level after 9-12 months

\- Repeat disc herniation revision with expected functional gain

\- Tumor/cancer/infection

\- Chronic low back pain/degenerative disc disease after at least 6
months of active non-operative care and mandatory cognitive-behavioral
rehabilitation, case-by-case

\- \*\*Fusion, multi-level\*\*

\- All of the following:

\- Same symptom/failure/imaging requirements as above

\- At least one of:

\- Multiple-level spondylolisthesis

\- Tumor/trauma/cancer/infection affecting multiple levels

\- Intra-operative segmental instability

\- \*\*Repeat fusion\*\*

\- Case-by-case

\- Must document:

\- Remediable pathology on records/imaging

\- Why surgery is preferred over non-invasive/less invasive options

\- Signed documentation showing informed participation and understanding
of high failure/complication risk

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* None listed

\- \*\*CPT/HCPCS:\*\*

\- Microdiscectomy: 62380, 63030, +63035

\- Decompression: 63005, 63012, 63017, 63042, +63044, 63047, +63048,
63056, +63057

\- Fusion, single level: 22533, 22558, 22612, 22630, 22633, +63052,
+63053

\- Fusion, multiple levels: +22534, +22585, +22614, +22632, +22634,
+63052, +63053

\### Required Documentation

\- History and physical exam

\- MRI/CT imaging

\- Conservative treatment details

\- If unable to complete treatment, medical reason must be documented

\- Repeat fusion requests need rationale and signed risk acknowledgment

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Office closures, inconvenience, or unexplained noncompliance do not
count as inability to complete conservative treatment

\- Relative contraindications:

\- Medical contraindications to surgery

\- Psychosocial risk factors

\- Active tobacco/nicotine use

\- Morbid obesity

\## Hip Arthroscopic Procedures

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- \*\*General requirements\*\*

\- Clinical correlation between symptoms, exam, and imaging

\- Functional limitation

\- Medically optimized

\- No active local/systemic infection

\- No untreated active drug dependency

\- \*\*Diagnostic arthroscopy\*\*

\- Rarely necessary; case-by-case

\- All of the following:

\- At least 6 months of hip pain with documented loss of function

\- Indeterminate radiographs and MRI

\- No radiographic evidence of:

\- Significant arthritis

\- Femoroacetabular impingement

\- Hip dysplasia, unless with periacetabular osteotomy

\- Femoral head or acetabular fracture

\- Labral tear

\- \*\*Labral repair\*\*

\- All of the following:

\- Hip/groin pain in flexion/rotation, possibly with
locking/popping/catching

\- Positive FADIR test

\- Labral tear on MRI, with or without contrast

\- No significant arthritis

\- No severe/advanced dysplasia unless combined with periacetabular
osteotomy

\- Failure of at least 6 weeks of non-operative treatment, including at
least 2 measures

\- No cortisone injection within 3 months

\- \*\*CAM / pincer / combined impingement repair\*\*

\- All of the following:

\- Positional hip pain

\- Skeletally mature

\- BMI \< 40, otherwise case-by-case

\- Positive impingement sign/FADIR

\- Radiographic/CT/MRI findings consistent with CAM, pincer, or combined
impingement

\- No significant arthritis

\- No severe/advanced dysplasia unless combined with periacetabular
osteotomy

\- Failure of at least 6 weeks of non-operative treatment, including at
least 2 measures

\- No cortisone injection within 3 months

\- \*\*Synovectomy / biopsy / loose or foreign body removal\*\*

\- Either:

\- Imaging evidence of acute post-traumatic intra-articular foreign body
or displaced fracture fragment; or

\- All of the following:

\- Hip pain with grinding/catching/locking/popping

\- Painful range of motion on exam

\- Imaging shows synovial proliferation, calcifications, nodularity,
inflammation, pannus, or loose body

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of non-operative treatment, including at
least 2 measures

\- No cortisone injection within 3 months

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* None listed

\- \*\*CPT/HCPCS:\*\*

\- Femoroacetabular impingement surgery: 29914, 29915, 29916

\- Other hip arthroscopy: 29860, 29861, 29862, 29863

\### Required Documentation

\- Symptom onset, duration, severity

\- Functional loss

\- Type and duration of non-operative treatment

\- MRI/CT/X-ray findings

\- Evidence of physical exam correlation

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- No support for arthroscopy in asymptomatic individuals with
impingement/labral findings

\- Not indicated solely for hip osteoarthritis

\- Severe dysplasia rarely appropriate unless combined with
periacetabular osteotomy

\- Some state-specific programs exclude impingement surgery entirely

\## Hip Joint Replacement Procedures

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- \*\*General requirements\*\*

\- Functional limitation

\- Medically optimized

\- No active infection

\- Clinical notes should include symptom course, loss of function,
non-operative care, and decision-making

\- Non-operative management generally requires at least 2 of:

\- Rest/activity modification

\- Weight reduction

\- Protected weight-bearing

\- Physical therapy

\- Physician-supervised exercise/home exercise

\- Oral/topical NSAIDs, acetaminophen, analgesics

\- Intra-articular injections

\- \*\*Total hip replacement\*\*

\- Either:

\- \*\*Section One:\*\* persistent pain and loss of function with
radiographic advanced disease from:

\- Rheumatoid arthritis

\- Femoral neck fracture

\- Malignancy

\- Dysplasia

\- Avascular necrosis confirmed by imaging

\- Bone-on-bone articulation

\- \*\*Section Two:\*\* persistent pain and loss of function for at
least 12 weeks plus:

\- Physical exam showing one or more of:

\- Painful limited ROM or antalgic gait

\- Contracture

\- Crepitus

\- Leg length difference

\- Radiographic advanced arthritic changes such as Tönnis grade 2-3 or
severe narrowing/bone-on-bone collapse/subchondral
sclerosis/cysts/osteophytes/bony deformity

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of non-operative treatment including at
least 2 measures

\- No corticosteroid injection within 12 weeks

\- No medical necessity for severe radiographic disease without
symptoms, except malignancy

\- \*\*Simultaneous bilateral replacement\*\*

\- Must clearly document why simultaneous surgery is preferable to
staged procedures

\- Risks must be discussed and documented

\- \*\*Hip resurfacing\*\*

\- Case-by-case

\- All of the following:

\- Pain and loss of function for at least 12 weeks

\- 12 weeks of failed non-operative treatment

\- Typical physical exam findings of hip pathology

\- Imaging with Tönnis grade 2-3 disease or avascular necrosis involving
\< 50% of femoral head

\- Male \< 65 or female \< 55

\- BMI \< 40

\- No corticosteroid injection within 12 weeks

\- \*\*Revision/conversion\*\*

\- Either:

\- Prior infected prosthesis removed, infection adequately treated, off
antibiotics, and infection ruled out/appropriately evaluated; or

\- Failed prior arthroplasty/ORIF/other surgery with severe pain,
functional loss and/or instability plus radiographic/clinical evidence
of failure, damage, malposition, dislocation, wear, osteolysis,
fracture, malignancy, or other mechanical failure

\- If implant loosening is suspected, infection must be ruled out or
surgical plan to rule it out must be documented

\- No corticosteroid injection within 12 weeks

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* None listed

\- \*\*CPT/HCPCS:\*\*

\- Total hip replacement: 27130, S2118

\- Revision/conversion: 27132, 27134, 27137, 27138

\### Required Documentation

\- Office notes and imaging

\- Non-operative treatment history

\- For revision:

\- ESR/CRP

\- Aspiration with WBC, gram stain, cultures if needed

\- Clear statement that infection has been treated

\- Surgical plan if inflammatory markers elevated

\- For bilateral surgery:

\- Reason simultaneous approach preferred

\- Risk discussion

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Absolute contraindications to total replacement:

\- Active infection

\- Corticosteroid injection within 12 weeks

\- Relative contraindications:

\- Prior infection unless negative workup

\- Component allergy

\- BMI \> 40 without risk discussion

\- Compromised soft tissue envelope

\- Uncontrolled comorbidities

\- Hip resurfacing absolute contraindications include:

\- Corticosteroid injection within 12 weeks

\- Osteoporosis/osteopenia

\- Poor bone quality

\- Femoral head-neck cystic degeneration

\- Proximal femur malignancy

\- Current or inadequately treated infection/sepsis

\- Female of child-bearing age

\- Chronic renal insufficiency

\- Metal allergy

\- Removal of infected prosthesis with antibiotic spacer is not counted
as revision arthroplasty

\## Knee Joint Replacement Procedures

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- \*\*General requirements\*\*

\- Knee pain with documented loss of function

\- Medically optimized

\- No active infection

\- No untreated active drug dependency

\- Good oral hygiene/no major planned dental work

\- Clinical notes should address symptom course, limitations,
non-operative treatment, and decision-making

\- Non-operative treatment generally requires at least 2 of:

\- Rest/activity modification

\- Weight reduction

\- Protected weight-bearing

\- Brace/orthosis

\- Physical therapy

\- Supervised exercise/home exercise

\- Heat/ice

\- Oral/topical NSAIDs, acetaminophen, analgesics

\- Intra-articular injections

\- \*\*Total knee replacement\*\*

\- Either:

\- \*\*Section One:\*\* persistent pain and loss of function with
advanced disease from:

\- Rheumatoid arthritis

\- Post-traumatic arthritis

\- Fracture

\- Avascular necrosis

\- Bone-on-bone articulation

\- \*\*Section Two:\*\* persistent pain and loss of function for at
least 12 weeks with:

\- Physical exam findings such as tenderness, swelling/effusion, limited
range of motion

\- Radiographic arthritic disease consistent with advanced degeneration

\- Appropriate failed non-operative treatment

\- No medical necessity for severe radiographic disease without symptoms

\- \*\*Medial/lateral partial replacement\*\*

\- All of the following:

\- At least 12 weeks of pain localized to medial or lateral compartment

\- Unless bone-on-bone articulation, failure of at least 12 weeks of
non-operative treatment including at least 2 measures

\- Total arc of motion \> 90 degrees

\- Normal ACL or stable reconstructed ACL

\- Contracture ≤ 10 degrees

\- Angular deformity ≤ 10 degrees and passively correctable to neutral

\- Weight-bearing radiographs show only unicompartmental disease, with
or without patellofemoral involvement

\- No corticosteroid injection within 12 weeks

\- No prior arthroscopic knee surgery within 6 months

\- Simultaneous bilateral partial replacements require clear
justification and documented risk discussion

\- \*\*Patellofemoral partial replacement\*\*

\- Must meet all criteria in one of two sections:

\- \*\*Section One\*\*

\- Failure of prior patellofemoral unloading procedure

\- If no bone-on-bone articulation, failure of at least 12 weeks of
non-operative treatment including at least 2 measures

\- Weight-bearing radiographs show isolated patellofemoral grade 3-4
disease with no medial/lateral compartment arthritis

\- \*\*Section Two\*\*

\- At least 6 months of isolated anterior/patellar pain

\- Pain worsened by stairs, inclines, transfers, or prolonged sitting

\- Reproducible patellofemoral pain on exam

\- No ligament instability

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of non-operative treatment including at
least 2 measures

\- Radiographs show isolated patellofemoral grade 3-4 disease

\- No cortisone injection within 12 weeks

\- \*\*Revision\*\*

\- Either:

\- Previous infected prosthesis removed, infection treated, patient off
antibiotics; or

\- Symptomatic prior implant with severe pain/instability/mechanical
symptoms and:

\- Physical findings attributable to implant

\- Imaging-confirmed loosening/instability/osteolysis/bone
loss/mechanical failure

\- Infection ruled out or surgical plan documented if inflammatory
markers elevated

\- No corticosteroid injection within 12 weeks

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* None listed

\- \*\*CPT/HCPCS:\*\*

\- Total knee replacement: 27447

\- Partial/unicompartmental replacement: 27438, 27446

\- Revision: 27486, 27487

\### Required Documentation

\- Weight-bearing radiographs

\- Physical exam findings

\- Non-operative treatment history

\- For infection concerns:

\- CBC with differential

\- ESR

\- CRP

\- Aspiration/cultures when indicated

\- Intra-operative biopsy when indicated

\- For bilateral procedures:

\- Why simultaneous surgery is preferable

\- Risk discussion documentation

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Absolute contraindications:

\- Active infection

\- Corticosteroid injection within 12 weeks

\- Relative contraindications:

\- Prior infection unless negative workup

\- Component allergy

\- BMI \> 40 without risk discussion

\- Severe peripheral vascular disease

\- Compromised soft tissue envelope

\- Uncontrolled comorbidities

\- Partial replacement contraindications:

\- Active infection

\- Inflammatory arthritis

\- Excessive angular deformity/contracture

\- Significant disease in opposite compartment

\- ACL instability

\- Poor bone quality/osteoporosis/osteopenia

\- Opposite-compartment meniscectomy \> 25%

\- Excess stiffness

\- MRI should not be the primary study to grade arthritic severity

\- Some state-specific programs exclude multi-compartmental arthroplasty

\## Knee Arthroscopic Procedures

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- \*\*General\*\*

\- No intra-articular cortisone injection within 4 weeks unless
otherwise specified

\- Clinical notes should address symptom course, loss of function, and
non-operative treatment

\- Non-operative treatment generally includes at least 2 of:

\- Rest/activity modification

\- Ice/heat

\- Protected weight-bearing

\- Oral/topical NSAIDs, acetaminophen, analgesics, tramadol

\- Brace/orthosis

\- Physical therapy

\- Supervised home exercise

\- Weight optimization

\- Injections

\- \*\*Diagnostic arthroscopy\*\*

\- Rare

\- Requires:

\- At least 12 weeks of pain with loss of function

\- Painful weight-bearing and/or exam findings such as joint line
tenderness, effusion, or limited motion

\- Indeterminate radiographs and MRI

\- No clear meniscal tear, ligament tear, loose body, stress fracture,
severe OA, or patellofemoral instability

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of non-operative treatment including at
least 2 measures

\- \*\*Meniscectomy / meniscal repair\*\*

\- Medically necessary under several sections, including:

\- MRI-confirmed repairable/peripheral/root tear with pain localized to
corresponding compartment

\- Age \< 21 with meniscus tear and pain/mechanical symptoms or positive
meniscal findings

\- Bucket-handle tear with acute locked knee/mechanical locking

\- Suspected symptomatic tear with characteristic exam findings and
imaging showing no moderate/severe OA

\- Failure of at least 6 weeks of non-operative treatment including at
least 2 measures

\- No cortisone injection within 4 weeks

\- \*\*Articular cartilage restoration/repair\*\*

\- \*\*Skeletally immature\*\*

\- Symptomatic displaced osteochondral lesion; or

\- Stable lesion after at least 12 weeks of failed non-operative
treatment

\- \*\*Skeletally mature marrow stimulation\*\*

\- Symptomatic

\- MRI-confirmed isolated full-thickness lesion \< 2.0 cm2

\- No instability

\- No prior meniscectomy in same compartment unless concurrent meniscal
transplant

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of non-operative treatment

\- No cortisone injection within 4 weeks

\- \*\*Restorative procedures\*\*

\- Skeletally mature adult

\- Symptoms for at least 6 months

\- Age \< 50

\- BMI \< 35

\- Isolated full-thickness osteochondral/chondral lesion on MRI

\- Normal alignment/stability or corrected concurrently

\- No more than Kellgren-Lawrence grade 2 OA

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of non-operative treatment

\- No cortisone injection within 4 weeks

\- \*\*Loose body removal\*\*

\- Documentation of pain/mechanical symptoms/stiffness/loss of
motion/instability or functional loss

\- X-ray/CT/MRI documentation of loose body

\- No cortisone injection within 4 weeks

\- \*\*Synovectomy\*\*

\- Example retrieved indications include proliferative synovial disease,
traumatic hypertrophic synovitis, cyclops lesion, fat pad syndrome,
hemarthrosis, and painful plica depending on subsection

\- Requires supporting history/imaging/biopsy and failed non-operative
care where specified

\- \*\*Manipulation under anesthesia\*\*

\- Less than 20 weeks after ligament/joint reconstruction

\- ROM \< 110 degrees flexion or lack of full extension

\- Failure to improve despite 6 weeks/12 visits of PT

\- \*\*Lysis of adhesions\*\*

\- More than 12 weeks post-surgery/fracture/resolved infection

\- ROM \< 110 degrees flexion or lack of full extension

\- Failure of 6 weeks/12 visits of PT

\- No cortisone injection within 4 weeks

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* None listed

\- \*\*CPT/HCPCS:\*\*

\- Manipulation under anesthesia: 27570, 29884

\- Ligament reconstruction/repair: 27405, 27407, 27409, 27427, 27428,
27429, 29888, 29889

\- Meniscectomy/repair/transplant: 27332, 27333, 27403, 29868, 29880,
29881, 29882, 29883

\- Other knee arthroscopy/surgery: 27412, 27415, 27416, 27418, 27420,
27422, 27424, 27425, 29866, 29867, 29870, 29873, 29874, 29875, 29876,
29877, 29879, 29885, 29886, 29887, G0289

\### Required Documentation

\- Exam findings

\- MRI/X-ray/CT as applicable

\- Weight-bearing films when OA status matters

\- Non-operative treatment details

\- PT documentation for MUA/lysis of adhesions

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Meniscectomy/repair never medically necessary with Kellgren-Lawrence
grade 4 OA

\- Meniscectomy/repair generally not medically necessary with grade 3 OA

\- Exclude cartilage restoration if meniscal deficiency/malalignment are
not being corrected concurrently

\- Abrasion arthroplasty included in coding but not indicated

\- Cell-free implants and AMIC are not covered

\- Multiple listed cartilage products/biologic techniques are excluded

\- Subchondroplasty and in-office diagnostic arthroscopy are not managed

\## Shoulder Joint Replacement Procedures

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- \*\*General requirements\*\*

\- Clinical correlation

\- Functional limitation

\- No active infection

\- No untreated active drug dependency

\- Good oral hygiene/no major planned dental work

\- Medically optimized

\- Clinical notes should address symptom course, loss of function, and
non-operative management

\- \*\*Total shoulder replacement\*\*

\- All of the following:

\- Painful osteoarthritis or inflammatory non-infectious arthritis with
functional limitation

\- Functional intact rotator cuff and deltoid

\- Complete or near-complete joint space loss on axillary/AP radiographs

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of non-operative treatment including at
least 1 listed measure

\- No cortisone injection within 12 weeks

\- No prior shoulder arthroscopy within 12 weeks

\- If bone-on-bone articulation is present, non-operative treatment is
not required

\- \*\*Hemiarthroplasty\*\*

\- Either:

\- Acute 3- or 4-part proximal humerus fracture; or

\- Meets total replacement criteria, or has avascular
necrosis/osteonecrosis of humeral head without advanced glenoid disease

\- No cortisone injection within 12 weeks

\- No prior arthroscopy within 12 weeks

\- \*\*Reverse replacement for arthritis\*\*

\- All of the following:

\- Painful osteoarthritis or inflammatory non-infectious arthritis with
functional limitation

\- Age \> 60, younger patients case-by-case

\- Complete or near-complete joint space loss or advanced glenoid bone
loss/retroversion

\- Non-repairable massive tear involving at least 2 tendons, or
substantial partial/focal full-thickness tear with major cuff
dysfunction, and intact deltoid

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of non-operative treatment including at
least 1 listed measure

\- No cortisone injection within 12 weeks

\- No prior arthroscopy within 12 weeks

\- \*\*Reverse replacement for proximal humeral fracture\*\*

\- Retrieved criteria include:

\- Acute 2-, 3-, or 4-part proximal humerus fracture, with or without
tuberosity involvement, or painful malunion with cuff dysfunction

\- Age \> 60, younger patients case-by-case

\- \*\*Reverse replacement for irreparable cuff tear without
arthritis\*\*

\- All of the following:

\- Non-repairable massive tear

\- Intact deltoid

\- Pseudoparalysis or failed prior cuff repair with severe
pain/functional disability

\- Age \> 60, younger patients case-by-case

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of PT/home exercise unless worsening

\- No arthroscopy within 12 weeks

\- No cortisone injection within 12 weeks

\- \*\*Revision\*\*

\- Retrieved revision indications include:

\- Hemiarthroplasty to total

\- Hemiarthroplasty to reverse

\- Total to total

\- Total to reverse

\- Reverse to reverse

\- Total/reverse to hemiarthroplasty

\- Common required elements:

\- Prior implant

\- Persistent pain and functional loss

\- Mechanical failure/component failure/malposition or negative
infection evaluation

\- If inflammatory markers elevated, clear surgical plan to rule out
infection

\- Intact deltoid and/or axillary nerve where applicable

\- Imaging showing failed component/periprosthetic fracture/loosening
where applicable

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* None listed

\- \*\*CPT/HCPCS:\*\*

\- Total/reverse/resurfacing: 23472

\- Partial/hemiarthroplasty: 23470

\- Revision: 23473, 23474

\### Required Documentation

\- Physical exam

\- Radiographs; MRI/CT when needed

\- Non-operative treatment history

\- For prior infection concerns:

\- CBC with differential

\- ESR

\- CRP

\- Blood cultures/soft tissue biopsy cultures/synovial aspiration when
indicated

\- Gram stain, cell count, differential, crystals

\- Aerobic/anaerobic/AFB/fungal cultures

\- Evidence patient has been off antibiotics if prior infection

\- Dental status documentation

\- For revision:

\- Infection evaluation

\- Surgical plan if markers elevated

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Contraindications:

\- Neurologic disease causing CRPS/Charcot/loss of deltoid or cuff

\- Active or recent infection

\- Poor dental hygiene

\- Cortisone injection within 12 weeks

\- Arthroscopic surgery within 12 weeks

\- Revision contraindications:

\- Active/recent infection

\- Neurogenic pain syndrome

\- Acromial fracture or overly thin acromion

\- Severe osteoporosis

\- Non-functioning deltoid or axillary nerve palsy

\- Arthroscopy within 12 weeks

\- Cortisone injection within 12 weeks

\## Shoulder Arthroscopic Procedures

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- \*\*General\*\*

\- Clinical correlation of symptoms/exam/imaging

\- Functional limitation

\- Medically optimized

\- No active infection

\- No untreated active drug dependency

\- Smoking cessation strongly recommended

\- Non-operative management may include PT/home exercise, rest/activity
modification, heat/ice, oral NSAIDs, and single corticosteroid/local
anesthetic injection

\- \*\*Diagnostic arthroscopy\*\*

\- Either:

\- Evaluation of a painful prior shoulder arthroplasty; or

\- Severe disabling pain/loss of function plus:

\- Limited ROM and/or measurable strength loss and/or positive
impingement signs

\- Inconclusive x-rays and MRI

\- Other diagnoses excluded

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of non-surgical management including at
least 2 measures

\- \*\*Rotator cuff repair\*\*

\- \*\*Partial-thickness tear/calcific tendinitis\*\*

\- Pain pattern consistent with cuff disease

\- Functional loss

\- Positive impingement testing

\- MRI/US confirms partial tear or calcific tendinitis

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of non-operative care, including at
least 6 weeks PT/home exercise plus one additional measure

\- No cortisone injection within 12 weeks

\- \*\*Small full-thickness tear\*\*

\- Similar symptom/exam/imaging requirements

\- At least 6 weeks non-operative care unless acute traumatic tear in
younger patient

\- No cortisone injection within 12 weeks

\- \*\*Medium/large full-thickness tear\*\*

\- Either significant tear progression on serial imaging or all of:

\- Characteristic pain

\- Functional loss

\- Positive impingement signs

\- Weakness/severe pain on cuff testing

\- MRI/US-confirmed medium/large full-thickness tear

\- No advanced fatty infiltration

\- Mild/no atrophy

\- No cortisone injection within 12 weeks

\- \*\*Massive tear with or without superior capsular reconstruction\*\*

\- Massive full-thickness tear

\- No advanced fatty changes

\- Mild/no atrophy

\- No chronic superior humeral escape

\- No advanced arthritis

\- No cortisone injection within 12 weeks

\- \*\*Subscapularis tear\*\*

\- Acute injury or chronic anterior shoulder symptoms

\- Positive subscapularis exam

\- MRI with significant partial/full-thickness tear or tear with biceps
subluxation

\- No cortisone injection within 12 weeks

\- \*\*Superior capsular reconstruction\*\*

\- Massive full-thickness tear with intact/reparable subscapularis

\- No chronic superior humeral escape

\- No advanced arthritis

\- \*\*Revision cuff repair\*\*

\- Within 1 year: case-by-case and must include MRI/CT arthrogram
showing failed healing/re-tear

\- After 1 year: must again meet tear-size-specific criteria

\- \*\*Labral repair\*\*

\- \*\*SLAP\*\*

\- Compatible history

\- Glenohumeral pain/catching/popping/locking

\- Functional limitation

\- Age \< 40, older case-by-case

\- Positive SLAP exam

\- MRI demonstrates type II or IV tear/subtypes

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of non-operative care

\- \*\*Bankart\*\*

\- Acute tear:

\- Acute instability event or acute pain onset

\- Age \< 30

\- Positive apprehension/relocation/labral grind/laxity

\- ROM not limited by stiffness

\- MRI/CT-confirmed tear

\- Recurrent instability:

\- At least 2 instability events

\- Positive exam or prior documented dislocation

\- ROM not limited by stiffness

\- MRI/CT labral tear with or without small bony Bankart

\- \*\*Posterior labral tear\*\*

\- Pain/catching/popping/instability

\- MRI confirms posterior tear

\- Positive posterior instability exam

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of non-operative treatment unless
traumatic competitive-athlete tear

\- Age \< 40

\- No degenerative disease

\- \*\*Combined tears\*\*

\- Acute instability event or recurrent instability depending on
subsection

\- Labral tear on MRI/CT

\- Positive instability/labral exam

\- Minimal/no degenerative change

\- \*\*Multidirectional instability\*\*

\- Pain and limited function

\- Recurrent instability due to hyperlaxity, not traumatic dislocation

\- Sulcus test \> 1 cm translation

\- X-ray/MRI excludes other injury

\- Failure of at least 6 months of formal PT and activity modification

\- \*\*Adhesive capsulitis\*\*

\- Pain, loss of motion, limited function

\- At least 50% loss of motion in 2 planes vs opposite shoulder

\- Other causes ruled out

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of non-operative treatment including
PT/home exercise and one additional modality

\- \*\*Distal clavicle excision\*\*

\- Pain over AC joint and/or positive cross-body adduction

\- Positive X-ray or MRI findings correlated to symptoms

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of non-operative treatment with at least
2 measures

\- If isolated procedure, AC joint injection is required and should
document pain relief

\- \*\*Biceps tenotomy/tenodesis\*\*

\- Retrieved indications include:

\- With total shoulder arthroplasty

\- With subscapularis repair

\- Type II or IV SLAP tear instead of repair

\- Pain localized to bicipital groove with localized exam findings plus
failed 12 weeks non-operative care

\- Long head biceps rupture in younger patients with
pain/weakness/fatigue/cosmetic concern after failed non-operative
treatment

\- \*\*Loose body removal\*\*

\- Pain, mechanical symptoms, stiffness, loss of motion, instability, or
functional loss

\- X-ray/CT/MRI documentation of loose body

\- \*\*Synovectomy\*\*

\- Usually reserved for primary synovial disease or documented secondary
hypertrophic synovitis

\- \*\*Subacromial decompression\*\*

\- Only as an add-on with another shoulder procedure

\- Requires radiographic type 3 acromion/mechanical outlet impingement

\- Not indicated as isolated surgery

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* None listed

\- \*\*CPT/HCPCS:\*\*

\- Rotator cuff repair: 23410, 23412, 23420, 29827

\- Labral repair: 23450, 23455, 23460, 23462, 23465, 23466, 29806, 29807

\- Adhesive capsulitis/frozen shoulder: 29825

\- Other shoulder arthroscopy/surgery: 23120, 23125, 23130, 23405,
23415, 23430, 23700, 29805, 29819, 29820, 29821, 29822, 29823, 29824,
29825, +29826, 29828

\### Required Documentation

\- Symptom onset/duration/severity

\- Functional loss/limitations

\- Non-operative management details

\- X-rays and MRI, or ultrasound/CT arthrogram where allowed

\- Procedure-specific physical exam findings

\- For isolated DCE:

\- Diagnostic AC joint injection response

\- For revision cuff repair:

\- MRI or CT arthrogram showing failed healing/re-tear

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- In-office diagnostic arthroscopy is not managed

\- US-guided percutaneous debridement/tenotomy is not managed

\- Rotator cuff repair contraindications:

\- Active infection

\- Asymptomatic full-thickness tear

\- Active bacteremia

\- Deltoid/cuff paralysis

\- Advanced/severe arthritis

\- Cortisone injection within 12 weeks

\- SLAP repair contraindications:

\- Degenerative disease on imaging

\- Smoker age \> 40

\- Poorly controlled diabetes with HbA1c \> 7

\- MRI changes representing normal variants rather than tear

\- Bankart contraindications:

\- Off-track Hill-Sachs lesion or \> 20% glenoid bone loss if only
Bankart planned

\- Pain only without recurrent instability in individuals \> 40

\- Significant degenerative arthritis

\- Subacromial decompression contraindications:

\- Type 1 or 2 acromion or thinned acromion

\- Failed response to diagnostic subacromial injection

\- Prior decompression with no remaining overhang

\- Open decompression should rarely be performed

\- Isolated acromioplasty is not supported

\## Adult Deformity Procedures

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- All requests reviewed case-by-case

\- \*\*Thoracic deformity\*\*

\- All of the following:

\- Significant pain/symptoms impairing daily activities for at least 6
months

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of focused non-operative
therapy/rehabilitation in past year

\- Imaging confirms curvature and at least one of:

\- Kyphosis \> 75 degrees

\- Severe kyphosis with chin-brow vertical angle \> 35 degrees

\- \*\*Lumbar deformity\*\*

\- All of the following:

\- Lumbar pain, claudication, and/or radicular leg pain without
significant motor deficit, impairing daily activities for at least 6
months

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of focused non-operative
therapy/rehabilitation in past year

\- Imaging corresponding to findings and showing at least one of:

\- Sagittal or coronal imbalance of at least 5 cm on full-spine standing
x-rays

\- Fixed scoliosis of at least 40 degrees

\- \*\*Non-operative care definition\*\*

\- At least 12 consecutive weeks in the past year of any 2:

\- Analgesics/steroids/NSAIDs

\- Structured PT for core strengthening

\- Structured home exercise program

\- Epidural steroid injections and/or facet injections/selective nerve
root block

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* None listed

\- \*\*CPT/HCPCS:\*\*

\- 22206, 22207, +22208, 22210, 22212, 22214, +22216, 22220, 22222,
22224, +22226, 22558, +22614, 22630, +22632, 22633, 22800, 22802, 22804,
22808, 22810, 22812, 22830

\### Required Documentation

\- Full-spine standing imaging

\- Symptom duration and impact

\- Non-operative treatment details

\- Neurologic exam

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Relative contraindications:

\- Medical contraindications

\- Psychosocial risk factors

\- Active tobacco/nicotine use before fusion

\- Morbid obesity

\## Thoracic Procedures

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- All requests reviewed case-by-case

\- \*\*Decompression only\*\*

thoracic compression; or

\- Persistent/recurrent symptoms with functional limitation despite at
least 6 consecutive weeks of physician-directed conservative treatment
including at least 2 measures, plus imaging-confirmed compression

\- \*\*Decompression with fusion\*\*

\- Deformity cases; or

\- Myelopathy/radiculopathy requiring decompression extensive enough to
destabilize the thoracic spine

\- Relative contraindications:

\- Medical contraindications

\## Other Spine Procedures for Neoplasm / Lesion / Infection

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- \*\*Fusion\*\*

\- Urgent if:

\- Progressive myelopathy from tumor/infection; or

\- Progressive neurologic deficit with MRI/CT-confirmed compression

\- Otherwise requires all:

\- Gross biomechanical instability with acute neurologic risk requiring
reconstruction/fusion

\- Imaging evidence of infection or neoplasm aligned with clinical
findings

\- \*\*Decompression\*\*

\- Urgent if:

\- Progressive myelopathy from tumor/infection; or

\- Progressive neurologic deficit with MRI/CT-confirmed compression

\- Otherwise requires all:

\- Significant radiculopathy or severe axial pain on exam

\- Imaging evidence of infection or neoplasm aligned with clinical
findings

\## Lumbar Disc Replacement

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- All of the following:

\- Age 18-60

\- Degenerative disc disease or significant discogenic back pain
confirmed by history, exam, and key radiographic studies

\- No more than grade 1 spondylolisthesis at operative level(s)

\- Imaging confirms absence of significant facet arthropathy

\- At least 6 months of failed multimodal conservative treatment

\- Must include PT/rehabilitation with cognitive-behavioral components

\- May include injections and active exercise

\- Must be clearly outlined in medical record

\- Device used at 1 or 2 consecutive levels from L3-S1 by anterior
retroperitoneal approach

\- Device is FDA-approved and used per labeling

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* None listed

\- \*\*CPT/HCPCS:\*\*

\- Single level: 22857, 22862, 22865

\- Multiple levels: 22860, +0164T, +0165T

\### Required Documentation

\- History, physical exam, radiographic studies

\- Detailed conservative treatment record

\- Level/location documentation

\- Device/approach documentation

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Contraindications:

\- Disease above L3-4

\- Active infection

\- Osteoporosis/osteopenia

\- Allergy to implant materials

\- Isolated or chronic radiculopathy

\- Spinal stenosis or scoliosis

\- Spondylolisthesis \> grade 1

\- More than 2 levels requiring treatment

\- Severe facet arthrosis

\- Free disc fragment

\- Poorly managed psychiatric disorder

\- Not medically necessary in all other circumstances, including:

\- More than 2 levels

\- Hybrid disc-plus-fusion procedures

\- Relative contraindications:

\- Medical contraindications

\- Psychosocial factors

\- Active tobacco/nicotine use

\- Morbid obesity

\## Sacroiliac Fusion

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Reviewed case-by-case

\- All of the following:

\- Unilateral low back/buttock pain caudal to lumbar spine localized
over SI joint for at least 6 months and impairing daily activities

\- Failure to improve after at least 6 months of active non-operative
treatment including medications, PT, and home exercise

\- Pain on palpation over sacral sulcus without equally severe
tenderness elsewhere

\- Absence of generalized pain behavior

\- Positive response to at least 3 provocative SI tests

\- Diagnostic imaging includes all of the following:

\- SI joint radiographs and CT or MRI excluding destructive lesions or
inflammatory arthropathy

\- AP pelvis radiograph excluding hip pathology

\- Lumbar CT or MRI excluding neural compression/other degenerative
cause

\- SI imaging showing injury and/or degeneration

\- At least 75% pain relief for expected anesthetic duration after
image-guided contrast-enhanced intra-articular SI injection on 2
separate occasions

\- At least one therapeutic intra-articular SI injection trial

\### Relevant Codes

\- \*\*ICD-10:\*\* None listed

\- \*\*CPT/HCPCS:\*\*

\- 27279

\### Required Documentation

\- SI-focused history and exam

\- Provocative test results

\- Imaging of SI joint, pelvis, and lumbar spine

\- Response to 2 diagnostic injections

\- Trial of therapeutic injection

\- Non-operative treatment history

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Relative contraindications:

\- Medical contraindications

\- Psychosocial risk factors

\- Active tobacco/nicotine use before fusion

\- Morbid obesity

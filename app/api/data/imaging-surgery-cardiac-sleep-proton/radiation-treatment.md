\## Representative Detailed Criteria and Subcriteria

\### Breast

\- Whole-breast or chest-wall treatment is generally approved with
3D-CRT.

\- IMRT is reserved for selected anatomy/target-volume situations.

\- APBI may be approved for carefully selected post-lumpectomy patients.

\- Common exclusions:

\- SBRT

\- Certain brachytherapy approaches

\- IORT for breast cancer as investigational/not medically necessary in
listed settings

\### Prostate

\- Risk-group definitions are used to determine modality and dose.

\- Hypofractionation is commonly preferred.

\- Conventional fractionation may be allowed only for patients exempt
from hypofractionation.

\- Exemption examples include:

\- Large prostate size

\- Inflammatory bowel disease

\- Higher symptom score documented in records

\- Prior TURP

\- Pelvic lymph node chains included in treatment plan for higher-risk
disease

\- SBRT monotherapy is allowed in defined localized settings.

\- Brachytherapy monotherapy or boost is allowed in selected risk
groups.

\- Some settings exclude:

\- IORT

\- SBRT or brachytherapy in node-positive disease

\### Metastatic Disease

\- Non-spine palliation:

\- 3D-CRT palliative schedules allowed for painful or symptomatic
metastases.

\- Spine palliation:

\- SBRT allowed when all apply:

\- Painful MRI-confirmed spinal metastases

\- No more than 3 consecutive spinal segments in target volume

\- No unstable spine score or major neurologic compromise listed

\- 3D-CRT also allowed for spinal palliation

\- Oligometastatic disease:

\- SBRT allowed for limited metastatic burden

\- Requires recent full-body radiology documentation

\- Oligoprogressive disease:

\- SBRT allowed for limited progression after successful systemic
therapy

\- Requires controlled primary/other metastatic disease

\- Requires recent full-body radiology documentation

\### Lymphoma

\- IMRT is generally limited to head/neck and mediastinal presentations.

\- 3D-CRT is generally used for other body regions.

\- Disease-specific fractionation limits are listed for:

\- Hodgkin lymphoma

\- Follicular lymphoma

\- Marginal zone lymphoma

\- Mantle cell lymphoma

\- DLBCL

\- HGBL

\- PMBL

\- PTCL/ENKL

\- Primary cutaneous lymphoma subtypes

\- Common exclusions:

\- Brachytherapy

\- SBRT

\- IORT

\### Skin Cancer

\- 2D/3D-CRT or electron beam may be approved.

\- Superficial/orthovoltage radiation is approved by lesion size/site.

\- Brachytherapy may be approved for basal and squamous cell carcinoma.

\- IMRT is limited to cases involving lymph node chains.

\- Electronic brachytherapy is described as investigational/not
medically necessary.

\### Gynecologic Cancers

\- Pelvic IMRT/3D-CRT commonly used.

\- Brachytherapy alone or as a boost is frequently required/allowed
depending on disease/site.

\- IORT may be appropriate in select close/positive margin or recurrent
scenarios.

\- Recurrent themes:

\- Pelvic treatment

\- Vaginal cuff boost

\- Lymph node boost using SIB

\- Toxicity minimization using image guidance

\### Thoracic/Head and Neck

\- IMRT or 3D-CRT commonly approved for definitive and postoperative
treatment.

\- Hyperfractionation appears in selected thoracic/head and neck
scenarios.

\- Palliative 3D-CRT options are repeatedly included.

\- SBRT may be limited by tumor size, nodal status, or stage.

\- IORT may be allowed in select margin/recurrent settings for some
disease sites.

\### Pediatric Malignancies

\- All listed radiation treatment types may be approved for pediatric
cancer patients.

\- Cases beyond age 18 with pediatric-type histology may be considered
individually.

\- Specialized radiation oncology expertise and multidisciplinary input
are emphasized.

\## Required Documentation

\### General Required Documentation

\- Recent pertinent office visit notes

\- Laboratory data

\- Results of special testing

\- Prior relevant imaging results, when applicable

\- Reason alternative imaging cannot be performed, when applicable

\### Condition-Specific Documentation Examples

\- Full-body radiology documentation for oligometastatic or
oligoprogressive disease:

\- PET/CT report, or

\- CT chest/abdomen/pelvis reports

\- Must be recent and no more than 2 months before treatment start

\- MRI confirmation for spinal metastases in spine SBRT scenarios

\- Documented symptom score in prostate scenarios where symptom
threshold is part of criteria

\- Conventional imaging evidence in node-positive or metastatic prostate
definitions

\- Imaging guidance/planning support in pelvic and boost-based
gynecologic treatment planning

\## Limitations and Exclusions

\### Global Limitations

\- Clinical appropriateness does not guarantee benefit coverage.

\- The listed code set is not all-inclusive and does not by itself
determine coverage.

\- Some services may be reviewed case by case.

\### Common Exclusion Patterns Across Disease Sections

\- Brachytherapy often excluded unless specifically allowed

\- SBRT often excluded unless explicitly listed for that disease/setting

\- SRS limited to selected intracranial/benign/CNS indications

\- IORT commonly excluded except select margin/recurrent scenarios

\- IMRT may be excluded when not justified by anatomy, nodal treatment,
or specialized planning need

\- Electronic brachytherapy may be investigational in listed settings

\## Practical Summary

\- This policy is modality- and disease-specific.

\- Approval depends on:

\- Exact diagnosis and stage/risk group

\- Anatomic target volume

\- Presence/absence of metastases

\- Imaging confirmation

\- Fractionation schedule

\- Required supporting records

\- The document relies heavily on explicit dose ceilings, fraction
limits, and setting-specific exclusions.

\## 1) PAP-Only Office Visit Service

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- The individual has diagnosed sleep-disordered breathing that would
benefit from treatment with a positive airway pressure device.

\- All of the following must apply:

\- The chief purpose of the office visit is to initiate PAP treatment or
address PAP-related issues.

\- The individual requires education and/or problem-solving related to
the PAP device.

\- The visit does not include discussion of other health issues beyond
PAP initiation/management.

\### Relevant Codes

\- CPT:

\- 94660

\- HCPCS:

\- None listed

\- ICD-10:

\- None listed

\### Required Documentation

\- Care/services must be provided by a licensed clinician.

\- Appropriate supporting documentation must be provided, including:

\- Recent pertinent office visit notes

\- Laboratory data

\- Results of any special testing

\- If applicable:

\- All prior relevant imaging/results

\- Reason alternative imaging cannot be performed

\- Documentation should support:

\- Diagnosis of sleep-disordered breathing

\- Need for PAP-related education/problem-solving

\- That the visit is solely for PAP initiation/management

\- Background expectations noted in the document:

\- OSA diagnosis should be established using objective sleep apnea
testing.

\- Adequate follow-up should include troubleshooting and monitoring of
objective efficacy and usage data to ensure treatment and adherence.

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- This service should not occur on the same date as an evaluation and
management service.

\- Not appropriate when other health issues are addressed during the
same visit.

\- The service is limited to PAP initiation/management-related
encounters.

\## 2) Unattended Home Sleep Test

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Intended to confirm obstructive sleep apnea in adults with high
clinical suspicion.

\- Should be performed with:

\- A comprehensive sleep evaluation

\- Adequate follow-up

\- The comprehensive sleep evaluation must include:

\- Sleep history (snoring, apneas, daytime sleepiness)

\- BMI

\- Neck circumference

\- Cardiopulmonary examination

\- Identification of comorbid sleep disorders

\- Identification of relevant medical conditions

\- Adults must be older than 18 years.

\- There must be no contraindication to home sleep testing.

\- One of the following clinical pathways must be met:

\#### A. Signs and symptoms pathway

\- Excessive daytime sleepiness

\- And any 2 of the following:

\- Habitual loud snoring

\- Witnessed apneas or gasping/choking

\- Diagnosed hypertension

\- BMI 30 or higher, or large neck circumference

\- Men: 17 inches or greater

\- Women: 16 inches or greater

\#### B. High-risk population pathway

\- Member of a high-risk population, including:

\- Congestive heart failure, class I or II

\- Atrial fibrillation

\- Chronic kidney disease, stage III or higher with eGFR under 60

\- Treatment-refractory hypertension

\- Type 2 diabetes

\- Nocturnal dysrhythmias

\- Pulmonary hypertension

\- High-risk driving populations

\- Class 2 or 3 obesity (BMI 35 or higher)

\- Preoperative bariatric surgery

\- Craniofacial or upper-airway soft-tissue abnormalities

\- And any 2 of the following:

\- Excessive daytime sleepiness

\- Habitual loud snoring

\- Witnessed apneas or gasping/choking

\- Hypertension (if not already the defining high-risk feature) or BMI
30 or higher (if not already the defining high-risk feature)

\#### C. Commercial driver / safety-sensitive occupation pathway

\- Any of the following:

\- BMI 40 or higher

\- BMI 33 or higher plus either:

\- Type 2 diabetes, or

\- Hypertension requiring 2 or more medications

\- Sleepiness-related crash or accident by report or observation

\- Fatigue or sleepiness during the duty period

\#### Repeat Home Sleep Study

\- Previously diagnosed OSA and reevaluation is required for:

\- Response to upper-airway surgical procedures

\- Response after initial treatment with oral appliances

\- Reevaluation after non-PAP treatment when the individual:

\- Has recurrent symptoms, or

\- Develops or has a change in cardiovascular disease

\- Reevaluation after a body-weight change of 10% or more

\- Remote history of untreated OSA with need to reevaluate diagnosis
and/or initiate PAP

\- Upper-airway stimulation therapy:

\- Pre-implant reevaluation of known OSA with:

\- PAP failure or PAP intolerance, and

\- BMI 35 or lower, and

\- No recent sleep study or a significant change in weight and/or
symptoms

\- Post-implant reevaluation when prior titration had insufficient
clinical response, or there has been weight gain and/or return of
symptoms

\### Relevant Codes

\- CPT:

\- 95800

\- 95801

\- 95806

\- HCPCS:

\- G0398

\- G0399

\- G0400

\- ICD-10:

\- None listed

\### Required Documentation

\- Care/services must be provided by a licensed clinician.

\- Appropriate supporting documentation must be provided, including:

\- Recent pertinent office visit notes

\- Laboratory data

\- Results of special testing

\- If applicable:

\- Prior relevant imaging/results

\- Reason alternative imaging cannot be performed

\- Documentation should include the comprehensive sleep evaluation
elements:

\- Sleep history

\- BMI

\- Neck circumference

\- Cardiopulmonary exam

\- Comorbid sleep disorders

\- Relevant medical conditions

\- Documentation should support:

\- High pre-test probability of moderate to severe OSA

\- Absence of contraindications to home sleep testing

\- Need for repeat testing, if applicable

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Adult use only.

\- Intended for suspected OSA; not appropriate when contraindications
are present.

\- Contraindications / exclusions include:

\#### Comorbid medical conditions

\- Moderate to severe pulmonary disease with:

\- FEV1/FVC 0.7

\- FEV1 less than 80% predicted

\- Oxygen use, daytime hypercapnia, or hypoxemia

\- Obesity hypoventilation syndrome:

\- BMI 30 or higher with PCO2 over 45 on arterial blood gas, or

\- BMI 35 or higher with inability to lie flat, hypoxemia, or serum
bicarbonate 27 or higher

\- Chronic opiate medication use

\- Neuromuscular disease

\- Advanced heart failure:

\- Class III or IV, or

\- Left ventricular ejection fraction under 45%

\- Stroke

\- Relative contraindication; either attended or unattended testing may
be performed

\#### Comorbid sleep disorders, known or suspected

\- Periodic limb movement disorder

\- Parasomnia

\- REM behavior disorder

\- Nocturnal seizures

\- Narcolepsy or idiopathic hypersomnia

\- Circadian rhythm disorder

\- Central sleep apnea or complex sleep apnea

\- Hypoventilation

\- Sleep-related hypoxemia

\- Severe insomnia

\#### Technical contraindications

\- Inability to follow instructions

\- Lack of mobility or dexterity to use portable equipment without a
competent caregiver

\- Previous negative or technically inadequate home sleep study

\#### Special considerations

\- If a single home sleep test is inconclusive, technically inadequate,
or negative and clinical suspicion remains, attended testing is
recommended.

\- If there is low pre-test probability of sleep apnea but
well-documented ongoing concern for a functionally impairing sleep
disorder (for example, upper-airway resistance syndrome or mild OSA),
attended testing may be appropriate.

\- Home testing may still be considered when attended testing is not
possible because of immobility, safety concerns, or critical illness.

\## 3) Attended In-Lab Polysomnography

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Used to assess:

\- Sleep-related breathing disorders

\- Narcolepsy and idiopathic hypersomnia

\- Parasomnias and seizure disorders

\- Periodic limb movement disorder

\- Polysomnography requires, at minimum:

\- EEG

\- EOG

\- Chin EMG

\- Airflow

\- Oxygen saturation

\- Respiratory effort

\- Heart rate

\- The study is attended by a technologist.

\- Used for both initial diagnosis and follow-up in adults and children.

\### Adult Criteria

\#### A. Suspected obstructive sleep apnea

\- High pre-test probability of moderate to severe OSA, shown by one of
the following:

\##### Signs and symptoms pathway

\- Excessive daytime sleepiness

\- And any 2 of the following:

\- Habitual loud snoring

\- Witnessed apneas or gasping/choking

\- Diagnosed hypertension

\- BMI 30 or higher, or large neck circumference

\- Men: 17 inches or greater

\- Women: 16 inches or greater

\##### High-risk population pathway

\- High-risk population includes:

\- Congestive heart failure

\- Atrial fibrillation

\- Chronic kidney disease

\- Treatment-refractory hypertension

\- Type 2 diabetes

\- Nocturnal dysrhythmias

\- Stroke

\- Pulmonary hypertension

\- Class 2 or 3 obesity (BMI 35 or higher)

\- Preoperative bariatric surgery

\- Craniofacial or upper-airway soft-tissue abnormalities

\- And any 2 of the following:

\- Excessive daytime sleepiness

\- Habitual loud snoring

\- Witnessed apneas or gasping/choking

\- Hypertension (if not already the defining high-risk feature) or BMI
30 or higher (if not already the defining high-risk feature)

\##### Commercial driver / safety-sensitive occupation pathway

\- Any of the following:

\- BMI 40 or higher

\- BMI 33 or higher plus either:

\- Type 2 diabetes, or

\- Hypertension requiring 2 or more medications

\- Sleepiness-related crash or accident by report or observation

\- Fatigue or sleepiness during the duty period

\#### B. Suspected central sleep apnea

\- Documented clinical concern based on:

\- Sleep symptoms such as fragmented sleep, insomnia, apneas, or daytime
sleepiness

\- And comorbid medical conditions such as heart failure, opioid use, or
neurological disorders

\#### C. Suspected central hypersomnia

\- PSG must be performed with a multiple sleep latency test.

\- PSG must occur the night before the latency test.

\- It is used to:

\- Rule out other sleep disorders

\- Document adequate nocturnal sleep time (6 hours)

\- Indicated for:

\- Excessive daytime sleepiness despite adequate sleep and not thought
due to another sleep disorder

\- Suspected narcolepsy or idiopathic hypersomnia

\- Narcolepsy-associated symptoms may include:

\- Cataplexy

\- Hypnagogic hallucinations

\- Sleep paralysis

\#### D. Suspected parasomnias / nocturnal seizure disorders

\- PSG with expanded bilateral montage and video recording is indicated
for:

\- Suspected nocturnal seizures with abnormal or inconclusive EEG
findings

\- Suspected REM behavior disorder

\- Sleep behaviors suggestive of parasomnias that are unusual or
atypical because of:

\- Age at onset

\- Time, duration, or frequency

\- Violent or otherwise potentially injurious behaviors

\- Motor pattern features such as stereotyped, repetitive, or focal
activity

\- Lack of response to conventional therapy

\#### E. Suspected periodic limb movement disorder

\- Indicated when there is no known concurrent untreated sleep disorder
and there are repetitive limb movements during sleep with any of the
following:

\- Frequent awakenings

\- Difficulty maintaining sleep

\- Excessive daytime sleepiness

\- Also notes:

\- Not indicated for other sleep-related movement disorders such as
restless leg syndrome, bruxism, sleep-related leg cramps, rhythmic
movement disorder, or sleep-related myoclonus unless another underlying
sleep disorder is suspected

\### PAP Titration and Follow-Up Criteria

\#### Split-night study

\- Indicated when attended study criteria are met and both of the
following apply:

\- AHI 15 or higher in the first 2 hours

\- At least 3 hours available for PAP titration

\#### CPAP/BiPAP titration study

\- After diagnostic PSG if:

\- AHI 15 or higher and a split-night study was not performed, or

\- AHI 5 to 15 with significant daytime sleepiness, comorbid
hypertension, or impaired self-related quality of life

\- After split-night study if:

\- The diagnostic portion did not show AHI 15 or higher in the early
portion, but the overall study reached that threshold later in the
night, or

\- Titration during the split-night study was unsuccessful due to
residual apneas/hypopneas

\#### Attended study following home sleep testing

\- Indicated when:

\- The home study is technically inadequate

\- A single home study is inconclusive or negative but suspicion remains
high

\- Home study is positive with AHI over 15 and attended PAP titration is
needed

\- Home study shows AHI 5 to 15 with significant daytime sleepiness,
comorbid hypertension, or impaired self-related quality of life and
attended PAP titration is needed

\- Home study shows prolonged hypoxemia or central apneas

\#### Repeat attended sleep studies in diagnosed OSA

\- Indicated if there is a contraindication to home testing or for PAP
titration; otherwise, home testing should be used

\- May be performed up to twice yearly for:

\- Ongoing symptoms despite adequate adherence

\- Defined as 4 hours/night for 70% of nights over a 30-day period

\- Need to change device because of intolerance

\- Need to change PAP settings

\- Determining whether PAP is still necessary after significant weight
loss

\- Determining need to reinstitute or change treatment after significant
weight gain or recurrent symptoms

\- Assessing response after upper-airway surgery or initial
oral-appliance treatment

\- Remote history of OSA without PAP and need to re-establish diagnosis
and/or initiate CPAP

\- Reassessment of sleep-related hypoxemia and/or hypoventilation after
OSA treatment initiation

\- Reevaluation when cardiovascular disease develops or changes

\- Follow-up PSG when PAP device-generated data are unexplained

\- Upper-airway stimulation therapy:

\- Pre-implant reevaluation of known OSA with PAP failure/intolerance
and BMI 35 or lower and no recent sleep study, or significant change in
weight/symptoms

\- Post-implant:

\- Initial PSG titration

\- Repeat titration after insufficient response, weight gain, and/or
recurrent symptoms

\### Pediatric Criteria

\#### Respiratory indications

\- Habitual snoring with one or more listed signs/symptoms of
obstructive sleep apnea syndrome to differentiate from primary snoring.

\- Listed symptoms/signs include:

\- Frequent snoring (3 or more nights per week)

\- Gasps / observed apneas / snorting noises

\- Labored breathing during sleep

\- Cyanosis

\- Sleeping in a seated position or with an extended neck

\- Attention-deficit / hyperactivity symptoms

\- Learning problems

\- Daytime sleepiness

\- Sleep enuresis, especially secondary enuresis

\- Underweight or overweight status

\- Tonsillar hypertrophy

\- Adenoidal facies

\- Micrognathia / retrognathia

\- High-arched palate

\- Failure to thrive

\- Hypertension

\- Other respiratory indications:

\- Child being considered for adenotonsillectomy to treat obstructive
sleep apnea syndrome

\- Suspected congenital central alveolar hypoventilation syndrome

\- Suspected sleep-related hypoventilation due to chest wall deformity
or neuromuscular disorder

\- Respiratory disorders only when there is clinical suspicion of
accompanying sleep-related breathing disorder, including:

\- Chronic asthma

\- Cystic fibrosis

\- Pulmonary hypertension

\- Bronchopulmonary dysplasia

\- Chest wall abnormality such as kyphoscoliosis

\- Following an apparent life-threatening event when there is clinical
evidence of sleep-related breathing disorder

\- Neurological disorders

\- Certain genetic disorders

\#### Non-respiratory indications

\- Suspected narcolepsy with PSG / multiple sleep latency testing when:

\- There is excessive daytime sleepiness despite adequate sleep and not
thought due to another sleep disorder

\- Narcolepsy-associated symptoms may include:

\- Cataplexy

\- Hypnagogic hallucinations

\- Sleep paralysis

\- Hypersomnia from suspected causes other than narcolepsy

\- Suspected parasomnia or seizure disorders, including:

\- Non-REM parasomnias, epilepsy, or nocturnal enuresis when there is
suspicion of a comorbid sleep disorder such as sleep-disordered
breathing or periodic limb movement disorder

\- Confirmation of an atypical or potentially injurious parasomnia

\- Differentiation of parasomnia from sleep-related epilepsy when
initial clinical evaluation and standard EEG are inconclusive

\- Suspected restless leg syndrome or periodic limb movement disorder:

\- Repetitive limb movements during sleep with frequent awakenings,
fragmented sleep, difficulty maintaining sleep, or excessive daytime
sleepiness

\- To document periodic limb movements when periodic limb movement
disorder is suspected

\- To provide supportive data when restless leg syndrome is suspected

\#### Pediatric titration and follow-up studies

\- PAP titration in children with obstructive sleep apnea syndrome:

\- Children treated with an oral appliance, to assess response

\- Following adenotonsillectomy or other pharyngeal surgery when any of
the following are present

\- Moderate to severe preoperative disease

\- Cardiac complications

\- Craniofacial anomalies

\- Neurological disorders

\- Obesity

\- Persistent symptoms after treatment

\- After rapid maxillary expansion

\- Follow-up PSG in children on chronic PAP support when pressure
requirements may have changed due to:

\- Growth/development

\- Recurrent symptoms

\- Additional or alternative treatment

\- Noninvasive positive-pressure ventilation titration in children with
other sleep-related breathing disorders

\- Children on mechanical ventilation to adjust settings

\- Children with tracheostomy for sleep-related breathing disorders as
part of evaluation before decannulation

\- Results of special testing

\- If applicable:

\- Prior relevant imaging/results

\- Reason alternative imaging cannot be performed

\- Contraindications to home testing when attended testing is being

\- AHI thresholds and symptom burden when titration/follow-up testing is
requested

\- Adequate nocturnal sleep time before latency testing

\- Need for repeat or pediatric follow-up testing, when applicable

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Not indicated for:

\- Management of oxygen therapy

\- Nap (abbreviated) polysomnography

\- In adults, home testing should generally be used for repeat OSA
reassessment unless there is a contraindication to home testing or PAP
titration is needed.

\- Multiple sleep latency testing:

\- Other indications outside central hypersomnia evaluation are
considered experimental/investigational.

\- Should not be performed after a split-night study.

\- PSG is not indicated in other sleep-related movement disorders unless
another underlying sleep disorder is suspected.

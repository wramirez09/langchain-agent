\## Epidural Spine Injections

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- New episode of care:

\- Any injection at least 2 years from prior injections in the same
region is treated as a new episode of care

\- Surgery in the same spinal region or new pathology also triggers a
new episode

\- General criteria for all caudal, interlaminar, and transforaminal
injections:

\- Fluoroscopic guidance should be used for caudal and interlaminar
injections

\- Fluoroscopic guidance is necessary for transforaminal injections

\- Pain must cause functional disability or average pain level must be
\`\>= 6/10\` in the requested spinal region

\- Treatment purposes:

\- Acute pain or exacerbation of chronic radicular pain:

\- Neck or back pain with acute radicular symptoms

\- Duration of pain \`\< 3 months\`

\- Failure of non-operative conservative treatment for at least 2 weeks,
unless medically unable to complete

\- Spinal stenosis causing axial or radicular pain:

\- Failure of non-operative conservative treatment for at least 6 weeks
within the last 6 months

\- Or documentation of ongoing non-operative conservative treatment if
prior spinal injections occurred in the same region

\- Failed back surgery syndrome or epidural fibrosis causing axial or
radicular pain:

\- Documentation clearly explaining why injection is needed

\- Failure of non-operative conservative treatment for at least 6 weeks
within the last 6 months

\- Or documentation of ongoing non-operative conservative treatment if
prior spinal injections occurred in the same region

\- Failure of conservative treatment is defined as one of the following:

\- Lack of meaningful improvement after a full course of treatment

\- Progression or worsening of symptoms during treatment

\- Documented medical reason the patient cannot participate

\- Diagnostic purposes:

\- Transforaminal injection to identify the pain generator for surgical
planning

\- Requires pre-operative evaluation and surgical plan

\- No more than 2 levels of transforaminal blocks in 1 day

\- Repeat injections:

\- Initial treatment phase:

\- Up to 3 epidural injections

\- No sooner than 2 weeks apart

\- Requires at least 30% pain relief or significant documented
functional improvement

\- If unsuccessful, another injection may be done at a different level
in the same region or with a different technique if pain generator is
uncertain or multilevel pathology exists

\- Therapeutic phase:

\- Requires at least 50% pain relief or significant documented
functional improvement for at least 2 months before each therapeutic
injection

\- Ongoing pain causing functional disability or average pain \`\>=
6/10\`

\- Ongoing active conservative treatment unless medically unable

\- First year: maximum 6 epidural injections per spinal region total

\- After first year: maximum 4 epidural injections per 12 months per
spinal region

\- With special documented circumstances: may allow up to 6 injections
per 12 months per spinal region

\- Different spinal regions should usually be treated at least 7 days
apart unless a medical reason is documented

\### Relevant Codes

\- ICD-10:

\- None listed

\- CPT:

\- Cervical/thoracic: \`62320, 62321, 64479, +64480\`

\- Lumbar/sacral: \`62322, 62323, 64483, +64484\`

\### Required Documentation

\- Pain score and/or functional disability

\- Conservative treatment history and failure details

\- Ongoing conservative treatment details when applicable

\- Medical reason if conservative treatment cannot be done

\- Pre-operative evaluation and surgical plan for diagnostic
transforaminal injections

\- Documentation of response to prior injections/interventions

\- Recent office notes, labs, special testing, and prior imaging as
applicable

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Exclusions:

\- Intrathecal injections for pain or spasticity prior to permanent pump
insertion

\- Implantation of intrathecal catheters or ports for chemotherapy

\- Post-operative pain control

\- Caudal or spinal anesthesia for surgery

\- Contraindications:

\- Active systemic or spinal infection

\- Skin infection at needle puncture site

\- Severe spinal stenosis causing intraspinal obstruction

\- Additional limitations:

\- Different interventional pain procedures in the same spinal region
should not be done on the same day

\- Exception: facet injection plus epidural steroid injection in the
same session for imaging-confirmed synovial cyst

\## Epidural Spine Injections and Single Injection Trials for
Intrathecal Pumps

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Epidural injection criteria:

\- Same core epidural criteria as the section above:

\- Imaging guidance requirements

\- Pain causing functional disability or average pain \`\>= 6/10\`

\- Acute radicular pain, spinal stenosis, or failed back surgery
syndrome/epidural fibrosis pathways

\- Same repeat injection rules and interval limits

\- Intraspinal drug trial:

\- Chronic intractable pain in non-terminal individuals:

\- Pain significantly interferes with activities of daily living, work,
or quality of life, or persistent pain \`\>= 6/10\` despite treatment

\- Failure of non-operative conservative therapy for at least 12 weeks
unless medically unable

\- Spasticity in non-terminal individuals:

\- Intractable spasticity causing inability to maintain upright posture,
severe balance impairment with ambulation, or significant ADL
interference due to one of:

\- Spinal cord injury

\- Multiple sclerosis

\- Stiff person syndrome

\- Other medical conditions causing intractable spasms

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of non-operative conservative therapy

\- Additional trials:

\- A second intraspinal drug trial is allowed when the first trial
caused:

\- Less than 50% pain relief

\- Intolerable side effects

\- Limit of 2 intraspinal drug trials

\- Intrathecal trials are not indicated in opioid-naive individuals

\### Relevant Codes

\- ICD-10:

\- None listed

\- CPT:

\- Cervical/thoracic: \`62320, 62321, 64479, +64480\`

\- Lumbar/sacral: \`62322, 62323, 64483, +64484\`

\- Note:

\- No separate intrathecal trial code list was shown in the retrieved
content

\### Required Documentation

\- All common documentation listed above

\- Documentation of pain severity/functional impairment

\- Documentation of 12-week conservative therapy failure

\- Documentation of first trial result if requesting a second trial

\- Pre-operative evaluation and plan if using epidural transforaminal
injection for surgical planning

\- Prior imaging and reason alternate imaging cannot be performed when
relevant

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Epidural contraindications:

\- Active systemic or spinal infection

\- Skin infection at the puncture site

\- Severe spinal stenosis causing intraspinal obstruction

\- Intraspinal drug trial contraindications:

\- Active systemic or spinal infection

\- Body habitus insufficient to support the weight and bulk of the
device

\- Exclusions:

\- Implantation of intrathecal catheters or ports for chemotherapy

\- Post-operative pain control

\- Caudal or spinal anesthesia for surgery

\## Paravertebral Facet Joint Injections or Blocks

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- New episode of care:

\- Injection at least 2 years after prior injections in the same region
is a new episode

\- Surgery in the same region or new pathology also creates a new
episode

\- Special counting rule:

\- Unilateral right-versus-left injections at the same level within 1
month count as 1 procedure toward annual limits

\- To confirm non-radicular pain suggestive of facet joint or pars
interarticularis origin, all must be met:

\- Mainly axial pain or non-radicular pain unless stenosis is caused by
synovial cyst

\- Lack of evidence that the primary pain source is sacroiliac pain,
discogenic pain, disc herniation, or radiculitis

\- If chronic lumbar spondylolysis is the basis, imaging must confirm
pars interarticularis fracture/defect

\- Pain causing functional disability or average pain \`\>= 6/10\` in
the requested spinal region

\- Duration of pain at least 3 months

\- Failure of non-operative conservative treatment for at least 6 weeks
in the last 6 months, unless medically unable

\- Or ongoing non-operative conservative treatment if prior spinal
injections occurred in the same region

\- Failure of conservative treatment means one of:

\- Lack of meaningful improvement after full treatment

\- Progression or worsening during treatment

\- Documented medical reason treatment could not be completed

\- Imaging guidance:

\- CT or fluoroscopy commonly used

\- Ultrasound may be an alternative when CT/fluoroscopy is
contraindicated, but all procedures must be performed under imaging
guidance

\- Repeat injections:

\- Initial diagnostic phase:

\- Up to 2 diagnostic injections

\- No sooner than 2 weeks apart

\- Requires at least 50% pain relief or significant documented
functional improvement

\- If local anesthetic only, there must be at least 7 days between
injections

\- If first diagnostic injection is unsuccessful, second may be done at
a different spinal level or with a different technique

\- Therapeutic phase:

\- Requires at least 50% pain relief or significant documented
functional improvement for at least 2 months before each therapeutic
injection

\- Ongoing pain causing functional disability or average pain \`\>=
6/10\`

\- Ongoing active conservative treatment unless medically unable

\- Diagnostic injections within 1 month of the previous injection do not
require documentation of ongoing active conservative therapy

\- Maximum 2 procedures in the diagnostic phase

\- Repeat diagnostic injections after prior radiofrequency neurolysis
allowed if:

\- Pain generator remains uncertain

\- Different levels are being targeted

\- Surgery occurred in the same spinal region

\- Maximum 4 facet injections per 12 months per spinal region, except
unusual circumstances such as recurrent injury

\- Different spinal regions should usually be treated at least 7 days
apart unless medically necessary

\- Practice note:

\- Radiofrequency neurolysis should be considered after a successful
medial branch block with at least 70% pain relief or improved function
but insufficient sustained relief \`\< 2-3 months\`

\### Relevant Codes

\- ICD-10:

\- None listed

\- CPT:

\- Cervical/thoracic: \`64490, +64491, +64492, +0213T, +0214T, +0215T\`

\- Lumbar: \`64493, +64494, +64495, 0216T, +0217T, +0218T\`

\### Required Documentation

\- Pain history showing axial/non-radicular pattern

\- Functional disability and/or pain score

\- Imaging confirmation of pars defect if chronic lumbar spondylolysis
is relevant

\- Conservative treatment details and failure definition

\- Prior injection response

\- Ongoing conservative treatment documentation when applicable

\- Exercise prescription and follow-up if home exercise program is used

\- Recent office notes, labs, special testing, and prior imaging as
applicable

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Exclusions:

\- Sacral lateral branch blocks \`(S1, S2, S3)\`

\- Atlantoaxial joint injections \`(C1-2)\`

\- Occipital nerve blocks

\- Hardware injection/block for diagnosis or treatment of post-surgical
or other spine pain

\- Relative contraindications:

\- Active systemic or spinal infection

\- Skin infection at the needle puncture site

\- Inability to obtain percutaneous access to target facet joint

\- Medication or contrast allergy

\## Paravertebral Facet Joint Denervation (Radiofrequency Neurolysis)

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Special counting rules:

\- Unilateral procedures on opposite sides at the same level within 1
month count as one procedure

\- No minimum timeframe is required between denervation procedures

\- Opposite-side procedures within 1 month do not require follow-up
information submission

\- For facet-mediated pain, all must be met:

\- Lack of evidence that the primary pain source is sacroiliac pain,
discogenic pain, disc herniation, or radiculitis

\- Pain causing functional disability or average pain \`\>= 6/10\` in
the requested spinal region

\- Duration of pain at least 3 months

\- After diagnostic medial branch block:

\- At least one local anesthetic medial branch block with at least 70%
pain relief or improved function

\- Relief duration at least equal to expected anesthetic duration

\- Relief was not sustained for 3 months

\- Continued pain after diagnostic relief period causing functional
disability or average pain \`\>= 6/10\`

\- Failure of conservative treatment for at least 6 weeks in the last 6
months

\- Failure of conservative treatment means one of:

\- Lack of meaningful improvement after full treatment

\- Progression/worsening during treatment

\- Documented medical reason treatment could not be completed

\- Imaging guidance:

\- Fluoroscopy or CT

\- All procedures must use fluoroscopic or CT guidance

\- Repeat procedures:

\- Positive response to prior denervation with at least 50% pain relief
or improved function for at least 4 months

\- Ongoing pain causing functional disability or average pain \`\>=
6/10\`

\- Ongoing non-operative conservative treatment unless medically unable

\- Maximum 2 facet denervation procedures per 12 months per spinal
region

\### Relevant Codes

\- ICD-10:

\- None listed

\- CPT:

\- Cervical/thoracic: \`64633, +64634\`

\- Lumbar: \`64635, +64636\`

\### Required Documentation

\- Proof of prior diagnostic medial branch block response

\- Pain score and functional impairment

\- Conservative treatment history and failure details

\- Ongoing conservative treatment documentation when applicable

\- Prior denervation response for repeat requests

\- Recent office notes, labs, special testing, and prior imaging as
applicable

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Exclusions:

\- Radiofrequency denervation of the sacroiliac joint and/or sacral
lateral branches \`(S1, S2, S3)\`

\- Contraindications:

\- Active systemic or spinal infection

\- Skin infection at needle puncture site

\- Additional limitation:

\- Pulsed radiofrequency, cryo-ablation, and laser ablation are not
included in the definition of covered denervation in the retrieved text

\## Sacroiliac Joint Injections

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- New episode of care:

\- Injection at least 2 years after prior injections in the same region
is a new episode

\- Surgery in the same spinal region or new pathology also creates a new
episode

\- Sacroiliac joint pain:

\- Primarily axial low back pain below L5, possibly radiating to groin
or lower extremity

\- Pain causing functional disability or average pain \`\>= 6/10\` in
the requested spinal region

\- At least 3 positive provocation tests from the following:

\- Pelvic distraction

\- Pelvic compression

\- Sacral thrust

\- FABER

\- Posterior shear

\- Yeoman

\- Gaenslen

\- Thigh thrust

\- Duration of pain at least 3 months

\- Failure of non-operative conservative treatment for at least 6 weeks
in the last 6 months unless medically unable

\- Or active engagement in ongoing non-operative conservative treatment
if prior spinal injections occurred in the same region

\- Spondyloarthropathy:

\- Low back pain for \`\>= 3 months\`

\- Age of onset \`\< 45 years\`

\- Comprehensive pain management program in place, such as:

\- Physical therapy

\- Home exercise

\- Patient education

\- Psychosocial support

\- Oral medication

\- Prior evidence of sacroiliitis on imaging:

\- Active inflammation on MRI, or

\- Definite radiographic sacroiliitis grade \`2-4\` bilaterally, or
grade \`3-4\` unilaterally

\- At least 1 spondyloarthropathy feature, including:

\- Inflammatory back pain evidence with at least 4 of:

\- Onset before age 40

\- Insidious onset

\- Improvement with exercise

\- No improvement with rest

\- Night pain improving on getting up

\- Arthritis

\- Heel enthesitis

\- Uveitis

\- Dactylitis

\- Psoriasis

\- Crohn disease/colitis

\- Good response to NSAIDs

\- Family history of spondyloarthropathy

\- Positive HLA-B27

\- Elevated CRP

\- Imaging guidance:

\- Fluoroscopy or CT commonly used

\- Ultrasound may be an alternative when fluoroscopy/CT is
contraindicated

\- All procedures must be performed under imaging guidance

\- Diagnostic purposes for surgical planning:

\- Image-guided, contrast-enhanced intra-articular injection

\- At least 75% pain relief for expected anesthetic duration after each
diagnostic injection

\- Persistent pain after diagnostic relief period causing functional
disability or average pain \`\>= 6/10\`

\- No more than 2 diagnostic injections per diagnostic phase

\- Requires pre-operative evaluation and SI joint surgery plan

\- Repeat injections:

\- Initial treatment phase:

\- Up to 2 injections

\- No sooner than 2 weeks apart

\- Requires at least 50% pain relief or significant documented
functional improvement

\- Therapeutic phase:

\- Requires at least 50% pain relief or significant documented
functional improvement for at least 2 months before each therapeutic
injection

\- Ongoing pain causing functional disability or average pain \`\>=
6/10\`

\- Ongoing active conservative treatment unless medically unable

\- If other lumbar/sacral interventional injections were done since the
last SI injection, at least 1 repeat positive provocative exam finding
is required

\- Maximum 4 SI joint injections per 12 months per region

\### Relevant Codes

\- ICD-10:

\- None listed

\- CPT/HCPCS:

\- \`27096\`

\- \`G0260\`

\### Required Documentation

\- Pain pattern and severity

\- Functional disability

\- Provocative exam findings

\- Conservative treatment history and failure

\- Imaging evidence of sacroiliitis when using spondyloarthropathy
pathway

\- Comprehensive pain management plan for spondyloarthropathy pathway

\- Pre-operative evaluation and surgical plan for diagnostic
surgical-planning injections

\- Response to prior SI injections

\- Repeat provocative exam finding if other lumbar/sacral interventions
occurred since last SI injection

\- Exercise prescription and follow-up if home exercise program is used

\- Recent office notes, labs, special testing, and prior imaging as
applicable

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Exclusions:

\- Sacral lateral branch blocks \`(S1, S2, S3)\`

\- Radiofrequency denervation of the sacroiliac joint

\- Absolute contraindications:

\- Active systemic or spinal infection

\- Skin infection at needle puncture site

\- Local malignancy

\- Septic joint

\- Relative contraindications:

\- Coagulopathy

\- Pregnancy

\- Uncontrollable diabetes

\- Current uninterrupted use of blood-thinning medication

\- Additional limitation:

\- SI joint injections cannot be performed by telehealth; in-person
provocative physical exam is expected before injection

\## Implantable Infusion Pump Insertion

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Intraspinal drug trial:

\- Chronic intractable pain in non-terminal individuals:

\- Pain significantly interferes with ADLs, work, or quality of life, or
persistent pain \`\>= 6/10\` despite treatment

\- Failure of non-operative conservative therapy for at least 12 weeks
unless medically unable

\- Spasticity in non-terminal individuals:

\- Intractable spasticity causing inability to maintain upright posture,
severe ambulatory balance impairment, or significant ADL interference
due to:

\- Spinal cord injury

\- Multiple sclerosis

\- Stiff person syndrome

\- Other medical conditions causing intractable spasms

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of non-operative conservative therapy

\- Additional trial:

\- Second trial allowed if first intraspinal trial resulted in:

\- Less than 50% pain relief

\- Intolerable side effects

\- Limit 2 total trials

\- Intrathecal trials are not indicated in opioid-naive individuals

\- Permanent implanted infusion pump:

\- Chronic intractable pain in non-terminal individuals:

\- Pain significantly interferes with ADLs, work, or quality of life, or
persistent pain \`\>= 6/10\`

\- Failure of non-operative conservative therapy for at least 12 weeks
unless medically unable

\- At least 12 weeks of oral or transdermal opioid or nonopioid pain
medication

\- Successful intraspinal medication trial with at least 50% pain relief
and tolerable side effects

\- Completed psychological assessment documenting cognitive ability,
physical capability, and willingness to participate

\- Spasticity in non-terminal individuals:

\- Intractable spasticity due to qualifying conditions listed above

\- Failure of at least 12 weeks of conservative therapy

\- Successful trial of intraspinal antispasmodic medication by bolus or
continuous infusion with at least 50% spasm relief and tolerable side
effects

\- Completed psychological assessment documenting cognitive ability,
physical capability, and willingness to participate

\- Pump replacement, revision, or removal:

\- Indicated for any of:

\- Loss of effectiveness, including battery depletion

\- Intolerance

\- Infection

\- Painful generator site

\- Patient demand

\- Pump or catheter malfunction impairing function or safety

\- Other medically appropriate reason

\### Relevant Codes

\- ICD-10:

\- None listed

\- CPT:

\- \`62350, 62351, 62355, 62360, 62361, 62362\`

\### Required Documentation

\- Pain severity or spasticity severity and functional impact

\- Conservative therapy failure for at least 12 weeks

\- Medication history, including at least 12 weeks of oral/transdermal
opioid or nonopioid therapy for chronic pain pathway

\- Documentation of successful intraspinal trial results

\- Psychological assessment before permanent implantation

\- If requesting replacement of a programmable pump, pump analysis
report should accompany the request

\- Recent office notes, labs, special testing, and prior imaging as
applicable

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Contraindications:

\- Active systemic or spinal infection

\- Body habitus insufficient to support device weight and bulk

\- Additional limitations:

\- No medical indications for intrathecal treatments except chronic pain
and intractable spasticity

\## Spinal Cord Stimulation

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Trial stimulation:

\- Duration of pain at least 6 months

\- Pain causing functional disability or average pain \`\>= 6/10\`

\- Failure of non-operative conservative treatment for at least 6 months
unless medically unable

\- Completed psychological assessment documenting:

\- Pain is not due to psychiatric disorders such as depression, anxiety,
somatic symptom disorder, or sequelae of substance use

\- Personality and psychiatric disorders are satisfactorily managed

\- Substance use disorder in recovery is satisfactorily managed

\- Cognitive ability to manage the stimulator

\- Pain caused by at least one qualifying diagnosis/pathway, including
retrieved examples:

\- Failed spine surgery syndrome/post-laminectomy syndrome

\- Complex regional pain syndrome type I or II meeting Budapest criteria

\- Chronic neuropathic pain of certain origins, including:

\- Lumbosacral arachnoiditis

\- Postherpetic neuralgia

\- Radiculopathy

\- Chronic ischemic leg pain

\- Diabetic peripheral neuropathy

\- Phantom limb syndrome

\- Peripheral neuropathy

\- Chronic back pain of neuropathic type in a non-surgical candidate

\- Chronic refractory angina pectoris, with all of:

\- Continued angina after PCI or CABG

\- Not a candidate for further revascularization

\- NYHA class III or IV symptoms

\- At least 1 month of optimal pharmacotherapy with intolerance or
inadequate response

\- Permanent stimulator:

\- Successful temporary trial with at least 50% reduction in pain

\- Significant functional improvement for at least 3 days

\- Clear medical reason if the permanent stimulator device differs from
the temporary trial device

\- Revision or removal:

\- Migration of leads

\- Loss of effectiveness

\- Intolerance

\- Infection

\- Painful generator site

\- Development of neurologic deficits

\- Patient demand

\### Relevant Codes

\- ICD-10:

\- None listed

\- CPT:

\- \`63650, 63655, 63661, 63662, 63663, 63664, 63685, 63688\`

\### Required Documentation

\- Duration of pain

\- Pain score and/or functional disability

\- Conservative treatment failure for at least 6 months

\- Psychological assessment

\- Documentation of qualifying diagnosis

\- Temporary trial results showing at least 50% pain reduction

\- Documentation of significant functional improvement for at least 3
days

\- Medical reason if permanent device differs from trial device

\- Recent office notes, labs, special testing, and prior imaging as
applicable

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Contraindications:

\- Active systemic or spinal infection

\- Body habitus insufficient to support device weight and bulk

\- Coagulation disorder

\- Pregnancy

\- Additional limitation:

\- Multiple interventional pain procedures on the same date are
generally not medically necessary without a documented medical reason

\## Sympathetic Nerve Blocks

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- New episode of care:

\- Injection at least 2 years after prior injections in the same region
is a new episode

\- Surgery in the same region or new pathology also creates a new
episode

\- General indications:

\- Acute or chronic noncancer pain causing functional disability or
average pain \`\>= 6/10\` before injection and continuation after the
relief period

\- Cancer pain affecting quality of life before injection and
continuation after the relief period

\- Each block must be performed under image guidance

\- Multiple interventional pain procedures on the same day are generally
not medically necessary

\- Stellate ganglion block:

\- Applies to face, upper extremities, and upper thoracic region

\- Diagnostic evaluation or acute management:

\- Acute herpes zoster for prevention of postherpetic neuralgia, with:

\- Pain duration \`\< 4 weeks\`

\- Active antiviral therapy or documented inability to tolerate

\- Cancer pain or phantom limb pain

\- Failure of diagnosis-appropriate conservative treatment unless
medically unable

\- Previous block gave at least 50% relief or significant functional
improvement for at least anesthetic duration

\- Up to 6 sympathetic blocks per 12 months

\- Diagnostic, acute, or chronic management:

\- CRPS type I or II meeting Budapest criteria

\- Failure of functional restoration modalities unless medically unable

\- Active participation in ongoing functional restoration unless
medically unable

\- Previous block gave at least 50% relief or significant functional
improvement for at least anesthetic duration

\- Initial treatment phase: up to 6 blocks in first 12 weeks

\- After initial phase: maximum 4 sympathetic blocks in 12 months

\- General limitations:

\- At least 1 week since prior injection in same or different region

\- No bilateral stellate ganglion blocks on same day

\- Thoracic or lumbar sympathetic block:

\- Applies to thoracic region and lower extremities

\- Diagnostic evaluation or acute management:

\- Acute herpes zoster for prevention of postherpetic neuralgia, with:

\- Pain duration \`\< 4 weeks\`

\- Active antiviral therapy or documented inability to tolerate

\- Cancer pain, phantom limb pain, or nonsurgical ischemic limb pain

\- Failure of diagnosis-appropriate conservative treatment unless
medically unable

\- Previous block gave at least 50% pain relief, significant functional
improvement, or 50% reduction in PTSD symptoms for at least anesthetic
duration

\- Up to 6 blocks per 12 months

\- Diagnostic, acute, or chronic management:

\- CRPS type I or II meeting Budapest criteria

\- Failure of functional restoration modalities unless medically unable

\- Active participation in ongoing functional restoration unless
medically unable

\- Previous block gave at least 50% relief or significant functional
improvement for at least anesthetic duration

\- Initial treatment phase: up to 6 blocks in first 12 weeks

\- After initial phase: maximum 4 sympathetic blocks in 12 months

\- General limitations:

\- At least 1 week since prior injection in same or different region

\- No bilateral thoracic or lumbar sympathetic blocks on same day

\- Ultrasound guidance is not included

\- Celiac plexus block:

\- Applies to upper abdomen

\- Diagnostic evaluation of sympathetically maintained visceral pain:

\- Upper abdominal pain associated with malignancy

\- Conservative treatment not required

\- Up to 2 diagnostic blocks in the initial diagnostic phase for planned
neurolysis

\- Acute or chronic management:

\- Acute pancreatitis, or

\- Chronic relapsing pancreatitis

\- Failure of diagnosis-appropriate conservative treatment unless
medically unable

\- If first injection unsuccessful, a second initial injection may be
performed, maximum 2 injections in initial phase

\- After initial phase, therapeutic block may be performed every 3
months in a 12-month period

\- Previous block gave at least 50% relief or significant functional
improvement for at least anesthetic duration

\- Each therapeutic block must have resulted in at least 50% relief
lasting 3 months

\- General limitations:

\- At least 1 week between diagnostic blocks or initial-phase injections

\- Superior hypogastric block:

\- Applies to pelvic and rectal regions

\- Diagnostic evaluation of sympathetically maintained visceral pain:

\- Pelvic or rectal pain associated with malignancy

\- Conservative treatment not required

\- Up to 2 diagnostic blocks in initial diagnostic phase for planned
neurolysis

\- Acute or chronic management:

\- Chronic noncancer pain of pelvic and rectal viscera

\- Failure of diagnosis-appropriate conservative treatment unless
medically unable

\- If first injection unsuccessful, second initial injection allowed,
maximum 2 injections in initial phase

\- After initial phase, therapeutic block may be performed every 3
months in a 12-month period

\- Previous block gave at least 50% relief or significant functional
improvement for at least anesthetic duration

\- Each therapeutic block must have resulted in at least 50% relief
lasting 3 months

\- General limitations:

\- At least 1 week between diagnostic blocks or initial-phase injections

\- Ultrasound guidance is not included

\### Relevant Codes

\- ICD-10:

\- None listed

\- CPT:

\- \`64510, 64517, 64520, 64530\`

\### Required Documentation

\- Pain severity and/or functional disability before injection

\- Continuation of symptoms after relief period

\- Diagnosis-specific conservative treatment history or reason it could
not be done

\- Antiviral therapy documentation for herpes zoster pathways, or
documented intolerance

\- CRPS diagnosis meeting Budapest criteria where applicable

\- Functional restoration participation or reason it could not be done

\- Response to previous block, including percent pain relief, functional
improvement, or duration of benefit

\- Prior imaging and reason alternative imaging cannot be performed when
relevant

\- Recent office notes, labs, and special testing as applicable

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Exclusions:

\- Sphenopalatine ganglion block

\- Ganglion impar block

\- Other parasympathetic ganglion blocks

\- Inferior hypogastric block

\- Contraindications by block type:

\- Stellate ganglion block:

\- Patient refusal

\- Local or systemic infection

\- Coagulopathy or recent myocardial infarction

\- Contralateral pneumothorax or severe emphysema

\- Contralateral recurrent laryngeal nerve palsy or phrenic nerve palsy

\- Allergy to anesthetic medication

\- Thoracic/lumbar sympathetic block:

\- Patient refusal

\- Local or systemic infection

\- Coagulopathy, hypotension, or recent myocardial infarction

\- Contralateral pneumothorax

\- Allergy to anesthetic medication

\- Celiac plexus block:

\- Patient refusal

\- Local or systemic infection

\- Coagulopathy, hypotension, or recent myocardial infarction

\- Contralateral pneumothorax

\- Allergy to anesthetic medication

\- Abnormal anatomy

\- Superior hypogastric block:

\- Patient refusal

\- Local or systemic infection

\- Coagulopathy, hypotension, or recent myocardial infarction

\- Allergy to anesthetic medication

\- Abnormal anatomy

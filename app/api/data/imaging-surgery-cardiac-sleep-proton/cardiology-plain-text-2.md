\## Guideline 7305 -- Inferior Vena Cava Filter Device

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Presence of DVT or PE with any of the following:

\- Failure or major complication of anticoagulation

\- Contraindication to anticoagulation

\- Recurrent PE despite anticoagulation

\- Poor compliance with anticoagulation

\- Massive PE with residual DVT in a patient at risk for further PE

\- PE with limited cardiac reserve

\- High risk of clinically significant procedure-related PE:

\- Prophylaxis in severe trauma, spinal cord injury, or paraplegia

\- Prophylaxis before surgery in a patient with DVT

\- Protection during DVT thrombolysis

\- Removal or repositioning:

\- Indication for removal or repositioning must be documented in
provider notes

\### Relevant Codes

\- CPT:

\- 37191

\- 37192

\- 37193

\- ICD-10:

\- None explicitly listed in the retrieved section

\### Required Documentation

\- Recent office notes, labs, special testing, and prior imaging when
applicable

\- Provider notes documenting indication for removal/repositioning, if
requested

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Absolute contraindication:

\- Lack of access into the IVC

\- Relative contraindications:

\- Bleeding diathesis

\- Total thrombosis of the IVC

\- Bacteremia or sepsis

\- Caval diameter less than 15 mm

\## Guideline 7317 -- Percutaneous Closure of Patent Foramen Ovale

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- All of the following:

\- Prior cryptogenic stroke or TIA

\- Age 60 years or younger

\- TEE evidence of interatrial communication amenable to percutaneous
closure

\### Relevant Codes

\- CPT:

\- 93580

\- ICD-10:

\- None explicitly listed in the retrieved section

\### Required Documentation

\- Medical notes from both:

\- Cardiologist

\- Neurologist

\- Notes must support the need for the procedure and document that no
other obvious etiology for the neurologic event has been found

\- TEE report documenting:

\- Presence of the defect

\- Suitability of anatomy for device placement

\- Diagnostic testing to rule out other causes of neurologic event,
including at least:

\- Carotid duplex or CTA/MRA

\- Evidence of hematologic workup

\- Evidence of heart rhythm monitoring

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Other stroke risk factors not expected to be corrected by closure,
including but not limited to:

\- Other cardiac embolic source apart from PFO

\- Rheumatic mitral stenosis

\- Significant carotid or intracranial atherosclerosis

\- Protruding or mobile aortic plaque

\- Coagulopathy

\- Atrial fibrillation or flutter

\- Vasculitis involving the carotid circulation

\- Presence of an inferior vena cava filter

\- Elevated bleeding risk or coagulopathy preventing:

\- Dual antiplatelet therapy for 6 months

\- Aspirin indefinitely thereafter

\## Guideline 7277 -- Device Programming (PPM, AICD, CRT-D, CRT-P,
S-ICD, ILR, Wearable Defibrillator)

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Routine device programming:

\- Within 72 hours of implantation or pulse generator change

\- May be indicated during routine follow-up 2 to 12 weeks after
implantation

\- May be indicated every:

\- 3 to 12 months for pacemakers

\- 3 to 6 months for ICDs and resynchronization devices

\- Patient-related indications:

\- Change in clinical status or cardiovascular symptoms affecting device
function

\- Change in therapy or medication regimen influencing rhythm or device
function

\- Disease-specific programming:

\- Heart failure device optimization, including AV optimization

\- Reprogramming to reduce unnecessary shocks or oversensing

\- Device-related indications:

\- Lead malfunction

\- Lead recall

\- Battery approaching end of life

\- Frequent or inappropriate shocks requiring optimization of:

\- Rate threshold sensing

\- Duration thresholds for VT/VF

\- Antitachycardia pacing

\- SVT vs VT discrimination

\- T-wave or lead-related oversensing

\- Excessive battery depletion caused by current settings

\- New permanent changes required after recent evaluation or remote
interrogation

\- Remote monitoring indications:

\- Over-reporting or under-reporting actionable cardiac events

\- ILR undersensing or oversensing

\- Other considerations:

\- Defibrillation threshold testing for S-ICD may be appropriate at
implantation or generator replacement

\## Guideline 7329 -- Subcutaneous ICD Device Implantation and Removal

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Patient should be on maximally tolerated GDMT

\- Preimplant ECG is needed to assess QRS-T morphology and reduce risk
of undersensing/inappropriate shocks

\- Appropriate for patients with:

\- Congenital heart disease

\- No venous access and unsuitable for transvenous ICD

\- No current or anticipated need for:

\- Bradycardia pacing

\- VT termination pacing

\- CRT

\- High infection risk, including:

\- Prior device infection

\- Hemodialysis

\- ESRD

\- Diabetes mellitus

\- Chronic immunosuppression or immunodeficiency

\- Artificial heart valves

\- Candidate for cardiac transplant

\- Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy without indication for ATP

\- Primary prevention in ischemic or nonischemic dilated cardiomyopathy
when bradycardia pacing need or first-time monomorphic VT likelihood is
low

\- Lead repositioning or replacement is appropriate for:

\- Lead complications

\- Inappropriate shocks

\- Oversensing

\- Other specified lead failure

\### Relevant Codes

\- CPT:

\- 33270

\- 33271

\- 33272

\- 33273

\- 93644

\- ICD-10:

\- None explicitly listed in the retrieved section

\### Required Documentation

\- Progress note prompting request

\- Echo, MUGA, or cardiac cath for LV function

\- Previous Holter, event monitor, or loop recorder report

\- Standard supporting documentation as noted above

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Not indicated for:

\- Symptomatic bradycardia requiring permanent pacing

\- Systolic heart failure with left bundle branch block and indication
for CRT

\- Recurrent sustained monomorphic VT treatable with ATP

\- Recurrent idiopathic VF treated with catheter ablation due to high
risk of T-wave oversensing

\- Thin patients with poor subcutaneous tissue or abnormal chest wall
such as pectus excavatum

\- Also must consider predicted or observed lack of adequate response to
maximally tolerated GDMT

\## Guideline 7341 -- Venogram / Invasive Vein Mapping

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Diagnosis of DVT when:

\- Duplex ultrasound is limited or negative but suspicion remains high

\- CT or MR venogram cannot be done or is limited

\- Venous mapping before, during, or after surgical/interventional
procedures such as dialysis access

\- Evaluation of venous conditions including:

\- Perforator incompetency before sclerotherapy, thermal ablation, or
subfascial endoscopic ligation

\- Venous stenosis, hypertension, or malformations

\- Valvular insufficiency before treatment

\- Anatomic entrapment

\- Deep pelvic, thoracic, or caval thrombosis when CT/MR venogram is not
feasible or limited

\- Preoperative evaluation for tumor involvement/encasement when CT/MR
venogram is not feasible or limited

\- Evaluation for CVC placement when anatomic landmarks, duplex, CT
venography, or MR venography are not feasible

\- May also assess suspected CVC malfunction

\- Acute iliofemoral thrombophlebitis

\- Workup for possible iliac vein stenosis/obstruction before stent
placement in scenarios such as:

\- Post-thrombotic syndrome with venous claudication

\- Symptomatic unilateral C3 swelling, especially left-sided

\- Bilateral C3 swelling above the knee or severe calf swelling without
another explanation

\- Lower abdominal wall or groin varicosities

\- Ipsilateral recurrent DVT

\- CEAP C5-C6

\- CEAP C3 or C4 plus additional suggestive findings

\## Guideline 7315-01 -- Pacemaker Implantation

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Applies to bradycardia pacing indications; excludes conditions
expected to resolve

\- Visible adult indications include:

\- Symptomatic sinus bradycardia, including frequent pauses

\- Symptomatic chronotropic incompetence documented by stress testing or
cardiac monitoring

\- Third-degree/complete AV block, intermittent or persistent,
regardless of symptoms

\- High-grade AV block, regardless of symptoms

\- Atrial fibrillation while awake with pauses of 5 seconds or more or
symptomatic bradycardia

\- Sinus rhythm with AV block while awake with pauses of 3 seconds or
more, heart rate under 40 bpm, or infra-nodal escape rhythm

\- Following AV junction ablation

\- Symptomatic AV block due to necessary medical therapy without
alternative treatment

\- Exercise-induced second- or third-degree AV block without myocardial
ischemia

\- Neuromuscular disorders with marked first-degree or higher AV block
or H-V interval 70 ms or greater

\- Alternating bundle-branch block

\- Syncope with bundle branch block after exclusion of other likely
causes

\- Syncope with bundle branch block and HV interval 70 ms or greater or
infranodal block at EPS

\- Recurrent syncope with cardioinhibitory carotid sinus syndrome or
documented asystole

\- Symptomatic recurrent supraventricular tachycardia terminable by
pacing when ablation/drug therapy failed

\- Pause-dependent VT prevention

\- Selected HCM cases with medically refractory symptoms and LV outflow
obstruction

\- Leadless pacemaker indications visible in excerpt:

\- Need for single-chamber RV pacing in VVI/VVIR mode

\- High-grade AV block with AF

\- High-grade AV block without AF when atrial lead placement is
difficult, high risk, or unnecessary

\- Symptomatic bradycardia-tachycardia syndrome or SND when atrial lead
placement is difficult, high risk, or unnecessary

\- Rate-responsive pacing when needed

\- Congenital heart disease pacing indications visible in excerpt:

\- Symptomatic age- or activity-inappropriate bradycardia

\- Complex CHD with sinus bradycardia and marked low resting rate or
long pauses

\- Hemodynamic impairment from sinus bradycardia or loss of AV synchrony

\- Certain asymptomatic postoperative CHD patients with very low resting
rates or long pauses

\- Second- or third-degree AV block with symptoms, ventricular
dysfunction, or low output

\- Congenital complete AV block in infants/children/adults under listed
heart-rate or symptom thresholds

\- Unexplained syncope after congenital heart surgery complicated by
transient complete heart block

\## Guideline 7303 -- Interventions for Adults with Congenital Heart
Defects

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Visible criteria from the retrieved excerpt include:

\- ASD:

\- Surgical repair for patients with symptoms, right atrial and/or RV
enlargement, left-to-right shunt, Qp:Qs 1.5:1 or greater, no
resting/exercise cyanosis, and acceptable pulmonary pressures/resistance

\- Secundum ASD: percutaneous or surgical repair for symptomatic or
asymptomatic patients meeting hemodynamic criteria

\- Suspected paradoxical embolism with ASD and no other embolic source

\- VSD:

\- Surgical or percutaneous closure with left ventricular volume
overload, Qp:Qs 1.5:1 or greater, net left-to-right shunt, and
acceptable pulmonary pressures/resistance

\- Surgical closure for infective endocarditis caused by VSD

\- Surgical closure for perimembranous or supracristal VSD with
worsening aortic regurgitation

\- APVC:

\- Partial APVC surgical repair with RV volume overload, Qp:Qs 1.5:1 or
greater, and acceptable pulmonary pressures/resistance

\- Scimitar vein repair with RV volume overload and Qp:Qs 1.5:1 or
greater

\- Repair of partial APVC at time of sinus venosus defect or ASD closure

\- AVSD:

\- Primary repair or residual shunt repair when Qp:Qs 1.5:1 or greater
and pulmonary pressures/resistance are acceptable

\- Surgical repair for LVOT obstruction meeting gradient or
symptom/valve regurgitation criteria

\- Surgical repair for severe left AV valve regurgitation according to
severe MR criteria

\- TGA/atrial switch sequelae:

\- Percutaneous intervention for baffle stenosis or selected baffle
leaks

\- Surgical intervention for symptomatic pulmonary venous atrial
obstruction, symptomatic baffle stenosis not amenable to catheter
therapy, symptomatic baffle leaks not amenable to percutaneous therapy,
or severe tricuspid regurgitation without major systolic dysfunction

\- ccTGA:

\- Tricuspid valve replacement for symptomatic severe TR with
preserved/mildly depressed systemic ventricular function or asymptomatic
severe TR with dilation/mild dysfunction

\- Fontan:

\- Catheter ablation for atrial tachyarrhythmias

\- Fontan revision/arrhythmia surgery in selected refractory patients

\- Reoperation/intervention for structural abnormalities causing
symptoms or Fontan failure

\- Coronary artery anomalies:

\- Surgical intervention for listed anomalous coronary origins and
coronary fistula based on symptoms, ischemia, ventricular dysfunction,
or arrhythmias

\## Guideline 7345 -- Wireless Pulmonary Artery Pressure Device
Placement and Monitoring

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Patient must have NYHA class II or III heart failure, systolic or
diastolic, with any of the following:

\- Hospitalization for heart failure in the past year

\- Persistent or worsening symptoms when hemodynamics are uncertain

\- Elevated natriuretic peptides

\- Documentation must confirm optimal GDMT for heart failure, including
standard medication and, when indicated:

\- Coronary revascularization

\- Biventricular pacing

\## Guideline 7274 -- Coronary Intravascular Ultrasound (IVUS)

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Recommended for:

\- Assessing angiographically indeterminate left main lesion severity
before revascularization

\- Post-cardiac transplant evaluation at 4 to 6 weeks and 1 year to
exclude donor CAD and assess allograft vasculopathy

\- Evaluating stent failure, restenosis, or thrombosis

\- Assessing intermediate non-left-main coronary stenoses (50% to 70%)

\- Guiding coronary stent implantation, especially:

\- Left main stenting

\- Complex stenting

\- Adequate stent expansion/apposition in selected patients

\- Assessing plaque burden/characteristics in left main disease

\- Assessing severity and optimizing treatment of unprotected left main
lesions

\### Relevant Codes

\- CPT:

\- 92978

\- 92979

\- ICD-10:

\- None explicitly listed in the retrieved section

\### Required Documentation

\- Progress note prompting request

\- Prior diagnostic coronary angiogram

\- Standard supporting documentation as noted above

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Not indicated for:

\- Routine lesion assessment when PCI or CABG is not being contemplated

\- Extreme vessel tortuosity and angulation

\- Patients not suitable for systemic anticoagulation, angiography, or
cardiac catheterization

\## Guideline 7301-01 -- Implantable Cardioverter Defibrillator

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- General eligibility visible in excerpt:

\- Expected meaningful quality of life for at least 1 year

\- Ability to live with and manage a shock-delivering device

\- No completely reversible cause of ventricular arrhythmia

\- Completion of at least 3 months of GDMT for heart failure unless a
pacemaker indication intervenes

\- Visible criteria include:

\- Ischemic heart disease:

\- LVEF 35% or less with NYHA II-III despite GDMT and at least 40 days
post-MI

\- LVEF 30% or less with NYHA I, ischemic disease, GDMT, and at least 40
days post-MI

\- LVEF 40% or less with prior MI, NSVT, and inducible sustained VT/VF
at EP testing

\- Secondary prevention after documented VF, unstable VT, sustained VT,
or certain syncope scenarios

\- Nonischemic cardiomyopathy:

\- Lamin A/C mutation with at least 2 risk factors

\- LVEF 35% or less with NYHA II-III despite at least 3 months of GDMT

\- Secondary prevention for documented VF/VT or unexplained syncope
presumed due to ventricular arrhythmia

\- Advanced heart failure/transplant:

\- Non-hospitalized NYHA IV patients who are candidates for transplant
or LVAD

\- LVAD patient with sustained ventricular arrhythmias

\- Ambulatory NYHA IV with appropriate CRT indications

\- Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy:

\- Previously documented cardiac arrest or sustained VT

\- Adult HCM with at least 1 sudden-death risk factor listed in the
guideline excerpt

\- Additional disease categories are listed in the table of contents but
were not fully visible in the retrieved excerpts:

\- Cardiac sarcoidosis

\- Neuromuscular disorders

\- Arrhythmogenic right ventricular cardiomyopathy

\- Channelopathies

\- Adult and pediatric congenital heart disease

\- Special pacing modality situations

\## Guideline 7292 -- Infrainguinal Open Arterial Vascular Surgery

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Claudication: all of the following

\- Impairment of ADLs and/or work

\- No other condition likely to limit exercise despite improvement

\- On GDMT

\- Inadequate response to supervised or structured exercise program for
12 weeks

\- No untreated clinically significant proximal aortoiliac disease, or
it will be treated concurrently

\- Objective ischemia by ABI/TBI/exercise testing or equivalent abnormal
Doppler/PVR if vessels noncompressible

\- Anatomically suitable lesion with imaging showing:

\- At least 70% stenosis, or

\- Duplex criteria with absent flow or marked velocity elevation and
monophasic pattern

\- Duplex scan of ipsilateral great saphenous vein to evaluate bypass
conduit suitability

\- CLTI: all of the following

\- Gangrene or nonhealing wound \>2 weeks despite wound care, or
qualifying WIfI wound grade, with required ischemic testing thresholds

\- Or rest pain with required severe ischemic testing thresholds

\- Anatomically suitable lesion with imaging showing:

\- At least 50% stenosis, or

\- Duplex criteria with absent velocities or high PSV/ratio and
monophasic distal flow

\- Saphenous vein duplex for conduit assessment

\- If non-autogenous graft will be used, reason must be documented

\- Other indications or specific procedures:

\- Femoral-popliteal aneurysm with:

\- Size 20 mm or greater

\- Or \<20 mm with extensive thrombus

\- Or distal embolization

\- Or poor distal runoff

\- Common femoral endarterectomy for claudication or CLTI under same
policy framework

\- Distal cuff/patch to maintain graft patency

\- Patch treatment for vein bypass graft stenosis with concerning duplex
findings

\- To allow local podiatric/orthopedic intervention when circulation is
tenuous

\- Embolectomy or thrombectomy for acute ischemia

\## Guideline 7312-01 -- Myocardial Perfusion Imaging

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Special note:

\- Preference may be given to stress echocardiography when more
appropriate unless contraindicated

\- Visible indications include:

\- Symptomatic patients without known CAD and no imaging stress test
within 12 months

\- Less likely anginal symptoms when treadmill testing cannot be
performed or baseline ECG limits accuracy

\- Likely anginal symptoms in specified age/risk settings

\- Repeat testing for new/worsening symptoms after prior negative study
at least 1 year earlier

\- Asymptomatic patients without known CAD with previously unevaluated:

\- Ischemic ECG changes

\- Pathologic Q waves

\- Complete LBBB

\- Abnormal calcium scores:

\- Stable symptoms with Agatston \>100

\- Asymptomatic high-risk patient with Agatston \>100

\- Asymptomatic patient with Agatston \>400

\- Inconclusive CAD evaluation with ongoing concern:

\- Low-risk Duke treadmill score but increasing symptom concern

\- Intermediate Duke treadmill score

\- Intermediate CCTA lesion (40% to 70%) within 90 days

\- Nondiagnostic exercise stress test

\- Equivocal prior stress imaging

\- Coronary stenosis of unclear significance on angiography

\- Follow-up after PCI/CABG:

\- Asymptomatic high-risk follow-up at least 2 years later in select
cases

\- High occupational risk public safety jobs

\- New, recurrent, or worsening symptoms if results would alter
management

\- Follow-up of known CAD:

\- Prior significant CAD evaluation \>2 years ago without intervening
revascularization if results would alter management

\- Special diagnostic conditions:

\- Unevaluated ACS

\- New systolic or diastolic heart failure with suspected ischemia

\- Viability assessment for revascularization decision-making

\- Suboptimal/incomplete revascularization assessment

\- Ventricular arrhythmias

\- Evaluation before and during Class IC antiarrhythmic therapy in
risk-based scenarios

\- Coronary anomalies, myocardial bridging, or coronary aneurysm
evaluation

\- Post-radiation surveillance

\- Cardiac sarcoidosis with PET

\- Cardiac transthyretin amyloidosis diagnosis

\- Preoperative evaluation before certain noncardiac surgery or
transplant

\- Annual post-cardiac-transplant surveillance for first 5 years when
not undergoing invasive angiography

\## Guideline 7290 -- Treatment of Varicose Veins

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Requires documentation of symptomatic venous disorder with clinical
issues attributed to venous reflux

\- Qualifying reflux findings include:

\- Great saphenous or anterior accessory saphenous vein:

\- Axial reflux 500 ms or more

\- Diameter 5 mm or more

\- Small saphenous vein:

\- Axial reflux 500 ms or more

\- Diameter 3 mm or more

\- Posterior accessory vein:

\- Axial reflux \>500 ms

\- Diameter 5 mm or more

\- Other axial reflux absent or successfully treated more than 3 months
earlier

\- Persistent CEAP C2s or C4-C6

\- Perforator vein:

\- Reflux 500 ms or more

\- Diameter 3.5 mm or more

\- Plus qualifying ulcer/symptom circumstances

\- Clinical issues attributed to reflux include any of:

\- Leg or foot ulceration

\- Hemorrhage or recurrent bleeding from ruptured
varicosity/spider/telangiectasia/reticular vein

\- Superficial thrombophlebitis

\- Severe persistent pain and/or swelling interfering with daily life
and persisting despite 6 weeks of conservative treatment unless
contraindicated

\- Spider/reticular veins that have bled or pose substantial hemorrhage
risk

\- C6 with below-knee great saphenous reflux meeting threshold

\- Treatment plan for both legs may be provided if completed within 90
days, with specific sequencing rules

\- Prolonged immobility

\- Foam sclerotherapy with symptomatic right-to-left shunt

\- Anticipated future need for great or small saphenous vein as bypass
conduit

\## Guideline 7291 -- Enhanced External Counter Pulsation

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Initial treatment course:

\- 35 one-hour sessions

\- 5 days per week

\- Eligible patients:

\- Chronic coronary disease with refractory angina pectoris

\- Class III or IV angina by NYHA or CCS

\- On maximally tolerated GDMT

\- Not amenable to PCI or CABG because of:

\- Inoperative condition or high operative risk

\- Recurrent angina despite multiple revascularizations

\- Unsuitable coronary anatomy

\- Comorbidities creating excessive risk

\- Repeat courses:

\- Case-by-case

\- Must still meet medical necessity criteria

\- Prior EECP caused sustained improvement

\- Greater than 25% reduction in angina frequency

\- Improvement by at least 1 angina class

\- At least 3 months since prior treatment

\### Relevant Codes

\- HCPCS:

\- G0166

\- ICD-10:

\- I20.0

\- I20.1

\- I20.8

\- I20.9

\### Required Documentation

\- Progress note prompting request, including current medications

\- Records from prior EECP, if applicable

\- Most recent echocardiogram

\- Most recent stress test

\- Most recent cardiac catheterization report

\- Standard supporting documentation as noted above

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Contraindications:

\- Decompensated heart failure

\- Severe aortic regurgitation

\- Severe peripheral artery disease

\- MI within last 3 months

\- Recent surgery within 6 weeks

\- Recent cardiac catheterization or femoral arterial puncture

\- Unstable angina

\- Severe hypertension \>180/110

\- Heart rate \<35 or \>125

\- Arrhythmias interfering with EECP triggering

\- Severe venous disease including thrombophlebitis, DVT, or PE

\- Severe lower-extremity vaso-occlusive disease

\- Documented aortic aneurysm requiring surgical repair

\- Pregnancy

\- Out of scope:

\- Services performed as part of surveillance protocol for patients in
clinical trials

\## Guideline 7300 -- Hemodialysis Access Maintenance

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Diagnostic fistulogram indications include any of:

\- Physical exam changes:

\- Decreased/absent thrill or bruit

\- Increased pulsatility

\- Ipsilateral limb swelling

\- New collateral veins

\- Distal steal syndrome

\- Ischemic monomelic neuropathy

\- Aneurysm or pseudoaneurysm

\- High-flow signs such as high-output heart failure

\- Dialysis abnormalities:

\- Access thrombosis

\- Aspiration of clots

\- Persistent/new cannulation difficulty

\- Elevated venous pressures

\- Increased bleeding from puncture sites over 3 consecutive dialysis
sessions

\- Decreased flow with:

\- Inadequate dialysis

\- Low Kt/V without change in dialysis duration

\- Duplex ultrasound abnormalities:

\- Increased pulsatility

\- Decreased flow volume

\- Fistula size \<3 mm

\- Severe tortuosity

\- Aneurysm or pseudoaneurysm

\- Depth making cannulation difficult

\- Therapeutic intervention on AV access circuit:

\- Failure of fistula maturation after 4 to 6 weeks

\- Symptomatic/complicated aneurysm or pseudoaneurysm

\- AV access infection

\- High-flow complications

\- Erosion of overlying skin

\- Severe tortuosity or excessive depth impairing cannulation

\- Indicated fistulogram showing causative lesion such as:

\- Anastomotic stenosis

\- Proximal inflow stenosis

\- Venous outflow stenosis/obstruction

\- Intraluminal high-grade stenosis

\- Aberrant draining veins

\- Aneurysm intervention for:

\- Pain

\- Access dysfunction

\- Thrombus

\- Limited cannulation sites

\- High-output CHF

\- Unacceptable cosmetic deformity

\- Rapid enlargement

\- Covered intraluminal stents:

\- May be used for aneurysm/pseudoaneurysm

\- Reserved for surgery contraindication, lack of surgical option, or
temporary control of active bleeding

\### Relevant Codes

\- CPT:

\- 36901

\- 36902

\- 36903

\- 36904

\- 36905

\- 36906

\- 36907

\- 36908

\- 36909

\- 36831

\- 36832

\- 36833

\- 37607

\- ICD-10:

\- None explicitly listed in the retrieved section

\### Required Documentation

\- Standard supporting documentation as noted above

\- Clinical findings showing a new problem with access function

\- Documentation of shared decision-making reflecting:

\- Reason for procedure

\- Type of procedure options

\- Potential outcomes

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Diagnostic fistulogram should not be performed without new clinical
findings

\- Routine surveillance fistulogram is not appropriate

\- Preemptive endovascular intervention for stenosis without clinical
indicators is not appropriate

\- Aneurysm size alone is usually not enough for treatment without
symptoms or threatened skin

\## Guideline 7256 -- Aorto-Renal Endarterectomy or Bypass Surgery

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Request is for patients not suitable for percutaneous intervention

\- Visible indications in retrieved excerpt include:

\- Fibromuscular dysplastic renal artery stenosis with:

\- Complex disease extending into segmental arteries

\- Macro-aneurysms

\- Atherosclerotic renal artery stenosis with:

\- Multiple small renal arteries, or

\- Early primary branching of the main renal artery

\- Atherosclerotic renal artery stenosis combined with pararenal aortic
reconstruction for:

\- Aortic aneurysm treatment, or

\- Severe aortoiliac occlusive disease

\### Relevant Codes

\- CPT:

\- 35560

\- ICD-10:

\- None explicitly listed in the retrieved section

\### Required Documentation

\- Cardiologist, nephrologist, or vascular surgeon note prompting
request

\- One or more of the following reports:

\- Renal artery duplex

\- Retroperitoneal duplex

\- MRA renal

\- CTA renal

\- Renal angiogram

\- Standard supporting documentation as noted above

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Potential exclusions:

\- Advanced disease with creatinine \>3 to 4 mg/dL

\- Kidney length \<8 cm

\- Limited life expectancy

\- Bleeding diathesis

\- Recent MI

\- Pregnancy

\- Clinical-trial surveillance requests considered out of scope

\## Service: Fractional Flow Reserve CT

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Intermediate stenosis of 40% to 90% on coronary CT angiography to
guide decision-making and identify patients who may benefit from
revascularization.

\- May also be supported when:

\- Lesions are in the same 40% to 90% range, and

\- Coronary calcification makes percentage stenosis interpretation
difficult.

\### Relevant Codes

\- ICD-10: None listed

\- CPT:

\- 75580

\- HCPCS: None listed

\### Required Documentation

\- Common submission requirements above

\- Coronary CT angiography showing intermediate stenosis (40% to 90%)

\- Documentation that the study is being used to guide revascularization
decision-making

\- If applicable, documentation that coronary calcification limits
stenosis interpretation

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Problematic artifacts or body habitus that may interfere with exam
quality

\- Heavy calcium

\- BMI \> 35 may make suitability vendor-dependent

\- Known ischemic coronary disease not revascularized with no change in
status or CCTA images

\- Myocardial infarction within 30 days

\- Prior CABG

\- Complex congenital heart disease

\- Ventricular septal defect with pulmonary-to-systemic flow ratio \>
1.4

\- Metallic coronary stents 3.0 mm or smaller

\- Coronary lesions with vessel diameter \< 1.8 mm

\- Severe wall motion abnormality on CCTA

\- Severe myocardial hypertrophy

\- High-risk indicators on stress testing

\- Coronary angiography within the past 90 days

\- Marginal image quality due to motion, blooming, misalignment,
arrhythmia, or similar issues

\## Service: Cardiac Resynchronization Therapy

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Applies to patients who:

\- Have been on guideline-directed medical therapy for 3 months, or

\- Have been on medical therapy and are 40 days after myocardial
infarction, or

\- Need implantation for special pacing/defibrillation indications

\#### Cardiomyopathy

\- Class I through IV:

\- Ischemic cardiomyopathy

\- LVEF ≤ 30%

\- QRS ≥ 150 ms

\- LBBB

\- Sinus rhythm

\- Class II through IV:

\- Ischemic or non-ischemic cardiomyopathy

\- LVEF ≤ 35%

\- QRS ≥ 120 ms

\- LBBB

\- Sinus rhythm

\- Also includes:

\- Nonobstructive hypertrophic cardiomyopathy

\- LVEF \< 50%

\- LBBB

\- Therapy used for symptom reduction

\- Class III through IV:

\- Ischemic or non-ischemic cardiomyopathy

\- LVEF ≤ 35%

\- QRS ≥ 150 ms

\- Non-LBBB

\- Sinus rhythm

\#### Special Situations Regardless of NYHA Class

\- Indication for ventricular pacing with:

\- High-degree AV block, or

\- Expected ventricular pacing \> 40%

\- Atrial fibrillation with:

\- LVEF ≤ 35%, and

\- Need for ventricular pacing or otherwise meeting criteria, and

\- AV nodal ablation or rate control allowing nearly 100% pacing

\- Atrial fibrillation with:

\- LVEF ≤ 50%, and

\- Failed rhythm-control strategy, and

\- Persistently rapid ventricular rates despite medical therapy, making
AV nodal ablation with CRT reasonable

\- HFrEF with transthyretin amyloid cardiomyopathy may follow class
II-IV criteria

\#### Adult Congenital Heart Disease

\- Class I through IV:

\- Systemic ventricle with any EF

\- Intrinsic narrow QRS

\- New device placement or replacement

\- Anticipated significant ventricular pacing (\> 40%)

\- Class II through IV:

\- Systemic LV EF ≤ 35%

\- Sinus rhythm

\- Wide QRS ≥ 130 ms

\- Also:

\- Any congenital heart disease

\- Complete RBBB with QRS ≥ 150 ms

\- Severe subpulmonary RV dysfunction and dilation despite interventions
to reduce RV volume overload

\- Class IV:

\- Severe ventricular dysfunction

\- Otherwise candidate for heart transplant or mechanical circulatory
support

\#### Additional Indication

\- Appropriate pacing modality in special situations with \< 3 months of
medical therapy when:

\- Criteria are met for a non-elective ICD or pacemaker, and

\- Recovery of symptoms/LVEF is unlikely despite waiting period,
avoiding a second implant procedure

\### Relevant Codes

\- ICD-10: None listed

\- CPT:

\- 33221

\- 33224

\- 33225

\- 33231

\- HCPCS: None listed

\### Required Documentation

\- Common submission requirements above

\- LVEF

\- QRS duration and morphology

\- Sinus rhythm vs atrial fibrillation

\- NYHA class

\- Cardiomyopathy type/etiology

\- Duration of guideline-directed medical therapy

\- MI and revascularization timing

\- Anticipated pacing burden

\- Evidence that reversible causes have been excluded

\- Expected survival and functional status \> 1 year

\- If relevant, documentation of ACE-I/ARB/ARNI and beta-blocker therapy

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Not indicated for:

\- NYHA class I with non-LBBB and QRS \< 150 ms, except special pacing
situations

\- Comorbidities/frailty limiting survival with good functional capacity
to \< 1 year

\- Active bloodstream infection

\- Reversible cardiomyopathy causes until corrected and reassessed

\- Cardiogenic shock or symptomatic hypotension in stable baseline
rhythm

\- Adult congenital disease:

\- Not indicated when comorbidities/frailty limit survival with good
functional status to \< 1 year

\## Service: Implantable Cardioverter Defibrillator

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\#### Ischemic Heart Disease

\##### Primary Prevention

\- LVEF ≤ 35% due to ischemic or non-ischemic heart disease with:

\- NYHA class II or III

\- Despite guideline-directed medical therapy

\- At least 40 days after MI

\- LVEF ≤ 30% due to ischemic heart disease with:

\- NYHA class I

\- On medical therapy

\- At least 40 days after MI

\- LVEF ≤ 40% with:

\- Prior MI

\- Nonsustained VT

\- Inducible sustained VT or VF on electrophysiology testing

\##### Secondary Prevention

\- Documented VF, hemodynamically unstable VT, or sustained VT after
reversible causes are excluded

\- Syncope of undetermined origin with inducible VF or sustained VT on
electrophysiology study

\- Syncope of undetermined origin with EF ≤ 35%

\#### Nonischemic Cardiomyopathy

\##### Primary Prevention

\- Lamin A/C mutation with at least 2 of:

\- NSVT

\- LVEF \< 45%

\- Male sex

\- Missense mutation

\- LVEF ≤ 35% with NYHA class II or III despite at least 3 months of
medical therapy

\- LVEF ≤ 35% with NYHA class I may be considered after at least 3
months of medical therapy

\##### Secondary Prevention

\- Documented VF, hemodynamically unstable VT, or sustained VT after
reversible causes are excluded

\- LVEF ≤ 50% with unexplained syncope presumed due to ventricular
arrhythmia when primary-prevention criteria are not otherwise met

\#### Advanced Heart Failure / Transplantation

\- Non-hospitalized NYHA class IV patient who is a candidate for
transplant or LVAD

\- LVAD patient with sustained ventricular arrhythmias

\- Ambulatory NYHA class IV patient with otherwise appropriate CRT
indications

\#### Myocardial Diseases

\##### Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy

\- Prior cardiac arrest or sustained VT

\- Adult with at least 1 risk factor:

\- First-degree relative with sudden death attributable to HCM at age ≤
50

\- LVH ≥ 30 mm

\- Recent likely arrhythmic syncope

\- LV apical aneurysm

\- EF \< 50%

\- Pediatric patient with at least 1 risk factor:

\- Unexplained syncope

\- LVH ≥ 30 mm

\- Nonsustained VT

\- Family history of HCM-related sudden cardiac death

\##### Cardiac Sarcoidosis

\- Cardiac arrest or documented sustained VT

\- LVEF ≤ 35%

\- LVEF \> 35% with inducible sustained ventricular arrhythmia at
electrophysiology testing

\- Syncope and/or scar on CMR or PET

\- Requirement for permanent pacemaker

\##### Neuromuscular Disorders

\- Same primary and secondary prevention indications as nonischemic
cardiomyopathy

\- Includes progressive cardiac involvement in certain muscular
dystrophies

\##### Arrhythmogenic Right Ventricular Cardiomyopathy

\- Resuscitated sudden cardiac arrest

\- Sustained VT

\- RV or LV systolic dysfunction with EF ≤ 35%

\- Syncope with documented or presumed ventricular arrhythmia

\#### Channelopathies

\##### Congenital Long QT Syndrome

\- Sudden cardiac arrest

\- Sustained VT or recurrent syncope when beta blocker ineffective or
not tolerated

\- QTc \> 500 ms on beta blocker

\- Strong family history of sudden cardiac death

\- High-risk genotype

\##### Brugada Syndrome / Spontaneous Type 1 Pattern

\- Cardiac arrest

\- Documented sustained ventricular arrhythmia

\- Syncope presumed due to ventricular arrhythmia

\##### Catecholaminergic Polymorphic VT

\- Sudden cardiac arrest

\- Syncope or sustained VT

\##### Early Repolarization / Short QT Syndrome

\- Cardiac arrest

\- Sustained ventricular arrhythmia

\##### Idiopathic Polymorphic VT/VF

\- Cardiac arrest due to polymorphic VT or VF

\#### Adult and Pediatric Congenital Heart Disease

\- Cardiac arrest due to VF or VT, or unstable VT, after exclusion of
reversible etiology

\- Systemic LVEF ≤ 35%, biventricular physiology, and NYHA class II or
III on medical therapy

\- Tetralogy of Fallot with:

\- Spontaneous sustained VT, or

\- Inducible VF or sustained VT, or

\- At least 1 risk factor:

\- Prior palliative systemic-to-pulmonary shunt

\- Unexplained syncope

\- Frequent PVCs

\- Atrial tachycardia

\- LV dysfunction or diastolic dysfunction

\- NSVT

\- QRS ≥ 180 ms

\- Dilated RV

\- Residual pulmonary regurgitation or stenosis

\- RV hypertension

\- Single or systemic RVEF \< 35% plus additional risk factor such as:

\- NSVT

\- Unexplained syncope

\- NYHA class II or III despite medical therapy

\- QRS ≥ 140 ms

\- Severe systemic AV valve regurgitation

\- Syncope of unknown origin with:

\- At least moderate ventricular dysfunction, or

\- Marked hypertrophy, or

\- Inducible sustained VT/VF

\- Syncope with moderate or severe complexity congenital disease and
high clinical suspicion of ventricular arrhythmia

\- Non-hospitalized congenital heart disease patient awaiting transplant

\- LV non-compaction meeting nonischemic cardiomyopathy indications,
including family history of sudden cardiac death

\#### Special Situations

\- ICD criteria otherwise met and elevated troponin is not due to MI

\- ICD criteria otherwise met, but:

\- MI occurred within 40 days, or

\- Revascularization occurred within 3 months,

\- and a non-elective permanent pacemaker is required,

\- and recovery of LVEF \> 35% is uncertain or not expected

\- ICD criteria otherwise met, but cardiomyopathy has not yet had full
3-month response to medical therapy and urgent pacemaker is needed

\- Patient met primary-prevention criteria before revascularization and
LVEF recovery \> 35% is unlikely despite 90-day wait

\### Relevant Codes

\- ICD-10: None listed

\- CPT:

\- 33230

\- 33240

\- 33249

\- HCPCS: None listed

\### Required Documentation

\- Common submission requirements above

\- EF/LVEF

\- NYHA class

\- Guideline-directed medical therapy duration and agents

\- MI/revascularization timing

\- Arrhythmia history

\- Electrophysiology study results

\- Syncope workup

\- Evidence reversible causes were excluded

\- Imaging evidence such as scar on CMR/PET when relevant

\- Family history/genetic results when relevant

\- Congenital anatomy and risk features when relevant

\- Anticipated pacing burden if CRT may be more appropriate

\- Meaningful life expectancy \> 1 year

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- All indications are predicated on meaningful life expectancy \> 1
year

\- Some patients may need CRT rather than standard ICD if significant
ventricular pacing is expected

\- Special early-implant scenarios are limited to situations where delay
is unlikely to improve LVEF enough to avoid device need

\## Service: Pacemaker

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\#### Adult Indications

\- Excludes conditions expected to resolve

\##### Sinus Node Dysfunction

\- Documented symptomatic sinus bradycardia, including frequent sinus
pauses

\- Symptomatic chronotropic incompetence documented by stress test or
rhythm monitoring

\- Symptomatic sinus bradycardia due to required medical therapy with no
alternative

\- Awake heart rate \< 40 even without definite symptom correlation

\- Tachycardia-bradycardia syndrome with symptoms attributable to
bradycardia

\- Unexplained syncope with clinically significant sinus node
dysfunction documented or provoked at electrophysiology study

\##### Acquired AV Block

\###### First-Degree

\- Marked first-degree block with symptoms clearly attributable to AV
block

\- First-degree block with pacemaker syndrome symptoms or hemodynamic
compromise

\###### Second-Degree

\- Marked Mobitz I with symptoms clearly attributable to AV block

\- Second-degree block with pacemaker syndrome symptoms or hemodynamic
compromise

\- Mobitz II regardless of symptoms

\- Advanced second-degree AV block

\- Second-degree block with wide QRS or EPS-documented intra-/infra-His
conduction

\- Symptomatic bradycardia associated with second-degree AV block

\###### Third-Degree / High-Grade

\- Third-degree AV block, intermittent or persistent, regardless of
symptoms

\- High-grade AV block regardless of symptoms

\##### Atrial Fibrillation / Other

\- Atrial fibrillation while awake with pauses ≥ 5 seconds or
symptomatic bradycardia

\- Sinus rhythm with AV block while awake and:

\- Pauses ≥ 3 seconds, or

\- Heart rate \< 40 bpm, or

\- Escape rhythm below AV node

\- Following catheter ablation of the AV junction

\- Symptomatic AV block resulting from required medical therapy with no
alternative

\- Exercise-induced second- or third-degree AV block without myocardial
ischemia

\##### Neuromuscular Disorders

\- Marked first-degree or higher AV block, or H-V interval ≥ 70 ms,
associated with neuromuscular disease regardless of symptoms

\##### Chronic Fascicular Block

\- Alternating bundle-branch block

\- Unexplained syncope after excluding other likely causes, especially
VT

\- Syncope with bundle-branch block and:

\- HV interval ≥ 70 ms, or

\- Evidence of infranodal block at EPS

\- Incidental EPS finding of:

\- H-V interval ≥ 100 ms, or

\- Non-physiologic pacing-induced infra-His block in an asymptomatic
patient

\##### Hypersensitive Carotid Sinus Syndrome / Neurocardiogenic Syncope

\- Recurrent syncope due to spontaneous carotid sinus stimulation and:

\- Ventricular asystole ≥ 3 seconds, or

\- AV block, or

\- ≥ 50 mmHg systolic BP drop

\- Syncope without clear provocative events and hypersensitive
cardioinhibitory response with asystole ≥ 3 seconds

\- Recurrent syncope and documented asystole:

\- ≥ 3 seconds with syncope, or

\- ≥ 6 seconds without symptoms or with presyncope

\##### Pacing to Terminate or Prevent Tachycardia

\- Symptomatic recurrent SVT documented to terminate with pacing after
failed ablation and/or drug therapy

\- Prevention of pause-dependent VT

\##### Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy

\- May be considered in medically refractory symptomatic patients with
significant resting or provoked LV outflow tract obstruction

\##### Leadless Pacemaker

\- Bradycardia requiring only single-chamber RV pacing in VVI/VVIR mode:

\- Symptomatic high-grade AV block with AF

\- Symptomatic high-grade AV block without AF when atrial lead placement
is difficult, high-risk, or unnecessary

\- Symptomatic bradycardia-tachycardia syndrome or sinus node
dysfunction when atrial lead placement is difficult, high-risk, or
unnecessary

\- Need for rate-responsive pacing

\#### Congenital Heart Disease Pacing

\##### Sinus Node Dysfunction

\- Symptomatic age- and activity-inappropriate bradycardia

\- Complex congenital disease with resting HR \< 40 bpm or pauses \> 3
seconds

\- Impaired hemodynamics due to sinus bradycardia or loss of AV
synchrony

\- Asymptomatic sinus bradycardia after congenital repair with resting
HR \< 40 bpm or pauses \> 3 seconds

\- Congenital disease with sinus/junctional bradycardia to prevent
recurrent intra-atrial reentrant tachycardia

\##### AV Block

\- Second- or third-degree AV block with symptomatic bradycardia,
ventricular dysfunction, or low cardiac output

\- Congenital third-degree AV block with:

\- Wide QRS escape rhythm, or

\- Complex ventricular ectopy, or

\- Ventricular dysfunction

\- Infant congenital third-degree AV block with ventricular rate \< 55
bpm, or \< 70 bpm if congenital heart disease present

\- After age 1 year, congenital third-degree AV block with:

\- Average HR \< 50 bpm, or

\- Abrupt pauses 2 to 3 times basic cycle length, or

\- Symptoms due to chronotropic incompetence

\- Adults with congenital complete AV block with:

\- Symptomatic bradycardia, or

\- Wide QRS escape rhythm, or

\- Mean daytime HR \< 50 bpm, or

\- Complex ventricular ectopy, or

\- Ventricular dysfunction

\- Adults with congenital complete AV block regardless of symptoms

\- Unexplained syncope after congenital heart surgery complicated by
transient complete heart block with residual fascicular block

\- Asymptomatic children/adolescents with congenital third-degree AV
block and acceptable rate, narrow QRS, and normal ventricular function

\### Relevant Codes

\- ICD-10: None listed

\- CPT:

\- 33206

\- 33207

\- 33208

\- 33212

\- 33213

\- HCPCS: None listed

\### Required Documentation

\- Common submission requirements above

\- ECG/Holter/MCOT/stress test/EPS evidence of rhythm disorder

\- Heart rate/pause duration

\- Symptom correlation

\- Medication necessity if bradycardia is treatment-induced

\- AV/HV conduction data when relevant

\- History of syncope/presyncope

\- Congenital heart disease history and prior surgical history when
relevant

\- Failed ablation/drug therapy for tachycardia indications

\- For leadless device use:

\- Need for single-chamber RV pacing

\- Why atrial lead placement is difficult, high-risk, or unnecessary

\- Confirmation condition is not expected to resolve

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Not indicated for:

\- Asymptomatic sinus node dysfunction, or symptoms without documented
bradycardia

\- Asymptomatic first-degree AV block

\- Asymptomatic Mobitz I second-degree AV block with narrow QRS

\- Asymptomatic fascicular block

\- Asymptomatic bifascicular block with or without first-degree AV block
when higher-grade block has not been demonstrated

\- Hypersensitive cardioinhibitory carotid sinus response without
symptoms or only vague symptoms

\- Asymptomatic bifascicular block after congenital heart surgery
without prior transient complete AV block

\## Service: Transthoracic Echocardiography

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\#### Adult Indications

\##### Cardiac Structure and Function

\- Initial evaluation when history, physical exam, ECG, rhythm monitor,
or other testing suggests cardiac etiology for:

\- Chest pain when another study is not planned

\- Shortness of breath

\- Palpitations

\- Hypotension suggestive of cardiac etiology not due to:

\- Medication

\- Dehydration

\- Infection

\- ECG abnormalities:

\- Pathologic Q waves

\- New LBBB

\- Previously unevaluated LVH

\##### Murmur / Click

\- Initial evaluation when there is reasonable suspicion of structural
or valvular disease, including:

\- High-grade murmur

\- Holosystolic murmur

\- Continuous murmur

\- Diastolic murmur

\##### Arrhythmias

\- Frequent PVCs

\- Sustained or nonsustained VT/VF or ventricular bigeminy

\- New atrial fibrillation not previously evaluated by echo

\- Initial evaluation of SVT on ECG/monitoring without other evidence of
heart disease

\##### Syncope

\- History/physical/ECG suggesting cardiac cause, including:

\- Structural heart disease

\- Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy

\- Systolic heart failure

\- Exercise-induced syncope

\- Not for clearly noncardiac causes such as vasovagal or
orthostatic/dehydration-related syncope

\##### Perioperative

\- Preoperative LV function assessment for solid-organ transplant
candidates

\- Can be done yearly prior to transplant

\##### Pulmonary Hypertension

\- Suspected pulmonary hypertension with RV function / pulmonary
pressure assessment

\- Re-evaluation of known pulmonary hypertension with:

\- New chest pain

\- Worsening shortness of breath

\- Syncope

\- Increased murmur

\- Worsening rales

\- Pulmonary embolism:

\- Initial risk stratification

\- Repeat at 3 to 6 months for persistent dyspnea to evaluate for
chronic thromboembolic pulmonary hypertension

\- Annual screening in:

\- Scleroderma

\- Portal hypertension

\- BMPR2 mutation carriers

\- Sickle cell disease

\##### Valvular Heart Disease

\- Symptomatic known valvular disease with new or worsening symptoms

\- Native stenosis surveillance:

\- Mild/bicuspid aortic valve: every 3 years or more

\- Moderate stenosis: at least yearly

\- Severe aortic stenosis: every 6 to 12 months

\- Repeat after BP control in low-flow/low-gradient severe AS

\- Native regurgitation surveillance:

\- Mild: every 3 years or more

\- Moderate: at least yearly

\- Severe: every 6 to 12 months

\##### Prosthetic Valves / Native Valve Repair

\- Baseline study about 6 weeks to 3 months post-op

\- Routine surveillance:

\- Surgical bioprosthetic valve: every 3 years

\- Surgical mechanical valve: at 10 years, then annually

\- Surgical mitral valve repair: at 1 year, then every 2 to 3 years

\- Suspected dysfunction with:

\- Chest pain

\- Shortness of breath

\- New/increased murmur

\- Rales

\- Elevated JVP

\##### Transcatheter Interventions

\- TAVR:

\- Pre-procedure

\- Post-procedure at 30 days and annually

\- Reassessment for suspected dysfunction or stroke

\- Percutaneous mitral valve repair:

\- Pre-procedure

\- Reassessment at 1 month, 6 months, and annually

\- Reassessment if suspected dysfunction

\- PFO/ASD closure:

\- Pre-procedure

\- Routine post-procedure device follow-up

\- Concern for infection, malposition, embolization, persistent shunt

\- LAA occlusion:

\- Pre-procedure

\##### Pericardial Disease

\- Suspected pericarditis or pericardial effusion

\- Re-evaluation of significant known effusion if management may change

\- Suspected pericardial constriction or re-evaluation when management
may change

\##### Embolic Source / Cardiac Mass

\- Recent TIA, stroke, or peripheral emboli

\- Intracardiac mass evaluation or re-evaluation

\##### Infective Endocarditis

\- Initial evaluation with positive blood cultures or new murmur

\- Re-evaluation with:

\- Changing murmur

\- Embolic phenomena

\- New chest pain, shortness of breath, or syncope

\- Ongoing fever/positive cultures/new AV block requiring treatment
change

\- High-risk progression/complication

\- At completion of antimicrobial therapy and follow-up at 1, 3, 6, and
12 months

\##### Thoracic Aortic Disease

\- Screening first-degree relatives of patients with:

\- Thoracic aortic aneurysm/dissection

\- Bicuspid aortic valve

\- Aortopathy syndromes

\- Imaging second-degree relatives when first-degree relatives of an
affected patient are also found to have disease

\- Follow-up:

\- 6 months after initial dilated thoracic aorta finding

\- Annually for enlarged thoracic aorta

\- Twice yearly for aortic root ≥ 4.5 cm or rapid growth

\- Connective tissue/aortopathy syndrome:

\- At diagnosis

\- At 6 months

\- Then annually, or twice yearly if larger/expanding

\- Turner syndrome:

\- Baseline at diagnosis

\- Every 5 years in children if initial imaging normal

\- Every 10 years in adults if initial imaging normal

\- Prior to planned pregnancy

\- Annually if abnormality found

\- Re-evaluation for new symptoms/signs:

\- Chest pain

\- Shortness of breath

\- Syncope

\- TIA/CVA

\- New/increased aortic murmur

\- Rales or increased JVP

\- Acute dissection:

\- 1 month, 6 months, 12 months, then annually

\- Chronic dissection:

\- Annually

\- Sinus of Valsalva aneurysm evaluation

\##### Hypertension

\- Adult:

\- Suspected hypertensive heart disease

\- Uncontrolled/resistant hypertension on 3 or more drugs

\- Pediatric:

\- Initial evaluation when pharmacologic treatment is being considered

\- Re-evaluation every 6 to 12 months for persistent HTN, concentric
LVH, or reduced LVEF

\- Annual re-evaluation without prior LVH for stage 2, secondary, or
incompletely treated chronic stage 1 hypertension

\##### Heart Failure

\- Initial evaluation of suspected systolic or diastolic heart failure
based on symptoms/signs/abnormal testing, including:

\- Dyspnea

\- Orthopnea

\- Paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea

\- Worsening edema

\- Elevated BNP

\- Re-evaluation of known heart failure:

\- Change in clinical status/cardiac exam

\- Asymptomatic patient with medication change

\##### Cardiomyopathy

\- Initial evaluation of suspected inherited or acquired cardiomyopathy:

\- Restrictive

\- Infiltrative/depositional

\- Dilated

\- Hypertrophic

\- Re-evaluation for medication changes or new symptoms:

\- Chest pain

\- Shortness of breath

\- Palpitations

\- Syncope

\- Recovered EF follow-up:

\- Every 6 months until 12 to 18 months after recovery

\- Then annually for 2 years

\- Then every 3 to 5 years

\- Screening first-degree relatives of inherited cardiomyopathy

\- Suspected cardiac sarcoidosis

\- Suspected cardiac amyloid and monitoring:

\- AL amyloid: every 3 to 6 months

\- ATTR amyloid: every 6 to 12 months

\##### Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy

\- Initial evaluation

\- Re-evaluation with clinical change/new event

\- After myectomy or alcohol septal ablation

\- Stable disease: every 1 to 2 years

\- After septal reduction: within 3 to 6 months

\- Screening:

\- Children/adolescents: every 1 to 2 years or every 2 to 3 years
depending on family/genotype pattern

\- Adults: every 3 to 5 years

\- Therapy guidance:

\- Baseline before specific disease-directed therapy

\- Repeat during treatment at specialist discretion

\##### Cardiotoxic Exposure / Radiation

\- Cardiotoxic therapy:

\- Baseline before therapy

\- Monitoring during therapy, generally not more often than every 6
weeks without new findings

\- Long-term surveillance generally every 6 to 12 months

\- Prior radiation with cardiac exposure:

\- Long-term surveillance at 5 years and 10 years

\- More often if clinically indicated

\##### Device / VAD / Transplant / Pregnancy / Adult Congenital /
Inflammatory / COVID / Neuromuscular

\- Device candidacy or optimization:

\- ICD planning after revascularization/MI/3 months therapy

\- Initial CRT optimization

\- Re-evaluation of worsening HF after CRT

\- Symptoms suggesting pacing-device complication or suboptimal settings

\- VAD:

\- Determine candidacy

\- Optimize settings

\- Re-evaluate for complications

\- Cardiac transplant:

\- Monitoring for rejection at transplant-center discretion

\- Pregnancy:

\- Valvular disease surveillance by severity

\- Pre-pregnancy evaluation with prosthetic valves

\- Peripartum cardiomyopathy serial follow-up

\- Aortopathy monitoring before/during/after pregnancy

\- Adult congenital heart disease:

\- Initial evaluation when suggested by history/exam/ECG/other imaging

\- Screening first-degree relatives of bicuspid aortic valve

\- Change in clinical status or prior to procedure

\- Inflammatory/autoimmune:

\- Rheumatic fever

\- Lupus

\- Takayasu arteritis

\- MIS-C

\- Kawasaki disease with specified serial intervals

\- COVID-related:

\- Acute or post-acute cardiopulmonary symptoms

\- Post-vaccination myocarditis symptoms/signs

\- Neuromuscular surveillance with diagnosis-specific intervals

\#### Pediatric Initial Indications

\- Hypertension

\- Renal failure

\- Palpitations with:

\- Family history of sudden death/arrest or pacing/defibrillator, or

\- Personal/family history of cardiomyopathy

\- Chest pain with:

\- Exertional symptoms, or

\- Abnormal ECG, or

\- Family history of unexplained sudden death/cardiomyopathy

\- Syncope with:

\- Abnormal ECG

\- Exertional syncope

\- Family history of sudden death/device/cardiomyopathy

\- Signs/symptoms of heart failure:

\- Respiratory distress

\- Poor peripheral pulses

\- Feeding difficulty

\- Decreased urine output

\- Edema

\- Hepatomegaly

\- Abnormal physical findings:

\- Clicks/snaps/gallops

\- Fixed/abnormal split S2

\- Decreased pulses

\- Central cyanosis

\- Arrhythmias:

\- SVT

\- VT

\- Murmur:

\- Pathologic/harsh/diastolic/holosystolic/continuous/late
systolic/grade 3/6 or louder

\- Presumed innocent murmur if accompanied by concerning
signs/symptoms/findings

\- Abnormal basic data:

\- ECG

\- Cardiac biomarkers

\- Pulse oximetry desaturation

\- Chest x-ray

\- Sickle cell screening for pulmonary hypertension risk in children age
8+

\- Structural suspicion:

\- Prematurity with suspected PDA

\- Vascular ring concern

\- Genetic/syndromic/fetal-maternal factors:

\- Cardiomyopathy genotype

\- Known syndrome associated with heart disease

\- Visceral/cardiac situs abnormality

\- Connective tissue disorder

\- First-degree relative with relevant genetic abnormality

\- Maternal infection

\- Maternal phenylketonuria

\- Suspected abnormal fetal echo

\#### Congenital Follow-Up

\- Evaluation before procedure and within 30 days after
surgical/catheter-based repair

\- All lesions: re-evaluate for new signs/symptoms or change in status

\- Infant unrepaired valvular AS/AR may need echo every 1 to 4 weeks

\- Infant unrepaired MS may need echo every 1 to 4 weeks

\- After repair, 3 to 12 month evaluation for heart failure symptoms

\- Annual surveillance for incomplete or palliative repair

\- In some years, TTE may be unnecessary when cardiac MRI is performed
unless clinical indication exists

\- Extensive lesion-specific unrepaired and post-procedural interval
tables are included for:

\- AS/AR

\- ASD

\- VSD

\- PDA

\- Coarctation

\- Tetralogy of Fallot

\- Truncus arteriosus

\- d-TGA

\- ccTGA

\- TAPVC

\- DORV

\- Tricuspid valve interventions

\- Anomalous coronary arteries

\- Subvalvular and supravalvular AS

\## Service: Transesophageal Echocardiography

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\#### General

\- After nondiagnostic transthoracic echocardiogram due to inadequate
visualization

\- When there is high likelihood that transthoracic echocardiography
will be nondiagnostic

\#### Aortic Pathology

\- Suspected acute aortic pathology such as dissection

\- Dilated aortic sinuses or ascending aorta on transthoracic echo

\- Bicuspid aortic valve assessment when morphology cannot be assessed
by transthoracic echo and CT/MRI have not been done

\#### Valvular Disease

\- Discordance between clinical assessment and transthoracic assessment
of mitral regurgitation severity

\- Mitral stenosis when clinical findings and transthoracic study
disagree and transthoracic imaging is inadequate

\- Discordance between clinical assessment and transthoracic assessment
of aortic regurgitation severity

\- Native or prosthetic valve evaluation when symptoms/signs suggest
dysfunction and transthoracic study is inadequate

\- Re-evaluation of known prosthetic valve dysfunction when it will
change management and transthoracic imaging is inadequate

\#### Infective Endocarditis

\- Suspected native-valve, prosthetic-valve, or lead-related infection
with positive blood cultures or new murmur

\- Moderate/high pretest probability of endocarditis when transthoracic
echo is negative

\- Re-evaluation with change in clinical status or cardiac exam

\- Re-evaluation when elevated risk of progression/complication or
findings would alter therapy and transthoracic imaging is inadequate

\#### Cardiac Mass / Source of Emboli

\- Initial evaluation to exclude cardiac source of TIA or ischemic
stroke

\- Evaluation of suspected cardiac mass, tumor, or thrombus when other
cardiac imaging is inconclusive

\- Re-evaluation of prior finding when interval change would alter
therapy

\#### Atrial Fibrillation / Flutter

\- Evaluation for decisions regarding:

\- Anticoagulation

\- Cardioversion

\- Radiofrequency ablation

\#### TAVR

\- Pre-procedure annular/cusp/calcification assessment when CT or CMR
cannot be done

\- Post-procedure evaluation of aortic regurgitation or suspected valve
dysfunction when transthoracic imaging is inadequate

\#### PFO / ASD

\- Anatomy evaluation

\- Cardiac embolic-source evaluation

\- Suitability for percutaneous closure

\- Post-closure concern for infection, malposition, embolization, or
persistent shunt

\#### Left Atrial Appendage Occlusion

\- Anatomy and embolic-source evaluation

\- Suitability for occlusion device placement

\- Surveillance at 45 days and 1 year

\- Reassessment at 6 months if incomplete closure at 45-day study

\#### Percutaneous Mitral Valve Repair

\- Determine eligibility

\- Procedural evaluation

\- Exclude intracardiac mass, thrombus, or vegetation within 3 days
before procedure

\#### Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy

\- When transthoracic echo is inconclusive for:

\- Myectomy planning

\- Excluding subaortic membrane

\- Assessing mitral regurgitation

\- Assessing need for septal ablation

\#### Adult Congenital Heart Disease

\- Provocative maneuver imaging for right-to-left shunt

\- Prior to repair of:

\- Isolated secundum ASD

\- Sinus venosus defect/partial anomalous pulmonary venous connection

\- Congenital mitral stenosis/regurgitation

\- Subvalvular aortic stenosis

\- Transposition of the great arteries

\- Postoperative/post-catheter repair with change in status when
TTE/CMR/CT are inadequate

\#### Ventricular Assist Devices

\- Preoperative suitability evaluation

\- Re-evaluation for device complication or suspected infection

\## Service: Stress Echocardiography

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\#### Suspected Coronary Artery Disease

\- Symptomatic patients without known CAD and no imaging stress test in
the last 12 months

\- Less-likely anginal symptoms when baseline ECG makes standard
exercise testing inaccurate

\- Likely anginal symptoms:

\- Age \< 50 with ≤ 1 risk factor if exercise ECG cannot be done

\- Age ≥ 50

\- Repeat testing if:

\- Negative test was at least 1 year ago, and

\- Symptoms are new or worsening, and

\- Current criteria are otherwise met

\- Asymptomatic patients without known CAD with:

\- Previously unevaluated ischemic ST/T abnormalities

\- Pathologic Q waves

\- Complete LBBB

\#### Abnormal Coronary Calcium Scores

\- Stable symptoms and calcium score \> 100 without prior stress imaging
in last 12 months

\- Asymptomatic high global CAD risk with calcium score \> 100 without
prior stress imaging in last 12 months

\- Asymptomatic patient with calcium score \> 400 without prior stress
imaging in last 12 months

\#### Inconclusive CAD Evaluation / Persistent Concern

\- Low-risk Duke treadmill score but symptoms suggest increasing
likelihood of disease

\- Intermediate Duke treadmill score

\- Previously unevaluated ventricular wall motion abnormality on another
study when testing is being done for ischemia

\- Intermediate CCTA lesion:

\- 40% to 70%

\- Coronary stenosis of unclear significance on prior coronary
angiography not previously evaluated

\#### Follow-Up After Revascularization

\- Asymptomatic follow-up at least 2 years after CABG or PCI in:

\- High-risk patients

\- Prior silent ischemia

\- Prior left-main stent

\- High public-safety occupational risk

\- New/recurrent/worsening symptoms after medical treatment or
revascularization if testing will alter management and no imaging stress
test within 12 months

\#### Follow-Up of Known CAD

\- Routine follow-up when prior invasive/noninvasive assessment showed
hemodynamically significant CAD over 2 years ago without intervening
revascularization

\#### Special Diagnostic Conditions Requiring Coronary Evaluation

\- Prior acute coronary syndrome within 12 months without stress test or
coronary angiography since

\- Newly diagnosed systolic or diastolic heart failure with reasonable
suspicion of ischemia, unless invasive angiography is already planned

\- Ventricular arrhythmias:

\- Sustained VT

\- VF

\- Exercise-induced VT

\- Nonsustained VT or frequent PVCs when exercise ECG cannot be
performed

\- Intermediate/high-risk patients needing class IC antiarrhythmic drugs

\- Hemodynamic assessment of ischemia in:

\- Anomalous coronary arteries

\- Myocardial bridging

\- Coronary aneurysms in Kawasaki disease

\- Prior anterior/left chest radiation at 5 years and every 5 years
thereafter

\#### Chronic Vascular Disease / Doppler Use

\- Severe aortic stenosis with low flow/low gradient and EF \< 50%

\- Asymptomatic moderate/severe aortic stenosis for hemodynamic change
assessment

\- Non-severe aortic regurgitation with symptoms

\- Mitral stenosis with exertional dyspnea worse than resting echo
suggests

\- Mitral regurgitation with exertional dyspnea or unclear moderate vs
severe severity

\- Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy with no resting/provocable LVOT gradient
≥ 50 mmHg

\- Asymptomatic HCM without resting/provocable gradient ≥ 50 mmHg

\#### Diastolic Function

\- Unexplained dyspnea and suspected HFpEF with normal/equivocal resting
diastolic function

\#### Prior to Elective Non-Cardiac Surgery

\- Intermediate/high-risk surgery plus:

\- At least 1 risk factor

\- Inability to walk or \< 4 METs

\- No imaging stress test within 1 year

\#### Pre-Organ Transplant

\- Any organ or stem cell transplant workup when no conclusive stress
evaluation, CTA, or catheterization within the past year

\#### Post Cardiac Transplant

\- Annual testing if invasive coronary arteriography is not being done

\## Service: Heart Catheterization

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\#### General / Invasive Coronary Arteriography

\- Typical angina with new onset or evolving ischemic ECG changes

\- Prinzmetal/variant angina on medical therapy

\- New or worsening angina in a patient with prior CABG or PCI

\- Symptomatic patient with high pretest probability

\- Unheralded syncope with unclear etiology

\- CAD with anginal symptoms plus intermediate/high-risk noninvasive
stress findings, including stress-induced LV dysfunction

\#### Stable Ischemic Heart Disease

\- High-risk exercise ECG findings such as:

\- Duke score ≤ -11

\- ST-segment elevation

\- Hypotension

\- Exercise-induced VT

\- Persistent ST depression into recovery

\- Ischemia at low threshold on stress testing

\- High-risk stress imaging

\- Intermediate-risk stress imaging plus one of:

\- Symptoms unresponsive to medical therapy

\- Unsatisfactory quality of life due to angina

\- EF \< 50%

\- Low-risk noninvasive testing but new/worsening/limiting symptoms with
reasonable cardiac suspicion despite or intolerant of therapy

\- New/worsening/limiting symptoms with known unrevascularized
obstructive CAD in a revascularization candidate

\- Post-STEMI culprit-only revascularization with plan for staged PCI of
non-culprit lesion

\- Before high-risk non-cardiac surgery when noninvasive testing shows
ischemia

\- Discordant/equivocal/inconclusive noninvasive evaluation, including:

\- Low-risk imaging with high-risk ECG response or typical angina

\- Equivocal or uninterpretable stress imaging

\#### CCTA Abnormalities

\- Symptomatic patient with:

\- One vessel ≥ 50% stenosis

\- 40% to 90% stenosis and FFR-CT ≤ 0.8

\- Left main stenosis ≥ 50%, even if asymptomatic

\#### Heart Failure / LV Dysfunction

\- New heart failure, cardiomyopathy, or wall motion abnormality in
patient who may undergo revascularization, including:

\- Newly recognized HF with known/suspected CAD

\- HF/ischemia with new unexplained wall motion abnormality

\- Severe MR or VSD with suspected ischemic origin

\- Deteriorating HF/cardiomyopathy requiring invasive guidance

\- Clarification of myocarditis vs acute coronary syndrome

\#### Ventricular Arrhythmias

\- After recovery from unexplained sudden cardiac arrest

\- Sustained VT or VF

\- Exercise-induced VT

\#### Prior to Non-Coronary Intervention / Cardiac Surgery

\- Coronary anatomy evaluation prior to cardiac surgery with:

\- Angina

\- Stress ischemia

\- EF \< 50%

\- History of CAD

\- Coronary risk factors

\- Also when:

\- Noninvasive data are inconclusive

\- Severe valve disease is present

\- Detailed coronary anatomy is needed before Ross procedure, homograft
surgery, or aortic root procedure

\- Patient is undergoing TAVR or other transcatheter valve procedure

\- Pre-organ transplant protocol requires it in place of, not in
addition to, imaging

\#### Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy

\- Candidate for septal reduction therapy with uncertain LVOT
obstruction on noninvasive imaging

\- Symptoms or evidence of myocardial ischemia

\- Prior to surgical myectomy when at risk for coronary atherosclerosis

\#### Post Cardiac Transplantation

\- Annual assessment for allograft vasculopathy

\#### Hemodynamic Assessment

\- Angiographic/hemodynamic assessment of valvular function or shunt
physiology when:

\- TTE/TEE inadequate for bioprosthetic valve and CMR/CCT unavailable

\- TTE/TEE inadequate for mechanical valve and CCTA unavailable

\- Noninvasive data and clinical impression are discordant

\- Shunt anatomy or flow ratio is indeterminate

\- Hemodynamic assessment only for:

\- Constrictive/restrictive physiology

\- Pulmonary hypertension when noninvasive data are inadequate or IV
therapy response must be assessed

\- HF, cardiomyopathy, or adult congenital disease when noninvasive data
are discordant or inadequate

\#### Ascending Aortography

\- Aortic root dilation in severe AS/AR before valve surgery

\- Aortic root or ascending aneurysm before repair

\- Central shunts, coarctation, or great vessels

\- Bypass graft identification during left heart cath

\- Aortic disease/coronary arteritis with suspected coronary involvement

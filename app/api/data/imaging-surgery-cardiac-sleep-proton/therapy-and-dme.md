\## 1. Therapy Services --- Habilitative Physical and Occupational Care

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Services must require:

\- Specific training, skills, and knowledge of a physical or
occupational therapist to:

\- Diagnose, correct, or significantly improve/optimize a condition.

\- Prevent deterioration or development of additional physical and
mental health conditions.

\- Complexity of care that can only be safely and effectively performed
by or under the general supervision of a skilled therapist.

\- For progressive disorders:

\- Treatment may be appropriate if it can prevent loss of a functional
skill or enhance adaptation to that loss.

\- Ongoing treatment is not appropriate when:

\- Sensorimotor function is at a steady state.

\- Treatment has yielded no measurable functional progress over a
reasonable period.

\### Relevant Codes

\- No specific ICD-10, CPT, or HCPCS codes listed.

\### Required Documentation

\- Written referral if required by state rules.

\- Initial evaluations and re-evaluations including:

\- Patient history, recent illness/injury/disability.

\- Diagnosis and onset/exacerbation date.

\- Prior and current level of function.

\- Underlying factors affecting current function.

\- Current objective measures.

\- Progress toward goals.

\- Requested frequency and duration.

\- Clinical rationale for continued skilled care.

\- Evidence of discharge planning.

\- Formal testing/functional assessments:

\- Age-appropriate.

\- Norm-referenced.

\- Standardized.

\- Specific to the therapy provided.

\- Linked to functional difficulty in daily tasks.

\- Raw scores alone are insufficient.

\- Score reports should include percentile ranks and/or standard
deviations when applicable.

\- Reason testing could not be completed, if applicable.

\- Re-testing at least yearly; may occur every 180 days.

\- If standardized testing is unavailable or normal:

\- Detailed clinical observations and objective data; or

\- Caregiver interview/questionnaire.

\- Coordination documentation when other agencies/schools provide
services:

\- No duplication.

\- Coordination of care.

\- Unavailable services.

\- Feeding-related documentation, when applicable:

\- Functional feeding delay tied to underlying impairment.

\- Examples of delay:

\- Gagging/choking.

\- Oral motor or upper-extremity coordination deficits.

\- Maladaptive behaviors due to food intolerance/aversion affecting
intake/nutrition/BMI.

\- Oral motor testing and detailed observations when delay is due to
oral motor/fine motor/sensory deficits.

\- Ongoing progress and home regimen carryover.

\- Plan of care:

\- Included with evaluations/re-evaluations.

\- Type, amount, duration, frequency.

\- SMART goals.

\- Goal start date and target date.

\- Goals tied to functional deficits, developmental milestones,
mobility, and ADLs.

\- Evidence-based interventions linked to targeted outcomes.

\- Reasonable anticipated timeframe.

\- Revisions when goals are not met.

\- Review intervals including:

\- Total visits.

\- Changes in objective measures.

\- Updated scores and interpretations.

\- Quantified progress by goal.

\- Intervention changes.

\- Updated goals.

\- Response/lack of response.

\- Prognosis.

\- Frequency updates for discharge planning.

\- Frequency support:

\- Intense frequency:

\- Case-by-case.

\- Greater than 3x/week for up to 4 weeks.

\- Requires letter of medical need, rehab potential, purpose, targeted
functional skill, specific measurable goals, expected goal date.

\- High frequency:

\- 3x/week for 2-6 weeks.

\- Severe delays.

\- Acute phase or rapidly changing home program.

\- Moderate frequency:

\- 2x/week.

\- Moderate delays.

\- Good progress, critical period, risk of regression, need for weekly
plan changes, complex caregiver education, non-repetitive skilled
interventions.

\- Low frequency:

\- 1x/week or less.

\- Slowed progress, risk of deterioration, periodic therapist
adjustment.

\- Maintenance frequency:

\- Every other week, monthly, or every 3 months when focused on
preventing deterioration.

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Services must not duplicate school/community services.

\- Parent report of limited food choices alone is not enough to support
feeding therapy.

\- Feeding therapy is not medically necessary for picky eaters who:

\- Eat/swallow normally.

\- Meet growth and developmental milestones.

\- Eat at least one food from all major food groups.

\- Eat more than 20 different foods.

\- Long-term goals should not target only underlying factors.

\- Short-term goals should not focus solely on strength, ROM, cognition,
etc.

\- Discontinue/wean when:

\- Goals attained.

\- No sustained measurable functional improvement.

\- Care can be carried out by caregivers/non-skilled personnel.

\- Expected level of function returned.

\- Maximum functional benefit reached.

\- Plateau/lack of significant progress.

\- Non-compliance.

\- Treatment no longer has therapeutic value.

\- Skilled care may resume if regression occurs despite a comprehensive
home program.

\## 2. Therapy Services --- Speech, Language, Communication, Feeding,
and Swallowing

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Services must be skilled and medically necessary.

\- Formal testing should establish a significant delay/deficit.

\- Test scores alone cannot determine need; they must be tied to daily
functional communication or feeding/swallowing problems.

\- For habilitative care:

\- There must be a reasonable expectation of significant improvement
within a reasonable timeframe.

\- For rehabilitative care:

\- Documentation must show the impact of injury/exacerbation on everyday
function.

\- Ongoing treatment is not appropriate when:

\- Function is steady.

\- Treatment no longer yields measurable and significant functional
progress.

\### Relevant Codes

\- No specific ICD-10, CPT, or HCPCS codes listed.

\### Required Documentation

\- Written referral.

\- Progress notes or updated plans of care every 60-90 days or per state
rules.

\- Documentation should include:

\- Current level of function.

\- Conditions affecting benefit from skilled intervention.

\- Objective progress toward each goal compared with prior reports.

\- Skilled treatment techniques used.

\- Patient response to therapy.

\- Rationale for lack of progress, when applicable.

\- Treatment goals that progress in complexity toward functional goals.

\- Re-evaluation or annual testing for habilitative care.

\- Clinical rationale for continued skilled speech-language care.

\- Coordination with schools/community agencies and documentation of
unavailable services.

\- Evaluation:

\- Establish delay/deficit.

\- Formal testing that is:

\- Age-appropriate.

\- Norm-referenced.

\- Standardized.

\- Specific to the therapy provided.

\- Test interpretation consistent with the test's scoring method.

\- Detailed clinical observations/objective data if testing is not
available or is normal.

\- Caregiver interview/questionnaire may support the request.

\- Reason formal testing could not be completed.

\- Functional skills documentation:

\- Baseline impairments tied to functional communication and/or
feeding/swallowing.

\- Specific measurable sustainable time-specific goals.

\- Special topic documentation:

\- Swallowing/feeding:

\- Oral, pharyngeal, and/or esophageal phase disorder, food intolerance,
or aversion.

\- Ongoing progress.

\- Consistent home regimen and caregiver education.

\- Reading/literacy goals:

\- Primary diagnosis must be speech/language disorder.

\- Deficits must affect functional ADLs.

\- Reading/literacy cannot be the primary focus.

\- Must require speech-language pathology skill.

\- Voice disorders:

\- Instrumental assessment by specialized medical physician to rule out
medical/structural cause.

\- Fluency disorders:

\- Evidence stuttering is medical and no longer developmental.

\- Nonspeech oral motor exercises:

\- Must be evidence-based and paired with functional articulation and/or
feeding/swallowing tasks.

\- Language mismatch/interpreter use:

\- Interpreter use should be documented.

\- Treatment plan should avoid targeting second-language influence.

\- Multilingual assessment should evaluate all language exposures and
use culturally/linguistically adapted tools.

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Therapy for picky eaters is not medically necessary when the patient:

\- Eats and swallows normally.

\- Meets growth and developmental milestones.

\- Eats from all major food groups.

\- Eats more than 20 different foods.

\- Reading/literacy treatment is limited as above.

\- Voice treatment requires prior medical workup.

\- Fluency treatment requires medical evidence.

\- If the patient is not progressing, revised treatment plan
documentation is required.

\- Frequency limits:

\- Intense:

\- 3x/week or more.

\- Requires additional testing/documentation and explanation why more
intense treatment is better.

\- Moderate:

\- 2x/week for moderate delays or clinically effective ongoing care.

\- Low:

\- 1x/week or less for mild delays, slowed progress, or risk of
deterioration.

\- Maintenance care:

\- Must focus on maintaining function/preventing regression.

\- Must document skilled interventions and why non-skilled care is
insufficient.

\- Discontinue/wean when:

\- No measurable functional improvement.

\- Caregiver/non-skilled personnel can perform the program.

\- Maximum therapeutic value reached.

\- No further improvement expected.

\- Treatment no longer has therapeutic value.

\## 3. Documentation Standards --- General Rehabilitation Services

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Documentation must clearly show why skilled professional services are
needed.

\- Initial evaluations and re-evaluations must support medical need
through:

\- Objective findings.

\- Subjective self/caregiver report.

\- Functional decline/delay tied to everyday tasks.

\- Scores alone are insufficient.

\- The plan of care must support need for skilled care rather than
self-management or non-skilled care.

\- Maintenance care may be considered when goals are to maintain
function and prevent regression.

\### Relevant Codes

\- No specific ICD-10 or HCPCS codes listed.

\- Services must be documented in accordance with CPT coding criteria.

\### Required Documentation

\- Legible complete records with signatures/credentials.

\- Appropriate chart dates and patient identifiers.

\- Correction process for handwritten/electronic records.

\- Evaluation/re-evaluation by a state-licensed qualified provider.

\- Pertinent history and demographics.

\- Baseline functional status.

\- Discharge summary/letter if changing providers under current
authorization.

\- Objective measures/discipline-specific standardized testing:

\- Valid.

\- Reliable.

\- Relevant.

\- Supported by best-practice guidance.

\- If no objective measures:

\- Detailed observations.

\- Interview/questionnaire or informal assessment.

\- Medical need.

\- Reason testing could not be completed.

\- Functional outcome results:

\- Raw scores.

\- Standardized scores.

\- Score interpretation.

\- Contraindications and management.

\- School/home/community program information for habilitative services.

\- Daily notes:

\- Evidence of skilled interventions.

\- Response/non-response and next-step plan.

\- Significant/unexpected status changes.

\- Plan of care:

\- Meaningful clinical observations.

\- Patient response to evaluation.

\- Prognosis.

\- Frequency/duration recommendations.

\- SMART short- and long-term goals.

\- Evidence-based interventions.

\- Amount, duration, frequency matching disability level and
medical/skilled needs.

\- Discharge planning.

\- Educational plan:

\- Home exercises.

\- ADL modifications.

\- Caregiver education.

\- Periodic review:

\- Total visits.

\- Objective measure changes.

\- Updated scores.

\- Quantified goal progress.

\- Intervention changes.

\- Goal updates.

\- Summary response.

\- Prognosis.

\- Prep for discharge.

\- Maintenance care:

\- Goals focused on maintaining current function and preventing
regression.

\- Clear documentation of skilled interventions preventing
deterioration.

\- Home program with evidence of compliance.

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Treatment should not focus on return to sports, recreation, or
work-specific tasks beyond normal daily living.

\- Visits/units requested must not exceed what the plan of care
supports.

\- Insufficient, unsigned, undated, or vague notes may result in denial
for lack of information.

\- Formal discharge should be considered when:

\- Services are unskilled.

\- Functional limitations do not support requested rate of care.

\- Treatment occurs without plan/goals/recent sustained improvement.

\## 4. Infant Manual Care

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- All of the following apply:

\- A therapeutic trial may be considered.

\- In the absence of conclusive evidence, clinical experience and
parent/patient preference must align.

\- If there is no clinically significant improvement toward measurable
goals after a trial, no additional care is indicated and referral may be
appropriate.

\- Manual-based therapy, active care, and passive therapies have not
been shown to improve health outcomes for:

\- Spine conditions.

\- Extremity-based musculoskeletal conditions.

\- Non-musculoskeletal conditions in infants.

\- There is no contemporary consensus supporting treatment of
non-musculoskeletal conditions such as:

\- Common cold.

\- Sinus congestion.

\- Allergies.

\- Sleep disturbances.

\- Difficulty nursing.

\- Infantile colic.

\- ADHD.

\- Asthma.

\- Autism.

\- Cerebral palsy.

\- Constipation.

\- Nocturnal enuresis.

\- Otitis media.

\### Relevant Codes

\- No specific ICD-10, CPT, or HCPCS codes listed in the retrieved text.

\### Required Documentation

\- Supporting clinical documentation, including recent notes/testing and
prior imaging when applicable.

\- Evidence of measurable goal-based improvement during the trial
period.

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Wellness care, well-baby checks, and preventive care are not covered.

\- Maintenance/preventive spinal or extraspinal manipulation is not
covered.

\- Evidence is insufficient for infantile colic.

\- Evidence is lacking for non-musculoskeletal infant conditions.

\- No high-quality evidence was identified for infant musculoskeletal
treatment using:

\- Spinal manipulation.

\- Extraspinal manipulation.

\- Mobilization.

\- Massage therapy.

\- Mechanical traction.

\- Electrical stimulation.

\- Ultrasound therapy.

\- Low-level laser therapy.

\## 5. Documentation Standards --- Chiropractic Care

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Services must require the training, skill, and knowledge of a
chiropractor to:

\- Diagnose.

\- Correct.

\- Significantly improve/optimize.

\- Prevent deterioration or development of additional physical health
conditions.

\- Services are not medically necessary if:

\- They can be omitted without adverse effect.

\- They are furnished only because a chiropractor performed them.

\- They can be self-administered or safely done by an unskilled person.

\- They consist of repetitive activities/exercises that do not require
chiropractic expertise.

\- They are for general fitness, flexibility, sports-specific
enhancement, or general academic tutoring.

\- Care must be:

\- Contractually covered.

\- Within scope of practice.

\- Within standard of practice.

\- Safe.

\- Medical rather than social/convenience-based.

\- Appropriate as to type, frequency, duration, extent, and location.

\- Delivered in the least intensive setting.

\- Cost-effective.

\### Relevant Codes

\- No specific ICD-10 list provided.

\- Documentation requires a working diagnosis similar to the appropriate
ICD code.

\- Services must meet CPT coding criteria.

\### Required Documentation

\- Legible records.

\- Patient demographics.

\- Signed/dated chart entries.

\- Patient ID on each page.

\- Subjective complaints.

\- Objective findings.

\- Assessment/diagnosis.

\- Treatment/ancillary studies.

\- Recommendations/instructions/patient education.

\- Working diagnosis consistent with findings.

\- Reason for encounter/referral.

\- Adverse events.

\- Relevant reports, labs, consultations, and correspondence.

\- Discharge copy if changing providers while authorized elsewhere.

\- Consent forms when applicable.

\- Abbreviation key if needed.

\- Correction procedure for records.

\- Evaluation must include:

\- Objective findings.

\- Detailed clinical observations.

\- Subjective self-report.

\- Prior medical/family/social history.

\- Baseline current/prior functional status.

\- Systems review.

\- Objective measures and/or standardized orthopedic/neurologic testing.

\- Raw scores, standardized scores, interpretations.

\- Prognosis and rehab potential.

\- Alternative evidence and reason if formal testing cannot be
completed.

\- Plan of care:

\- Individualized, goal-oriented, functional.

\- Age/date of birth.

\- Evaluation date.

\- Medical history/background.

\- All diagnoses.

\- Contraindications.

\- Safety risks.

\- Onset/current exacerbation date.

\- Baseline functional limitations.

\- Patient response to evaluation.

\- Prognosis.

\- Amount/frequency/duration recommendations.

\- Patient-specific measurable sustainable goals.

\- Specific interventions.

\- Predicted improvement.

\- Specific discharge plan.

\- Updates to time frame, total visits, objective changes, goal
progress, intervention changes, and collaboration.

\- Daily treatment notes:

\- SOAP-type format.

\- Date for return/follow-up.

\- Skilled interventions with rationale, time component, goals if
needed.

\- Response/non-response.

\- Clinical status changes.

\- Re-evaluation:

\- New condition.

\- Significant or unanticipated symptom change.

\- Functional decline.

\- Response/non-response at meaningful change point.

\- Need to change treatment plan/goals.

\- Utilization review elements:

\- Initial trial vs ongoing care.

\- Acute/subacute/chronic status.

\- Exacerbation.

\- Trauma/insidious/repetitive onset.

\- Onset date/mechanism.

\- History and interim history.

\- Pain details.

\- Measurable functional goals.

\- Outcome tools at set intervals.

\- Evidence of sustained improvement meeting MDC and/or MCIC.

\- CPT-supported billed services.

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Treatment must not focus on return to activities beyond normal daily
living.

\- Initial plan of care for a musculoskeletal condition should not
exceed 4 weeks.

\- Mild complaints/minimal functional limitations should be released to
a home exercise program.

\- Treatment exceeding 2-3 months for the same/similar condition is a
utilization concern.

\- "As needed" treatment without plan, goals, or sustained improvement
is not supported.

\- Inadequate, unsigned, undated, or insufficiently detailed notes may
result in denial.

\## 6. Active Procedures and Training Services

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Documentation must show:

\- Services are skilled.

\- Skilled services are required and provided by skilled clinicians.

\- Services are safe and effective.

\- Evaluation/plan of care must include:

\- Need for therapy supported by objective findings and subjective
report.

\- Patient-specific need involving ADLs, mobility, safety.

\- Timeline for starting, progressing, and discharging skilled care.

\- Specific treatment parameters: service type, frequency, intensity,
duration.

\- Measurable goals and precautions.

\- Progress/daily notes must include:

\- Medical necessity for treatment/treatment change.

\- Functional improvement from improved objective/outcome measures.

\- Clear recent significant progress.

\- Continued need for skilled provider.

\- Justification when progress is lacking.

\- Barriers to independent home program.

\- Skilled services must be part of a documented plan to improve/restore
impaired function.

\- Unskilled services, palliative repetition, general
fitness/flexibility activities, or services expected to improve
naturally are not reasonable/necessary.

\### Relevant Codes

\- CPT/HCPCS codes explicitly listed:

\- 97110

\- 97112

\- 97113

\- 97116

\- 97760

\- 97761

\- 97763

\- 97530

\- 97129

\- 97533

\- 97535

\- 97542

\- 97537

\- 97545

\- 97546

\- L-codes referenced for orthotics

\- No specific ICD-10 list provided.

\### Required Documentation

\- General:

\- Specific skilled services provided.

\- Medical necessity of interventions.

\- Support for number of visits.

\- Functional improvement.

\- Need for skilled provider beyond mere program setup.

\- Comprehensive rehab-program context.

\- Timed billing:

\- 15-minute unit methodology.

\- Medicare minimum time thresholds apply unless state rules differ.

\- By code:

\- \*\*97110\*\*

\- Which exercises were performed.

\- Targeted body area/muscle groups.

\- Amount/type of resistance.

\- Repetitions/sets.

\- Time component.

\- Objective validation of ROM/strength/flexibility/functional mobility
loss.

\- Home exercise competency/compliance.

\- Reason patient cannot participate in home program if prolonged
in-office exercise.

\- \*\*97112\*\*

\- Need for direct-contact skilled therapy.

\- Injury to neuromusculoskeletal system.

\- Therapeutic procedures.

\- Home care instructions/education.

\- At least one qualifying condition such as reflex/vibration loss with
paresthesia/burning/diffuse pain, nerve palsy, muscular
weakness/flaccidity from neurologic causes/trauma, compensatory movement
patterns, vestibular dysfunction.

\- \*\*97113\*\*

\- Need for direct-contact skilled therapy.

\- Safe pool environment.

\- Specific rationale for aquatic medium and transition plan to
land-based therapy.

\- \*\*97116\*\*

\- Need for individual skilled therapy.

\- Gait deficits:

\- Walking speed.

\- Cadence.

\- Stride length/balance.

\- Functional ambulation category scores.

\- Use of body-weight support/unweighting/assistive devices.

\- Assessment of gait phases and lower-extremity/low-back impact.

\- \*\*97760\*\*

\- Skilled need.

\- Proper examination.

\- Causal nexus for orthotic need.

\- Foot-specific deficit if custom orthotics needed.

\- \*\*97761\*\*

\- New prosthesis or adjustment to current prosthesis improving
function.

\- \*\*97763\*\*

\- Skilled need for follow-up orthotic/prosthetic management.

\- \*\*97530\*\*

\- Skilled need.

\- Condition expected to restore/improve function.

\- Inability to perform under non-skilled supervision.

\- Correlation between condition and activity prescribed.

\- \*\*97129\*\*

\- \*\*97533\*\*

\- Skilled need.

\- Sensory processing deficits affecting functional skills.

\- \*\*97535\*\*

\- Skilled need.

\- ADL/home-management instruction tied to expected functional goals and

\- \*\*97542\*\*

\- Current event prompting wheelchair assessment.

\- Prior wheelchair assessments.

\- Current/previous functional level.

\- Attempts by nursing/caregivers/patient to address

\- Correlation of training with expected functional goals.

\- Patient response to instruction/fitting.

\- \*\*97537\*\*

\- Skilled need.

\- \*\*97545/97546\*\*

\- Prior acute rehab services.

\- Return-to-work limitations and willingness to participate.

\- Neuromusculoskeletal deficits interfering with work.

\- Condition sufficiently resolved for conditioning.

\- Psychosocial/vocational issues and referrals.

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- \*\*97110\*\* generally not reimbursable for:

\- Endurance without deficits.

\- General fitness.

\- Weight loss.

\- Return to work.

\- Return to sports/recreation/aerobic conditioning.

\- Noncompliance with home instructions:

\- In-office instruction no longer medically necessary.

\- Discharge for noncompliance/against medical advice.

\- High-tech fitness equipment lacks evidence of improved outcomes over
standard equipment.

\- Orthotic management:

\- Cannot be used if orthotic is formed/fabricated without patient
present.

\- Patient typically should not be seen more than once per calendar year
for one set of orthotics.

\- \*\*97537\*\* is typically not covered.

\- Often bundled with other services.

\- Non-covered third-party assistive devices/equipment remain
non-covered for issue/modify/adjust/educate.

\- Employment opportunities, work skills, and work are excluded.

\- \*\*97545/97546\*\* are typically not covered.

\- Objective measures in isolation (such as ROM or manual muscle
testing) are generally not enough as functional assessments.

\## 7. Durable Equipment and Supplies

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- All of the following must be met:

\- Equipment is expected to improve specific measurable functional
deficits tied to documented illness/injury.

\- Provided by a health care professional.

\- Has significant medical uses.

\- Alternative options have been ruled out.

\- Clinical records clearly establish the medical need.

\### Relevant Codes

\- No specific ICD-10, CPT, or HCPCS codes listed.

\### Required Documentation

\- Diagnosis supporting the equipment/supply request.

\- Treatment plan with anticipated start and end dates for training/use.

\- Measurable functional deficits.

\- Expected outcomes and benefit related to those deficits.

\- Training/education, supervision, and monitoring by provider,
including provider type and signature.

\- Trial of conservative services that failed, unless contraindicated.

\- In-office trial use showing improvement when appropriate.

\- Home or vehicle assessment when appropriate.

\- Prior equipment used for similar purpose and why it no longer meets
needs.

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- If the health plan does not cover the requested equipment, any visit
solely for instruction on that equipment would not be covered.

\## 8. Experimental, Unproven, or Investigational Services

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- These services are not recognized as standard clinical care when
evidence is insufficient.

\- A service is considered experimental/investigational if any of the
following apply:

\- Required regulatory approval is not final.

\- Only restricted market approval exists.

\- It is only at an interim regulatory step.

\- Evidence is insufficient or inconclusive regarding:

\- Therapeutic value.

\- Beneficial effect on health outcomes.

\- Benefit compared with established alternatives.

\### Relevant Codes

\- No specific ICD-10 list provided.

\- Code explicitly referenced:

\- 97012 as the standard mechanical traction comparator for certain
higher-rate back traction devices.

\### Required Documentation

\- If such a service is provided:

\- The member must be informed in writing that the service is the
member's responsibility.

\- Services must not be performed instead of an appropriate examination
or referral.

\- General supporting documentation, including recent notes/testing and
prior imaging when applicable.

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Listed services are not eligible for reimbursement.

\- Coverage is subject to the benefit plan.

\- Retrieved non-exclusive list includes:

\- Advanced BioStructural Correction.

\- Alphabiotics.

\- Applied Kinesiology.

\- Applied Spinal Biomechanical Engineering.

\- Bio-Energetic Synchronization Technique.

\- Blood Flow Restriction Training.

\- Chiropractic Biophysics.

\- Chiropractic scoliosis-control/progression-reduction services
including specialized bracing/programs.

\- Coccygeal meningeal stress fixation.

\- Cold laser therapy.

\- Computerized muscle testing/analysis.

\- Cupping.

\- Craniosacral therapy.

\- Directional non-force technique.

\- Dry needling.

\- Horizontal electrotherapy.

\- High-density/surface/paraspinal/macro EMG methods.

\- Hippotherapy.

\- Impulse adjusting instrument.

\- Intersegmental traction/autotraction.

\- Kinesio taping.

\- Live cell analysis/hair analysis.

\- Manipulation under anesthesia.

\- Moire contourographic analysis.

\- Certain allergy testing/elimination techniques.

\- Certain upper cervical techniques.

\- Network chiropractic and certain emotion-based techniques.

\- Neural organizational/contact reflex/whole system scan methods.

\- Neurocalometer/Nervo-Scope/nerve conduction/surface EMG/spinoscopy
for non-specific neck/back pain.

\- Neurophysiologic pain profile/spine matrix scan.

\- Nimmo receptor-tonus method.

\- Pettibon methods.

\- Preventive/corrective chiropractic care.

\- Pro-Adjuster.

\- Sacro-occipital/neurocranial/cranial manipulation methods.

\- Sound-assisted soft tissue mobilization.

\- Spinal diagnostic ultrasound.

\- Repeat imaging to monitor conservative treatment progress.

\- Thermography.

\- Treatment for brachioradial pruritis.

\- Vascular studies including Doppler ultrasound and plethysmography.

\- Certain decompression/back traction devices charged above standard
mechanical traction.

\- Whole body vibration/vibration plate/vibration therapy.

\- Lab work outside CLIA certification or scope of practice.

\- Service exceptions/notes:

\- Whole body vibration for low back pain: evidence remains equivocal.

\- Low-level laser therapy for non-specific chronic low back pain may
give pain relief, but no significant effect was identified for
disability or spinal ROM; not superior to exercise alone.

\- Plethysmography may be a diagnostic modality for chronic venous
disease, arterial occlusive disease, or body plethysmography/pulmonary
function evaluation, but such testing should be referred out when
outside scope.

\- Future removal from this category requires high-quality evidence
demonstrating:

\- Safety.

\- Superior/comparable health outcomes.

\- Improved clinical decision-making.

\- Reliability/predictive value equal or superior to gold standard.

\- Comparable or lower cost with similar outcomes.

\## 9. Measurable Improvement Standards

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\- Outcome measures and pre-determined treatment goals must be used with
each patient.

\- Goals must be:

\- Specific.

\- Measurable.

\- Functional.

\- Meaningful progress must be supported by documented standardized
outcome assessments.

\- Records must document whether treatment is producing:

\- Progressive improvement.

\- Sustained improvement.

\- Clear measurable improvement in pain and function.

\- Progress should be assessed:

\- Every 2 weeks; or

\- At regular intervals appropriate to the condition.

\- If no functional tool exists for the condition:

\- The practitioner should create specific, measurable, functional
goals.

\### Relevant Codes

\- No specific ICD-10, CPT, or HCPCS codes listed.

\### Required Documentation

\- Baseline and updated standardized outcome measures.

\- Comparison of baseline to updated measures.

\- Periodic reassessment.

\- Goal-based documentation showing amount/degree of change over time.

\- Documentation sufficient for peer review to determine:

\- Baseline functional status.

\- Treatment response.

\- Definitions/metrics addressed include:

\- MCIC.

\- MCID.

\- MDC.

\- MIC.

\- MTB.

\- SDC.

\- SEM.

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Most people should notice measurable improvement in pain/disability
within 2-6 weeks.

\- If no improvement occurs within 6 weeks, further treatment is
unlikely to help.

\- When initial improvement occurs, studies generally show no additional
lasting improvement beyond 6-12 weeks.

\- Maximum therapeutic benefit may be appropriate when:

\- Pre-onset status is reached.

\- Meaningful improvement occurred but no further meaningful improvement
is expected.

\- No basis remains for further in-office treatment.

\- Standardized tools no longer show meaningful improvement.

\- Meaningful improvement was not achieved.

\- Documentation is insufficient to validate response.

\- When measures approximate normal or mild deficits manageable by
self-care/home exercise, maximum therapeutic benefit is appropriate.

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Clinically inappropriate use includes:

\- Patient safety jeopardized.

\- Can be safely/effectively administered by patient/another person.

\- Continued beyond initial treatment period.

\- Used as primary or sole therapy.

\- More than two passive treatments to the same body region(s).

\- Used for comfort/convenience.

\- Used as routine office protocol.

\- As condition progresses, passive care should be replaced by active
treatment.

with passive treatment except that manual therapy must be performed at a
separate anatomic site to be separately reimbursed under NCCI edits.

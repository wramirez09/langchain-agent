MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

1.  General Clinical Appropriateness Requirements

-   Before any intervention, the diagnosis or pretest likelihood must be
    > confirmed through:

    -   History and physical examination

        > Relevant laboratory studies, when applicable

        > Relevant diagnostic testing, when applicable

        > Response to prior therapeutic intervention, when applicable

    > The expected benefit of the intervention must outweigh potential
    > harms

    > Current literature, treatment guidelines, or standards of care
    > should support the requested intervention as the best option

    > There should be a reasonable likelihood that the intervention will
    > change management or improve outcome

2\. Overriding Clinical Conditions

-   An arrhythmia must be documented, meaning permanently recorded and
    > available for review if requested

    > An arrhythmia is symptomatic only when symptoms occur at the same
    > time as the arrhythmia

    > Pacemaker placement is generally not appropriate if the patient is
    > taking medications that are causing bradyarrhythmia or conduction
    > disturbance, unless:

    -   Those medications cannot be discontinued, or

        > Dose reduction/withholding is contraindicated

    > Reversible causes must be excluded, such as:

    -   Electrolyte disturbance

        > Hypothermia

        > Drug toxicity

        > Hypothyroidism

        > Infection

        > Inflammation

        > Ischemia

3\. Transvenous Permanent Pacemaker

A. Documented Sinus Node Dysfunction Permanent pacemaker placement is
medically necessary when:

-   The patient is not taking medications that would cause sinus node
    > dysfunction, or stopping/reducing them would be contraindicated

    > AND any of the following apply:

    -   Symptomatic arrhythmias with sinus node dysfunction and symptoms
        > clearly attributable to the arrhythmia

        > Symptomatic chronotropic incompetence

        > Symptomatic tachy-brady syndrome with symptoms clearly
        > attributable to bradyarrhythmia

Additional note

-   If symptoms cannot be temporally correlated with sinus node
    > dysfunction, there is no minimum heart rate or pause duration that
    > by itself supports permanent pacemaker placement

B. Documented Atrioventricular (AV) Block Permanent pacemaker placement
is medically necessary when:

-   Reversible causes are absent

    > The patient is not taking medications that would cause AV node
    > dysfunction, or withholding them would be contraindicated

    > AND any of the following apply:

    -   Acquired third-degree AV block, symptomatic or asymptomatic

        > Acquired high-grade second-degree block, symptomatic or
        > asymptomatic

        > Acquired Mobitz type II second-degree AV block, symptomatic or
        > asymptomatic

        > Symptomatic Mobitz type I second-degree AV block when symptoms
        > are clearly attributable to the AV block

        > Symptomatic first-degree AV block when:

        -   PR interval is 300 milliseconds or greater

            > Symptoms are clearly attributable to the AV block

        > Permanent atrial fibrillation with symptomatic bradycardia

        > Neuromuscular disease with expected survival greater than 1
        > year and any of the following:

        -   Third-degree AV block

            > Second-degree AV block, including:

            -   Type I

                > Type II

                > 2:1 AV block

                > High-grade AV block

            > PR interval greater than 240 milliseconds

            > HV interval 70 milliseconds or greater

        > Infiltrative cardiomyopathy with expected survival greater
        > than 1 year and any of the following:

        -   Third-degree AV block

            > Second-degree Mobitz type II AV block

            > High-grade AV block

        > Congenital heart disease with any of the following:

        -   Symptomatic bradycardia related to AV block

            > Congenital complete AV block with any of the following:

            -   Bradycardia, symptomatic or asymptomatic

                > Escape rhythm with wide QRS complex

                > Mean daytime heart rate less than 50 beats per minute

                > Complex ventricular ectopy

                > Ventricular dysfunction

        > Postoperative AV block not expected to resolve with any of the
        > following:

        -   Third-degree AV block

            > Second-degree Mobitz type II AV block

            > High-grade AV block

C. Bundle Branch Block or Fascicular Block Permanent pacemaker placement
is medically necessary for bundle branch block or fascicular block with
1:1 AV conduction when any of the following apply:

-   Alternating bundle branch block

    > Syncope of unknown cause in a patient with bundle branch block and
    > either:

    -   HV interval 70 milliseconds or greater

        > Evidence of infranodal block on electrophysiology study

    > Neuromuscular disease with expected survival greater than 1 year
    > and any of the following:

    -   HV interval 70 milliseconds or greater

        > QRS duration greater than 120 milliseconds

        > Fascicular block

    > Anderson-Fabry disease with expected survival greater than 1 year
    > and QRS duration greater than 110 milliseconds

4\. Leadless Pacemakers

A. Single-Chamber Leadless Pacemaker, Right Ventricular Medically
necessary only when both of the following are present:

-   The individual has an indication for a pacemaker

    > A leaded transvenous pacemaker cannot be placed because of one of
    > the following:

    -   Venous access issues

        > History of or high risk for cardiac implanted electronic
        > device infection

        > Prosthetic tricuspid valve

5\. Device Replacement Device replacement is medically necessary when
any of the following apply:

-   Device interrogation shows the device is nearing end of life

    > Device is malfunctioning or cannot be reprogrammed to provide
    > optimal pacemaker support

    > Device requires explantation due to infection

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

1.  Core Documentation

-   Complete history and physical examination

    > Relevant laboratory results, when applicable

    > Relevant diagnostic testing, when applicable

    > Documentation of response to prior therapeutic intervention, when
    > applicable

    > Clinical information showing the requested intervention is
    > expected to improve management or outcome

2\. Arrhythmia Documentation

-   Permanent recording of the arrhythmia so that a copy can be provided
    > on request

    > Rhythm documentation supporting the diagnosis, such as sinus node
    > dysfunction, AV block, or bundle branch block

    > Documentation showing whether the arrhythmia is symptomatic

3\. Symptom Correlation

-   Clear temporal relationship between symptoms and arrhythmia

    > Symptom details when applicable, including:

    -   Syncope

        > Presyncope

        > Dizziness/lightheadedness

        > Heart failure symptoms

        > Confusional episodes related to cerebral hypoperfusion

4\. Medication Review

-   Medication list showing whether the patient is taking drugs that
    > could cause sinus node dysfunction, bradyarrhythmia, or conduction
    > disturbance

    > If such medications are being continued, documentation explaining
    > why withholding or dose reduction is contraindicated

5\. Reversible Cause Evaluation

-   Documentation showing reversible causes have been excluded

6\. Condition-Specific Supporting Documentation

-   PR interval, HV interval, and QRS duration when used in criteria

    > Electrophysiology study findings when infranodal block is part of
    > the indication

    > Mean daytime heart rate, ventricular ectopy, ventricular
    > dysfunction, or escape rhythm details when congenital complete AV
    > block is the basis

    > Documentation of neuromuscular disease, infiltrative
    > cardiomyopathy, congenital heart disease, postoperative AV block,
    > or Anderson-Fabry disease when relevant

    > Expected survival greater than 1 year when that is part of the
    > criterion

7\. Leadless Pacemaker-Specific Documentation

-   Clinical indication for pacemaker placement

    > Reason transvenous leaded device cannot be placed, including:

    -   Venous access limitation

        > Infection history or high infection risk

        > Prosthetic tricuspid valve

8\. Device Replacement Documentation

-   Device interrogation report showing elective replacement indicator
    > or end-of-life status

    > Documentation of malfunction or failed reprogramming

    > Evidence of device infection if explantation is required

9\. Administrative/Review Documentation

-   Submitted records must accurately reflect the clinical status at the
    > time of the request

    > Submitted records must clearly show the ordering clinician's
    > intent

    > Multiple simultaneous interventions may require additional review
    > or peer-to-peer discussion

    > Repeat diagnostic or therapeutic requests may require added
    > justification

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

1.  Non-Covered/Not Medically Necessary in This Guideline

-   Single-chamber leadless pacemaker, right atrial: not medically
    > necessary in all scenarios

    > Dual-chamber leadless pacemaker: not medically necessary in all
    > scenarios

2\. Out of Scope

-   Temporary pacemakers

    > Pacemakers used for heart failure management/cardiac
    > resynchronization therapy

    > Implantable defibrillators

    > Certain inpatient situations where permanent pacemaker need arises
    > during hospitalization for another condition, such as after:

    -   Valve replacement

        > Bypass surgery

        > Myocardial infarction

3\. Additional Clinical Limitations

-   Lack of symptom-rhythm correlation weakens support for pacemaker
    > placement in sinus node dysfunction

    > If medication-induced bradyarrhythmia/conduction disturbance is
    > present and medications can be safely changed, pacemaker placement
    > is generally not appropriate

    > Reversible causes must be ruled out before permanent implantation

    > Device selection details such as manufacturer, capabilities, and
    > mode settings are outside the scope

4\. Repeat/Concurrent Service Limitations

-   Multiple interventions ordered simultaneously often require
    > additional review

    > Repeated diagnostic testing of the same area for the same
    > indication may require added review, especially when:

    -   Repeated because of technical issues

        > Repeated at a different facility due to preference or quality
        > concerns

        > Repeated despite persistent symptoms without clinical change
        > or treatment

        > Repeated by different providers over a short period

    > Repeat therapeutic intervention in the same area before expected
    > benefit has lapsed is generally not appropriate unless the prior
    > intervention was never actually performed

PRACTICAL BOTTOM LINE

-   The guideline supports permanent pacemaker implantation mainly for
    > clearly documented and clinically significant bradyarrhythmias or
    > conduction disease after reversible causes are excluded

    > Symptom correlation, rhythm documentation, and
    > medication/reversible-cause review are central to approval

    > Right ventricular single-chamber leadless pacemakers are limited
    > to patients who need pacing but cannot receive a transvenous
    > system

    > Right atrial single-chamber leadless and dual-chamber leadless
    > pacemakers are excluded as not medically necessary under this
    > guideline

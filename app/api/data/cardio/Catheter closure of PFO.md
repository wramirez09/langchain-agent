MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

-   Percutaneous transcatheter closure of patent foramen ovale is
    > considered medically necessary only when ALL of the following are
    > met:

    -   Age 60 years or younger

        > Embolic stroke of unknown cause despite comprehensive
        > evaluation

        > High RoPE score greater than 6, OR high-risk echocardiogram
        > with low RoPE score less than or equal to 6

        > No concurrent indication for anticoagulation, such as:

        -   Atrial fibrillation

            > Mechanical heart valve prosthesis

            > Pulmonary embolus

            > Deep vein thrombus

            > Other conditions requiring anticoagulation

        > Not scheduled for cardiac surgery

SUPPORTING DEFINITIONS AND SUBCRITERIA

-   High-risk echocardiogram includes any of the following:

    -   PFO with straddling thrombus

        > PFO with atrial septal aneurysm

        > Large-shunt PFO, defined as a single frame showing more than
        > 20 microbubbles in the left atrium within 3 cardiac cycles
        > after opacification of the right atrium with agitated saline
        > contrast, with or without Valsalva maneuver

    > Comprehensive evaluation of embolic stroke means no other
    > explanation for stroke is evident after all of the following:

    -   Brain imaging

        > Neurovascular imaging

        > Exclusion of atrial fibrillation by at least 30 days of rhythm
        > monitoring showing no atrial fibrillation episode lasting
        > longer than 1 hour

        > Cardiovascular imaging to exclude other embolic sources

        > Exclusion of hypercoagulable state

    > RoPE score:

    -   Tool used to estimate the likelihood that embolic stroke is
        > related to the PFO

        > Higher scores suggest a stronger causal relationship between
        > stroke and PFO

GENERAL CLINICAL APPROPRIATENESS REQUIREMENTS

-   Before any intervention, the clinician should confirm the diagnosis
    > or establish pretest likelihood through:

    -   History

        > Physical examination

        > Relevant laboratory studies, when applicable

        > Diagnostic testing, when applicable

        > Response to prior therapeutic intervention, when applicable

    > Additional appropriateness elements include:

    -   Expected benefit outweighs potential harms

        > Current literature, treatment guidelines, or standards of
        > practice support the intervention

        > Reasonable likelihood that the intervention will change
        > management or improve outcome

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

-   The guideline addresses only transcatheter closure of patent foramen
    > ovale. It does not address:

    -   Transcatheter delivery of devices for other congenital atrial
        > septal abnormalities

        > Surgical closure of PFO

    > Closure of PFO for indications other than those listed in the
    > guideline is considered not medically necessary, including but not
    > limited to:

    -   Treatment of migraine

    > Patients with an indication for long-term anticoagulation are
    > unlikely to benefit from PFO closure because anticoagulation
    > already lowers the risk of venous or intracardiac thrombosis and
    > subsequent PFO-related stroke

    > For patients who require short-term anticoagulation, such as for
    > concomitant PE or DVT at the time of stroke, PFO closure can be
    > deferred until the anticoagulation course is complete

    > Requests for multiple interventions at the same time may require
    > peer-to-peer review if the need for simultaneous performance is
    > unclear

    > Repeat diagnostic testing or repeat therapeutic intervention may
    > be subject to additional review in certain circumstances,
    > including persistent symptoms without clinical change, duplicate
    > testing, technical issues, or early repeat intervention without
    > justification

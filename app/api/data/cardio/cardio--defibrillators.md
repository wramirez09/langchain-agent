MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

General clinical threshold

-   ICD placement must be expected to provide benefit in a patient
    > anticipated to survive more than 1 year with good functional
    > status.

    > The clinical record must support diagnosis, expected benefit over
    > risk, consistency with accepted practice, and likelihood that the
    > intervention will change management or improve outcome.

Transvenous ICD Considered medically necessary when any one of the
following is met:

-   Prior cardiac arrest due to ventricular fibrillation or ventricular
    > tachycardia without a completely reversible cause.

    > Spontaneous sustained ventricular tachycardia in a patient with
    > structural heart disease.

    > Unexplained syncope in a patient with structural heart disease.

    > Unexplained syncope in ischemic heart disease with inducible
    > sustained monomorphic VT on EP study.

    > Nonischemic dilated cardiomyopathy, age 70 or younger, after 90
    > days of guideline-directed medical therapy, with both:

    -   LVEF 35% or less

        > NYHA class II or III

    > Ischemic cardiomyopathy with any of:

    -   LVEF 30% or less due to MI at least 40 days earlier, NYHA class
        > I despite medical therapy, and at least 90 days after
        > revascularization if revascularization occurred

        > LVEF 35% or less due to MI at least 40 days earlier, NYHA
        > class II or III despite medical therapy, and at least 90 days
        > after revascularization if revascularization occurred

        > LVEF 40% or less due to prior MI plus spontaneous nonsustained
        > VT and a positive EP study performed at least 96 hours after
        > MI

    > Congenital heart disease with any of:

    -   Prior cardiac arrest believed due to ventricular arrhythmia

        > Hemodynamically unstable VT not suitable for other treatment
        > options, after medical therapy for ventricular dysfunction if
        > present

        > Unexplained syncope with repaired congenital heart disease
        > plus moderate LV dysfunction or marked LV hypertrophy

    > Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy with any of:

    -   Prior cardiac arrest

        > Ventricular fibrillation or sustained VT

        > Syncope within the prior 6 months suspected to be arrhythmic

        > Maximum LV wall thickness 30 mm or greater

        > Sudden cardiac death in a first- or second-degree relative age
        > 50 or younger attributed to hypertrophic cardiomyopathy

        > LV apical aneurysm

        > LVEF below 50%

        > Late gadolinium enhancement involving 15% of LV mass in a
        > patient age 19 or older

        > Nonsustained VT on 24- to 48-hour ambulatory ECG monitoring

    > Arrhythmogenic right ventricular dysplasia with any of:

    -   Prior cardiac arrest

        > Sustained VT

        > Left and/or right ventricular EF 35% or less despite medical
        > therapy

        > Syncope thought due to ventricular arrhythmia

    > Long QT syndrome with syncope or VT despite beta blocker therapy,
    > or when beta blockers are contraindicated.

    > Short QT syndrome with prior cardiac arrest or sustained VT/VF.

    > Brugada syndrome with spontaneous type 1 ECG pattern plus any of:

    -   Prior cardiac arrest

        > Sustained VT or VF

        > Syncope thought due to ventricular arrhythmia

    > Catecholaminergic polymorphic VT with recurrent sustained VT or
    > recurrent syncope despite beta blocker therapy, or when beta
    > blockers are contraindicated.

    > Cardiac sarcoidosis with any of:

    -   Prior cardiac arrest

        > LVEF 35% or less despite medical therapy

        > Spontaneous or induced sustained VT

        > Need for permanent pacemaker

        > LVEF above 35% plus syncope history or extensive scar on
        > cardiac MRI or PET

    > Phospholamban cardiomyopathy with either:

    -   LVEF below 45%

        > Nonsustained VT

    > FLNC cardiomyopathy with LVEF below 45%.

    > Lamin A/C cardiomyopathy with either:

    -   Need for permanent pacemaker

        > At least 2 of the following:

        -   LVEF below 45%

            > Nonsustained VT

            > Male sex

    > Outpatient awaiting heart transplant or ventricular assist device
    > who is NYHA class IV.

    > Device replacement when either:

    -   Generator end-of-life criteria are present

        > Pocket must be opened for another reason and the device is
        > within 3 years of end-of-life criteria.

Subcutaneous ICD Considered medically necessary only when all of the
following are met:

-   The patient already meets at least one transvenous ICD indication.

    > No need for bradycardia pacing, overdrive pacing for VT
    > termination, or cardiac resynchronization.

    > No incessant ventricular tachycardia.

    > At least one of the following:

    -   Unable to obtain venous access

        > Immunocompromised

        > Recurrent transvenous lead-related, pocket, or systemic
        > infection

        > Endocarditis

        > Subcutaneous system preferred because of younger patient age.

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

Explicitly required by the policy

-   Complete clinical evaluation establishing the diagnosis or pretest
    > likelihood

    > History and physical exam

    > Relevant laboratory studies when applicable

    > Relevant diagnostic testing when applicable

    > Response to prior therapeutic intervention when applicable

    > Documentation showing expected benefit outweighs risk

    > Documentation showing the request aligns with accepted clinical
    > practice

    > Documentation showing the intervention is likely to change
    > management or improve outcome

    > Clinical documentation that accurately reflects the patient's
    > condition at the time of request

    > Documentation that clearly reflects the ordering clinician's
    > intent.

Condition-specific supporting records that would be needed to prove the
chosen criterion

-   LVEF measurement

    > NYHA functional class

    > Evidence of duration and adequacy of guideline-directed medical
    > therapy

    > Dates of MI and any revascularization

    > EP study results

    > Documentation of syncope, cardiac arrest, sustained VT, or VF

    > Congenital heart disease status and repair history

    > Family history where hypertrophic cardiomyopathy criteria rely on
    > it

    > LV wall thickness measurements

    > Ambulatory ECG monitoring results for nonsustained VT

    > MRI or PET findings where scar burden is part of the criterion

    > Pacemaker indication if used to justify ICD in lamin A/C
    > cardiomyopathy or cardiac sarcoidosis

    > Documentation of venous access issues, immunocompromised status,
    > infection history, endocarditis, or age-related preference for
    > subcutaneous ICD

    > Generator end-of-life evidence or reason for pocket reopening for
    > replacement requests.

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

-   Substernal ICD use is considered not medically necessary for all
    > indications.

    > The guideline does not address external defibrillators or cardiac
    > resynchronization devices.

    > The code list is not all-inclusive.

    > Nonspecific or unlisted codes may require extra documentation and
    > review.

    > Simultaneous multiple interventions may require peer-to-peer
    > review.

    > Repeat testing of the same area for the same indication may
    > undergo extra review, especially when there is no meaningful
    > clinical change, when repeat testing is due to technical or site
    > issues, or when multiple providers order similar testing over a
    > short period.

MEDICAL NECESSITY: BASIC SCREEN

-   Confirm the request is for standard CRT, not wireless CRT. Wireless
    > CRT is not medically necessary in any scenario.

    > Confirm heart failure evaluation is complete:

    -   History and physical completed

        > Relevant labs/testing reviewed

        > Prior treatment response reviewed

        > Requested service is expected to provide net clinical benefit
        > and improve management/outcome

    > Confirm reversible/correctable causes of heart failure have been
    > addressed, such as ischemia or tachycardia-mediated
    > cardiomyopathy.

    > Confirm the patient has had an adequate trial of
    > guideline-directed medical therapy, defined as:

    -   3 months after diagnosis, or

        > 40 days after the most recent myocardial infarction

    > Confirm expected survival with meaningful quality of life is more
    > than 1 year.

APPROVAL CHECKLIST: INITIAL CRT FOR SINUS RHYTHM Approve when ALL are
met:

-   Sinus rhythm

    > LVEF 35% or less

    > QRS:

    -   150 ms or more, any morphology, or

        > 130 to 149 ms with LBBB morphology

    > NYHA class II, III, or ambulatory IV symptoms

    > Correctable causes addressed

    > Adequate GDMT trial completed

APPROVAL CHECKLIST: INITIAL CRT FOR ATRIAL FIBRILLATION Approve when ALL
are met:

-   Atrial fibrillation

    > LVEF 35% or less

    > QRS 130 ms or more

    -   Post-AV node ablation paced QRS 130 ms or more also qualifies

    > NYHA class III or ambulatory IV symptoms

    > Plan to ensure at least 90% biventricular capture

    -   Rate control strategy, or

        > Planned AV node ablation, or

        > Expected restoration of sinus rhythm

    > Correctable causes addressed

    > Adequate GDMT trial completed

APPROVAL CHECKLIST: CRT WHEN HIGH VENTRICULAR PACING IS EXPECTED Approve
when ALL are met:

-   Sinus rhythm or atrial fibrillation

    > Meets criteria for permanent pacemaker implantation

    > LVEF less than 50%

    > NYHA class I to III

    > Expected high ventricular pacing burden, close to 100%

    > Correctable causes addressed

APPROVAL CHECKLIST: CRT-P OR CRT-D REPLACEMENT Approve when EITHER is
met:

-   Generator end-of-life criteria are present, or

    > Generator pocket must be opened for another reason, such as lead
    > revision, and device is within 3 years of end-of-life

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION TO SUBMIT Include documentation that accurately
reflects the current clinical status and the ordering provider's intent.
A practical submission should include:

-   Office note/history and physical

    > Heart failure diagnosis and current symptoms

    > NYHA functional class

    > Rhythm documentation:

    -   Sinus rhythm or atrial fibrillation

        > ECG with QRS duration

        > LBBB morphology, if applicable

    > Most recent LVEF measurement

    > Guideline-directed medical therapy history:

    -   Medications tried/currently used

        > Duration of therapy

        > Contraindications/intolerance, if any

    > Documentation that reversible causes were evaluated and addressed

    > For atrial fibrillation requests:

    -   Plan for at least 90% biventricular capture

        > Rate control strategy and/or AV node ablation plan

        > Or expectation that sinus rhythm will be restored

    > For expected pacing burden requests:

    -   Permanent pacemaker indication

        > Expected ventricular pacing burden

    > For replacement requests:

    -   Generator end-of-life evidence, or

        > Reason pocket must be reopened and timing relative to
        > end-of-life

LIMITATIONS / EXCLUSIONS

-   Wireless CRT is not medically necessary in all scenarios.

    > Requests for multiple diagnostic or therapeutic interventions at
    > the same time may require peer-to-peer review.

    > Repeat testing may receive additional review if:

    -   Same test is repeated due to technical issues

        > Same test is requested at another facility for
        > preference/quality concerns

        > Persistent symptoms without clinical change or intervention

        > Different providers order repeated testing over a short
        > interval

    > Repeat therapeutic intervention in the same anatomic area is
    > generally appropriate only when prior benefit has lapsed; early
    > repeat treatment is not appropriate unless the prior intervention
    > was never actually performed.

    > Nonspecific or unlisted procedure codes may require extra
    > documentation/review. Code list is not all-inclusive and
    > plan-specific requirements may vary.

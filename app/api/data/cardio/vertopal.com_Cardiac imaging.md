MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

1.  Coronary artery calcium testing

-   Considered medically necessary only to help with management
    > decisions for hypercholesterolemia when all of the following
    > apply:

    -   No known atheromatous vascular disease

        > Not diabetic

        > Age 40 to 75

        > LDL 70 to 190 mg/dL

        > 10-year ASCVD risk 5% to 20%

        > None of the following risk-enhancing factors:

        -   Family history of premature ASCVD

            > LDL 160 mg/dL or higher

            > Triglycerides above 175 mg/dL

            > Metabolic syndrome

            > Chronic kidney disease

            > Chronic inflammatory condition

            > Menopause before age 40

            > History of preeclampsia

            > High-risk race/ethnicity such as South Asian ancestry

            > Elevated hs-CRP

            > Elevated lipoprotein(a)

            > ApoB above 130 mg/dL

            > Ankle-brachial index below 0.9

2\. CCTA

-   Suspected CAD in symptomatic patients not evaluated within prior 60
    > days:

    -   Chest pain or other ischemic symptoms with pretest probability
        > above 15%

        > Dyspnea without chest pain with pretest probability above 15%

        > Cardiac symptoms plus conditions commonly associated with CAD:

        -   Abdominal aortic aneurysm

            > Symptomatic peripheral vascular disease

            > Prior stroke/TIA/CEA or carotid stenosis greater than 70%

            > Chronic kidney disease

    > Established flow-limiting CAD with new or worsening symptoms:

    -   Persistent symptoms despite maximal anti-ischemic therapy or
        > contraindication to it

    > Established or suspected CAD:

    -   Post-cardiac transplant with new/worsening symptoms or exam
        > findings

        > Stable transplant patients without CAD evaluation in prior
        > year

        > New arrhythmias without CAD evaluation since onset:

        -   Sustained or nonsustained VT

            > Atrial fibrillation/flutter with high or intermediate CAD
            > risk

            > Atrial fibrillation/flutter with established CAD

            > Frequent PVCs \>30/hour

        > New CHF or newly recognized LV systolic dysfunction without
        > CAD evaluation since onset

        > New abnormal resting EKG:

        -   LBBB

            > ST depression 1 mm or greater

            > LVH with repolarization abnormality

        > Resting EKG findings making exercise ECG uninterpretable:

        -   LBBB

            > Ventricular paced rhythm

            > LVH with repolarization abnormality

            > Digoxin effect

            > ST depression 1 mm or more

            > Pre-excitation/WPW

        > Abnormal or inconclusive treadmill test without imaging since
        > test

        > Preoperative non-emergency non-cardiac surgery when all apply:

        -   At least 2 of: age 75+, heart disease history,
            > angina/dyspnea, hemoglobin under 12, vascular surgery

            > Poor or unknown functional capacity

            > Elevated-risk surgery

        > Solid organ transplant evaluation:

        -   Asymptomatic without CAD evaluation in prior year

            > Symptoms consistent with ischemia

    > Miscellaneous CCTA indications:

    -   Unable to perform exercise EKG for non-obesity reasons

        > Kawasaki disease surveillance:

        -   Up to 1 year after diagnosis if prior imaging showed
            > coronary abnormalities, LV dysfunction, pericardial
            > effusion, more than trivial valvular regurgitation, or
            > aortic dilation

            > Annual if small/medium aneurysms

            > Every 6 months if large/giant aneurysms or coronary
            > obstruction

        > Suspected congenital coronary anomaly with:

        -   Exertional syncope

            > First-degree relative with anomalous coronary artery

            > Prior angiography unable to define origin/course

            > Abnormal coronary ostia position on echo

3\. FFR-CT

-   Retrieved criteria show FFR-CT is medically necessary when all
    > retrieved conditions are met:

    -   Symptoms consistent with myocardial ischemia

        > Symptoms persist despite maximal guideline-directed medical
        > therapy

        > CCTA performed within the preceding 90 days

        > Retrieved text also indicates an anatomic stenosis threshold
        > on prior CCTA, but the full criterion was truncated in the
        > search result.

4\. Stress cardiac MRI

-   Appropriate as initial stress imaging when pharmacologic stress is
    > preferable.

    > Suspected CAD in symptomatic patients not evaluated within prior
    > 60 days:

    -   Chest pain/ischemic symptoms with pretest probability above 15%

        > Dyspnea predominance with pretest probability above 15%

        > Cardiac symptoms plus AAA, symptomatic PVD, prior
        > stroke/TIA/CEA/high-grade carotid stenosis, or CKD

    > Established flow-limiting CAD in asymptomatic patients:

    -   More than 5 years after CABG and no CAD evaluation within 2
        > years

        > Incomplete revascularization: 3 years after procedure and then
        > every 2 years

        > Acute coronary syndrome within prior 90 days without
        > angiography at the acute event and no evaluation since

        > Viability assessment for revascularization candidates with LV
        > systolic dysfunction, EF under 55%

    > Established CAD with new/worsening symptoms:

    -   Persistent symptoms despite maximal anti-ischemic therapy or
        > contraindication

        > Viability assessment in revascularization candidates with LV
        > EF under 55%

    > Established or suspected CAD:

    -   Post-transplant patients with new/worsening symptoms or exam
        > findings

        > Stable transplant patients without CAD evaluation in prior
        > year

        > New arrhythmias:

        -   VT

            > Atrial fibrillation/flutter with high/intermediate CAD
            > risk

            > Atrial fibrillation/flutter with established CAD

            > Frequent PVCs \>30/hour

        > New CHF or newly recognized LV systolic dysfunction without
        > CAD evaluation since onset

    > Miscellaneous stress MRI indications:

    -   Inability to perform exercise treadmill testing

        > Kawasaki disease:

        -   Surveillance up to 1 year if prior imaging showed coronary
            > abnormalities, LV dysfunction, pericardial effusion, more
            > than trivial valvular regurgitation, or aortic dilation

            > Annual if small/medium coronary aneurysms

            > Every 6 months if large/giant aneurysms or obstruction

        > Prior to Interleukin-2 initiation

    > Resting cardiac MRI indications retrieved:

    -   Myocarditis:

        -   Symptoms such as chest pain, dyspnea, or palpitations

            > Recent negative CAD evaluation or low pretest CAD
            > probability

            > Evidence of myocardial injury:

            -   Elevated troponin

                > Ventricular dysfunction

                > EKG changes such as VT/VF, new Q waves, LBBB, or AV
                > block

            > Follow-up LV function assessment in established
            > myocarditis when TTE is technically suboptimal

        > Cardiomyopathy:

        -   Discordant or technically suboptimal prior studies

            > Annual surveillance in stable patients with chronic
            > progressive non-CAD disease predisposing to cardiomyopathy
            > and no recent non-MRI imaging evidence

            > Annual iron quantification in chronic
            > transfusion-dependent disease

            > Suspected ARVD with severe RV dysfunction, precordial
            > T-wave inversion without RBBB, first-degree relative with
            > ARVD or sudden death under age 35, or VT/frequent PVCs

            > Fabry disease evaluation/management

        > Cardiac aneurysm/pseudoaneurysm evaluation

        > Congenital heart disease:

        -   Congenital coronary anomalies under specified scenarios

            > New complex congenital heart disease suggested on echo

            > Less than 1 year post-surgical correction

            > New/worsening symptoms or exam change

            > Surgical planning

            > Annual surveillance if asymptomatic and no MRI/CT within
            > prior year

        > Valvular heart disease:

        -   Inconclusive echo or echo not feasible

            > Moderate/severe disease needing better definition to
            > affect management

            > Assess regurgitant volume/fraction, EF, ventricular
            > volumes, or surgical timing

        > Suspected or established intracardiac/paracardiac masses
        > suggested on other imaging and no MRI/CT in prior 60 days

5\. PET perfusion and metabolic PET

-   PET perfusion is appropriate as the initial noninvasive stress
    > imaging test when either applies:

    -   At least one relative contraindication to conventional nuclear
        > imaging

        > Pharmacologic testing is preferable

    > Relative contraindications to conventional nuclear imaging:

    -   Morbid obesity, BMI 40 or greater

        > Breast implants

        > Prior suboptimal conventional nuclear perfusion imaging due to
        > attenuation artifact

        > Prior conventional nuclear imaging discordant with angiography

        > Known pericardial or pleural effusion

        > Prior mastectomy

        > Chest wall deformity

    > Situations where pharmacologic testing is preferable:

    -   Resting EKG abnormalities:

        -   Complete LBBB

            > Ventricular paced rhythm

            > ST depression 1 mm or greater

            > LVH with repolarization abnormalities

            > Digoxin effect

            > Pre-excitation/WPW

            > Prior false-positive EKG stress test

        > Exercise-limiting conditions:

        -   Orthopedic or neurologic impairment

            > Severe COPD

            > Severe heart failure

            > Severe claudication

            > Prior failure to achieve target HR

            > Negative chronotropic medication that cannot be held

        > Severe valvular stenosis

        > Presence of ICD

    > Suspected CAD in symptomatic patients not evaluated within prior
    > 60 days:

    -   Chest pain/ischemic symptoms with pretest probability above 15%

        > Dyspnea without chest pain with pretest probability above 15%

        > Cardiac symptoms plus AAA, symptomatic PVD, prior
        > stroke/TIA/CEA/high-grade carotid stenosis, or CKD

    > Established flow-limiting CAD in asymptomatic patients:

    -   More than 5 years after CABG and no CAD evaluation within 2
        > years

        > Incomplete revascularization: 3 years after procedure and then
        > every 2 years

        > Acute coronary syndrome within prior 90 days without
        > angiography at event and no evaluation since

        > Viability assessment for revascularization candidates with LV
        > EF under 55%

    > Established CAD with new/worsening symptoms:

    -   Symptoms persist despite maximal anti-ischemic therapy or
        > contraindication

        > Viability assessment for revascularization candidates with LV
        > EF under 55%

    > Established or suspected CAD:

    -   Post-transplant patients with new/worsening symptoms or exam
        > findings

        > Stable transplant patients without CAD evaluation in prior
        > year

        > New arrhythmias:

        -   VT

            > AF/flutter with high or intermediate CAD risk

            > AF/flutter with established CAD

            > Frequent PVCs \>30/hour

        > New CHF or newly recognized LV systolic dysfunction without
        > CAD evaluation since onset

        > Newly recognized abnormal resting EKG

        > Resting EKG abnormalities that make exercise ECG
        > uninterpretable

        > Abnormal or inconclusive exercise treadmill test

        > Prior stress imaging within 60 days only if the prior imaging
        > was technically suboptimal, limited, inconclusive,
        > indeterminate, or equivocal and ischemia could not be excluded

    > Preoperative non-emergency non-cardiac surgery:

    -   All of A-C must apply:

        -   At least 2 of age 75+, heart disease history,
            > angina/dyspnea, hemoglobin under 12, vascular surgery

            > Poor or unknown functional capacity

            > Elevated-risk surgery

        > Also includes solid organ transplant surveillance/evaluation
        > as specified

    > Miscellaneous PET perfusion indications:

    -   Inability to perform exercise EKG for non-obesity reasons

        > Kawasaki disease with coronary involvement:

        -   Every 2 years for small/medium aneurysm

            > Annual for giant/multiple/complex aneurysm or coronary
            > obstruction

        > Prior to Interleukin-2 initiation

    > PET perfusion with metabolic PET:

    -   Suspected or established cardiac sarcoidosis

    > Metabolic PET:

    -   Myocardial viability when all apply:

        -   Established CAD

            > LV systolic dysfunction

            > Viability not defined by other testing

            > Revascularization being considered

        > Cardiac sarcoidosis, with or without perfusion imaging

    > Miscellaneous indications:

    -   Inability to perform exercise EKG for non-obesity reasons

7\. Cardiac blood pool imaging / MUGA / first-pass radionuclide
ventriculography

-   LV function indications include:

    -   Initial evaluation of known/suspected heart failure

        > Reevaluation of known LV dysfunction with deterioration

        > Resting EKG abnormalities

        > Reevaluation of known heart failure with change in status

        > Cardiotoxic therapy monitoring:

        -   Baseline before treatment

            > Serial during treatment or within 6 months after
            > completion

            > Annual surveillance after that

        > Screening every 2 years in stable first-degree relatives of
        > inherited cardiomyopathy

        > Suspected restrictive, infiltrative, or genetic cardiomyopathy

        > Suspected or diagnosed myocarditis

        > Evaluation before CRT/ICD/VAD

        > CRT optimization after implantation

        > Persistent/new HF symptoms during CRT for optimization

        > VAD setting optimization or complication evaluation

        > Pre- and post-cardiac transplant evaluation with defined
        > surveillance intervals

    > RV function indications:

    -   Suspected RV dysfunction

        > Established RV dysfunction with clinical change

        > Pulmonary hypertension

        > Conditions known to cause RV dysfunction

        > MI with suspected RV involvement

        > Cardiac or lung transplant evaluation/follow-up

    > CAD indications:

    -   Initial LV assessment after recent ACS

        > Reevaluation during recovery phase up to 6 months

        > Reevaluation later if new heart-failure-type symptoms

        > Prior MI when considering ICD or CRT

    > Congenital heart disease:

    -   Detection/localization of shunts

        > Ventricular function assessment in established complex
        > congenital heart disease

    > Valvular heart disease:

    -   Established valvular disease with new/worsening signs or
        > symptoms

        > Severe asymptomatic aortic regurgitation to help time surgery

8\. Resting transthoracic echocardiography

-   Retrieved indications include:

    -   Suspected endocarditis with positive blood cultures and/or new
        > murmur

        > Reevaluation of established endocarditis with virulent
        > organism, severe hemodynamic lesion, aortic involvement,
        > persistent bacteremia, or clinical deterioration

        > Suspected CAD:

        -   Chest pain when echo may identify a nonischemic cause

            > Suspected aberrant/anomalous coronary origins or coronary
            > fistula

        > Known CAD:

        -   Recent ACS with hemodynamic instability or suspected
            > complication

            > Recent ACS for initial LV function assessment

            > Reassessment during recovery phase

            > Reassessment after recovery phase if new HF signs/symptoms

            > Prior MI when considering ICD or CRT

        > Kawasaki disease:

        -   Suspected disease

            > Follow-up at 2 to 4 weeks and 6 to 8 weeks after diagnosis

            > Surveillance up to 1 year if persistent fever or abnormal
            > prior echo

            > Annual if small/medium aneurysms

            > Every 6 months if large/giant aneurysm or obstruction

        > New symptoms such as dyspnea, syncope, reduced functional
        > capacity, orthopnea, PND, TIA/stroke,
        > lightheadedness/presyncope

9\. Infarct imaging

-   Considered medically necessary for:

    -   Suspected acute MI likely within the last 7 days, especially
        > when there are negative past-peak enzymes, abnormal baseline
        > ECG from prior MI, or LBBB

        > Differentiating subendocardial infarction from ischemia

        > Post-cardioversion

        > Chest pain after significant chest trauma or major surgery

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

General

-   Genetic tests not specifically addressed are considered not
    > medically necessary.

    > Simultaneous ordering of multiple tests may trigger peer-to-peer
    > review.

    > Repeated testing of the same anatomic area for the same indication
    > is generally limited to post-intervention follow-up or a
    > meaningful change in status.

    > Nonspecific or unlisted procedure codes may need extra
    > documentation and review.

Coronary calcium CT

-   Not covered by most insurers when used as a screening study.

    > Does not apply to CCTA or cardiac CT structure/morphology.

Cardiac CT / CCTA

-   Acute coronary syndrome workups done in ER/inpatient settings are
    > outside this elective preauthorization guideline.

    > Limitations include:

    -   Radiation exposure

        > Need for iodinated contrast

        > Lower image quality in morbid obesity, heavy coronary
        > calcification, or small stents

        > Frequent need for beta blockers

        > Potential difficulty in claustrophobic patients

    > Safety concerns include prior contrast allergy/atopy, renal
    > impairment, and cumulative radiation exposure.

    > CCTA is not clinically indicated for infrequent premature atrial
    > or ventricular depolarizations.

Cardiac MRI

-   ER/inpatient acute coronary syndrome evaluation is outside this
    > elective guideline.

    > Potential limitations/exclusions:

    -   Irregular rhythms can reduce image quality

        > MRI safety issues with noncompatible pacemakers, ICDs,
        > aneurysm clips, and other unsafe implanted/external devices

    > Cardiac MRI is not clinically indicated for infrequent premature
    > atrial or ventricular depolarizations.

    > For valvular disease, MRI is generally not first-line and is
    > rarely appropriate in asymptomatic patients unless further
    > definition will change management.

PET

-   Contraindications include:

    -   Pregnancy or breastfeeding

        > Inability to remain motionless for several minutes or follow
        > simple instructions

    > PET is intended mainly when conventional nuclear imaging is
    > relatively contraindicated or pharmacologic testing is preferred.

    > Perfusion PET is not clinically indicated for infrequent premature
    > atrial or ventricular depolarizations.

    > Perfusion PET is not appropriate after a recent clearly normal or
    > clearly abnormal stress imaging study; it is reserved when the
    > prior study was technically inadequate or equivocal.

Blood pool imaging

-   If LV function was recently evaluated by echocardiography, repeating
    > assessment with blood pool imaging is generally not necessary.

    > Certain medications and disease states can interfere with red
    > blood cell labeling.

Resting echocardiography

-   No role for screening asymptomatic patients for CAD.

    > If CAD is the likely diagnosis and echo cannot be done during
    > chest pain, provocative testing is preferred.

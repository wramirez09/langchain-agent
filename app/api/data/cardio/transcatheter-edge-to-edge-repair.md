MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

Transcatheter edge-to-edge repair (TEER) uses a catheter-delivered clip or approximation device to mechanically coapt the mitral or tricuspid leaflets, reducing regurgitation without open-heart surgery. Commercial payers evaluate TEER under two distinct clinical pathways — primary (degenerative) mitral regurgitation and secondary (functional) mitral regurgitation — each with separate anatomic and hemodynamic thresholds derived from the EVEREST II and COAPT trial evidence bases. Tricuspid TEER is a separate, emerging indication subject to more restricted coverage.

TEER is considered medically necessary when the criteria for the appropriate indication pathway below are met, the procedure is performed at a qualified structural heart disease center with a multidisciplinary heart team, and the anticipated benefit outweighs procedural risk as documented by heart team consensus.

INDICATIONS

Primary (Degenerative) Mitral Regurgitation

    TEER for primary (degenerative) MR is considered medically necessary when ALL of the following are met:

    -   Symptomatic severe primary MR, defined as grade 3+ or 4+ (moderate-severe or severe) by quantitative or semi-quantitative echocardiographic criteria (e.g., EROA 0.40 cm² or greater for primary MR, or vena contracta 0.7 cm or greater), confirmed on TTE and/or TEE.
    -   Symptoms attributable to MR, corresponding to NYHA functional class II, III, or IV despite optimal medical management; or evidence of LV dysfunction (LVEF less than 60% or LVESD greater than 40 mm) even if minimally symptomatic.
    -   Surgical risk is deemed prohibitive or high by the multidisciplinary heart team:
        -   Prohibitive surgical risk: STS predicted risk of mortality 8% or greater, or heart team consensus that co-morbidities preclude surgery regardless of STS score (e.g., frailty, hostile chest, severe COPD, severe liver disease, severe pulmonary hypertension, or other factors conferring unacceptably high operative mortality).
        -   High surgical risk: STS predicted risk of mortality 6% or greater, or heart team agreement that individual patient factors substantially elevate operative risk above the STS estimate.
    -   Mitral valve anatomy is suitable for TEER, as confirmed by TEE and/or CT, including ALL of the following:
        -   Primary jet origin at or near the A2-P2 segments (central or paracentral lesion); commissural jets may be considered case-by-case with heart team review.
        -   Mitral valve area (MVA) sufficient to accommodate the clip without inducing clinically significant iatrogenic stenosis (MVA greater than 4.0 cm² prior to procedure as a general threshold; lower values require individualized heart team assessment).
        -   Adequate leaflet tissue for grasping: coaptation length 2 mm or greater or flail gap less than 10 mm and flail width less than 15 mm.
        -   Absence of severe leaflet or annular calcification in the grasping zone.
        -   Absence of cleft or perforation in the grasping zone.
        -   Absence of rheumatic disease causing leaflet thickening or restricted mobility (Carpentier type IIIa) that would preclude effective grasping.
    -   The patient has acceptable vascular access and anatomy suitable for transseptal catheterization.

Secondary (Functional) Mitral Regurgitation

    TEER for secondary (functional) MR is considered medically necessary when ALL of the following are met:

    -   Symptomatic moderate-severe or severe secondary MR (grade 3+ or 4+) persisting despite at least 3 months of maximally tolerated guideline-directed medical therapy (GDMT) for heart failure, including:
        -   ACE inhibitor or ARB or ARNI at target or maximally tolerated dose.
        -   Beta-blocker at target or maximally tolerated dose.
        -   Mineralocorticoid receptor antagonist when indicated and tolerated.
        -   Cardiac resynchronization therapy (CRT) if indicated by current heart failure guidelines and not already optimized; CRT optimization should precede TEER consideration when applicable.
        -   Diuretic therapy appropriately titrated for volume management.
    -   Left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF) within the range of 20% to 50% (inclusive); TEER benefit in secondary MR has not been established outside this LVEF range.
    -   Left ventricular end-systolic dimension (LVESD) 70 mm or less; severely enlarged ventricles beyond this threshold are associated with diminished procedural benefit.
    -   NYHA functional class II, III, or ambulatory class IV symptoms attributable to heart failure and MR despite GDMT.
    -   Absence of primary structural leaflet pathology as the predominant mechanism of MR.
    -   Echocardiographic criteria consistent with secondary (functional) mechanism: annular dilation, tethering, or restricted leaflet motion (Carpentier type I or IIIb) rather than degenerative prolapse or flail.
    -   Mitral valve anatomy suitable for TEER confirmed on TEE:
        -   Coaptation depth less than 11 mm.
        -   Coaptation length 2 mm or greater.
        -   MVA greater than 4.0 cm² prior to the procedure as a general threshold.
        -   Absence of severe leaflet or annular calcification in the grasping zone.
    -   Multidisciplinary heart team evaluation with documented consensus that TEER is the preferred treatment strategy, including assessment of surgical suitability.
    -   Absence of indication for coronary revascularization that would independently address MR (i.e., ischemia-driven MR that would be resolved by revascularization should be addressed by revascularization first).

Tricuspid TEER

    TEER for symptomatic tricuspid regurgitation is considered medically necessary when ALL of the following are met:

    -   Symptomatic severe or greater tricuspid regurgitation (TR grade 3+ or 4+) confirmed by echocardiography.
    -   Symptoms attributable to TR, NYHA functional class III or IV, despite guideline-directed medical therapy including optimized diuretic therapy.
    -   Surgical risk is prohibitive or high as determined by the multidisciplinary heart team (STS mortality 8% or greater or heart team consensus of prohibitive operative risk due to co-morbidities).
    -   Tricuspid valve anatomy suitable for TEER on TEE:
        -   TR jet arising from a central or para-central coaptation gap accessible to clip placement.
        -   Leaflet tissue adequate for grasping without severe calcification or perforation in the target zone.
        -   Tricuspid annular diameter and coaptation gap within device-specific IFU parameters.
        -   Absence of severe right ventricular dysfunction expected to preclude clinical benefit (TAPSE less than 14 mm or equivalent severe RV dysfunction is a relative contraindication requiring individualized heart team review).
    -   The procedure is performed at a center with demonstrated structural heart disease experience and tricuspid TEER procedural volume meeting payer or CMS threshold requirements where applicable.
    -   FDA device clearance or approval applicable to the tricuspid indication; coverage may be contingent on the specific device's regulatory status at the time of authorization request.

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

Heart Team Evaluation

    -   Written multidisciplinary heart team note documenting:
        -   Participation of at minimum a cardiac surgeon and an interventional cardiologist (or structural heart specialist) with structural heart disease expertise.
        -   Consensus conclusion that TEER is the preferred treatment, including explicit assessment of surgical candidacy and the rationale for non-surgical management (e.g., surgical risk determination with supporting data, patient preference after informed discussion, anatomic suitability).
        -   For secondary MR: documentation that GDMT has been optimized and a statement regarding CRT status and/or revascularization status.
        -   STS operative risk score or equivalent validated risk score, with the input variables used; if heart team overrides the STS estimate, a narrative explanation is required.

Echocardiographic Documentation

    -   Transthoracic echocardiogram (TTE) performed within 6 months of the authorization request documenting:
        -   MR severity graded by integrated criteria (vena contracta, EROA by PISA, regurgitant volume, regurgitant fraction, pulmonary vein flow, MV morphology).
        -   LVEF and LVESD measurements.
        -   LV volumes and dimensions.
        -   Pulmonary artery systolic pressure estimate.
        -   Right ventricular size and function.
        -   Mitral valve morphology including leaflet mobility, calcification, and coaptation assessment.
    -   Transesophageal echocardiogram (TEE) performed within 6 months of the authorization request documenting:
        -   Detailed 2D and 3D mitral valve anatomy including segmental analysis (A1-3, P1-3), coaptation length, coaptation depth (for functional MR), flail gap and flail width (for degenerative MR), presence and location of calcification.
        -   Confirmation of anatomic suitability for TEER and absence of exclusionary anatomic features.
        -   Estimated mitral valve area.
        -   For tricuspid TEER: detailed tricuspid leaflet anatomy, coaptation assessment, and RV function.
    -   If 3D TEE was performed, those images and measurements should be included.

Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy Documentation (Secondary MR)

    -   Medication reconciliation confirming current GDMT regimen with doses and duration.
    -   Documentation of therapeutic trials, dose escalations, and any documented contraindications or intolerances to each GDMT component that was not achieved at target dose.
    -   Cardiology or heart failure specialist notes spanning at least 3 months of GDMT optimization, demonstrating persistent symptomatic MR and heart failure symptoms despite treatment.
    -   CRT status documentation: if CRT is indicated and has been implanted, documentation of device optimization; if CRT is indicated but not implanted, rationale for why CRT was not pursued prior to TEER.
    -   Coronary artery disease status and revascularization history if applicable.

Functional Status and Symptom Documentation

    -   NYHA functional class assessment documented by the treating clinician within 3 months of the authorization request.
    -   Clinical notes reflecting symptoms attributable to MR or heart failure (dyspnea, exercise intolerance, orthopnea, paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea, lower extremity edema) and their functional impact.
    -   For ambulatory class IV: documentation of outpatient status and absence of refractory end-stage heart failure for which TEER would not be expected to provide benefit.

Procedural Planning

    -   Confirmation of FDA approval or clearance for the specific device and indication being requested.
    -   Vascular access assessment (femoral venous anatomy, interatrial septum anatomy for transseptal approach).
    -   For tricuspid TEER: documentation of center volume and operator experience meeting applicable threshold criteria.

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

Anatomic Exclusions — Mitral

    -   MVA less than 4.0 cm² prior to the procedure (risk of iatrogenic mitral stenosis); lower thresholds require documented heart team discussion of individualized benefit-risk.
    -   Severe or extensive leaflet or annular calcification in the intended grasping zone that would prevent effective clip deployment or leaflet grasping.
    -   Rheumatic mitral valve disease with significant leaflet thickening or restricted motion (Carpentier type IIIa) as the primary mechanism, unless heart team determines anatomy is acceptable on detailed TEE.
    -   Cleft or perforation in the grasping zone.
    -   Commissural MR as the predominant mechanism is a relative contraindication; central or paracentral jet location is required for standard device use, though highly experienced centers may consider commissural cases under specific protocols.
    -   Presence of intracardiac thrombus, particularly left atrial or left atrial appendage thrombus; anticoagulation and thrombus resolution must be documented prior to TEER.
    -   Presence of a pre-existing mechanical or bioprosthetic mitral valve.

Contraindications — General

    -   Active endocarditis or structural valve disease attributable to active infection.
    -   Presence of active systemic infection.
    -   Intracardiac thrombus not resolved with anticoagulation.
    -   Contraindication to anticoagulation required for transseptal catheterization and periprocedural management.
    -   Severe right ventricular failure or severe pulmonary arterial hypertension at a level that would render the procedure futile or excessively risky, as determined by heart team review.
    -   Cardiogenic shock or hemodynamic instability at the time of the procedure (emergent conditions not applicable to elective PA pathway).

Non-Covered or Excluded Scenarios

    -   Primary MR in patients who are average or low surgical risk and for whom mitral valve surgery (repair or replacement) is the preferred and feasible treatment option per the heart team; TEER is not a default alternative to surgery in good surgical candidates.
    -   Secondary MR with LVEF below 20% or LVESD above 70 mm, outside the range supported by COAPT enrollment criteria, unless exceptional clinical circumstances are documented with heart team consensus.
    -   Secondary MR with LVEF above 50%; regurgitation in this range is more consistent with primary MR pathophysiology and should be evaluated under the primary MR pathway.
    -   MR attributable to a reversible cause (e.g., acute ischemic MR in the setting of an acute MI amenable to revascularization) without prior revascularization attempt.
    -   TEER performed as a bridge to heart transplantation or LVAD implantation is not separately covered unless clinical evidence supports independent benefit.
    -   Repeat TEER solely for device failure without documentation of persistent severe symptomatic MR, anatomic suitability for re-intervention, and heart team consensus.
    -   Tricuspid TEER using a device without applicable FDA clearance or approval for the tricuspid indication at the time of service.
    -   TEER performed at a facility that does not meet structural heart disease program requirements, including the requirement for on-site cardiac surgery.

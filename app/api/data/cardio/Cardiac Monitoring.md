Medical Necessity Criteria

-   General clinical appropriateness framework:

    -   Before any intervention, the clinician should confirm the
        > diagnosis or establish pretest likelihood based on:

        -   Complete history

            > Physical examination

            > Relevant laboratory studies, when applicable

            > Relevant diagnostic testing, when applicable

            > Response to prior therapeutic intervention, when
            > applicable

        > The anticipated benefit of the intervention should outweigh
        > potential harms.

        > Current literature, treatment guidelines, and standards of
        > medical practice should support the intervention.

        > There should be a reasonable likelihood that the intervention
        > will change management or improve outcomes.

    > Implantable recording device:

    -   Considered medically necessary for detection of atrial
        > fibrillation/flutter following cryptogenic stroke when ALL of
        > the following apply:

        -   No prior history of atrial fibrillation/flutter

            > External ambulatory monitoring performed for at least 14
            > days and non-diagnostic

            > No contraindication to anticoagulation

            > No existing indication for anticoagulation, such as
            > prosthetic valve

            > No implanted device capable of monitoring for atrial
            > fibrillation/flutter, including:

            -   Pacemaker

                > Cardiac resynchronization therapy (CRT) device

                > Implantable cardioverter defibrillator (ICD)

        > Considered medically necessary for evaluation of recurrent
        > syncope when ALL of the following apply:

        -   Cause of syncope remains unclear despite:

            -   History

                > Physical examination, including orthostatic blood
                > pressure measurements

                > Electrocardiogram

                > Echocardiogram

            > Ambulatory monitoring performed for at least 14 successive
            > days and non-diagnostic

            > Syncopal events occur less frequently than every 30 days

            > No implanted device capable of monitoring for arrhythmia,
            > including:

            -   Pacemaker

                > CRT device

                > ICD

        > Considered medically necessary following successful pulmonary
        > vein isolation for atrial fibrillation when BOTH of the
        > following apply:

        -   Syncope due to post-conversion pause prior to ablation

            > No permanent pacemaker

    > Mobile cardiac telemetry:

    -   Considered medically necessary for evaluation of symptoms
        > suggestive of arrhythmia when ALL of the following apply:

        -   Symptoms occur less frequently than every 48 hours

            > Ambulatory event monitoring performed for at least 14 days
            > and non-diagnostic

            > No implanted device capable of monitoring for arrhythmia,
            > including:

            -   Pacemaker

                > CRT device

                > ICD

        > Considered medically necessary for detection of atrial
        > fibrillation following cryptogenic stroke when ALL of the
        > following apply:

        -   No prior history of atrial fibrillation/flutter

            > Ambulatory event monitoring performed for at least 14 days
            > and non-diagnostic

            > No contraindication to anticoagulation

            > No existing indication for anticoagulation, such as
            > prosthetic valve

            > No implanted device capable of monitoring for atrial
            > fibrillation/flutter, including:

            -   Pacemaker

                > CRT device

                > ICD

Required Documentation

-   Clinical documentation may be required to support the request.

    > Submitted documentation must:

    -   Accurately reflect the clinical situation at the time of the
        > requested service

        > Sufficiently document the ordering provider's clinical intent

    > Based on the specific criteria in this policy, relevant supporting
    > documentation would include:

    -   History and physical examination

        > Orthostatic blood pressure measurements, when evaluating
        > syncope

        > Electrocardiogram results

        > Echocardiogram results

        > Results of prior ambulatory monitoring

        > Duration of prior monitoring showing at least 14 days, when
        > required

        > Evidence that prior monitoring was non-diagnostic

        > Symptom frequency documentation

        > Confirmation of cryptogenic stroke evaluation, when applicable

        > Confirmation of no prior atrial fibrillation/flutter history,
        > when applicable

        > Confirmation of no contraindication to anticoagulation, when
        > applicable

        > Confirmation of no existing indication for anticoagulation,
        > when applicable

        > Confirmation that the patient does not already have a
        > pacemaker, CRT device, or ICD capable of monitoring arrhythmia

        > Documentation of prior pulmonary vein isolation and
        > pre-ablation syncope due to post-conversion pause, when
        > applicable

        > Confirmation that no permanent pacemaker is present, when
        > applicable

Limitations and Exclusions

-   Scope limitation:

    -   This guideline applies only to:

        -   Implantable recording devices

            > Mobile cardiac outpatient telemetry

        > In the outpatient ambulatory setting

        > It does not address all forms of ambulatory rhythm monitoring.

    > Device-related limitations:

    -   Holter monitors are not suitable for symptoms occurring less
        > frequently than every 48 hours.

        > Cardiac telemetry is generally reserved for patients with
        > non-diagnostic ambulatory event monitoring.

        > Implantable recorders have the most restrictive indications.

    > Exclusions embedded in criteria:

    -   Not medically necessary under the listed criteria if the patient
        > already has an implanted pacemaker, CRT device, or ICD capable
        > of monitoring the relevant arrhythmia.

        > Not medically necessary for post-cryptogenic stroke atrial
        > fibrillation detection if there is:

        -   Prior history of atrial fibrillation/flutter

            > Contraindication to anticoagulation

            > Existing indication for anticoagulation

        > Not medically necessary for recurrent syncope if the syncopal
        > cause is already evident from standard evaluation.

        > Not medically necessary for recurrent syncope if episodes
        > occur more frequently than every 30 days under the implantable
        > device criteria.

        > Not medically necessary for mobile telemetry if symptoms occur
        > every 48 hours or more often.

        > Not medically necessary if required prior ambulatory/event
        > monitoring of at least 14 days has not been performed or was
        > not non-diagnostic.

    > Repeat testing limitations:

    -   Repeated testing of the same anatomic location for the same
        > indication should generally be limited to:

        -   Evaluation following an intervention

            > A change in clinical status requiring additional testing

            > Clarification of an original study using different
            > techniques or protocols

        > Repeated diagnostic testing may be subject to additional
        > review or peer-to-peer discussion when:

        -   Repeated at the same facility due to technical issues

            > Repeated at a different facility due to provider
            > preference or quality concerns

            > Requested for persistent symptoms without clinical change,
            > treatment, or intervention since the prior study

            > Ordered by different providers for the same member over a
            > short period of time

MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

-   The study is considered medically necessary only when the patient
    > does not already have a clear indication for an implantable
    > cardioverter-defibrillator and the request also meets the general
    > clinical appropriateness framework, including:

    -   A complete evaluation with history, physical exam, and relevant
        > prior testing/labs

        > Expected benefit outweighs risk

        > Support from accepted clinical practice or literature

        > Reasonable likelihood the study will change management or
        > improve outcome

    > For patients WITHOUT documented arrhythmia, medical necessity is
    > met if any of the following apply:

    -   Syncope without an obvious cause after standard evaluation, plus
        > at least one of:

        -   LVEF 35% or less

            > Prior myocardial infarction or myocardial scar from
            > another cause

            > Bifascicular block

            > Cardiac sarcoidosis

            > Complex congenital heart disease

            > Myotonic dystrophy

            > Recurrent syncope/presyncope when arrhythmia is suspected
            > and prior noninvasive testing is inconclusive or equivocal

            > Preexcitation pattern on surface EKG

        > Survivor of sudden cardiac death, plus at least one of:

        -   No cause identified by standard evaluation

            > Myotonic dystrophy

            > Preexcitation pattern on surface EKG

        > Preexcitation, with both:

        -   Asymptomatic individual younger than 35 years

            > Preexcitation is not intermittent

        > Cardiac sarcoidosis to determine candidacy for ICD

        > Congenital heart disease, plus at least one of:

        -   Ebstein anomaly with preexcitation

            > Ebstein anomaly before tricuspid valve surgery

            > Tetralogy of Fallot with QRS duration over 180 msec

            > Tetralogy of Fallot with left ventricular dysfunction

        > Myotonic dystrophy, plus at least one of:

        -   Palpitations

            > Syncope

            > Survival of sudden cardiac death

            > PR interval 240 msec or longer

            > QRS duration 120 msec or longer

            > Age 40 years or older with significant late gadolinium
            > enhancement on cardiac MRI

    > For patients WITH documented arrhythmia, medical necessity is met
    > if any of the following apply:

    -   Syncope with either:

        -   Sinus bradycardia where ambulatory monitoring has not
            > established a temporal correlation with syncope

            > Preexcitation/WPW syndrome

        > Evaluation of spontaneous nonsustained ventricular tachycardia
        > after myocardial infarction, with both:

        -   LVEF 40% or less

            > NSVT documented more than 96 hours after MI

        > Myotonic dystrophy in a patient age 40 or older, with either:

        -   Documented SVT

            > Documented VT

        > Congenital heart disease with NSVT

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

-   The guideline explicitly requires supporting clinical documentation
    > that:

    -   Accurately reflects the patient's clinical status at the time of
        > request

        > Clearly documents the ordering provider's clinical intent

    > Based on the criteria in the policy, documentation should include,
    > when applicable:

    -   Complete history and physical exam

        > Prior laboratory and diagnostic testing

        > Response to prior therapeutic intervention

        > Standard syncope evaluation results

        > Ambulatory monitoring results

        > Noninvasive test results, especially if inconclusive or
        > equivocal

        > LVEF measurement

        > Evidence of prior MI or myocardial scar

        > EKG/conduction findings such as bifascicular block, PR
        > interval, QRS duration, or preexcitation

        > Documentation of congenital heart disease subtype

        > Documentation of cardiac sarcoidosis

        > Cardiac MRI evidence of late gadolinium enhancement when
        > relevant

        > For documented arrhythmia, an electronic or paper rhythm
        > record that can be submitted if requested

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

-   The guideline applies to electrophysiology studies performed
    > separately from ablation; when EPS is performed with ablation on
    > the same date of service, EPS is considered integral to the
    > ablation and should not be billed separately.

    > If a comprehensive EPS is performed, the comprehensive code set
    > should be used; if not, only the individual service components
    > should be reported.

    > Requests for multiple diagnostic or therapeutic interventions at
    > the same time may require additional review or peer-to-peer
    > discussion if the necessity for all services at once is unclear.

    > Repeat diagnostic testing is generally limited to:

    -   Follow-up after an intervention, or

        > A change in clinical status requiring further evaluation

        > Clarification of a prior finding using a different technique
        > or protocol

    > Repeat diagnostic testing may face additional review if it
    > involves:

    -   Technical issues at the same facility

        > Repeat testing at a different facility due to provider
        > preference or quality concerns

        > Persistent symptoms without clinical change, treatment, or
        > intervention

        > Multiple providers ordering similar testing over a short
        > period

    > Repeat therapeutic intervention in the same area is generally
    > appropriate only if the prior intervention was beneficial and its
    > expected duration of relief has passed; early repeat intervention
    > is not appropriate unless the prior service was never actually
    > performed.

    > The policy does not address plan-specific coverage or benefit
    > determinations.

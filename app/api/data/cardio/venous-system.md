MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

1.  General Clinical Appropriateness Requirements

-   Before any intervention, the clinician should establish or confirm
    > the diagnosis through a complete evaluation.

    > Evaluation should include:

    -   History and physical exam

        > Relevant lab studies when applicable

        > Relevant diagnostic testing when applicable

        > Review of response to prior treatment when applicable

    > The expected clinical benefit should outweigh potential harm.

    > The requested intervention should be supported by accepted
    > clinical literature, guidelines, or standards of care.

    > The intervention should be reasonably expected to change
    > management or improve outcome.

2\. Truncal Vein Treatment Includes treatment of:

-   Great saphenous vein

    > Small saphenous vein

    > Anterior accessory/anterior saphenous vein

    > Posterior accessory saphenous vein

A. Thermal Ablation Includes:

-   Endovenous laser ablation

    > Radiofrequency ablation

Medically necessary when all are met:

-   Symptoms interfere with vocation or lifestyle

    > Duplex ultrasound shows axial reflux greater than 500 msec in the
    > vein being treated

    > The saphenous vein has not been treated with thermal or
    > non-thermal ablation within the last 3 months

Symptoms recognized by the policy include:

-   Painful varicose veins

    > Heaviness

    > Itching

    > Burning

    > Leg pain not explained by another cause

    > Leg swelling not explained by another cause

    > Venous stasis skin changes

    > Superficial phlebitis

    > Bleeding

    > Ulceration

B. Non-Thermal Ablation Includes:

-   Cyanoacrylate ablation

    > Ultrasound-guided foam sclerotherapy

    > Mechanochemical ablation

Medically necessary when all are met:

-   Symptoms interfere with vocation or lifestyle

    > Duplex ultrasound shows axial reflux greater than 500 msec in the
    > vein being treated

    > The saphenous vein has not been treated with thermal or
    > non-thermal ablation within the last 3 months

    > The patient cannot undergo thermal ablation for at least one of
    > the following reasons:

    -   Intolerance to tumescent anesthesia

        > Partially occluded vein

        > Tortuous vein

        > Superficial vein

        > Short vein length

C. Surgical Treatment of Truncal Veins Includes:

-   High ligation with or without stripping of the great, small, or
    > anterior saphenous veins

Medically necessary when all are met:

-   Symptoms interfere with vocation or lifestyle

    > Duplex ultrasound shows axial reflux greater than 500 msec in the
    > vein being treated

    > The patient cannot undergo thermal ablation for at least one of
    > the following reasons:

    -   Partially occluded vein

        > Tortuous vein

        > Superficial vein

        > Aneurysmal vein

        > Short vein length

        > Recurrent reflux

3\. Perforator Vein Treatment Includes:

-   Ultrasound-guided foam sclerotherapy

    > Endovenous laser treatment

    > Radiofrequency ablation

Medically necessary when all are met:

-   Patient has an active or healed venous ulcer

    > The perforator vein being treated meets all of the following:

    -   It lies beneath the area of ulceration

        > Duplex ultrasound shows outward flow greater than 500 msec

        > Diameter is greater than 3.5 mm

    > If significant truncal reflux is present in the same leg and
    > contributes to the ulcer area, it must either:

    -   Be treated at the same session, or

        > Have been previously treated with no significant residual
        > reflux on duplex ultrasound

4\. Tributary and Varicose Vein Treatment Includes:

-   Liquid sclerotherapy

    > Foam sclerotherapy

    > Ultrasound-guided foam sclerotherapy

    > Phlebectomy

Medically necessary when all are met:

-   Symptoms interfere with vocation or lifestyle

    > Veins being treated are greater than 3 mm in diameter

    > If significant truncal reflux is present in the same leg and is
    > causing the varicosities, it must either:

    -   Be treated during the same session, or

        > Have been treated more than 3 months earlier, with duplex
        > ultrasound showing no significant residual reflux

Symptoms recognized here include:

-   Painful varicose veins

    > Heaviness

    > Leg swelling not explained by another cause

    > Itching

    > Burning

    > Venous stasis skin changes

    > Superficial phlebitis

    > Bleeding

    > Ulceration

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

General documentation expected

-   Complete history and physical examination

    > Clinical diagnosis or pretest likelihood supporting the request

    > Relevant prior laboratory data, if applicable

    > Relevant prior diagnostic testing, if applicable

    > Prior treatment history and response, if applicable

    > Clinical rationale showing expected benefit of the procedure

    > Documentation that supports medical necessity at the time of the
    > request

    > Documentation that clearly reflects the ordering provider's intent

Procedure-specific documentation commonly required by the policy

-   Duplex ultrasound findings

    > Reflux duration measurements

    > Vein diameter measurements when required

    > Documentation of symptoms and how they impair lifestyle or
    > vocation

    > Documentation of ulcer presence or healed ulcer status for
    > perforator treatment

    > Documentation that the target perforator lies beneath the ulcer
    > area

    > Documentation of significant truncal reflux and whether it is
    > being treated concurrently or was previously treated

    > Documentation of prior ablation history within the last 3 months

    > For non-thermal truncal treatment, documentation showing why
    > thermal ablation cannot be performed

    > For surgical truncal treatment, documentation showing why thermal
    > ablation cannot be performed

Additional administrative note

-   Requests may require additional records if the submitted information
    > does not sufficiently establish appropriateness.

    > Multiple interventions requested at the same time may trigger
    > additional review or peer-to-peer discussion.

    > Repeated diagnostic testing or repeated therapeutic intervention
    > may also trigger additional review depending on timing and
    > clinical change.

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

General exclusions

-   Diagnoses, procedures, or indications not specifically addressed are
    > considered not medically necessary.

    > The policy is focused on medical necessity and does not address
    > plan coverage or benefit rules.

Clinical limitations

-   Reticular veins and telangiectasias alone are not an indication for
    > treatment.

    > Routine intervention for small cosmetic veins is not supported.

    > For tributary/varicose vein treatment, veins must be greater than
    > 3 mm.

    > For perforator treatment, treatment is limited to perforators
    > associated with a healed or active venous ulcer and meeting reflux
    > and size thresholds.

    > Non-thermal truncal treatment is limited to patients who cannot
    > undergo thermal ablation for specified reasons.

    > Surgical truncal treatment is limited to patients who cannot
    > undergo thermal ablation for specified reasons.

Repeat treatment limitations

-   Repeat therapeutic intervention in the same area is generally
    > appropriate only when prior treatment was beneficial and the
    > expected duration of relief has passed.

    > A repeat intervention requested before the expected duration of
    > relief is not appropriate unless it is confirmed that the prior
    > intervention was never performed.

    > A truncal saphenous vein is not eligible if it has been treated
    > with thermal or non-thermal ablation within the previous 3 months.

Repeat diagnostic testing limitations

-   Repeat testing of the same anatomic area for the same indication is
    > generally limited to:

    -   Post-intervention evaluation

        > A change in clinical status

        > Clarification of a prior result using a different technique or
        > protocol

    > Repeat testing may receive added scrutiny when:

    -   Repeated because of technical issues

        > Repeated at another facility due to preference or quality
        > concerns

        > Requested for persistent symptoms without clinical change or
        > interval treatment

        > Ordered by different providers over a short period

Concise takeaways

-   The policy supports intervention when symptoms are clinically
    > meaningful and objective duplex findings confirm disease.

    > Duplex ultrasound is central to most approvals.

    > Thermal ablation is the default truncal approach when feasible.

    > Non-thermal and surgical approaches require documented reasons
    > thermal ablation is not suitable.

    > Cosmetic-only venous disease is excluded.

Medical Necessity Criteria

General clinical appropriateness requirements

-   Before any intervention, the diagnosis or pretest likelihood must be
    > confirmed through a complete evaluation.

    > Evaluation should include:

    -   History and physical examination

        > Relevant laboratory studies, when applicable

        > Relevant diagnostic testing, when applicable

        > Response to prior therapeutic intervention, when applicable

    > The anticipated benefit of the intervention must outweigh
    > potential harms.

    > Current literature, treatment guidelines, and standards of
    > practice should support the requested intervention as the best
    > option among alternatives.

    > There should be a reasonable likelihood that the intervention will
    > change management and/or improve outcome.

Pulmonary vein isolation (PVI) for atrial fibrillation

-   Initial and one repeat PVI are medically necessary when any of the
    > following apply:

    > Paroxysmal atrial fibrillation: ANY of the following

    -   Persistent symptoms despite treatment with at least one Class I
        > or Class III antiarrhythmic medication

        > Symptomatic disease and all Class I and Class III
        > antiarrhythmic medications are contraindicated

        > As initial therapy in patients with recurrent paroxysmal
        > atrial fibrillation

    > Persistent atrial fibrillation of less than 12 months duration:
    > EITHER of the following

    -   Persistent symptoms despite treatment with at least one Class I
        > or Class III antiarrhythmic medication

        > Symptomatic disease and all Class I and Class III
        > antiarrhythmic medications are contraindicated

    > Persistent or paroxysmal atrial fibrillation: ANY of the following

    -   Symptoms related to atrial fibrillation in patients with heart
        > failure

        > Symptomatic atrial fibrillation in patients with hypertrophic
        > cardiomyopathy when pharmacologic rhythm control fails, is
        > contraindicated, or is not desired

        > To avoid pacemaker implantation in patients with atrial
        > fibrillation-related bradycardia or symptomatic
        > post-conversion pause

Additional linear or focal intracardiac catheter ablation performed at
the time of PVI

-   Additional linear or focal left or right atrial ablation for
    > treatment of atrial fibrillation at the time of PVI is considered
    > not medically necessary because it has not been shown to improve
    > outcomes.

AV node ablation for atrial fibrillation

-   Medically necessary only when ALL of the following apply:

    -   Ventricular response rate remains unacceptably high despite
        > maximum tolerated doses of rate-control medications, or the
        > individual has tachycardia-mediated cardiomyopathy

        > The individual is not a candidate for, or has failed,
        > pharmacologic rhythm control

        > The individual is not a candidate for, or has failed, PVI

Accessory pathway ablation for pre-excited atrial fibrillation

-   Medically necessary when EITHER of the following applies:

    -   Symptomatic pre-excited atrial fibrillation

        > Asymptomatic preexcitation with a high-risk electrophysiology
        > study in an individual who either has a high-risk occupation
        > or participates in competitive athletics

Required Documentation

General documentation required

-   Clinical documentation may be required to support the request.

    > Documentation must:

    -   Accurately reflect the clinical situation at the time of the
        > request

        > Sufficiently document the ordering provider's clinical intent

Documentation that should be present to support medical necessity

-   Confirmed diagnosis and atrial fibrillation type:

    -   Paroxysmal

        > Persistent less than 12 months

        > Permanent, if relevant to treatment planning

    > Symptom history and severity

    > History and physical examination

    > Relevant labs and diagnostic testing

    > Prior treatment history and response

    > Antiarrhythmic medication history, including:

    -   At least one prior Class I or Class III antiarrhythmic trial
        > when required

        > Failure, intolerance, or contraindication to antiarrhythmic
        > therapy when applicable

    > For AV node ablation requests:

    -   Evidence of uncontrolled ventricular response despite maximum
        > tolerated rate-control therapy, or tachycardia-mediated
        > cardiomyopathy

        > Evidence patient is not a candidate for, or has failed,
        > rhythm-control therapy

        > Evidence patient is not a candidate for, or has failed, PVI

    > For PVI requests based on special indications:

    -   Heart failure-related symptoms

        > Hypertrophic cardiomyopathy with failed, contraindicated, or
        > undesired pharmacologic rhythm control

        > AF-related bradycardia or symptomatic post-conversion pause
        > when pacemaker avoidance is the goal

    > For accessory pathway ablation:

    -   Symptomatic pre-excited AF, or

        > High-risk EP study plus high-risk occupation or competitive
        > athletics

    > Rationale showing expected net benefit and likelihood of improving
    > management/outcomes

Limitations and Exclusions

Procedure-related limitations/exclusions

-   Only the initial and one repeat PVI are clearly supported under
    > standard criteria.

    > After PVI has been attempted twice, additional PVI requests
    > require higher-level review and are considered case by case.

    > Additional linear or focal intracardiac ablation at the time of
    > PVI is considered not medically necessary for AF treatment.

Scope limitations

-   The guideline addresses:

    -   PVI

        > Additional atrial ablation performed with PVI

        > AV node ablation

        > Accessory pathway ablation for pre-excited AF

    > Ablation for other supraventricular or ventricular arrhythmias is
    > outside the scope of this guideline.

Review/authorization limitations

-   Requests for multiple procedures at the same time may require
    > peer-to-peer review.

    > Repeated diagnostic testing of the same area for the same
    > indication may require additional review, especially when:

    -   Repeated due to technical issues

        > Requested at another facility for provider preference/quality
        > concerns

        > Persistent symptoms exist without clinical change, treatment,
        > or intervention since prior study

        > Multiple providers request repeat testing over a short time

    > Repeat therapeutic intervention in the same anatomic area is
    > generally appropriate only when prior intervention was beneficial
    > and expected duration of relief has lapsed.

    > Repeat therapeutic intervention before expected duration of relief
    > is not appropriate unless the prior intervention was never
    > administered.

Bottom-line summary

-   Medically necessary use is primarily limited to:

    -   PVI for defined symptomatic paroxysmal or early persistent AF
        > scenarios

        > PVI in selected patients with heart failure, hypertrophic
        > cardiomyopathy, or AF-related bradycardia/post-conversion
        > pause

        > AV node ablation only after failure/ineligibility for rate and
        > rhythm strategies including PVI

        > Accessory pathway ablation for symptomatic pre-excited AF or
        > selected high-risk asymptomatic patients

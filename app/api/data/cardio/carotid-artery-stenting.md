MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

Carotid artery stenting (CAS), including transcarotid artery revascularization (TCAR), is an endovascular technique used to treat significant extracranial carotid artery stenosis for the purpose of reducing the risk of ischemic stroke. CAS may be performed via transfemoral access (TF-CAS) or via direct transcarotid access with proximal flow reversal (TCAR). Both approaches require the use of a cerebral embolic protection system.

CAS or TCAR is considered medically necessary when ALL of the following apply:

-   Significant carotid artery stenosis is confirmed by imaging meeting the stenosis thresholds defined under INDICATIONS below.
-   The patient has symptomatic or asymptomatic carotid stenosis meeting the clinical criteria defined under INDICATIONS below.
-   The patient is at high risk for carotid endarterectomy (CEA) OR meets criteria for CAS/TCAR as an equivalent alternative to CEA per current SVS/AHA-ASA guidelines (see high-surgical-risk criteria below).
-   A qualified vascular surgeon, interventional cardiologist, or neurointerventionalist with documented experience in carotid stenting will perform the procedure.
-   An embolic cerebral protection device is used during the procedure.
-   Antiplatelet therapy (dual antiplatelet therapy with aspirin plus a P2Y12 inhibitor) has been initiated or planned peri-procedurally, unless contraindicated.

High-risk criteria for CEA (one or more of the following):

-   Clinically significant cardiac disease (congestive heart failure, LVEF less than 30%, unstable angina, myocardial infarction within 30 days, or need for open heart surgery within 30 days).
-   Severe pulmonary disease (for example, FEV1 less than 30% predicted, oxygen-dependent COPD).
-   Contralateral carotid artery occlusion.
-   Contralateral laryngeal nerve palsy.
-   Previous radical neck surgery or external beam radiation to the neck.
-   Surgically inaccessible lesion (for example, high cervical stenosis above C2, or intrathoracic lesion below the clavicle).
-   Recurrent stenosis after prior ipsilateral CEA.
-   Age 80 years or older (relative high-risk factor, particularly for transfemoral CAS; TCAR may mitigate embolic risk in older patients).
-   Severe tandem intracranial stenosis that increases operative complexity.

INDICATIONS FOR CAROTID ARTERY STENTING

SYMPTOMATIC CAROTID STENOSIS

Symptomatic carotid stenosis is defined as stenosis ipsilateral to a transient ischemic attack (TIA), ischemic stroke with acceptable functional recovery, or amaurosis fugax (transient monocular blindness) occurring within the past 6 months. CAS or TCAR is indicated for symptomatic stenosis when:

-   Stenosis is 50% or greater by NASCET criteria on catheter angiography, OR
-   Stenosis is 50% or greater confirmed by at least two of the following non-invasive modalities:
    -   Carotid duplex ultrasound demonstrating:
        -   Peak systolic velocity (PSV) of 125 cm/sec or greater in the internal carotid artery (ICA), AND
        -   ICA-to-common carotid artery (ICA/CCA) peak systolic velocity ratio of 2.0 or greater.
    -   Computed tomography angiography (CTA) confirming 50% or greater luminal narrowing.
    -   Magnetic resonance angiography (MRA) confirming 50% or greater luminal narrowing.
-   The neurological event is attributed to ipsilateral carotid stenosis (not cardioembolism, small-vessel lacunar disease, or other etiology).
-   The patient is neurologically stable (no major disabling stroke precluding benefit from revascularization; mRS 0 to 2 preferred, mRS 3 or less generally acceptable).
-   Ideally, intervention is performed within 2 weeks of the qualifying event when the patient is medically stable, consistent with AHA/ASA stroke guideline recommendations.

ASYMPTOMATIC CAROTID STENOSIS

Asymptomatic carotid stenosis is defined as stenosis in the absence of any ipsilateral neurological symptoms within the past 6 months. CAS or TCAR is indicated for asymptomatic stenosis when ALL of the following apply:

-   Stenosis is 70% or greater by NASCET criteria on catheter angiography, OR
-   Stenosis is 70% or greater confirmed by carotid duplex ultrasound demonstrating:
    -   Peak systolic velocity (PSV) of 125 cm/sec or greater in the ICA (values of 230 cm/sec or greater more specifically correlate with 70% or greater stenosis in many published velocity criteria), AND
    -   ICA/CCA peak systolic velocity ratio of 2.0 or greater (ratios of 4.0 or greater more specifically correlate with 70% or greater stenosis).
    -   When duplex findings are discordant or borderline, confirmatory CTA or MRA is required.
-   The patient is at high risk for CEA as defined above, OR the treating team has documented a shared decision-making discussion regarding the comparative risks and benefits of CAS versus CEA versus optimal medical therapy.
-   Life expectancy is sufficient to derive benefit from stroke prevention (generally 3 to 5 years or more).
-   The procedural stroke and death rate at the performing center or by the performing operator is less than 3% for asymptomatic patients (per SVS/AHA quality benchmarks).
-   Optimal medical therapy (antiplatelet agent, statin, antihypertensive therapy, smoking cessation) is in place or initiated concurrently.

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

-   Physician attestation confirming the category of stenosis (symptomatic versus asymptomatic) with dates of qualifying neurological events, if applicable.
-   Duplex ultrasound report with documented peak systolic velocity (PSV) in the ICA and CCA, ICA/CCA PSV ratio, and estimated percent stenosis; performed within 90 days of the planned procedure.
-   Confirmatory CTA or MRA report (if duplex results are equivocal or if catheter angiography has not been performed); findings must quantify percent luminal stenosis using NASCET criteria.
-   Documentation of high-surgical-risk factors for CEA, specifying which criteria are met, or documentation of shared decision-making discussion regarding CAS versus CEA versus medical therapy for standard-risk patients.
-   Current medication list confirming antiplatelet therapy and statin are prescribed or planned; documentation of contraindications if not prescribed.
-   Pre-procedure neurological assessment including neurological symptom history, dates of TIA or stroke, and modified Rankin Scale (mRS) score for symptomatic patients.
-   Results of any brain imaging (CT or MRI of brain) performed to characterize prior stroke or rule out hemorrhagic lesion for symptomatic patients.
-   Operator and facility credentialing documentation confirming appropriate procedural volume and acceptable peri-procedural stroke and death rates, where required by payer.
-   For TCAR specifically, documentation of anatomical eligibility (adequate common carotid artery access site, absence of contraindications to flow-reversal neuroprotection).

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

-   CAS is not covered for asymptomatic stenosis less than 70% by NASCET criteria unless the patient is enrolled in an approved clinical trial or registry.
-   CAS is not covered for symptomatic stenosis less than 50% by NASCET criteria.
-   CAS without an embolic protection device is not covered except in rare anatomical circumstances where device delivery is not technically feasible, which must be documented prospectively.
-   CAS is not covered for acute ischemic stroke in the hyperacute phase when the primary goal is thrombus retrieval; intracranial mechanical thrombectomy under separate codes applies in that setting.
-   CAS is not covered as a re-do procedure for in-stent restenosis without documentation of recurrent symptomatic stenosis of 50% or greater or asymptomatic in-stent restenosis of 80% or greater.
-   CAS is generally not covered for radiation-induced carotid stenosis in the absence of high-surgical-risk criteria or documented symptomatic stenosis meeting standard thresholds; individual payer review applies.
-   CAS is not a covered preventive service in standard-risk asymptomatic patients with stenosis less than 70% who are otherwise candidates for CEA when the peri-procedural risk of CAS exceeds that of CEA.
-   Routine surveillance carotid duplex ultrasound post-stenting beyond standard protocol intervals is subject to separate coverage review.
-   Contralateral (non-index) carotid lesions treated during the same operative session require individual documentation of medical necessity meeting the criteria above independently for each vessel.
-   CAS is not indicated for non-atherosclerotic carotid lesions (for example, fibromuscular dysplasia, carotid dissection, carotid body tumor) unless specific payer criteria are met under separate policy provisions.

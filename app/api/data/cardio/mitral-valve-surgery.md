MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

Mitral valve surgery — including open repair or replacement — is considered medically necessary when the clinical indication is supported by objective evidence of hemodynamically significant mitral valve disease and the expected benefit of surgical intervention outweighs procedural risk. Decisions should reflect current ACC/AHA Valvular Heart Disease guidelines and be corroborated by echocardiographic, hemodynamic, and clinical data.

Repair is the preferred approach when feasible and likely to be durable (Class I recommendation). Replacement is appropriate when repair is not technically achievable or is unlikely to produce a durable result.

Authorization requires documentation of the specific mitral valve pathology, severity classification, relevant cardiac imaging findings, symptom status, LV functional parameters, and the rationale for the surgical approach chosen.

INDICATIONS

Primary Mitral Regurgitation (Structural/Organic MR)

Acute Severe MR

-   Surgery is indicated on an urgent or emergent basis for acute severe MR regardless of LV function, including causes such as:
    -   Papillary muscle rupture complicating myocardial infarction
    -   Infective endocarditis with flail leaflet
    -   Chordae tendineae rupture from trauma or myxomatous disease
    -   Symptomatic presentation with hemodynamic instability, pulmonary edema, or cardiogenic shock

Symptomatic Chronic Severe Primary MR

-   Surgery is indicated for symptomatic chronic severe primary MR (NYHA class II, III, or IV) regardless of LV systolic function, provided surgical risk is acceptable.
-   Severity thresholds for severe primary MR:
    -   Effective regurgitant orifice area (EROA) >= 0.40 cm² (proximal isovelocity surface area method), or
    -   Regurgitant volume >= 60 mL/beat, or
    -   Regurgitant fraction >= 50%, or
    -   Vena contracta >= 0.7 cm

Asymptomatic Severe Primary MR — LV Dysfunction

-   Surgery is indicated for asymptomatic patients with severe primary MR and evidence of LV dysfunction defined as:
    -   LVEF <= 60%, or
    -   Left ventricular end-systolic dimension (LVESD) >= 40 mm

Asymptomatic Severe Primary MR — Preserved LV Function with High-Risk Features

-   Surgery is indicated (or reasonable to consider) for asymptomatic patients with severe primary MR and preserved LV function (LVEF > 60%, LVESD < 40 mm) when at least one of the following is present:
    -   New-onset atrial fibrillation (AF) attributable to MR, or
    -   Resting pulmonary hypertension with systolic pulmonary artery pressure (SPAP) > 50 mmHg at rest
-   Surgery may also be considered in experienced centers for asymptomatic patients with preserved LV function when the likelihood of successful durable repair is high (> 95% probability, low operative mortality) and anatomy is favorable for repair.

Secondary (Functional) Mitral Regurgitation

-   For secondary MR (caused by LV remodeling rather than primary leaflet pathology), surgical intervention may be considered in select cases when:
    -   Severe secondary MR persists despite optimal guideline-directed medical therapy (GDMT) including cardiac resynchronization therapy (CRT) if indicated, and
    -   The patient is undergoing concomitant coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG) or other cardiac surgery, or
    -   Transcatheter mitral valve repair (e.g., MitraClip/TEER) is not feasible or has failed.

Mitral Stenosis (MS)

Severe Symptomatic Mitral Stenosis

-   Mitral valve surgery (commissurotomy, repair, or replacement) is indicated for symptomatic severe MS when percutaneous mitral balloon commissurotomy (PMBC) is not feasible, contraindicated, or has failed, and:
    -   Mitral valve area (MVA) <= 1.5 cm² by echocardiography (planimetry or pressure half-time method), and
    -   Mean transmitral gradient >= 5 mmHg at rest (or > 15 mmHg with exercise in borderline cases), and
    -   Symptoms of NYHA class II, III, or IV heart failure, and
    -   Valve anatomy unfavorable for PMBC (Wilkins score > 8, significant subvalvular disease, moderate-to-severe mitral regurgitation, or left atrial thrombus not resolved with anticoagulation)
-   Very severe MS (MVA <= 1.0 cm²) with hemodynamic compromise may require urgent intervention regardless of symptom classification.

Concomitant Mitral Valve Surgery

-   Mitral valve surgery at the time of other cardiac surgery (e.g., CABG, aortic valve surgery, tricuspid repair) is reasonable when:
    -   Moderate-to-severe or severe primary MR is present and repair is likely to be durable, or
    -   Severe MS is present with anatomy suitable for open commissurotomy or replacement

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

Clinical and Symptom Documentation

-   Complete history and physical examination with NYHA functional class assessment
-   Symptom onset, duration, and progression (dyspnea, orthopnea, exercise intolerance, palpitations, presyncope)
-   Documentation of whether symptoms are attributable to mitral valve disease versus comorbid conditions
-   Cardiology or cardiothoracic surgery consultation note with surgical recommendation and risk-benefit assessment

Echocardiographic Data (Required)

-   Transthoracic echocardiogram (TTE) with complete quantitative assessment:
    -   Mitral valve morphology and mechanism of regurgitation or stenosis
    -   Severity grading: EROA, regurgitant volume, regurgitant fraction, vena contracta width (for MR)
    -   Mitral valve area by planimetry and pressure half-time (for MS), and mean gradient
    -   LVEF (biplane Simpson's method preferred) and LVESD
    -   Left atrial size and volume index
    -   Estimated RVSP/SPAP (tricuspid regurgitation jet velocity)
    -   Right ventricular size and function
-   Transesophageal echocardiogram (TEE) if TTE is technically limited or if surgical planning requires detailed leaflet/subvalvular anatomy assessment
-   Serial echocardiography in asymptomatic patients demonstrating interval progression supporting surgical timing

Hemodynamic and Additional Testing

-   Cardiac catheterization (invasive hemodynamics) when non-invasive data is discordant with clinical symptoms or when coronary artery disease assessment is required pre-operatively
-   Exercise stress testing (with or without hemodynamic imaging) in patients with borderline symptoms or discordant findings
-   CT scan for annular sizing, mitral anatomy, or minimally invasive surgical planning as applicable

Operative Planning Documentation

-   Surgical risk assessment using STS Predicted Risk of Mortality (PROM) calculator or equivalent
-   Documentation of repair feasibility versus replacement decision, including surgeon experience with repair at the performing center
-   For secondary MR: documentation that GDMT has been optimized, including CRT status if applicable

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

-   Isolated mild or moderate primary MR without evidence of LV dysfunction, AF, or pulmonary hypertension does not meet criteria for surgical intervention.
-   Moderate MS (MVA 1.5–2.0 cm²) without significant symptoms or hemodynamic compromise is not an indication for surgery.
-   Surgical intervention for secondary MR is not covered as a primary stand-alone procedure when GDMT has not been optimized or when transcatheter options (e.g., TEER/MitraClip) are feasible and indicated.
-   Repeat mitral valve surgery for structural valve deterioration of a prior bioprosthesis requires documentation of hemodynamically significant dysfunction (severe stenosis or regurgitation of the prosthesis) and is subject to the same severity and symptom criteria.
-   Prophylactic mitral valve surgery in asymptomatic patients with mild-to-moderate MR or MS, without the specific LV dysfunction thresholds or high-risk features defined above, is not covered.
-   Surgery is not covered when the patient has been deemed an unacceptably high surgical risk and no clinical consensus supports proceeding; in such cases, transcatheter alternatives (TEER, transcatheter mitral valve replacement) should be evaluated.
-   Requests without objective echocardiographic severity data meeting thresholds defined above will be returned for additional documentation.

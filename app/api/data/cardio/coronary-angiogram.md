MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

General clinical appropriateness framework

-   Before any intervention, the clinician should confirm the diagnosis
    > or establish pretest likelihood based on:

    -   history and physical examination

        > relevant laboratory studies when applicable

        > diagnostic testing

        > response to prior therapeutic intervention

    > The expected benefit should outweigh potential harms.

    > Current clinical literature, treatment guidelines, and standards
    > of care should support the intervention.

    > There should be a reasonable likelihood that the intervention will
    > change management or improve outcomes.

Scope and baseline requirements

-   Applies to nonemergency coronary angiography.

    > Elective coronary angiography is generally considered only after
    > noninvasive evaluation.

    > Coronary angiography requires conscious sedation and should be
    > performed where cardiac monitoring and resuscitation equipment are
    > available.

    > Coronary angiography is never clinically appropriate as a
    > screening test in asymptomatic individuals.

Definitions used in the criteria

-   Established CAD includes any of the following:

    -   at least 70% stenosis on CCTA or invasive coronary angiography

        > at least 50% stenosis in the left main coronary artery on CCTA
        > or invasive angiography

        > history of unstable coronary syndrome

        > history of PCI or CABG

    > Advanced chronic kidney disease:

    -   dialysis, or

        > GFR less than 30 ml/min/1.73 m2

    > Recent:

    -   within the past 90 days

    > Sustained ventricular tachycardia:

    -   persists at least 30 seconds or requires termination for
        > hemodynamic instability

    > Unstable angina:

    -   myocardial ischemia at rest or minimal exertion without acute
        > myocardial injury/necrosis

        > elective outpatient angiography for unstable angina requires
        > recent hospitalization for that condition

Guideline-directed medical therapy (GDMT)

-   Risk factor management:

    -   healthy lifestyle, including tobacco avoidance, activity, weight
        > management, healthy diet

        > antiplatelet therapy

        > maximally tolerated high-intensity statin when appropriate

        > beta blocker in selected patients

        > ACE inhibitor or ARB in selected patients

        > antidiabetic therapy for diabetic patients

    > Symptom control:

    -   beta blockers first line unless contraindicated

        > calcium channel blockers and/or long-acting nitrates if beta
        > blockers are contraindicated, not tolerated, or insufficient

        > ranolazine when other antianginal therapy is contraindicated,
        > not tolerated, or inadequate

Clinical indications

1.  Patients with established CAD Diagnostic coronary angiography is
    > medically necessary when any of the following apply:

-   Significant stenosis of 50% or more in an unprotected left main
    > coronary artery on recent CCTA

    > Lesions of unclear severity in an unprotected left main coronary
    > artery on recent CCTA

    > Persistence or recurrence of unacceptable symptoms despite GDMT

    > Recent noninvasive stress testing with any of the following:

    -   stress-induced left ventricular dilation

        > stress-induced fall in left ventricular ejection fraction

        > increased lung/heart isotope uptake on stress imaging

        > significant fall in systolic blood pressure during exercise
        > greater than 10 mmHg

        > stress-induced ventricular fibrillation or sustained
        > ventricular tachycardia

    > Intermediate- or high-risk findings on recent noninvasive stress
    > testing, with any of the following:

    -   suspected left main CAD and CCTA is unavailable or
        > contraindicated

        > NYHA class III or IV heart failure

        > left ventricular ejection fraction less than 35%

        > persistent or recurrent unacceptable symptoms despite GDMT

        > CABG or PCI within the preceding year

    > Low-risk findings on noninvasive stress testing with persistent
    > unacceptable ischemic equivalent symptoms despite GDMT when CCTA
    > is unavailable or contraindicated

    > Angina, heart failure, arrhythmia, or abnormal stress testing
    > despite GDMT within 90 days of inpatient evaluation for ACS

    > Within 45 days of STEMI in a patient with significant non-culprit
    > vessel stenosis when angiography is being considered for possible
    > percutaneous revascularization

2\. Patients with suspected CAD Diagnostic coronary angiography is
medically necessary when any of the following apply:

-   Lesions of unclear severity in an unprotected left main coronary
    > artery on recent CCTA

    > Newly recognized resting LV systolic dysfunction with ejection
    > fraction 40% or less after nonischemic etiologies have been
    > excluded, in a patient at intermediate or high CAD risk

    > Persistence or recurrence of unacceptable symptoms in patients
    > with greater than 50% stenosis on CCTA

    > Recent noninvasive stress testing with any of the following:

    -   stress-induced left ventricular dilation

        > stress-induced fall in left ventricular ejection fraction

        > increased lung/heart isotope uptake on stress imaging

        > significant fall in systolic blood pressure during exercise
        > greater than 10 mmHg

        > stress-induced ventricular fibrillation or sustained
        > ventricular tachycardia

    > Intermediate- or high-risk findings on recent noninvasive stress
    > testing, with any of the following:

    -   suspected left main CAD and CCTA is unavailable or
        > contraindicated

        > NYHA class III or IV heart failure

        > persistence of unacceptable symptoms despite GDMT

    > Low-risk findings on noninvasive stress testing with persistent
    > unacceptable ischemic equivalent symptoms despite GDMT when CCTA
    > is unavailable or contraindicated

    > Equivocal or uninterpretable noninvasive stress testing in a
    > patient with persistent symptoms when CCTA is unavailable or
    > contraindicated

3\. Patients with either suspected or established CAD Diagnostic
coronary angiography is medically necessary when any of the following
apply:

-   Resuscitated sudden cardiac death, or documented ventricular
    > fibrillation or sustained ventricular tachycardia, when coronary
    > angiography has not been performed since the event or arrhythmia
    > identification

    > Following cardiac transplant if coronary angiography has not been
    > done in the prior 6 months

    > Evaluation for TAVR in any of the following:

    -   men age 41 or older

        > postmenopausal women

        > established CAD

        > intermediate or high CAD risk

        > recent stress test or CCTA suggesting CAD

    > Evaluation for transcatheter valve replacement/repair other than
    > aortic valve replacement, or surgical valve replacement/repair, in
    > any of the following:

    -   chronic severe secondary mitral regurgitation

        > angina

        > decreased LV systolic function

        > established CAD

        > high CAD risk

        > recent stress test or CCTA suggesting CAD

    > Congenital heart disease in either of the following:

    -   to exclude coexistent atheromatous CAD before surgical repair in
        > a patient at intermediate or high CAD risk

        > to evaluate congenital coronary artery anomalies when any of
        > the following apply:

        -   diagnosis already established by CCTA or cardiac MR and
            > angiography will provide additional management-changing
            > information

            > CCTA or cardiac MR performed but diagnosis could not be
            > excluded

            > neither CCTA nor MRI is available to establish or exclude
            > diagnosis in a patient with suspected disease

            > both CCTA and MRI were considered but neither is
            > appropriate for the patient

Stress test risk categories used by the guideline

-   Low risk

    -   EKG treadmill: Duke treadmill score 5 or greater

        > SPECT MPI or stress PET: less than 5% ischemic myocardium

        > Stress echocardiography: no stress-induced wall motion
        > abnormality

    > Intermediate risk

    -   EKG treadmill: Duke treadmill score -10 to +4

        > SPECT MPI or stress PET: 5% to 10% ischemic myocardium

        > Stress echocardiography: stress-induced wall motion
        > abnormality in a single segment

    > High risk

    -   EKG treadmill: any of the following

        -   Duke treadmill score -11 or less

            > ST segment elevation

            > hypotension with exercise

            > ventricular tachycardia

            > prolonged ST segment depression

        > SPECT MPI or stress PET: any of the following

        -   more than 10% ischemic myocardium

            > stress-induced wall motion abnormality in 2 or more
            > segments

            > significant stress-induced LV dysfunction

            > transient ischemic LV dilation

        > Stress echocardiography: any of the following

        -   stress-induced wall motion abnormality in 2 or more segments

            > significant stress-induced LV dysfunction

            > transient ischemic LV dilation

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

The document does not provide a single checklist by name, but it states
that supporting documentation should include:

-   Current clinical situation at the time of the request

    > Ordering provider's clinical intent

    > Complete history and physical examination

    > Relevant laboratory studies when applicable

    > Prior diagnostic testing results

    > Response to prior therapeutic intervention

    > Noninvasive evaluation results before elective angiography

    > Evidence of GDMT and whether symptoms persisted despite treatment

    > Details of stress test findings, CCTA findings, LV function,
    > ACS/STEMI timing, transplant status, valve procedure workup, or
    > congenital heart disease workup as applicable to the requested
    > indication

    > Information sufficient to support medical necessity; if not
    > established, peer-to-peer review may be needed

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

Explicit exclusion

-   Invasively derived fractional flow reserve used to create
    > 3-dimensional mapping of the coronary arteries is not medically
    > necessary in any scenario.

Scope limitations

-   The guideline applies only to nonemergency coronary angiography.

    > It does not apply to:

    -   coronary angiography performed during an inpatient stay

        > urgent coronary angiography for unstable coronary syndrome,
        > including myocardial infarction and/or unstable angina

        > diagnostic cardiac catheterization procedures that do not
        > include coronary angiography, such as isolated right heart
        > catheterization, isolated left heart catheterization, combined
        > right and left heart catheterization, or aortography

Clinical limitations and cautionary statements

-   Asymptomatic screening use is never clinically appropriate.

    > Only clinically relevant additional procedures should be done at
    > the time of coronary angiography.

    > Advanced age, advanced renal disease, advanced malignancy, and
    > coagulopathy are relative contraindications.

    > Iodinated contrast raises concern in patients with prior contrast
    > allergy or atopy and in those with renal impairment.

    > Radiation exposure should be minimized.

    > In most stable CAD patients, GDMT should generally be tried before
    > coronary angiography.

    > In stable CAD with advanced chronic kidney disease,
    > revascularization provides no benefit over medical management and
    > angiography risks are higher.

    > Repeat testing of the same anatomic area for the same indication
    > is generally limited to:

    -   post-intervention evaluation, or

        > changed clinical status requiring new testing

    > Repeat testing may require additional review or peer-to-peer
    > discussion if:

    -   repeated at the same facility due to technical issues

        > repeated at a different facility due to provider preference or
        > quality concerns

        > repeated for persistent symptoms without clinical change,
        > treatment, or intervention since the prior study

        > repeated by different providers over a short period of time

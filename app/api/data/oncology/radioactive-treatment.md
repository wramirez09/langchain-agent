\## General Medical Necessity Requirements

Before any requested therapeutic radiopharmaceutical is considered
medically necessary, the record should support all of the following:

\- Confirmed diagnosis or sufficient pretest likelihood based on:

\- History and physical examination

\- Relevant laboratory studies, when applicable

\- Relevant diagnostic testing, when applicable

\- Response to prior therapeutic intervention, when applicable

\- Expected benefit is likely to outweigh potential harms

\- Current treatment guidelines, literature, or standards of care
support the requested intervention over alternatives

\- The intervention is reasonably likely to change management or improve
outcome

\## General Required Documentation

Submit clinical documentation that supports the request and includes, as
applicable:

\- History and physical exam

\- Confirmed diagnosis

\- Relevant lab results

\- Relevant diagnostic imaging or other diagnostic test results

\- Prior treatment history

\- Response to prior treatment

\- Documentation that accurately reflects the patient's status at the
time of the request

\- Documentation of the ordering clinician's intent

\## General Limitations

\- Multiple therapeutic or diagnostic interventions ordered at the same
time may require additional review.

\- Repeat diagnostic testing of the same area for the same indication is
generally limited to:

\- Post-intervention evaluation, or

\- A change in clinical status requiring additional testing

\- Repeat testing may receive additional review if requested:

\- Because of technical issues

\- At a different facility due to preference or quality concerns

\- For persistent symptoms without clinical change, treatment, or
intervention

\- By different providers over a short period

\- Repeat therapeutic intervention in the same anatomic area is
generally appropriate only when:

\- Prior treatment was effective or beneficial, and

\- Expected duration of relief has lapsed

\- A repeat intervention requested earlier than expected is not
appropriate unless the prior intervention was never administered

\## 1) Strontium Sr-89 Chloride

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

A single dose is medically necessary for \*\*symptomatic bone
metastases\*\* when both are met:

\- Confirmed osteoblastic bone metastases from solid organ cancer

\- Pain is not adequately controlled by conventional therapy

\### Relevant Codes

\#### CPT/HCPCS

\- A9600

\- 77750

\#### ICD-10

\- C61

\- C79.51

\- R97.21

\- Z19.2

\### Required Documentation

\- Imaging or other confirmation of osteoblastic bone metastases

\- Documentation that metastases arise from a solid organ malignancy

\- Pain assessment

\- Failure or inadequate control with conventional therapy

\- Relevant oncology treatment history

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Not medically necessary if the above criteria are not met

\- Not medically necessary for all other indications

\- Use caution in patients with:

\- Poor renal function

\- Poor bone marrow function

\## 2) Ibritumomab Tiuxetan

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

A single course is medically necessary when either of the following
applies:

\#### A. Follicular B-cell CD20-positive non-Hodgkin lymphoma

All must be met:

\- Age 18 years or older

\- Relapsed or refractory disease, or use after initial therapy with
partial or complete response

\- Adequate marrow reserve:

\- Cellularity greater than 15%

\- Less than 25% marrow involvement by lymphoma

\- Platelets greater than 100,000

\#### B. Other low-grade B-cell CD20-positive non-Hodgkin lymphoma

Examples include marginal zone or MALT lymphoma. All must be met:

\- Age 18 years or older

\- Relapsed or refractory disease

\- Adequate marrow reserve:

\- Cellularity greater than 15%

\- Less than 25% marrow involvement by lymphoma

\- Platelets greater than 100,000

\### Relevant Codes

\#### CPT/HCPCS

\- A9543

\- 79403

\#### ICD-10

\- C81.00-C88.9

\- C82.00-C82.99

\- C83.80-C83.89

\- C83.90-C88.9

\- C88.4

\### Required Documentation

\- Pathology confirming lymphoma subtype and CD20 positivity

\- Age

\- Disease status showing relapsed, refractory, or response after
initial therapy as applicable

\- Bone marrow findings:

\- Cellularity

\- Percent marrow involvement

\- Platelet count

\- Prior treatment history and response

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Not medically necessary if criteria are not met

\- Not medically necessary as a repeat course

\- Not medically necessary as part of a pre-transplant conditioning
regimen

\- Not medically necessary for other indications not listed above

\## 3) Lutetium Lu 177 Dotatate

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

A single course of up to 4 planned injections is medically necessary for
either of the following:

\#### A. Gastroenteropancreatic neuroendocrine tumors

\- Locally advanced, inoperable, or metastatic

\- Well-differentiated

\- Somatostatin receptor-positive

\- Includes foregut, midgut, and hindgut neuroendocrine tumors in adults

\#### B. Bronchopulmonary and thymic neuroendocrine tumors

All must be met:

\- Locally advanced or metastatic disease

\- Age 18 years or older

\- Imaging documents somatostatin receptor overexpression

\- Disease progression after at least 4 months of somatostatin receptor
analog therapy, documented by radiographic imaging

\- ECOG performance status 0-2

\- No prior treatment with a radiolabeled somatostatin analog

\#### C. Pheochromocytoma or paraganglioma

Primary treatment for unresectable or metastatic disease when all are
met:

\- Age 18 years or older

\- Imaging documents somatostatin receptor overexpression

\- ECOG performance status 0-2

\- No prior treatment with a radiolabeled somatostatin analog

\- A9513

\- C74.90-C74.92

\### Required Documentation

\- Pathology or clinical diagnosis of neuroendocrine tumor,

\- Somatostatin receptor-positive imaging

analog treatment

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Not medically necessary if criteria are not met

\- Not medically necessary for all other indications

\- Not medically necessary as a repeat course

\- A course is limited to up to 4 planned injections

\## 4) Lutetium Lu 177 Vipivotide Tetraxetan

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

A single course, up to 6 doses given every 6 weeks, is medically
necessary for prostate cancer when all are met:

\- Age 18 years or older

\- Castrate-resistant metastatic prostate cancer

\- PSMA-positive disease demonstrated by positive PSMA-11 PET scan

\- Previous treatment with taxane-based chemotherapy

\- Previous treatment with one of the following androgen receptor
pathway inhibitors:

\- Abiraterone

\- Apalutamide

\- Enzalutamide

\- Darolutamide

\### Relevant Codes

\#### CPT/HCPCS

\- A9607

\- 79101

\#### ICD-10

\- C61

\- R97.21

\- Z19.2

\### Required Documentation

\- Diagnosis of metastatic castrate-resistant prostate cancer

\- Age

\- Positive PSMA-11 PET scan

\- Prior taxane-based chemotherapy history

\- Prior androgen receptor pathway inhibitor history, including at least
one qualifying agent

\- PSA history and oncology treatment summary, as applicable

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Not medically necessary if criteria are not met

\- Not medically necessary for all other indications

\## 5) Radium Ra-223 Dichloride

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

A single course as monotherapy, up to 6 planned monthly injections, is
medically necessary for prostate cancer when all are met:

\- Age 18 years or older

\- Good performance status: ECOG 0-2

\- Metastatic castrate-resistant prostate cancer

\- Serum testosterone less than 50 ng/dL

\- Symptomatic bone metastases only, with no visceral involvement

\- No bulky lymph node metastases greater than 3 cm

\- Disease is worsening or progressing, demonstrated by either:

\- Imaging showing worsening bone metastases, or

\- PSA greater than 5 ng/mL and rising over 2 consecutive lab
evaluations

\### Relevant Codes

\#### CPT/HCPCS

\- A9606

\- 79101

\#### ICD-10

\- C61

\- C79.51

\- R97.21

\- Z19.2

\### Required Documentation

\- Diagnosis of metastatic castrate-resistant prostate cancer

\- Age

\- ECOG performance status

\- Testosterone level less than 50 ng/dL

\- Evidence of symptomatic bone-only metastases

\- Documentation excluding visceral metastases

\- Lymph node assessment showing no bulky nodes greater than 3 cm

\- Disease progression evidence:

\- Imaging showing worsening bone metastases, or

\- PSA values over time with at least 2 consecutive rises and PSA
greater than 5 ng/mL

\- Prior therapy history, including timing of chemotherapy, biologic
therapy, and radioisotope therapy

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Not medically necessary if criteria are not met

\- Not medically necessary for all other indications

\- Not medically necessary as a repeat course

\- Not medically necessary if systemic radiotherapy with radioisotopes
was given within the previous 24 weeks

\- Not medically necessary if chemotherapy or biologic therapy was given
within the previous 4 weeks

\- Not medically necessary if used concurrently with docetaxel or any
other systemic therapy except androgen deprivation therapy

\- Not medically necessary if used with abiraterone plus prednisone or
prednisolone

\- Not medically necessary if there is evidence of spinal cord
compression

\### Additional Safety/Practical Considerations

\- Adequate bone marrow reserve should be confirmed before initial and
subsequent treatment

\- Treatment should be discontinued if hematologic recovery does not
occur within 6 to 8 weeks after a dose

\## 6) Sodium Iodide I 131

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

Medically necessary for \*\*differentiated thyroid cancer\*\* when any
of the following are met:

\- Definitive treatment for low-risk patients when surgery is not
planned

\- Example reasons: comorbidities or patient refusal

\- Adjuvant treatment after total thyroidectomy or partial
thyroidectomy, except in low-risk patients

\- Treatment of unresectable gross residual disease after total
thyroidectomy or partial thyroidectomy

\- Treatment of known or suspected metastatic disease with
radioiodine-avid thyroid scintigraphy

\- Treatment of persistent disease found on post-therapy imaging

\- Treatment of suspected recurrence based on biochemical testing

\### Low-Risk Thyroid Cancer Features Referenced by the Guideline

\#### Low-risk papillary thyroid cancer

\- Classic papillary thyroid cancer

\- Largest primary tumor less than 2 cm

\- Intrathyroidal

\- Unifocal or multifocal, with all foci less than 1 cm

\- No detectable anti-thyroglobulin antibodies

\- Postoperative unstimulated thyroglobulin less than 1 ng/mL

\#### Low-risk follicular and Hurthle cell thyroid cancer

\- Largest primary tumor less than 2 cm

\- Intrathyroidal

\- No vascular invasion

\- Clinical N0

\- Postoperative unstimulated thyroglobulin less than 1 ng/mL

\### Relevant Codes

\#### CPT/HCPCS

\- A9528

\- A9531

\- 78012

\- 78013

\- 78014

\- 78015

\- 78016

\- 78018

\#### ICD-10

\- C73

\### Required Documentation

\- Diagnosis of differentiated thyroid cancer

\- Surgical history, if surgery performed

\- Risk stratification showing whether disease is low risk or not

\- Pathology and staging details

\- Evidence of unresectable residual disease, metastatic disease,
persistent disease, or suspected recurrence as applicable

\- Radioiodine-avid thyroid scintigraphy results when used for
metastatic disease

\- Post-therapy imaging findings when persistent disease is claimed

\- Biochemical testing supporting recurrence when applicable

\- Prior I-131 treatment history and response if repeat treatment is
requested

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Not medically necessary if criteria are not met

\- Not medically necessary for all other indications

\- Repeat treatment should be limited to patients with prior response

\- Repeat treatment should not occur sooner than 6 to 12 months after
prior treatment

\- Full coverage details may depend on separate inpatient policy

\## Practical Documentation Checklist

For most requests, the submission should include:

\- Diagnosis and pathology

\- Current disease stage and extent

\- Performance status when required

\- Imaging proving required receptor positivity or disease progression

\- Lab values required by the criterion

\- Prior treatment history and treatment response

\- Confirmation that exclusionary treatments or time windows do not
apply

\- Clinical rationale showing why the requested therapy is expected to
improve management or outcome

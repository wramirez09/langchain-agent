#Radiation Therapy (Excluding Proton) - Summary

\## Document Overview

\- Document type: Radiation oncology medical necessity guideline.

\- Scope includes:

\- General clinical appropriateness framework

\- Image guidance

\- Special treatment procedure and special physics consult

\- Bone metastases

\- Breast cancer

\- CNS cancers

\- Colorectal and anal cancers

\- Non-colorectal GI cancers

\- Genitourinary cancers

\- Gynecologic cancers

\- Head and neck cancers including thyroid

\- Lung cancer

\- Lymphoma

\- Oligometastatic extracranial disease

\- Other tumor types

\- Prostate cancer

\- Skin cancer

\## General Medical Necessity Framework

\### Core Criteria

Services are clinically appropriate when all of the following are
supported:

\- Diagnosis is confirmed or its pretest likelihood is established.

\- Evaluation includes:

\- History

\- Physical examination

\- Relevant laboratory studies, when applicable

\- Diagnostic testing, when applicable

\- Response to prior therapeutic intervention, when applicable

\- Expected benefit outweighs potential harm.

\- Widely used treatment guidelines, current literature, and/or
standards of practice support the requested intervention.

\- There is a reasonable likelihood the intervention will:

\- Change management, and/or

\- Improve outcome

\### Repeat or Concurrent Services

\- Multiple interventions requested at the same time may require
additional review.

\- Repeat diagnostic testing is generally limited to:

\- Post-intervention evaluation

\- Change in clinical status

\- Clarification of a prior study using a different technique/protocol

\- Repeated testing may face added review when due to:

\- Technical issues

\- Different facility/provider preference

\- Persistent symptoms without clinical change

\- Duplicate short-interval testing

\- Repeat therapeutic intervention in the same area is generally
appropriate only if:

\- Prior treatment was beneficial, and

\- The expected duration of benefit has lapsed

\## Required Documentation

\### General Documentation

Submit documentation that:

\- Accurately reflects the patient's clinical status at the time of
request

\- Clearly documents the ordering clinician's intent

\### Documentation Commonly Needed Across Requests

\- Confirmed diagnosis

\- Disease site and staging when applicable

\- Prior treatment history

\- Prior irradiation status

\- Relevant imaging and treatment-planning studies

\- Evidence supporting the chosen modality over alternatives

\- Clinical rationale for repeat treatment or concurrent services, if
applicable

\### Additional Documentation Required in Specific Situations

\- IMRT comparison/planning data when dose constraints or organ-at-risk
exposure are at issue

\- Documentation of tumor motion management when motion affects
treatment planning

\- Special treatment procedure:

\- Extra planning time/effort must be documented

\- Special physics consult:

\- Physician request required

\- Customized written report required

\- Dosimetric parameters and organ tolerance reporting when applicable

\- Breast IMRT:

\- 3D planning performed first when cardiac exposure/hot spots are the
rationale

\- Prostate post-prostatectomy:

\- PSA trend

\- Adverse pathology findings when adjuvant therapy is requested

\- Image guidance:

\- Clinical rationale for need based on technique, anatomy, motion,
prior irradiation, obesity, fiducials, etc.

\## Image Guidance in Radiation Oncology

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

Image guidance is appropriate when \*\*any\*\* of the following apply:

\- IMRT is being used

\- Proton therapy is being used

\- Use of image guidance allows significant dose reduction to sensitive
normal structures

\- Example: left-sided breast treatment using deep inspiration breath
hold for cardiac sparing

\- Implanted fiducial markers are present

\- Head and neck cancer

\- Prone breast radiotherapy

\- Treatment field abuts a previously irradiated field

\- Significant setup variation affects the target, such as:

\- Morbid obesity (BMI \> 35) for mediastinal, abdominal, or pelvic
tumors

\- Significant organ motion due to respiration with 4D CT documentation
showing motion is addressed

\### Frequency

\- Perform at the minimum frequency needed to assure proper positioning.

\### Relevant Codes

\- CPT/HCPCS:

\- 77014

\- 77387

\- G6001

\- G6002

\- G6017

\- ICD-10:

\- All inclusive

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Not medically necessary when criteria above are not met.

\- Not medically necessary for:

\- Use with superficial x-rays or electron beam therapy for non-melanoma
skin cancer

\- Electronic brachytherapy

\- CT acquisition work for 3D or IMRT planning is bundled with:

\- 77295

\- 77301

\- 77014 should not be billed for CT acquisition in that setting.

\## Special Treatment Procedure and Special Physics Consult

\### Special Treatment Procedure - Medical Necessity Criteria

Covered when extra planning time and effort are documented for
\*\*any\*\* of the following:

\- Cytotoxic chemotherapy, targeted therapy, and/or immunotherapy within
90 days of radiation therapy

\- Brachytherapy combined with external radiation therapy

\- Proton therapy

\- Total body or hemibody radiation

\- Pediatric patient requiring daily anesthesia and daily physician
supervision

\- Reconstruction of a prior radiation plan, complex planning, and
physics input

\- SBRT in a complex medical setting

\- Example: ventilated patient

\- Other special circumstances requiring time-consuming planning

\### Important Limitation

\- Special treatment procedure is \*\*not\*\* medically necessary for
uncomplicated SBRT

\- Example: single bone metastasis

\### Special Physics Consult - Medical Necessity Criteria

Covered when requested by a physician and documented for \*\*any\*\* of
the following:

\- Brachytherapy combined with external beam therapy

\- Special brachytherapy equipment customized by a qualified medical
physicist

\- Fusion of CT/MRI/PET image sets by the medical physicist

\- Dosimetric analysis of previous field overlapping or abutting current
field

\- Fetal dose analysis

\- Pacemaker dose analysis

\- SRS/SBRT with report of dosimetric parameters and organ tolerances
met or exceeded

\- Other specific physics work not otherwise coded

\### Frequency

\- Each may be billed only once per course of therapy.

\### Relevant Codes

\- CPT/HCPCS:

\- 77470 (special treatment procedure)

\- 77370 (special physics consult)

\## Bone Metastases

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\#### 2D/3D Conformal EBRT

Appropriate when \*\*any one\*\* of the following is present:

\- Pain at the metastatic site

\- Lytic lesion in a weight-bearing bone

\- Spinal cord compression

\- Postoperative treatment after surgical stabilization

\#### IMRT

Appropriate only when \*\*both\*\* apply:

\- Previously irradiated field

\- Re-treatment with EBRT would create significant risk of adjacent
organ injury

\#### SRS/SBRT

Appropriate only when \*\*both\*\* apply:

\- Previously irradiated field

\- Re-treatment with EBRT would create significant risk of adjacent
organ injury

\### Fractionation

\- Single fraction appropriate when:

\- Goal is pain relief

\- Fractionated radiotherapy, 2 to 10 fractions, appropriate when
\*\*any\*\* apply:

\- Pathologic fracture

\- Soft tissue involvement by tumor

\- Spinal cord compression

\- Spine metastasis

\- Oligometastatic disease (1 to 5 lesions) when goal is long-term
stabilization

\- More than 10 treatments is not medically necessary.

\### Relevant Codes

\- 2D/3D Conformal CPT/HCPCS:

\- 77295

\- 77402

\- 77407

\- 77412

\- G6003-G6014

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Fractionation beyond 10 treatments is not medically necessary.

\- If SRS/SBRT is requested for potentially curative oligometastatic
disease, separate oligometastatic criteria apply.

\## Breast Cancer

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\#### 2D/3D Conformal

Appropriate when \*\*any\*\* of the following apply:

\- Adjunct after lumpectomy for localized breast cancer or DCIS

\- Adjunct after mastectomy for locally advanced disease

\- Recurrent disease

\- Palliative treatment of metastatic disease, including symptomatic
breast or chest wall disease

\#### IMRT

Appropriate when \*\*any\*\* of the following apply:

\##### Left-sided breast lesions with excessive cardiac exposure risk

All of the following must be met:

\- 3D planning has been done using appropriate toxicity-limiting
techniques

\- Despite these techniques, unacceptable cardiac dose constraints
remain, with \*\*either\*\*:

\- Heart V25 \> 10%, or

\- LAD V15 \> 10%

\- IMRT plan improves exposure to within safe ranges

\##### Internal mammary node irradiation

Based on \*\*any\*\* of the following:

\- Pathologically enlarged internal mammary node(s) on imaging

\- Pathologically involved internal mammary node(s) on cytology/biopsy

\- High risk of internal mammary nodal involvement based on \*\*any\*\*:

\- Four or more positive axillary nodes

\- Medial quadrant tumor plus at least one positive axillary node

\- Medial quadrant T3 tumor

\##### Dosimetric hot spots

\- 3D conformal plan results in hot spots \> 2 cm3 receiving \> 110% of
prescribed dose despite forward field-in-field blocking and/or mixed
beam energy

\##### Other approved IMRT indications

\- Accelerated partial breast irradiation (APBI)

\- Previously irradiated field

\#### Brachytherapy

Appropriate only for:

\##### Intraoperative Radiation Therapy (IORT)

All of the following:

\- Age 50 or older

\- Tumor 3 cm or less with grossly uninvolved surgical margins

\- Lymph nodes grossly negative and negative on frozen section if
performed

\- Distance from applicator edge to skin at least 6 mm

\##### Accelerated Partial Breast Irradiation (APBI)

All of the following:

\- Age 40 or older for invasive disease, or over 50 for DCIS

\- Tumor 2 cm or less with pathologically negative margins

\- Nodes negative or only immunohistochemical involvement: N0 or N0(i+)

\- Distance from applicator edge to skin at least 6 mm

\#### SBRT

\- Appropriate only to treat a previously irradiated field

\### Fractionation

\- Whole breast irradiation (WBI) 17 to 28 fractions only when
\*\*any\*\* apply:

\- Need to treat supraclavicular or internal mammary nodes

\- Mastectomy or breast reconstruction performed

\- 3D conformal plan produces dose inhomogeneity \> 7% in central axis

\- Concurrent chemotherapy planned, excluding trastuzumab or endocrine
therapy

\- If none of the above are present:

\- Up to 16 fractions are medically necessary

\- Boost irradiation:

\- Up to 8 fractions if patient qualifies for 17 to 28 fraction WBI

\- Up to 5 fractions otherwise

\- More than 36 total fractions including WBI and boost is not medically
necessary.

\- APBI:

\- Up to 10 fractions, twice daily

\- More than 10 fractions is not medically necessary

\- IORT:

\- Single fraction only

\- More than one fraction is not medically necessary

\### Relevant Codes

\- 2D/3D Conformal CPT/HCPCS:

\- 77295

\- 77402

\- 77407

\- 77412

\- G6003-G6014

\- Common IMRT code family used in guideline:

\- 77301

\- 77338

\- 77385/G6015/G6016 as applicable

\- Brachytherapy codes are listed in the appendix by
delivery/handling/isodose and anatomic service groupers.

\### Limitations and Exclusions

\- Electronic brachytherapy is not medically necessary.

\- Five-fraction APBI should not be billed as SBRT.

\## Lung Cancer

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\#### 2D/3D Conformal

Appropriate when \*\*any\*\* apply:

\- Primary lung cancers for adjuvant, neoadjuvant, or definitive local
treatment

\- Palliation of metastatic lung lesions, especially symptomatic tumors
needing local control

\- Prophylactic cranial irradiation when indicated

\### Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC)

\#### IMRT

Appropriate when \*\*any\*\* apply:

\- Adjuvant or definitive treatment of stage I or II disease in curative
setting when 3D planning shows unacceptable normal lung toxicity and
\*\*all\*\* apply:

\- V20 \> 30% with 3D conformal plan

\- IMRT reduces V20 by 10% compared with 3D plan

\- V5 with IMRT would be \< 65%

\- Tumor motion accounted for in planning

\- Adjuvant or definitive treatment of stage I or II disease when 3D
plan shows unacceptable cardiac toxicity and \*\*any\*\* apply:

\- Heart V30 \> 50%

\- Heart V45 \> 35%

\- Heart V50 \> 25%

\- LAD V15 \> 10%

\- Adjuvant or definitive treatment of stage III disease in curative
setting

\- Previously irradiated field

\#### SBRT

Appropriate when \*\*any\*\* apply:

\- Alternative to surgical resection when \*\*all\*\* apply:

\- Curative intent

\- No nodal or distant metastases by conventional staging

\- Stage IA, IB, or IIA with negative nodes

\- Single lesion 5 cm or less

\- Lesion inoperable due to:

\- Tumor location, or

\- Patient not being a surgical candidate

\- Previously irradiated field

\#### Endobronchial Brachytherapy

Appropriate when \*\*any\*\* apply:

\- Unresectable primary bronchial tumors not manageable with standard
external beam techniques

\- Palliative treatment of obstructing endobronchial tumors

\### Small Cell Lung Cancer (SCLC)

\#### IMRT

Appropriate when \*\*any\*\* apply:

\- Definitive curative treatment when normal lung dose or cardiac dose
constraints are exceeded on 3D plan using the same dose-constraint
standards above

\- Previously irradiated field

\#### SBRT

Appropriate when \*\*any\*\* apply:

\- Alternative to surgery when:

\- Curative intent

\- Stage IA, IB, or IIA with negative nodes

\- Single lesion 5 cm or less

\- Inoperable due to tumor location or surgical candidacy

\- Previously irradiated field

\#### Endobronchial Brachytherapy

Appropriate when \*\*any\*\* apply:

\- Unresectable primary bronchial tumors not treatable with standard
external beam techniques

\- Palliative treatment of obstructing endobronchial tumors

\### Metastatic Lesions in the Lung

\#### IMRT

\- Previously irradiated field only

\#### SBRT

Appropriate when \*\*either\*\* applies:

\- Oligometastatic disease

\- Previously irradiated field

\#### Endobronchial Brachytherapy

\- Palliative treatment of obstructing endobronchial tumors

\### Fractionation

\- NSCLC stage I-III with concurrent chemoradiotherapy:

\- Up to 35 fractions

\- Palliative stage IV NSCLC:

\- Up to 15 treatments

\- Curative stage IV NSCLC with oligometastatic disease:

\- Up to 35 treatments

\- Maximum SBRT fractions:

\- 5

\## Other Malignancies

\### IMRT

Appropriate when \*\*either\*\* applies:

\- Risk of critical structure exposure would be excessive with 3D
conformal and \*\*both\*\* apply:

\- 3D planning was done with toxicity-limiting techniques but
organ-at-risk limits were exceeded

\- IMRT improves exposure to safe ranges

\- Previously irradiated field

\### QUANTEC Organ-at-Risk Limits Referenced

\- Lung V20 \<= 30%

\- Heart V25 \<= 10%

\- Pericardium:

\- Mean dose \<= 26 Gy, or

\- V30 \< 46%

\- Spinal cord:

\- Mean dose \<= 45 Gy, or

\- Maximum dose 50 Gy

\- Esophagus mean dose \< 34 Gy

\- Small bowel Dmax \< 54 Gy

\- Liver mean dose \< 30 Gy

\- Kidney mean dose \< 18 Gy

\- If one kidney, \< 15% receives 18 Gy

\### SBRT

\- Previously irradiated field only

\## Prostate Cancer

\### Disease Definitions

\- Low risk: all must be present

\- T1-T2a

\- Gleason 6

\- PSA \< 10 ng/mL

\- Intermediate risk: any one

\- T2b-T2c

\- Gleason 7

\- PSA 10-20 ng/mL

\- High risk: any one

\- T3a

\- Gleason 8-10

\- PSA \> 20 ng/mL

\- Localized disease:

\- T1-3a and N0

\- Locally advanced disease:

\- Any T with N1, or

\- T3b and above without distant metastasis beyond local nodes

\- Distant metastatic disease:

\- Beyond local lymph nodes

\### Medical Necessity Criteria

\#### 2D/3D Conformal

Appropriate when \*\*any\*\* apply:

\- Primary treatment of prostate cancer

\- Palliative treatment of advanced disease

\#### Low-Risk Prostate Cancer

\- IMRT:

\- Primary treatment, or

\- Previously irradiated field

\- SBRT:

\- Primary treatment, or

\- Previously irradiated field

\- Brachytherapy monotherapy:

\- LDR or HDR

\#### Intermediate-Risk Prostate Cancer

\- IMRT:

\- Primary treatment or in combination with brachytherapy, or

\- Previously irradiated field

\- SBRT:

\- Primary treatment, or

\- Previously irradiated field

\- Brachytherapy:

\- Monotherapy or boost with EBRT

\- LDR or HDR

\#### High-Risk Prostate Cancer

\- IMRT:

\- Primary treatment or in combination with brachytherapy, or

\- Previously irradiated field

\- SBRT:

\- Previously irradiated field only

\- Brachytherapy:

\- In combination with EBRT

\- LDR or HDR

\#### Post-Prostatectomy

IMRT appropriate when \*\*any\*\* apply:

\- Adjuvant therapy with no metastatic disease and \*\*either\*\*:

\- Detectable PSA, or

\- Adverse pathologic feature:

\- pT3 disease

\- Positive margin(s)

\- Gleason 8-10

\- Seminal vesicle involvement/invasion

\- Extracapsular extension

\- Salvage therapy when:

\- Previously undetectable PSA becomes detectable and rises on 2 or more
measurements

\- Previously irradiated field

SBRT:

\- Previously irradiated field only

\#### Local Recurrence After Radiotherapy

\- IMRT:

\- Locally recurrent disease without distant metastasis

\- SBRT:

\- Locally recurrent disease without distant metastasis

\- Brachytherapy:

\- LDR or HDR for locally recurrent disease without distant metastasis

\### Fractionation

\- Localized disease without pelvic node treatment:

\- 60 Gy in 20 fractions, or

\- 70 Gy in 28 fractions

\- Significant baseline obstructive urinary symptoms:

\- Up to 39 fractions

\- Localized or locally recurrent disease with pelvic node treatment:

\- Up to 28 fractions

\- Adjuvant treatment to prostate bed after prostatectomy:

\- Up to 32 fractions

\- Salvage treatment after prostatectomy:

\- Up to 37 fractions

\- Active surveillance is a reasonable alternative for low-risk disease.

\## Key Limitations and Global Exclusions

\- Services not fitting listed criteria are not medically necessary.

\- Electronic brachytherapy is repeatedly identified as not medically
necessary in multiple sections.

\- IGRT is not medically necessary with superficial x-rays/electron beam
therapy for non-melanoma skin cancer.

\- Neutron therapy is excluded unless specifically addressed.

\- Nonspecific or unlisted procedure codes may require additional
documentation and review.

\- Authorization requirements may vary by health plan.

\- Inclusion of a code in the guideline does not by itself guarantee
coverage or payment.

\## Practical Submission Checklist

\- Confirm diagnosis and disease site

\- Include stage/risk group where relevant

\- Document prior treatments and prior irradiation

\- Include planning studies and dose comparison when required

\- Document organ-at-risk constraints if IMRT is requested based on
toxicity reduction

\- Document motion management when relevant

\- Include pathology, margins, lymph node status, PSA, or imaging
findings when applicable

\- Provide specific rationale for:

\- SBRT

\- re-irradiation

\- image guidance

\- special treatment procedure

\- special physics consult

\-\--

\## Bottom Line

This guideline supports radiation therapy when the requested modality is
matched to:

\- Confirmed clinical indication

\- Disease site and stage/risk

\- Prior treatment history

\- Dosimetric need

\- Documented benefit over simpler alternatives

\# Perirectal Hydrogel Spacer for Prostate Radiotherapy - Summary

\## Document Overview

\- Topic: Perirectal hydrogel spacer for prostate radiotherapy

\- Service type: Radiation oncology

\- Focus: Medical necessity for implanted hydrogel spacer placement
between the prostate and rectum

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\### General Clinical Appropriateness Requirements

All of the following general requirements must be met:

\- The diagnosis must be confirmed, or the pretest likelihood must be
established, based on a complete patient evaluation.

\- Evaluation should include:

\- History

\- Physical examination

\- Relevant laboratory studies, when applicable

\- Relevant diagnostic testing, when applicable

\- Response to prior therapeutic intervention, when applicable

\- The expected benefit of the requested intervention must outweigh
potential harms.

\- Current treatment guidelines, clinical literature, and/or standards
of medical practice should support the requested intervention as the
best option among alternatives.

\- There must be a reasonable likelihood that the intervention will
change management and/or improve patient outcomes.

\### Specific Clinical Indication: Hydrogel Spacer

Use of an implanted hydrogel spacer between the prostate and rectum is
medically necessary when:

\- Primary definitive radiation therapy will be used to treat prostate
cancer, and

\- The radiation treatment is delivered using any form of external beam
radiation therapy, including:

\- 3D conformal radiation therapy

\- Intensity-modulated radiation therapy (IMRT)

\- Stereotactic body radiation therapy (SBRT)

\## Clinical Definitions Referenced in the Policy

\### Low Risk of Recurrence

All must be present:

\- Stage T1-T2a

\- Gleason score 6

\- PSA below 10 ng/mL

\### Intermediate Risk of Recurrence

Any one characteristic:

\- Stage T2b to T2c

\- Gleason score 7

\- PSA 10-20 ng/mL

\### High Risk of Recurrence

Any one characteristic:

\- Stage T3a

\- Gleason score 8-10

\- PSA greater than 20 ng/mL

\### Localized Disease

Both must apply:

\- T stage T1-3a

\- N0

\### Locally Advanced Disease

Either must apply:

\- Any T status with N1 disease

\- T3b and above, without distant metastatic disease beyond local lymph
nodes

\### Distant Metastatic Disease

\- Disease beyond the local lymph nodes

\## Limitations and Exclusions

\### Service-Specific Limits

\- Medical necessity is limited to use with \*\*primary definitive
external beam radiation therapy\*\* for prostate cancer.

\- The document does \*\*not\*\* identify hydrogel spacer use as
medically necessary for brachytherapy alone.

\- Nonspecific or unlisted procedure codes may require additional review
and documentation.

\### Repeat Diagnostic Intervention Limits

Repeated testing of the same anatomic area for the same indication is
generally limited to:

\- Follow-up after an intervention, or

\- A change in clinical status that requires additional testing for
management decisions

Additional review or peer-to-peer discussion may be required for:

\- Repeat testing at the same facility due to technical problems

\- Repeat testing at a different facility due to provider preference or
quality concerns

\- Repeat testing for persistent symptoms without clinical change,
treatment, or intervention since the prior study

time period

\### Repeat Therapeutic Intervention Limits

\- Repeat therapeutic intervention in the same anatomic area is
generally appropriate only when the prior intervention was beneficial
and the expected duration of relief has elapsed.

\- A repeat intervention requested before the expected duration of
benefit is not appropriate unless it can be confirmed that the prior
intervention was never performed.

\- Ongoing services may depend on completion of previously authorized
services when patient response affects appropriateness.

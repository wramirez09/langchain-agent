DOCUMENT SUMMARY

Common documentation requirements across sections

-   Submit documentation supporting medical necessity at the time of
    > request.

    > Documentation generally needs to show:

    -   Current clinical status and ordering intent

        > History and physical exam

        > Relevant prior testing, imaging, labs when applicable

        > Response to prior treatment/interventions

        > Conservative management already attempted when required

    > Conservative management generally must include:

    -   Physical therapy, supervised home therapy, or a clearly
        > documented exception

        > At least one complementary strategy such as
        > anti-inflammatory/analgesic medication, adjunctive medication,
        > acupuncture, chiropractic manipulation, massage, activity
        > modification, or rest when appropriate

    > Failure of conservative management generally requires:

    -   Completion of a full course

        > Worsening or no significant improvement on reevaluation

        > Consideration of more invasive therapy

    > Symptom severity/functional impairment standard used repeatedly:

    -   Pain at least 3/10

        > Inability to perform at least 2 ADLs and/or IADLs

    > Imaging, where required:

    -   Must be read by an independent radiologist

        > Must correlate with the clinical findings

    > Additional general note:

    -   Repeated diagnostic or therapeutic interventions may require
        > additional review if repeated without change in status or if
        > done prematurely.

1.  Epidural and intradiscal injection procedures and diagnostic
    > selective nerve root blocks Medical necessity criteria

-   Therapeutic epidural steroid injection may be medically necessary
    > when all are met:

    -   Significant radicular pain, radiculopathy, or lumbar neurogenic
        > claudication with functional impairment and completed physical
        > exam

        > Advanced imaging shows either:

        -   Nerve root compression from herniated disc, with imaging
            > within the prior 18 months, or

            > Spinal stenosis

        > Symptoms failed at least 4 weeks of appropriate conservative
        > management

        > If there is clear evidence of radiculopathy, injection may be
        > done after 2 weeks of conservative management

        > Initial lumbar epidural may proceed without confirmatory
        > advanced imaging if symptoms and exam clearly diagnose nerve
        > root compression or stenosis

    > Repeat therapeutic epidural steroid injection may be medically
    > necessary when all are met:

    -   Significant radicular pain, radiculopathy, or lumbar neurogenic
        > claudication with functional impairment

        > Prior injection produced at least 50% pain reduction and
        > functional improvement for at least 3 months

        > Imaging confirms nerve root compression or stenosis at
        > requested levels/laterality and correlates with clinical
        > findings

        > For herniated nucleus pulposus, imaging should be within the
        > prior 18 months

        > Conservative management continues between injections

    > Diagnostic selective nerve root block may be medically necessary
    > when all are met:

    -   Significant radicular pain or radiculopathy with functional
        > impairment

        > Advanced imaging completed

        > Failure of at least 4 weeks of conservative management

        > Surgery is being considered and documented by surgical consult
        > for one of:

        -   Confirming a compressed nerve root seen on imaging

            > Determining the most symptomatic level with multilevel
            > disease

            > Confirming suspected radiculopathy not proven on imaging

Required documentation

-   Physical exam

    > Advanced imaging when required

    > Conservative management details

    > For repeat epidural injections:

    -   Follow-up evaluation documenting at least 50% pain relief and
        > functional improvement lasting at least 3 months

    > If a second early epidural is requested after suboptimal first
    > response:

    -   Rationale and medical necessity must be documented in the
        > medical record

    > Steroid cumulative dose must be documented after 3 injections in
    > the same region

Procedural requirements, limitations, and exclusions

-   Must use fluoroscopy or CT guidance with contrast unless
    > contraindicated

    > Only 1 spinal region per session

    > Maximum 4 therapeutic injection sessions per spinal region per
    > rolling 12 months

    > If first injection in a newly diagnosed patient fails to provide
    > at least 50% pain relief for 3 months:

    -   One-time second injection allowed no sooner than 2 weeks later

        > Different approach, level, and/or steroid if appropriate

    > No more than 2 transforaminal injections in one setting

    > Only 1 caudal or interlaminar injection per session and not with
    > transforaminal injection

    > Epidural injections generally should not be combined with other
    > spinal procedures except special circumstances such as large facet
    > synovial cyst/effusion compressing a nerve root

    > Steroid cumulative dose limits after 3 injections in same region:

    -   240 mg methylprednisolone or triamcinolone

        > 36 mg betamethasone

        > 45 mg dexamethasone

    > Guideline does not apply to:

    -   Epidural injections without corticosteroid for postoperative
        > pain management

        > Epidural injection used for implantable infusion pump trial

    > Contraindications prompting further evaluation or excluding
    > treatment include:

    -   Suspected malignancy

        > Risk factors for spinal infection or osteoporotic fracture

        > Coagulopathy/anticoagulation

        > Acute trauma

        > Cauda equina syndrome

        > Conus medullaris syndrome

        > Inadequate epidural space at target level

        > Epidural hematoma/abscess/mass

        > Subarachnoid hemorrhage

        > Spinal cord ischemia

        > Spinal fracture less than 6 weeks old

        > Demyelinating disease or other CNS disorder predisposing to
        > transverse myelitis

        > Systemic infection

        > Local infection at injection site

        > Uncontrolled diabetes

    > Not medically necessary / excluded:

    -   Moderate-to-severe myelopathy

        > Myelopathy with intramedullary cord signal change on MRI

        > Isolated axial neck, thoracic, or low back pain

        > Intradiscal chemonucleolysis injections

        > Injection/infusion of neurolytic substance into subarachnoid
        > or epidural space

        > Diagnostic SNRB at more than 1 level in a single session

        > More than 2 SNRB sessions

        > Combined multiple spinal injections in one session except
        > limited special circumstance above

        > Epidural steroid injections with biologics or other substances
        > not FDA approved/conditionally approved for this use

        > Percutaneous intervertebral disc injection of allogeneic
        > cellular/tissue-based product

2\. Paravertebral facet injection / medial branch block / neurolysis
Medical necessity criteria Patient requirements for facet interventions

-   All of the following:

    -   Moderate-to-severe pain with functional impairment for at least
        > 3 months

        > Predominant axial pain not due to radiculopathy, myelopathy,
        > or neurogenic claudication

        > Physical exam consistent with facet pain source

        > No non-facet pathology explaining pain

        > No prior surgical fusion at proposed level

        > No improvement after at least 6 weeks of conservative
        > management

    > Procedures must be done with fluoroscopy or CT guidance.

Diagnostic medial branch block criteria

-   Positive response defined as at least 80% relief of primary pain
    > with onset/duration consistent with local anesthetic

    > Patient must be in pain at time of injection, generally at least
    > 3/10

    > Provocative maneuvers/positions should be documented before and
    > after

    > Local anesthetic only; steroids are not medically necessary for
    > diagnostic block

    > Dual medial branch blocks are required:

    -   Same locations

        > Two separate occasions

        > At least 1 week apart

    > Confirmatory injection only if first block gives positive response

    > Max 4 diagnostic joint sessions per covered spinal region per
    > rolling 12 months

    > Max 2 diagnostic sessions per level per year for RFA candidacy

    > Max 2 levels per session, unilateral or bilateral

    > A single or dual diagnostic MBB may also be medically necessary to
    > confirm pars defect as primary pain generator for low back pain,
    > except isolated pars defects in young athletes, and requires
    > documentation of planned surgery or surgical consult.

Therapeutic intra-articular facet injection criteria

-   Generally not medically necessary for facetogenic axial low back
    > pain

    > Exception may be medically necessary when all facet patient
    > requirements are met and any of the following are documented:

    -   Suspected inflammatory facetogenic pain from systemic
        > inflammatory arthropathy

        > Denervation is contraindicated

        > Patient is at increased risk of complication from
        > radiofrequency neurotomy

        > Nerve root compression due to facet synovial cyst or large
        > effusion, plus:

        -   MRI/CT within previous 12 months correlating with findings

            > Moderate-to-severe radicular pain and functional
            > limitations

            > Cyst aspiration/rupture may be repeated once in 12 months
            > only if there was at least 50% pain reduction for at least
            > 3 months

    > For conditions 1 to 3:

    -   Repeat same-site injection allowed only if prior injection gave
        > at least 50% pain reduction and functional improvement for at
        > least 3 months

        > Max 4 therapeutic IA facet sessions per spinal region per
        > rolling 12 months

        > Max 2 levels per spinal region per session

Thermal medial branch radiofrequency neurotomy criteria

-   May be offered when dual diagnostic medial branch blocks within last
    > 6 months each provided at least 80% relief and response consistent
    > with anesthetic used

    > Same level may be treated no more than twice annually and only if
    > initial lesion gave:

    -   At least 50% pain relief

        > Improvement in patient-specific ADLs for at least 6 months

    > Max 2 levels per spinal region per session

    > Repeat RFN for recurrent pain may be medically necessary without
    > repeating diagnostic blocks if prior RFA gave:

    -   At least 50% pain reduction for at least 6 months

        > Improvement in function

    > Max 2 radiofrequency sessions per covered spinal region per
    > rolling 12 months

    > Not allowed at C0-C1 or C1-C2

Intraosseous basivertebral nerve ablation criteria

-   All of the following:

    -   Skeletally mature patient

        > Chronic lumbar back pain at least 6 months

        > VAS at least 4 on most days

        > Significant functional impairment with ODI at least 30

        > Failure of at least 6 consecutive months of nonsurgical
        > management

        > Other pain sources ruled out

        > MRI shows Modic type I or II endplate changes from L3-S1

        > MRI shows no other non-vertebrogenic pathology explaining pain

        > Statement from PCP, neurologist, physiatrist, psychiatrist,
        > psychologist, or other licensed behavioral/medical provider
        > attesting there is no untreated underlying mental health
        > condition that is a major contributor to the chronic pain

Required documentation

-   Conservative treatment history and failure

    > Pain severity and functional impairment

    > Physical exam findings consistent with facet source

    > Imaging correlation

    > For diagnostic MBB:

    -   Pain level at time of injection

        > Provocative maneuvers before and after

        > Response to first block before confirmatory block

    > For pars defect indication:

    -   Planned surgery or surgical consultation

    > For therapeutic IA injection:

    -   Specific qualifying exception documented

        > Imaging within 12 months for synovial cyst/large effusion
        > scenario

    > For BVN ablation:

    -   MRI showing qualifying Modic changes and no alternate cause

        > ODI and VAS scores

        > Mental health attestation letter

        > Evidence other pain sources ruled out

Limitations and exclusions

-   Not medically necessary:

    -   Diagnostic MBB at same level as previously successful RFA,
        > except after long interval or uncertain pain source

        > Diagnostic intra-articular facet injection

        > Diagnostic MBB for reasons other than RFA candidacy

        > Therapeutic medial branch block using corticosteroid

        > Therapeutic IA facet injection for indications other than
        > listed exceptions

        > Thoracic facet injection/MBB/RFA except C7-T1 and T12-L1

        > Diagnostic MBB, therapeutic IA injection, or RFN at C0-C1 or
        > C1-C2

        > MBB or RFN with grade 3+ spondylolisthesis

        > MBB or RFN for isolated pars defect except presurgical
        > diagnostic workup

        > MBB, IA facet injection, or RFN at level of prior surgical
        > fusion

        > Chemical neurolysis

        > Laser neurolysis

        > Open surgical neurolysis

        > Endoscopic neurolysis/rhizotomy

        > Cryodenervation

        > Low-grade thermal RF less than 80 degrees C

        > Pulsed RF denervation

        > Any facet intervention under ultrasound guidance

        > Intraarticular or extraarticular facet joint prolotherapy

    > BVN ablation exclusions/contraindications:

    -   Skeletally immature

        > Severe cardiac or pulmonary compromise

        > Concurrent vertebral augmentation at intended levels

        > Modic changes outside L3-S1

        > Radicular pain

        > Prior lumbar surgery at intended level, except limited
        > circumstances

        > Symptomatic spinal stenosis

        > Osteoporosis, metabolic bone disease, fragility fracture
        > history, compression fracture/trauma, or spinal cancer

        > Spine or systemic infection

        > Bleeding diathesis

        > Other radiographic pain etiology including large disc
        > extrusion/protrusion, spondylolisthesis greater than 2 mm,
        > spondylolysis, facet arthrosis/effusion correlated with pain

        > Extended-release opioid use with addiction behaviors

        > BMI greater than 40

        > MRI contraindication, device allergy, or active implantable
        > devices

        > Pregnancy/lactation

        > Repeat procedure at same level after prior intraosseous
        > ablation

3\. Regional sympathetic nerve block Medical necessity criteria

-   Procedure must be image-guided with fluoroscopy or CT

    > Used for CRPS type I or II when diagnostic criteria are met:

    -   Continuing pain disproportionate to any inciting event

        > At least 1 symptom in at least 3 of 4 categories:

        -   Sensory

            > Vasomotor

            > Sudomotor/edema

            > Motor/trophic

        > At least 1 sign at evaluation in at least 2 categories:

        -   Sensory

            > Vasomotor

            > Sudomotor/edema

            > Motor/trophic

        > No better diagnosis explaining the symptoms

        > Plus all of:

        -   Moderate-to-severe pain/disability

            > Failure of at least 2 weeks of conservative management

            > Ongoing participation in comprehensive pain management
            > program

            > Procedure performed unilaterally

    > Initial diagnostic block is medically necessary to determine if
    > CRPS is sympathetically mediated

    > Therapeutic blocks after positive diagnostic response may be
    > medically necessary if:

    -   Prior block showed benefit with decreased pain medication use

        > Improved function

        > Improved tolerance to touch or other objective measures

        > Continued participation in comprehensive pain management
        > program

        > Maximum 6 blocks lifetime

Required documentation

-   CRPS symptom/sign documentation by category

    > Documentation that no alternative diagnosis better explains the
    > presentation

    > Documentation of moderate-to-severe pain/disability

    > Conservative management history

    > Ongoing comprehensive pain management participation

    > For initial diagnostic block:

    -   At least 80% pain reduction for duration of anesthetic

        > Functional improvement

        > Objective evidence physiologic block was effective

        > For limb procedures, temperature rise of at least 2 degrees C
        > in ipsilateral limb

        > Sensory exam showing no spread to adjacent nerve roots

    > For therapeutic blocks:

    -   Evidence of prior benefit

        > Ongoing comprehensive program documentation

Limitations and exclusions

-   Maximum 6 blocks per lifetime

    > No more than 2 per week

    > Only one unilateral sympathetic block per session

    > Stellate ganglion block cannot be performed same day as lumbar
    > sympathetic block

    > Not medically necessary:

    -   Sympathetic block coding for other blocks such as ganglion impar
        > block

4\. Sacroiliac joint injection Medical necessity criteria Patient
requirements for diagnostic or therapeutic intra-articular SI joint
injection

-   All of the following:

    -   Persistent, typically unilateral, non-radicular pain
        > predominantly below L5 and localized to SI joint region for at
        > least 3 months

        > Localized tenderness over sacral sulcus just inferior to PSIS
        > without equally severe tenderness elsewhere

        > At least one positive provocative test:

        -   Pelvic distraction

            > Lateral iliac compression

            > Sacral compression/thrust

            > Thigh thrust

            > FABER

            > Gaenslen

        > No acute/subacute radicular pain, radiculopathy, or neurogenic
        > claudication; if present, must be treated, stable, and
        > maximally optimized before SI intervention

        > Lack of adequate improvement after 6 weeks of conservative
        > management

    > Procedures must use fluoroscopy or CT with contrast to confirm
    > intra-articular placement unless documented contrast allergy

    > Diagnostic SI injection:

    -   Anesthetic only

        > Used to determine whether SI joint is the primary pain
        > generator in anticipation of planned SI fusion

        > Second confirmatory injection only if first gives at least 75%
        > primary pain relief with response consistent with anesthetic

        > Pain must be at least 3/10 on day of procedure

        > Maximum 2 unilateral diagnostic injections on separate
        > occasions

    > Therapeutic SI injection:

    -   Corticosteroid with or without anesthetic

    > Repeat therapeutic SI injection:

    -   Medically necessary if symptoms recur and prior injection gave
        > at least 50% pain relief and improved patient-specific ADLs
        > for at least 3 months

        > Repeat physical exam must reconfirm SI joint source

        > Must be accompanied by ongoing active rehabilitation/home
        > exercise/functional restoration program

Required documentation

-   Pain location and duration

    > Exam tenderness over SI region

    > Positive provocative testing

    > Documentation that radicular pain/radiculopathy/neurogenic
    > claudication is absent or already addressed/stable

    > Conservative management details

    > Imaging guidance/contrast use or documented contrast allergy

    > Diagnostic injection:

    -   Pre-injection pain level

        > Response to first injection if requesting confirmatory block

    > Therapeutic injection:

    -   If local anesthetic and steroid are both used, documentation of
        > biphasic response

        > Pain diary for first 24 hours if steroid administered

    > Repeat therapeutic injection:

    -   Prior at least 50% pain relief and ADL improvement lasting at
        > least 3 months

        > Repeat physical exam confirming SI joint as source

        > Participation in rehabilitation/home exercise/functional
        > restoration program

Limitations and exclusions

-   Diagnostic anesthetic volume limited to 1.5 cc

    > Diagnostic injection should not include steroid

    > Concurrent injection in other spinal areas is not appropriate for
    > diagnostic SI injection

    > Therapeutic total injection volume limited to 2.5 cc

    > Repeats no more often than every 3 months

    > Maximum 4 injections in 12 months

    > Ultrasound guidance is only appropriate during pregnancy

    > Not medically necessary:

    -   Intra-articular SI injection same day as other spine injections

        > Any substance other than corticosteroid with or without local
        > anesthetic for therapeutic SI injection

        > Therapeutic intra/periarticular injection in a previously
        > fused SI joint

        > Corticosteroid with diagnostic SI injection

        > Diagnostic or therapeutic sacral lateral branch blocks

        > Radiofrequency ablation of sacral lateral branch by any method

5\. Spinal cord stimulation and dorsal root ganglion stimulation Medical
necessity criteria Stimulator trial

-   All of the following:

    -   Chronic intractable neuropathic pain of trunk and/or limbs
        > associated with at least one of:

        -   Lumbosacral arachnoiditis documented by CSF protein
            > elevation and/or MRI/myelography

            > Post-surgical nerve root injuries after spine surgery

            > CRPS type I or II meeting CRPS diagnostic criteria

            > Painful diabetic neuropathy with all of:

            -   Painful PDN for at least 12 months

                > Lower limb pain intensity at least 5 on VAS

                > Moderate-severe neuropathy on EMG/NCS

                > Confirmation by at least one other specialist

                > BMI 35 or less

                > HbA1c 10% or less

                > Morphine equivalents 120 mg/day or less

                > Medical clearance as candidate

                > Other causes of neuropathy ruled out

                > Medical optimization of diabetes/other contributors

                > Failed or intolerant to multiple pharmacologic agents
                > in at least 2 categories

                > No upper limb pain intensity of 3 or more on VAS

        > Severe pain and disability with documented pathology or
        > objective basis for pain

        > Used as late/last resort after failure of at least 6
        > consecutive months of physician-supervised multimodal
        > conservative management

        > Failed regional sympathetic blocks if CRPS

        > No untreated drug addiction

        > Evaluated by pain management specialist before implantation

        > Required facilities/equipment/personnel available

        > At least one surgical opinion showing no surgically
        > correctable lesion, except CRPS and PDN

        > Mental health evaluation within 6 months confirming no
        > inadequately controlled mental health problem that would
        > reduce success or contraindicate placement

    > Repeat trial is not medically necessary if initial trial failed,
    > unless failure was due to inability to place percutaneous lead
    > because of anatomy

    > Trial should use percutaneous leads except in limited
    > circumstances such as prior fusion or narrow canal Permanent
    > implantation

    > All of the following:

    -   Meets all trial criteria

        > Trial lasted at least 3 days

        > Documented pain reduction and functional improvement with at
        > least 50% reduction of target pain or analgesic medication use
        > and specific evidence of improved function Revision or removal

    > Medically necessary for:

    -   Lead migration

        > Infection

        > Painful generator site

        > Loss of effectiveness

        > Patient intolerance

        > New neurologic deficits

        > Need for MRI when alternative tests are inconclusive

        > Automatic implantable cardioverter defibrillator DRG
        > stimulation

    > May be medically necessary as an alternative to dorsal column
    > stimulation in moderate-to-severe chronic intractable lower limb
    > pain from CRPS I or II if the patient otherwise meets spinal cord
    > stimulator criteria

Required documentation

-   Diagnosis and qualifying pain condition

    > Evidence of objective pathology

    > Full conservative management history and failure over at least 6
    > months

    > For PDN:

    -   EMG/NCS

        > Specialist confirmation

        > BMI

        > HbA1c

        > Opioid dose

        > Medical clearance

        > Rule-out of alternative neuropathy causes

        > Optimization of underlying medical contributors

        > Medication trial failure/intolerance details

        > VAS documentation for lower and upper limb pain

    > For CRPS:

    -   Failed regional sympathetic block trial

    > Pain management specialist evaluation

    > Surgical opinion when required

    > Mental health evaluation within 6 months

    > Trial response documenting at least 50% reduction in pain or
    > analgesic use plus functional improvement

    > For revision/removal:

    -   Complication or contraindication documentation

Limitations and exclusions

-   Not medically necessary:

    -   Use for critical limb ischemia to forestall amputation

        > Refractory angina

        > Heart failure

        > Cancer-related pain

        > Repeat trial after failed initial trial

        > Replacing conventional SCS with burst/high-frequency/DRG
        > device without indication for removal

        > DRG stimulation for non-CRPS lower-extremity indications

        > DRG stimulation for CRPS lower extremity when patient has
        > functioning SCS or previously failed SCS

        > Simultaneous dorsal column and DRG stimulator placement

        > Chronic nonsurgical low back pain, with or without neuropathic
        > component, in patients with no prior lumbar spine surgery

GENERAL DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS The general documentation framework
in the retrieved policy sections requires the following support for
medical necessity:

-   A clear plan of care

    > Clinical history and physical exam

    > Relevant imaging and diagnostic testing that correlate with
    > symptoms

    > Documentation of prior treatment and response

    > Evidence that the requested intervention is expected to improve
    > management or outcome

    > When conservative treatment is required:

    -   Physical therapy or a supervised home exercise program

        > At least one additional conservative measure, such as:

        -   anti-inflammatory medication or analgesics

            > adjunctive medications

            > epidural steroid injection

            > activity modification, rest, massage, chiropractic
            > manipulation, or acupuncture

        > Documentation of compliance with treatment

        > Clinical reevaluation showing no meaningful improvement or
        > worsening

    > Pain/function documentation:

    -   significant pain generally means at least 3/10

        > associated functional impairment affecting at least 2
        > ADLs/IADLs in the general spine framework

    > Imaging must be read by an independent radiologist and support the
    > requested procedure

    > Conservative treatment may be waived when there is progressive
    > neurologic compromise, myelopathy, bladder disturbance, cauda
    > equina syndrome, or conus medullaris syndrome, depending on the
    > section

1.  CERVICAL DISC ARTHROPLASTY

Medical Necessity Criteria

-   Radiculopathy

    -   One- or two-level degenerative disease from C3-C4 through C6-C7

        > With or without neck pain

        > All of the following:

        -   objective neurologic findings that match cervical nerve root
            > impingement, or progressive/severe neurologic deficit, or
            > persistent radicular pain not responding to at least 6
            > weeks of conservative care

            > imaging shows nerve root compression from disc herniation
            > or spondylotic osteophyte matching symptoms

            > skeletally mature patient

            > FDA-approved cervical artificial disc used according to
            > labeling through an anterior approach

    > Myelopathy or myeloradiculopathy

    -   One- or two-level degenerative disease from C3-C4 through C6-C7

        > With or without neck pain

        > All of the following:

        -   clinical signs/symptoms of myelopathy

            > imaging confirms cervical cord compression

            > skeletally mature patient

            > FDA-approved device used according to labeling through an
            > anterior approach

    > Failed cervical disc arthroplasty: revision or replacement at the
    > index level

    -   Either:

        -   imaging shows implant failure and symptoms are attributable
            > to that failure

            > or symptoms persist/recur without implant failure, but
            > radiculopathy or myelopathy criteria are still met

    > Two-level cervical arthroplasty

    -   Allowed for two contiguous levels, either at the same surgery or
        > as a second contiguous level after prior arthroplasty

        > Each level must independently meet criteria

        > Device must be approved for two levels

Relevant Codes

-   CPT/HCPCS:

    -   0095T

        > 0098T

        > 22856

        > 22858

        > 22861

        > 22864

Required Documentation

-   Objective neurologic findings, if present

    > Imaging showing nerve root or cord compression

    > Skeletal maturity

    > Failure of conservative treatment when required

    > Device type and confirmation it is FDA-approved for the intended
    > indication/level(s)

    > For revision/replacement:

    -   standard or advanced imaging showing implant failure, if
        > applicable

        > symptom recurrence/persistence documentation

    > Plan of care and symptom-function impact documentation

Limitations / Exclusions

-   Contraindications include:

    -   active infection

        > osteoporosis with DEXA T-score -2.5 or lower

        > marked cervical instability

        > compromised vertebral bodies due to trauma, deformity, or
        > malignancy

        > focal kyphosis

        > moderate/severe spondylosis

        > severe facet arthropathy

        > OPLL

        > allergy/sensitivity to implant materials

    > Exclusions include:

    -   more than 2 levels

        > 2 noncontiguous levels

        > previous fusion at another cervical level

        > hybrid fusion plus arthroplasty in one procedure

        > levels outside C3-C4 through C6-C7

2\. LUMBAR DISC ARTHROPLASTY

Medical Necessity Criteria

-   All of the following are required:

    -   age 18 to 60

        > primary complaint is axial back pain of discogenic origin

        > symptoms for at least 6 months

        > failure of a multifaceted conservative treatment program over
        > that period

        > single-level disease, or dual-level disease only when using a
        > two-level FDA-approved implant

        > treated level must be L3-L4, L4-L5, or L5-S1

        > MRI and plain radiographs show advanced disc disease with
        > moderate to severe degeneration and Modic changes

        > at least moderate pain/disability, ideally VAS 40/100 or 4/10
        > or higher, or functional limitation in at least one IADL

        > any psychiatric condition should be diagnosed and optimized
        > before surgery

        > no symptomatic degenerative disc disease at other lumbar
        > levels

        > FDA-approved implant for the intended level

Relevant Codes

-   CPT/HCPCS:

    -   0164T

        > 0165T

        > 22857

        > 22860

        > 22862

        > 22865

Required Documentation

-   Age

    > Symptom duration of at least 6 months

    > Documentation of multifaceted conservative management and lack of
    > improvement

    > MRI and plain radiographs showing qualifying disc degeneration and
    > Modic changes

    > Pain/disability severity

    > Documentation that other lumbar levels are not symptomatically
    > degenerated

    > Psychiatric optimization if relevant

    > Planned use of an FDA-approved implant

Limitations / Exclusions

-   Contraindications include:

    -   significant facet arthropathy

        > disease above approved levels

        > bony lumbar stenosis

        > pars defect

        > prior fusion at intended level

        > poorly controlled psychiatric disorder

        > chronic radiculopathy with predominant leg pain

        > compromised vertebral bodies from trauma

        > spondylolisthesis greater than grade 1

        > allergy/sensitivity to implant materials

        > infection or tumor

        > osteopenia/osteoporosis with DEXA T-score less than or equal
        > to -1.0

    > Exclusions include:

    -   more than one level unless FDA approved for more than one level

        > prior lumbar fusion

        > isolated radicular compression syndromes, especially from disc
        > herniation

        > hybrid lumbar arthroplasty plus fusion

        > non-approved devices or use outside FDA requirements

3\. LUMBAR LAMINECTOMY

Medical Necessity Criteria

-   Acute neurologic deterioration

    -   signs/symptoms of cauda equina or conus medullaris syndrome, or
        > rapidly progressive neurologic deficit confirmed by imaging

    > Lumbar spinal stenosis, with or without spondylolisthesis

    -   all of the following:

        -   neurogenic claudication or radicular pain with significant
            > functional impairment

            > failure of at least 6 weeks of conservative treatment

            > MRI, CT, or CT myelography within the past 12 months
            > documenting central, lateral recess, or foraminal stenosis

    > Lumbar synovial cyst

    -   all of the following:

        -   radicular pain or neurogenic claudication not responding to
            > at least 6 weeks of conservative care

            > CT or MRI within the past 6 months showing a synovial cyst
            > that matches symptoms/exam

    > Lumbar disc herniation

    -   retrieved excerpt shows criteria including:

        -   neurologic deficits such as motor, sensory, or reflex
            > findings

            > imaging within the past 9 months correlating with symptoms

            > failure of at least 6 weeks of conservative care

            > other pain sources ruled out

    > Additional laminectomy-related indications shown in the retrieved
    > excerpt:

    -   dorsal rhizotomy for spasticity

        > tethered cord syndrome under defined imaging/symptom criteria

        > biopsy, excision, or evacuation when imaging suggests tumor,
        > infection, AVM, or other mass

Required Documentation

-   Plan of care

    > Conservative management details and compliance when required

    -   imaging within 12 months

    > For disc herniation:

    -   imaging within 9 months

    -   MRI/CT/ultrasound showing low conus and/or thickened filum where

        > urodynamic confirmation for adult neurogenic bladder and
        > occult tethered cord scenarios

Limitations / Exclusions

-   Relative contraindications/exclusions explicitly shown for tethered

    -   unstable medical condition raising anesthesia/surgical risk

        > observation instead of surgery for some asymptomatic complex
        > lesions

        > prophylactic surgery in asymptomatic older children/adults

        > low back pain alone without urinary findings and with normal
        > imaging/urodynamics

    > Other laminectomy exclusions were not fully displayed in the
    > retrieved excerpts.

4\. NONINVASIVE ELECTRICAL BONE GROWTH STIMULATION

Medical Necessity Criteria

-   Thoracic or lumbar fusion

    -   medically necessary when high risk for pseudoarthrosis and any
        > one of the following:

        -   fusion revision, with at least 6 months since original
            > surgery and imaging confirming lack of healing progression
            > over the prior 3 months

            > fusion at 2 or more adjacent levels

            > any listed risk factor:

            -   diabetes

                > metabolic bone disease, including
                > osteoporosis/osteopenia

                > immunocompromised state

                > systemic vascular disease

                > long-term corticosteroid use

                > active nicotine use

    > Cervical fusion

    -   medically necessary when high risk for pseudoarthrosis and any
        > one of the following:

        -   fusion revision, with at least 6 months since original
            > surgery and imaging confirming no healing progression over
            > the prior 3 months

            > fusion at 3 or more adjacent levels for cervical fusion
            > when any of these are present:

            -   diabetes

                > osteoporosis

                > active nicotine use

Relevant Codes

-   CPT/HCPCS:

    -   20974

        > E0748

Required Documentation

-   Prior surgical history

    > Timing from original surgery

    > Imaging showing failed healing progression when used for revision

    > Number of levels fused

    > High-risk factors for pseudoarthrosis

Limitations / Exclusions

-   Not medically necessary for:

    -   spondylolysis or pars defect

        > semi-invasive electrical bone growth stimulation for any
        > indication

        > adjunctive use for primary healing of spinal fracture

        > nonsurgical treatment of established pseudoarthrosis

5\. VERTEBROPLASTY / KYPHOPLASTY

Medical Necessity Criteria

-   Percutaneous vertebroplasty or kyphoplasty is medically necessary
    > for:

    -   osteolytic vertebral metastasis, myeloma, or plasmacytoma with
        > severe back pain related to vertebral destruction not
        > involving most of the cortical bone

        > vertebral hemangioma with severe pain, nerve compression, or
        > aggressive radiologic features after failed radiation therapy

        > eosinophilic granuloma with pain and spinal instability

        > vertebral compression fracture due to osteoporosis or
        > osteopenia when all of the following are met:

        -   recent localized back pain at fracture site not responding
            > to at least 6 weeks of conservative treatment

            > tenderness over fracture site

            > advanced imaging confirms a nontraumatic acute compression
            > fracture

            > recent MRI or CT rules out disc herniation or other causes
            > of pain

            > no unsafe imaging features, including:

            -   spinal stenosis greater than 20% from retropulsed
                > fragments

                > vertebral body collapse to less than one-third of
                > original height

                > vertebral plana

                > anatomy preventing safe needle access

                > burst fracture with retropulsed fragments

Required Documentation

-   Diagnosis/indication

    > Failure of at least 6 weeks of conservative treatment when
    > treating osteoporotic/osteopenic compression fracture

    > Physical exam tenderness over fracture site

    > Imaging confirming acute fracture or lesion

    > Imaging excluding alternative pain sources

    > Imaging confirming absence of unsafe anatomic/risk features

Limitations / Exclusions

-   Contraindications:

    -   severe cardiopulmonary disease

        > coagulation disorder

        > allergy to procedural materials

        > active or incompletely treated infection

    > Exclusions:

    -   prophylaxis in patients without acute fracture

        > prophylaxis to prevent proximal junctional kyphosis/failure
        > after posterior fusion

        > nonpathologic acute high-energy traumatic vertebral fracture

        > fractures documented as more than 1 year old

        > asymptomatic compression fracture

        > sacroplasty for all indications due to insufficient evidence

6\. BONE GRAFT SUBSTITUTES AND BONE MORPHOGENETIC PROTEINS

Medical Necessity Criteria

-   Demineralized bone matrix (DBM)

    -   medically necessary as a bone graft extender or substitute when
        > autograft is not available

    > rhBMP-2

    -   medically necessary in skeletally mature patients for certain
        > instrumented lumbar fusion procedures:

        -   ALIF or XLIF

            -   appropriate in all patients except males with
                > reproductive intent

            > posterolateral or intertransverse lumbar fusion when
            > autograft is not feasible because:

            -   prior autograft use left no available tissue

                > insufficient autograft for intended procedure

                > patient is not an appropriate autograft candidate due
                > to:

                -   increased donor-site harvest risk

                    > poor quality bone

                    > obesity

                    > infection or fracture at donor site

                    > lumbar pseudoarthrosis

                    > fusion of 2 or more lumbar levels

    > The section also lists risk factors for pseudoarthrosis:

    -   diabetes

        > metabolic bone disease

        > immunocompromised state

        > systemic vascular disease

        > long-term corticosteroid use

        > active nicotine use

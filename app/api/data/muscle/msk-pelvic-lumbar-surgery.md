MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

1.  General Clinical Requirements Applying to Requests

-   Before any intervention, the clinical record should support:

    -   A confirmed diagnosis or strong pretest likelihood based on full
        > clinical evaluation

        > History and physical exam

        > Review of relevant labs and diagnostic testing when applicable

        > Review of response to prior treatment

        > Expectation that benefit outweighs risk

        > Expectation that the intervention will affect management or
        > improve outcome

    > If the above are not well established, additional review or
    > clinician discussion may be needed.

    > Requests for multiple interventions at the same time may need
    > additional review.

    > Repeat therapeutic intervention is generally appropriate only when
    > the prior intervention helped and the expected duration of relief
    > has passed.

2\. General Medical Necessity / Conservative Care Requirements

-   Documentation submitted with the request should include:

    -   Symptom duration and severity

        > Specific functional limitations related to symptoms

        > Type and duration of all treatments already tried

        > If conservative management is not appropriate, the reason must
        > be documented

    > Conservative management must include:

    -   Physical therapy, and

        > At least one additional conservative treatment strategy

    > Physical therapy requirement may be met by any of the following:

    -   Formal PT by a qualified provider

        > A supervised home treatment program that includes all of:

        -   Patient-specific program

            > Initial active instruction by MD/DO/PT with patient
            > demonstration

            > Documented compliance or clinician attestation at
            > follow-up

        > Exception when clearly documented, such as pain so severe that
        > PT is not feasible

    > Additional conservative treatment must include at least one of:

    -   Anti-inflammatory drugs or analgesics when not contraindicated

        > Adjunctive medications such as membrane stabilizers or muscle
        > relaxants when not contraindicated

        > Intra-articular corticosteroid injection

        > SI support belt or appropriate bracing

        > Other conservative measures such as acupuncture, chiropractic
        > care, massage, activity modification, or rest when appropriate

    > Failure of conservative management requires all of:

    -   Completion of a full course of conservative management for the
        > current episode

        > No significant improvement or worsening on reevaluation

        > Consideration of more invasive treatment

    > Symptom severity/functional impact threshold described in the
    > guideline:

    -   Pain at least 3/10

        > Inability to perform at least 2 ADLs and/or IADLs

3\. Open Sacroiliac Joint Fusion Open SI joint fusion may be medically
necessary when any one of the following is present:

-   As part of sacrectomy or partial sacrectomy for sacral tumor
    > involvement

    > As part of treatment for SI joint infection or sepsis, such as
    > osteomyelitis or pyogenic sacroiliitis

    > Severe traumatic injury with pelvic ring disruption, such as:

    -   Pelvic ring fractures

        > Acetabular fracture

        > Spinopelvic dissociation

    > During multilevel spinal constructs extending to the ilium as part
    > of medically necessary lumbar fusion

Open SI joint fusion is not medically necessary for indications outside
the listed scenarios, including:

-   Poorly defined low back pain

    > Sacral insufficiency fractures

4\. Percutaneous/Minimally Invasive SI Joint Fusion
Percutaneous/minimally invasive SI joint fusion using a transiliac or
lateral approach with an FDA-cleared/approved structural fixation device
may be medically necessary only when all criteria below are met:

A. Chronicity and functional impact

-   Pain has lasted at least 6 months

    > Pain interferes with function and is documented by both:

    -   VAS pain score 5 or greater

        > ODI 30 or greater

B. Failure of nonoperative treatment

-   At least 6 months of conservative management

    > Must include at least one therapeutic intra-articular SI joint
    > injection

C. SI joint confirmed as pain source All of the following are required:

-   Pain pattern consistent with SI joint pain, usually unilateral and
    > below L5 over the posterior SI region

    > Positive Fortin finger test

    > No equally severe tenderness elsewhere in the pelvis

    > Positive response to at least 3 provocative tests from the
    > following:

    -   Long ligament test

        > FABER/Patrick test

        > Active straight leg raise

        > Compression test

        > Distraction test

        > Thigh thrust test

        > Gaenslen test

    > Other pain generators excluded

D. Imaging requirements All of the following are required:

-   SI joint imaging by plain radiographs and CT, or MRI, excluding
    > destructive lesions such as tumor or infection and excluding
    > inflammatory arthropathy not appropriately treated by this
    > procedure

    > AP pelvis radiograph to evaluate for hip pathology

    > Lumbar CT or MRI to rule out neural compression or other
    > degenerative causes of low back/buttock pain

    > SI joint imaging showing injury and/or degeneration

E. Diagnostic block confirmation

-   At least 75% pain reduction for the expected duration of the
    > anesthetic after image-guided, contrast-enhanced intra-articular
    > SI joint injection

    > This response must occur on 2 separate occasions

5\. Revision Minimally Invasive SI Joint Fusion Revision or replacement
may be medically necessary when any of the following is present:

-   Symptomatic nonunion/pseudarthrosis

    > Symptomatic implant malposition with foraminal impingement

    > Infection involving or secondary to the implant/device

    > Implant fracture, breakage, or loosening

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

General request documentation

-   Symptom duration

    > Symptom severity

    > Specific functional limitations

    > Prior treatments with type and duration

    > Reason conservative management is not appropriate, if applicable

    > Clear treatment plan

Conservative care documentation

-   Details of PT or supervised home therapy

    > Evidence of patient participation/compliance

    > Record of complementary conservative treatment(s) attempted

    > Clinical reevaluation close to planned service date

    > Documentation showing failure of conservative management

For minimally invasive/percutaneous fusion

-   Pain duration of at least 6 months

    > VAS score

    > ODI score

    > Record of at least 6 months of conservative care

    > Record of at least one therapeutic intra-articular SI injection

    > Physical exam findings supporting SI joint pain

    > Results of provocative testing, with at least 3 positive tests

    > Evidence that other pain sources were excluded

    > Imaging results:

    -   SI joint imaging

        > AP pelvis radiograph

        > Lumbar CT or MRI

    > Proof of diagnostic SI joint injections with at least 75% pain
    > reduction on 2 separate occasions

For open fusion

-   Documentation of one of the qualifying indications:

    -   Tumor-related sacrectomy/partial sacrectomy

        > SI joint infection/sepsis

        > Severe trauma with pelvic ring disruption

        > Use as part of medically necessary multilevel lumbar construct
        > extending to the ilium

For revision minimally invasive fusion

-   Documentation of the complication or failure condition:

    -   Nonunion

        > Implant malposition with impingement

        > Implant infection

        > Implant fracture/breakage/loosening

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

General/operational limitations

-   The policy applies to elective, non-emergent SI joint fusion.

    > Approval depends on the clinical information submitted at the time
    > of review.

    > Use of an FDA-approved device alone does not establish medical
    > necessity.

Open fusion exclusions

-   Open SI joint fusion for indications not specifically listed

    > Examples called out as not medically necessary:

    -   Poorly defined low back pain

        > Sacral insufficiency fractures

Percutaneous/minimally invasive exclusions The following are considered
not medically necessary when they are the indication or clinical
context:

-   Infection or fracture unrelated to an implant

    > Tumor

    > Acute traumatic instability of the SI joint

    > Neural compression on imaging that better explains symptoms

    > Another more likely pain source

    > Generalized pain behavior or somatoform-type presentation

    > Generalized pain syndromes such as fibromyalgia

    > Ankylosing spondylitis

    > Rheumatoid arthritis

    > Posterior-approach minimally invasive SI fusion procedures
    > involving intra-articular allograft or devices, with or without a
    > transfixation device

ADDITIONAL PRACTICAL NOTES

-   The guideline strongly recommends:

    -   Tobacco abstinence for at least 6 weeks before surgery

        > Hemoglobin A1C 8% or less in patients with diabetes

        > Weight reduction efforts when BMI is 40 or higher

        > Documentation of shared decision-making when modifiable
        > comorbidities may affect outcome

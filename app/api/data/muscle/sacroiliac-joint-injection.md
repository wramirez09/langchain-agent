MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

Sacroiliac joint (SIJ) injection is considered medically necessary only for the treatment of sacroiliac joint-mediated pain when the procedure is performed under image guidance (fluoroscopy or CT) and ALL of the following criteria are met:

-   Primarily axial low back pain originating below the level of L5 that may radiate to the buttock, groin, or proximal lower extremity; the primary pain generator must be the sacroiliac joint rather than a disc herniation, lumbar facet, or nerve root
-   Pain causing functional disability or an average pain level of 6 or greater on a numeric pain scale of 0 to 10
-   Duration of pain of at least 3 months
-   A cluster of three or more of the following positive provocative physical examination maneuvers performed during an in-person visit (telehealth encounters do not satisfy this requirement):
    -   Distraction test (anterior innominate distraction / gapping test)
    -   Compression test (posterior pelvic compression / approximation test)
    -   FABER test (Flexion, ABduction, External Rotation; also called Patrick test)
    -   Gaenslen test (hyperextension of the hip over the table edge with contralateral hip flexed)
    -   Thigh thrust test (posterior shear test; axial load through the femur with the hip flexed at 90 degrees)
    -   Sacral thrust test (direct posteroanterior pressure over the sacrum in prone position)
-   Failure to achieve adequate relief after active conservative management for at least 6 weeks, including at minimum two of the following modalities directed at the sacroiliac joint:
    -   Structured physical therapy or chiropractic care with documented home exercise program (exercise prescription provided by physician, physical therapist, or chiropractor)
    -   NSAIDs or other analgesics unless contraindicated (with documentation of contraindication if not tried)
    -   Activity modification and pelvic stabilization exercises
-   Advanced imaging (MRI or CT of the pelvis/SI joint) or plain radiography that does not identify an alternative structural etiology better explaining the pain (for example, a lumbar disc herniation with nerve root compression at the level corresponding to the patient's symptoms)
-   Injection must be performed under fluoroscopic or CT image guidance with contrast confirmation of intraarticular needle placement; unguided or landmark-only injections are not covered

INDICATIONS

Sacroiliac joint injection (intraarticular or periarticular/ligamentous) is indicated for one of the following:

**Diagnostic SIJ injection:**

-   To confirm sacroiliac joint as the primary pain generator prior to consideration of further interventions (for example, sacroiliac joint radiofrequency ablation or fusion), when clinical criteria above are met and the diagnosis remains uncertain after history and physical examination
-   A positive diagnostic block is defined as at least 50% reduction in index pain for the expected duration of the local anesthetic used

**Therapeutic SIJ injection:**

-   Treatment of confirmed or clinically suspected sacroiliac joint-mediated pain with corticosteroid when clinical criteria above are met and the patient has failed the required conservative management period
-   Expected benefit is short-to-intermediate term reduction in pain and functional improvement sufficient to allow participation in physical therapy or other rehabilitation

**Spondyloarthropathy (sacroiliitis):**

-   SIJ injection for active inflammatory sacroiliitis meeting ASAS (Assessment of SpondyloArthritis International Society) criteria for axial spondyloarthropathy may be covered when consistent with payer benefit guidelines and CMS coverage determinations; documentation of inflammatory markers (ESR, CRP) and imaging findings consistent with sacroiliitis must be included

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

The following documentation must be submitted with the prior authorization request:

**Physical Examination Maneuvers:**

-   In-person physical examination note documenting the specific provocative tests performed, the technique used, and the patient's pain response for each test
-   Minimum of three positive provocative tests from the accepted cluster (distraction, compression, FABER/Patrick, Gaenslen, thigh thrust, sacral thrust) must be explicitly recorded with laterality
-   Examination must be performed by the treating clinician or a qualified consultant; telehealth-only evaluation does not satisfy this requirement

**Prior Conservative Care:**

-   Office notes, physical therapy records, or chiropractic records documenting at least 6 weeks of active conservative treatment directed at the sacroiliac joint
-   Documentation must include the modalities used, frequency, duration, and the patient's response (for example, lack of clinically meaningful improvement)
-   Documentation of a home exercise program provided by a physician, physical therapist, or chiropractor, with evidence that the patient has participated

**Pain and Functional Assessment:**

-   Numeric pain scale score (average pain level) of 6 or greater on a 0-to-10 scale related to the sacroiliac region
-   Description of functional limitations affecting activities of daily living, work, or quality of life attributable to sacroiliac joint pain
-   Validated outcome measure documentation (for example, Oswestry Disability Index, VAS, PROMIS) when available

**Imaging:**

-   Results of prior imaging of the lumbar spine and/or pelvis (MRI, CT, or plain radiographs) ruling out alternative pain generators
-   For suspected sacroiliitis: results of pelvic MRI or CT demonstrating SI joint pathology, and relevant laboratory results (ESR, CRP, HLA-B27 if tested)

**Prior Injection Response (for repeat injections):**

-   Documentation of the date, technique, medications used, and outcome of the prior sacroiliac joint injection(s)
-   Quantified pain relief (at minimum 50% reduction in average pain score from baseline) and the duration of benefit achieved
-   Functional improvement documented in clinical notes or patient-reported outcome measures

**Clinical Assessment:**

-   Ordering clinician's attestation that the primary source of pain is the sacroiliac joint and not a disc herniation, facet joint, or nerve root origin
-   Documentation that the proposed injection is consistent with the overall multimodal pain management plan
-   Any prior interventional procedures in the same region with their outcomes

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

-   Not covered when performed without image guidance (fluoroscopy or CT); landmark-guided or ultrasound-guided approaches without confirmed intraarticular contrast spread are not covered under most commercial payer policies
-   Not covered as the primary treatment for lumbar radiculopathy, disc herniation with radicular leg pain, or spinal stenosis where the nerve root or disc is the primary pain generator
-   Not covered when the primary diagnosis is piriformis syndrome, trochanteric bursitis, hip joint pathology, or another non-SIJ musculoskeletal condition without evidence of concurrent SIJ-mediated pain
-   Not covered in the absence of at least three positive provocative physical examination maneuvers from the accepted cluster documented during an in-person visit
-   Not covered when conservative management criteria have not been met, unless the treating clinician documents a specific medical contraindication to the required conservative modalities
-   Repeat injections are not covered unless the prior injection produced at least 50% pain relief or significant documented functional improvement for a minimum of 2 months before the repeat therapeutic injection
-   Initial treatment phase: up to 2 injections may be authorized, separated by no less than 2 weeks, when at least 50% pain relief or significant functional improvement is demonstrated after the first injection
-   Frequency limits: beyond the initial treatment phase, the treating clinician must document that prior injections provided at least 50% pain relief lasting a minimum of 2 months; most commercial payers limit therapeutic SIJ injections to 3 to 4 per joint per year; exceeding this frequency requires medical necessity documentation on a case-by-case basis
-   Multiple interventional pain procedures performed on the same date of service in different spinal regions are generally not covered without specific documentation of a medical reason (for example, undue risk of holding anticoagulation therapy on two separate dates)
-   Not covered via telehealth; both the pre-procedure physical examination and the injection procedure must be conducted in person
-   Not covered when the indication is for diagnostic purposes following a prior negative or non-diagnostic SIJ injection, unless documentation supports a change in clinical status or alternative technique justification
-   Prolotherapy, platelet-rich plasma (PRP), stem cell, or other regenerative injections into the sacroiliac joint are not covered under standard commercial benefit policies and require separate review if requested
-   Bilateral sacroiliac joint injections on the same date of service require documentation of bilateral clinical signs and bilateral positive provocation test findings; bilateral injections are not routinely authorized based on unilateral symptoms
-   Not covered for asymptomatic individuals or as prophylactic treatment

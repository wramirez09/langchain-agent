MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

General Clinical Appropriateness Framework

-   Before any intervention, the clinician should:

    -   Confirm the diagnosis or establish pretest likelihood.

        > Perform a complete evaluation including:

        -   History

            > Physical examination

            > Relevant laboratory studies, when applicable

            > Diagnostic testing, when applicable

            > Response to prior therapeutic intervention, when
            > applicable

    > The expected benefit of the intervention should outweigh potential
    > harms.

    > Current literature, treatment guidelines, and standards of
    > practice should support the requested intervention as offering the
    > greatest net benefit among alternatives.

    > There should be a reasonable likelihood that the intervention
    > will:

    -   Change management, and/or

        > Improve patient outcome

Acute Inpatient Surgical Setting Criteria

-   A planned acute inpatient surgical setting may be medically
    > necessary when ONE of the following is met:

1.  Current postoperative care needs cannot be safely provided in
    > observation or outpatient setting

-   Examples include need for:

    -   Hemodynamic monitoring or management

        > Cardiorespiratory monitoring or management

        > Vascular monitoring or management

        > Neurologic monitoring or management

        > Laboratory status monitoring or management

2\. Anticipated postoperative care needs cannot be met, even initially,
in an observation setting due to procedure complexity or patient risk

-   Examples include:

    -   Expected need for hemodynamic monitoring or management

        > Expected need for cardiorespiratory monitoring or management

        > Expected need for vascular monitoring or management

        > Expected need for neurologic monitoring or management

        > Expected need for laboratory monitoring or management

        > Preoperative ASA physical status of IV or higher

        > Patient residence or discharge location is so far from the
        > nearest emergency room that an urgent postoperative
        > complication would pose significant clinical risk

        > Inability to establish clinically appropriate supervision and
        > support at home or other discharge location despite
        > preoperative care coordination, even after hospital-based
        > observation

ASA-Related Risk Reference in Appendix

-   ASA IV:

    -   Severe systemic disease that is a constant threat to life

    > ASA V:

    -   Moribund patient not expected to survive without the operation

    > ASA VI:

    -   Brain-dead patient for organ donation

    > Emergency modifier:

    -   "E" indicates emergency surgery

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

General Documentation for Clinical Review

-   Clinical documentation supporting the request may be required.

    > Documentation must:

    -   Accurately reflect the patient's clinical condition at the time
        > of the request

        > Clearly document the ordering provider's clinical intent

Documentation to Support Preplanned Inpatient Admission

-   Provider office notes showing preoperative medical optimization

    > List of managed and unmanaged comorbidities

    > List of other surgical risk factors

    > If requested, the specific reason for an inpatient preoperative
    > day

    > Copies of medical consultations

    > Copies of medical clearances

    > ASA physical status, if needed

    > Charlson Comorbidity Index score, if needed

    > Other validated surgical risk score, if needed

Additional Review Situations

-   If the clinical elements are not adequately established, review may
    > require peer-to-peer discussion.

    > Simultaneous requests for multiple diagnostic or therapeutic
    > interventions may also require peer-to-peer review.

    > Nonspecific or unlisted/NOC procedure codes may require added
    > documentation.

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

Scope Limitations

-   This guideline addresses level of care, not whether the surgery
    > itself is medically necessary.

    > Procedure medical necessity is reviewed separately and must be met
    > before level-of-care determination.

    > It applies to non-emergent procedures routinely performed on an
    > outpatient basis.

    > Procedures performed in a physician office are out of scope.

Exclusions / Not Addressed

-   Does not address planned or unplanned observation-only admissions.

    > Does not address inpatient admissions resulting from unanticipated
    > intraoperative or observation-stay events.

    > Does not justify inpatient admission solely because of convenience
    > for the patient, family, or provider.

    > Inpatient admission should not be required solely because of
    > social needs that could reasonably be addressed through
    > preoperative planning.

Repeat / Multiple Service Limitations

-   Multiple simultaneous diagnostic or therapeutic requests may require
    > additional review.

    > Repeat diagnostic testing of the same anatomic area and same
    > indication may be subject to extra review when:

    -   Repeated due to technical issues

        > Requested at another facility due to provider preference or
        > quality concerns

        > Persistent symptoms exist without clinical change, treatment,
        > or intervention since prior study

        > Different providers request similar testing over a short
        > period

    > Repeat therapeutic intervention in the same anatomic area is
    > generally appropriate only when:

    -   Prior intervention was effective/beneficial, and

        > Expected duration of relief has lapsed

    > Repeat intervention before the expected duration of relief is not
    > appropriate unless the prior intervention was never actually
    > performed.

KEY TAKEAWAYS

-   The policy is primarily a site-of-care guideline.

    > Inpatient admission is justified only when the patient's
    > postoperative needs or preoperative risk make outpatient or
    > observation care unsafe or inadequate.

    > Strong clinical documentation is central to approval.

    > No diagnosis code list is provided; diagnosis coding must come
    > from the ICD-10-CM manual.

MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

Anterior cervical discectomy and fusion (ACDF) is a surgical procedure performed through an anterior approach to the cervical spine in which one or more intervertebral discs are removed, neural elements are decompressed, and the operative levels are stabilized with interbody graft and instrumentation. ACDF addresses cervical radiculopathy caused by nerve root compression and cervical myelopathy caused by spinal cord compression. The procedure is appropriate only in skeletally mature patients when clinical presentation, neurological examination, and advanced imaging are concordant.

ACDF is considered medically necessary when all of the following general requirements are satisfied AND the indication-specific criteria below are met:

-   The patient is skeletally mature
-   Advanced imaging (MRI or CT myelography) confirms nerve root compression, spinal cord compression, or significant foraminal stenosis at the level(s) corresponding to the patient's clinical symptoms and neurological findings
-   Symptoms and neurological findings are concordant with the imaging level(s)
-   The surgeon has documented that the anterior approach is appropriate based on anatomy, pathology, and surgical judgment
-   Instrumentation and grafting materials are limited to FDA-approved indications for the specific devices used

INDICATIONS

Cervical Radiculopathy

ACDF for cervical radiculopathy is considered medically necessary when all of the following criteria are met:

-   MRI or CT myelography demonstrates nerve root compression, foraminal stenosis, or significant disc herniation at a level concordant with the clinical presentation
-   The patient has objective neurological findings referable to the affected nerve root, which may include:
    -   Dermatomal upper extremity pain, numbness, or paresthesia corresponding to the compressed level
    -   Motor weakness in a myotomal distribution
    -   Diminished or absent deep tendon reflex in the affected root distribution
-   The patient has failed at least 6 weeks of appropriate conservative (non-operative) care within the last 6 months unless one of the exceptions listed below applies; conservative care must be multimodal and should include at least one active modality (physical therapy or supervised home exercise program) and at least one additional measure, which may include:
    -   Anti-inflammatory medication or analgesics
    -   Epidural steroid injection or selective nerve root block
    -   Activity modification, rest, massage, or acupuncture
    -   Chiropractic manipulation
    -   Adjunctive medications (muscle relaxants, neuropathic pain agents)
-   Pain and functional thresholds: significant pain generally means at least 3/10, with associated functional impairment affecting at least 2 ADLs/IADLs in the general spine framework; documentation must show compliance with treatment and clinical reevaluation showing no meaningful improvement or worsening.
-   Exceptions permitting earlier surgical consideration without completing 6 weeks of conservative care:
    -   Severe or rapidly progressive motor deficit (e.g., new or worsening upper extremity weakness that interferes with activities of daily living)
    -   Intractable pain refractory to appropriate analgesic management
    -   Documented significant neurological deterioration during conservative care

Cervical Myelopathy

ACDF for cervical myelopathy or myeloradiculopathy is considered medically necessary when all of the following criteria are met:

-   MRI or CT myelography demonstrates spinal cord compression, cord signal change (T2 hyperintensity indicating myelomalacia), or significant central canal stenosis at a level concordant with the clinical presentation
-   The patient has objective myelopathic signs or symptoms, which may include:
    -   Gait disturbance or imbalance
    -   Hand clumsiness, loss of fine motor function, or difficulty with hand coordination tasks (e.g., buttoning, writing)
    -   Upper motor neuron signs: hyperreflexia, clonus, Hoffman sign, Babinski sign, or inverted radial reflex
    -   Lhermitte sign (electric shock sensation with neck flexion)
    -   Bowel or bladder dysfunction attributable to spinal cord involvement
    -   Bilateral upper extremity numbness or paresthesia
-   A trial of conservative care is not required prior to surgical authorization for symptomatic cervical myelopathy; surgery may be considered without a mandatory conservative-care waiting period when myelopathy is confirmed clinically and radiographically
-   Urgent or emergent consideration is warranted for:
    -   Progressive myelopathy with increasing neurological deficits
    -   Acute or severe cord compression with significant functional impairment
    -   Rapid neurological deterioration

Other Indications

ACDF may be considered medically necessary for the following conditions without a mandatory conservative-care waiting period when the clinical and imaging criteria are met:

-   Cervical instability: radiographic evidence of segmental instability (e.g., dynamic translation greater than 3.5 mm on flexion-extension radiographs or angular motion greater than 11 degrees) with associated symptoms or neurological compromise
-   Traumatic injury: acute cervical fracture, fracture-dislocation, or ligamentous instability resulting in neural compromise or significant mechanical instability requiring surgical stabilization
-   Neoplastic involvement: primary or metastatic tumor causing spinal cord or nerve root compression, mechanical instability, or pathological fracture at one or more cervical levels
-   Spinal infection: epidural abscess, discitis, or osteomyelitis with neural compression, instability, or failure of non-surgical management
-   Pseudarthrosis following prior cervical fusion: symptomatic non-union at a previously fused level with evidence of failed fusion on CT and persistent or recurrent symptoms attributable to that level
-   Ossification of the posterior longitudinal ligament (OPLL): resulting in significant cord compression or myelopathic symptoms when the anterior approach is the appropriate surgical option

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

The following documentation must be present in the medical record to support authorization of ACDF:

Advanced Imaging

-   MRI of the cervical spine (preferred modality): report and images demonstrating the level(s) of nerve root or spinal cord compression, degree of canal or foraminal stenosis, disc herniation morphology, cord signal change if present, and correlation with the clinical presentation; MRI should generally have been obtained within the prior 12 months unless clinical status has been stable
-   CT myelography of the cervical spine: acceptable when MRI is contraindicated (e.g., pacemaker, implanted ferromagnetic device) or technically inadequate; report must describe the level(s) and degree of neural compression
-   Flexion-extension cervical radiographs: required when instability is the primary or contributing indication; must document dynamic segmental translation or angular motion
-   Plain cervical radiographs (AP and lateral): supporting documentation of degenerative changes, disc space height loss, osteophyte formation, or alignment abnormalities
-   Imaging must be read by an independent radiologist and support the requested procedure

Neurological Examination

-   Detailed neurological examination documented by the treating physician within a clinically appropriate interval, including:
    -   Upper and lower extremity motor strength graded by muscle group
    -   Upper and lower extremity sensation (light touch, pinprick) in dermatomal distribution
    -   Deep tendon reflex assessment (biceps C5-C6, brachioradialis C6, triceps C7, finger flexors, patellar, Achilles)
    -   Pathological reflex testing (Hoffman sign, Babinski sign, clonus)
    -   Gait assessment when myelopathy is suspected
    -   Functional impairment related to neurological deficits (e.g., impact on activities of daily living, work, or ambulation)

Conservative Care History

-   For cervical radiculopathy: documentation of at least 6 weeks of multimodal conservative treatment within the last 6 months, including clinician notes or physical therapy records specifying the type of treatment, duration, frequency, and outcome, unless a documented exception applies (severe or progressive motor deficit, intractable pain, or neurological deterioration during conservative care)
-   For cervical myelopathy: documentation confirming the myelopathic diagnosis; a conservative-care waiting period is not required; documentation should indicate the trajectory of symptoms (stable vs. progressive) and the rationale for surgical timing
-   For trauma, tumor, infection, or instability: operative report, imaging, oncology or infectious disease records, or other relevant clinical documentation demonstrating the acuity or nature of the underlying condition

Operative Planning Documentation

-   Surgical consultation or operative planning note specifying:
    -   Level(s) to be addressed and rationale for level selection
    -   Confirmation that the anterior approach is appropriate for the anatomy and pathology
    -   Type of interbody graft and instrumentation planned (limited to FDA-approved devices and indications)
    -   Number of levels (single-level vs. multi-level), with justification for multi-level procedures
-   For multi-level procedures: imaging must confirm compression and concordant clinical findings at each level to be fused; clinical justification for addressing more than two levels should be explicitly documented

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

-   ACDF is not indicated for axial cervical pain (neck pain) alone without objective evidence of nerve root or spinal cord compression and concordant neurological findings; isolated discogenic neck pain or cervicalgia without radiculopathy or myelopathy does not meet medical necessity criteria for fusion
-   ACDF is not indicated solely on the basis of radiographic degenerative changes (disc space narrowing, osteophytes, foraminal narrowing) without corresponding symptoms and neurological findings
-   Multi-level ACDF (three or more levels) requires documentation of compression and concordant clinical findings at each level; authorization is not supported for prophylactic fusion of asymptomatic adjacent levels
-   ACDF is not covered when the primary purpose is cosmetic, to alleviate chronic pain without demonstrable neural compression, or when alternative non-surgical management has not been appropriately considered for radiculopathy
-   Repeat or revision ACDF requires documentation of persistent or recurrent neural compression with concordant symptoms, or documented pseudarthrosis at the prior fusion level with radiographic confirmation (CT is preferred for pseudarthrosis evaluation)
-   Cases may not be approved when the following relative contraindications are present and have not been addressed:
    -   Active systemic infection not yet under appropriate treatment
    -   Medical contraindications to surgery that substantially increase operative risk without documented risk-benefit discussion (e.g., severe cardiopulmonary disease, uncontrolled coagulopathy, active malignancy with limited life expectancy)
    -   Osteoporosis or metabolic bone disease that substantially impairs fusion potential without a documented management plan
    -   Non-physiologic modifiers of pain presentation (e.g., peripheral neuropathy, psychosocial factors, symptom amplification) that have not been evaluated and that may be the primary driver of symptoms rather than neural compression
    -   Nicotine use without documentation of cessation counseling, as tobacco use significantly increases the risk of pseudarthrosis
-   Bone morphogenetic protein (rhBMP-2) use at cervical levels is not FDA-approved for ACDF and is associated with specific risks; use in the cervical spine is off-label and requires explicit documentation of clinical justification; coverage is subject to payer-specific policy

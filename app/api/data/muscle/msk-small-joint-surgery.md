1.  GENERAL MEDICAL NECESSITY FRAMEWORK

-   Applies to elective, non-emergent foot and ankle procedures.

    > Does not apply to treatment that is part of acute fracture care.

    > Before any intervention, the diagnosis or pretest likelihood
    > should be established through:

    -   History and physical exam

        > Relevant lab studies when applicable

        > Relevant diagnostic testing

        > Review of response to prior treatment

    > The requested intervention should have expected benefit that
    > outweighs risk.

    > The intervention should be supported by current literature,
    > treatment standards, or accepted practice.

    > There should be a reasonable expectation that the intervention
    > will change management or improve outcome.

    > Multiple procedures ordered at the same time may require added
    > review.

    > Repeat testing or repeat treatment may require added review if
    > clinical circumstances do not justify it.

2\. REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION General documentation required for requests
includes the following:

-   Clinical notes describing:

    -   Symptom duration

        > Symptom severity

        > Specific functional limitations

        > Type and duration of all treatments already tried

    > Clear plan of care

    > If conservative management is not appropriate, the medical record
    > must explain why.

    > Proof of compliance with conservative treatment, when conservative
    > care is appropriate.

    > Imaging report from the past 12 months.

    > Imaging findings should match the clinical presentation.

    > When applicable, foot radiographs should include weight-bearing AP
    > and lateral views.

    > Imaging reports should note, when relevant:

    -   Subchondral cysts

        > Subchondral sclerosis

        > Periarticular osteophytes

        > Joint subluxation

        > Avascular necrosis

        > Degree of joint-space narrowing

        > Key angular deformity measurements

    > Significant pain/functional impairment is defined as pain at least
    > 3/10 on VAS with difficulty performing daily activities such as
    > walking and wearing reasonable shoes.

Conservative management, when appropriate, should include:

-   Footwear modification and/or padding/accommodative devices such as
    > foot orthoses

    > And at least one of the following:

    -   Activity modification

        > Anti-inflammatory medication or analgesics

        > Corticosteroid injections

        > Debridement of corns/calluses or other hyperkeratotic lesions

Strongly recommended pre-op optimization points:

-   Tobacco/nicotine abstinence for at least 6 weeks before surgery

    > For patients with diabetes, HbA1c 8% or less before joint
    > replacement

    > Weight reduction attempts for BMI 40 or greater

    > Documentation of shared decision-making when modifiable
    > comorbidities may affect outcomes

3\. MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA BY PROCEDURE

A. Hallux rigidus surgery (including cheilectomy or osteotomy) All of
the following are required:

-   Skeletally mature patient

    > Mild or moderate hallux rigidus confirmed on radiography

    > And either:

    -   Limited and/or painful range of motion of the first MTP joint

        > Activity-limiting pain referable to the first MTP joint

    > Significant pain and functional impairment of the first MTP joint
    > that persists after at least 12 weeks of supervised conservative
    > management

    -   Or a nonhealing ulcer on the dorsum of the great toe

    > Adequate lower extremity vascular perfusion documented

B. First MTP arthrodesis for hallux rigidus All of the following are
required:

-   Skeletally mature patient

    > Limited and/or painful range of motion of the first MTP joint

    > Significant pain and functional impairment of the first MTP joint

    > Adequate lower extremity vascular perfusion documented

    > One of the following:

    -   Severe hallux rigidus confirmed by radiography

        > Failed prior hallux valgus or hallux rigidus surgery

C. First MTP arthroplasty All of the following are required:

-   Skeletally mature patient

    > One of the following implant types:

    -   Total prosthetic replacement arthroplasty with double-stemmed
        > silastic implants only

        > Metallic hemiarthroplasty, metatarsal-based or
        > phalangeal-based

    > Limited and/or painful range of motion of the first MTP joint

    > Significant pain and functional impairment of the first MTP joint

    > Adequate lower extremity vascular perfusion documented

    > One of the following:

    -   Severe hallux rigidus confirmed by radiography

        > Failed prior hallux rigidus surgery

D. Hallux valgus surgery All of the following are required:

-   Skeletally mature patient

    > Significant pain and functional limitation of the first MTP joint
    > and/or a pre-ulcer that persists after at least 12 weeks of
    > supervised conservative management

    -   Or a nonhealing ulcer at the bunion site or second toe

    > Radiographic confirmation of:

    -   Hallux valgus angle greater than 15 degrees

        > Or intermetatarsal angle greater than 9 degrees

    > Adequate lower extremity vascular perfusion documented

E. First MTP arthrodesis for hallux valgus All of the following are
required:

-   Skeletally mature patient

    > Significant pain and functional limitation of the first MTP joint
    > and/or a pre-ulcer that persists after at least 12 weeks of
    > supervised conservative management

    -   Or a nonhealing ulcer at the bunion site or second toe

    > Radiographic confirmation of:

    -   Hallux valgus angle greater than 15 degrees

        > Or intermetatarsal angle greater than 9 degrees

    > Adequate lower extremity vascular perfusion documented

    > Prior hallux valgus correction with recurrence of
    > moderate-to-severe deformity

    > Grade I arthritic change of the first MTP joint

F. Bunionette surgery All of the following are required:

-   Skeletally mature patient

    > Significant pain and functional limitation of the fifth MTP joint
    > and/or a pre-ulcer that persists after at least 12 weeks of
    > supervised conservative management

    -   Or a nonhealing ulcer at the bunionette site or fourth toe

    > Radiographic confirmation of elevated intermetatarsal angle
    > greater than 9 degrees

    -   Or a lateral bony prominence if only simple
        > exostectomy/resection of the lateral eminence is planned

    > Adequate lower extremity vascular perfusion documented

G. Metatarsal osteotomy All of the following are required:

-   Skeletally mature patient

    > Adequate lower extremity vascular perfusion documented

    > Significant pain and functional impairment caused by metatarsal
    > deformity and/or a pre-ulcer that persists after at least 12 weeks
    > of supervised conservative management

    -   Or a nonhealing ulcer due to the metatarsal deformity

    > Physical exam or imaging confirmation of an anatomic abnormality
    > requiring osteotomy

H. Lesser toe deformity surgery All of the following are required:

-   Skeletally mature patient

    > Adequate lower extremity vascular perfusion documented

    > Significant pain and functional impairment caused by the lesser
    > toe deformity and/or a pre-ulcer that persists after at least 12
    > weeks of supervised conservative management

    -   Or a nonhealing ulcer due to the deformity

    > Physical exam or imaging confirmation of lesser toe deformity

I. Ankle arthrodesis All of the following are required:

-   Skeletally mature patient

    > Radiographic confirmation of either:

    -   Kellgren-Lawrence grade 3 or 4 arthritis of the tibiotalar joint

        > Avascular necrosis of the talus

    > Significant pain and functional impairment from ankle arthritis
    > that persists after at least 12 weeks of supervised conservative
    > management

    -   Unless radiographs show grade 4 disease

    > Adequate lower extremity vascular perfusion documented

Additional note:

-   May also be indicated for revision of failed prior reconstruction if
    > there is enough bone stock and soft tissue integrity.

J. Total ankle arthroplasty All of the following are required:

-   Skeletally mature patient

    > Radiographic confirmation of either:

    -   Kellgren-Lawrence grade 3 or 4 arthritis of the tibiotalar joint

        > Avascular necrosis of the talus without collapse

    > Significant pain and functional impairment from ankle arthritis
    > that persists after at least 12 weeks of supervised conservative
    > management

    -   Unless radiographs show grade 4 disease

    > Adequate lower extremity vascular perfusion documented

    > FDA-approved device

Additional note:

-   May also be indicated for revision of failed prior reconstruction if
    > there is enough bone stock and soft tissue integrity.

K. Revision total ankle arthroplasty All of the following are required:

-   Skeletally mature patient

    > Aseptic loosening

    > Radiographic confirmation of implant problem

    > Significant pain and functional impairment due to the implant
    > problem

    > Adequate lower extremity vascular perfusion documented

    > FDA-approved device

    > Reconstruction after periprosthetic infection with lab and
    > clinical confirmation that infection has resolved

    > Ongoing disabling pain or implant loosening with recent prosthetic
    > joint infection evaluation, including:

    -   Preoperative serologic testing

        > If labs are abnormal, at least one of the following:

        -   Synovial fluid testing, such as:

            -   Leukocyte count

                > Neutrophil percentage

                > Aerobic and anaerobic bacterial cultures

                > Leukocyte esterase

                > Alpha defensin testing

                > Synovial fluid CRP

                > Synovial fluid PCR for bacteria

            > Intraoperative plan for:

            -   Histopathology

                > Aerobic and anaerobic tissue cultures

                > Implant sonication culture or PCR

4\. LIMITATIONS, CONTRAINDICATIONS, AND EXCLUSIONS

A. Hallux rigidus procedures contraindications

-   Active joint infection

    > Active systemic bacteremia

    > Active skin infection

    > Inadequate bone stock for osteotomy or arthrodesis

    > Inadequate vascular perfusion to the foot

Hallux rigidus exclusions:

-   Asymptomatic hallux rigidus

    > Surgery done only for cosmetic reasons

    > Arthroplasty using excluded implant categories, including:

    -   Ceramic prostheses

        > Modular implants

        > Molded cylindrical implants

        > Bioabsorbable implants

    > MTP arthroplasty for indications outside those specifically listed

    > Charcot neuroarthropathy

B. Hallux valgus and bunionette contraindications/exclusions
Contraindications:

-   Active joint infection

    > Active systemic bacteremia

    > Active skin infection

    > Inadequate bone stock for osteotomy or arthrodesis

    > Inadequate vascular perfusion to the foot

Exclusions:

-   Asymptomatic hallux valgus

    > Asymptomatic bunionette deformity

    > Surgery done only for cosmetic reasons

C. Metatarsal osteotomy contraindications/exclusions Contraindications:

-   Active joint infection

    > Active systemic bacteremia

    > Active skin infection

    > Inadequate bone stock for osteotomy or arthrodesis

    > Inadequate vascular supply to the foot

Exclusions:

-   Asymptomatic lesser toe deformities

    > Asymptomatic metatarsal deformities

    > Surgery done only for cosmetic reasons

D. Lesser toe deformity contraindications/exclusions Contraindications:

-   Active joint infection

    > Active systemic bacteremia

    > Active skin infection

    > Inadequate bone stock for osteotomy or arthrodesis

    > Inadequate vascular supply to the foot

Exclusions:

-   Asymptomatic lesser toe deformities

    > Surgery done only for cosmetic reasons

E. Ankle procedures contraindications/exclusions Ankle arthrodesis
contraindications:

-   Active joint infection

    > Active systemic bacteremia

    > Active skin infection

Ankle arthroplasty contraindications:

-   Intra-articular corticosteroid injection within the last 6 weeks in
    > the joint being replaced

    > Active joint infection

    > Active systemic bacteremia

    > Charcot neuroarthropathy

    > Active skin infection

    > Inadequate bone stock

    > Severe deformity in adjacent ankle structures, including hindfoot,
    > forefoot, and knee

    > Prior surgery or injury that worsened ankle bone quality

    > Extensive avascular necrosis of the talar dome

    > Malalignment greater than 15 degrees if not surgically correctable

    > Inadequate vascular supply to the foot/ankle

    > Absence of medial or lateral malleolus, or both

    > Severe osteoporosis/osteopenia or other poor bone-quality
    > conditions

    > High-demand sports participation

    > Immunosuppressive therapy

    > Insufficient ligament support not repairable with soft tissue
    > stabilization

    > Insufficient musculature preventing proper component alignment

    > Neurologic impairment with dynamic muscle imbalance across the
    > ankle

    > Prior ankle fusion

    > Psychiatric issues interfering with perioperative cooperation

Exclusions for all ankle procedures:

-   Asymptomatic ankle osteoarthritis

    > Surgery done only for cosmetic reasons

    > Non-FDA-approved total ankle replacement devices

6\. PRACTICAL SHORT FORM SUMMARY

-   Most covered foot procedures require:

    -   Skeletal maturity

        > Significant pain plus functional limitation

        > Failure of at least 12 weeks of supervised conservative
        > treatment, unless an exception is stated

        > Objective confirmation by imaging or physical exam where
        > relevant

        > Adequate vascular perfusion

    > Hallux rigidus criteria are split by procedure severity:

    -   Cheilectomy/osteotomy for mild-moderate disease

        > Arthrodesis or limited implant arthroplasty for severe disease
        > or failed prior surgery

    > Hallux valgus and bunionette require deformity confirmed by
    > imaging plus symptoms or ulcer-related need.

    > Metatarsal and lesser toe procedures require deformity
    > confirmation plus symptoms/ulcer persistence after conservative
    > treatment.

    > Ankle arthrodesis and total ankle replacement require advanced
    > radiographic arthritis or talar AVN, failed conservative care
    > unless very advanced disease is already shown, and adequate
    > perfusion.

    > Revision ankle replacement has the most intensive documentation
    > requirements, especially for infection workup.

    > Common noncoverage themes are:

    -   Asymptomatic deformity/arthritis

        > Cosmetic-only surgery

        > Poor vascular supply

        > Active infection

        > Inadequate bone stock

        > Nonapproved implants/devices in the situations where device
        > approval is required.

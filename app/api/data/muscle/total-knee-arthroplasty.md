MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

Total knee arthroplasty (TKA) is a surgical procedure in which the articular surfaces of the distal femur, proximal tibia, and typically the patella are resurfaced with prosthetic components to relieve pain and restore function in patients with end-stage knee joint disease. TKA is one of the most common and successful elective orthopedic procedures; however, it is an irreversible intervention and must be reserved for patients who have exhausted appropriate non-operative management and who have significant functional impairment attributable to advanced knee joint disease confirmed by clinical and radiographic evaluation.

There is no medical necessity to perform TKA in individuals with severe radiological disease and no symptoms. Symptomatic and functional criteria must be satisfied alongside radiographic evidence of advanced joint disease before surgical authorization is appropriate.

TKA is considered medically necessary when ALL of the following criteria are met:

**Diagnosis and Radiographic Confirmation**

-   Radiographic evidence of advanced knee osteoarthritis (primary, post-traumatic, or inflammatory arthritis) on weight-bearing plain radiographs (standing AP, lateral, and sunrise/Merchant views), demonstrated by at least one of the following:
    -   Kellgren-Lawrence grade 3 or grade 4 osteoarthritis in one or more compartments (medial tibiofemoral, lateral tibiofemoral, or patellofemoral)
    -   Joint space narrowing of 50% or greater relative to the contralateral compartment or age-matched norms on standing AP radiographs
    -   Bone-on-bone articulation (complete obliteration of joint space) in one or more compartments
    -   Radiographic evidence of avascular necrosis (AVN) or osteonecrosis of the distal femur or proximal tibia with subchondral collapse or joint destruction
    -   Destructive inflammatory arthropathy (e.g., rheumatoid arthritis, psoriatic arthritis, other seronegative spondyloarthropathy) with tricompartmental or severe bicompartmental involvement on plain films

**Symptoms and Functional Impairment**

-   Persistent moderate-to-severe knee pain that has not responded to conservative management (see below), AND
-   Documented loss of function affecting activities of daily living (ADLs), such as ambulation, stair-climbing, rising from a chair, or participation in routine daily activities, AND
-   At least one of the following physical examination findings consistent with the symptomatic knee:
    -   Crepitus with range-of-motion testing
    -   Decreased active or passive range of motion (flexion contracture, extension lag, or limited flexion less than 90 degrees)
    -   Bony enlargement or periarticular tenderness on palpation consistent with advanced degenerative change
    -   Angular deformity (varus or valgus malalignment) or medial/lateral joint-line tenderness
    -   Antalgic gait or use of ambulatory assistive device attributable to the affected knee
    -   Note: Physical exam findings as listed above are not required to be documented if standing weight-bearing radiographs demonstrate bone-on-bone articulation

**Failure of Conservative Management**

-   Failure of at least 3 months of structured conservative (non-operative) management, including ALL of the following modalities unless individually contraindicated and the contraindication is documented:
    -   Supervised physical therapy or a structured home exercise program with documented compliance, including:
        -   Range-of-motion exercises
        -   Quadriceps and periarticular muscle-strengthening exercises
        -   Patient-specific therapeutic exercise instruction by a qualified clinician
    -   Use of analgesic or anti-inflammatory medications (e.g., NSAIDs, acetaminophen, topical agents) unless contraindicated by comorbidities (e.g., renal disease, gastrointestinal disease, anticoagulation requirement)
    -   Activity modification (avoidance of impact loading activities, use of ambulatory assistive devices as indicated)
    -   At least one of the following complementary conservative measures when clinically appropriate:
        -   Intra-articular corticosteroid injection
        -   Intra-articular hyaluronic acid (viscosupplementation) injection
        -   Weight management counseling or program participation (for patients with BMI above 40)
        -   Unloader bracing or orthotic devices for isolated compartmental disease
-   Failure is defined as persistent pain with functional impairment after completion of the prescribed course, or worsening symptoms during the conservative management period that necessitates earlier surgical consideration

INDICATIONS FOR TOTAL KNEE ARTHROPLASTY

TKA may be considered medically necessary when documented indications fall into one of the following clinical categories:

**Section One — Advanced Symptomatic Knee Arthritis**

Persistent pain and documented loss of function with radiographic evidence of advanced disease from any of the following:

-   Primary knee osteoarthritis (medial, lateral, or tricompartmental)
-   Post-traumatic osteoarthritis following prior knee injury, fracture, or surgical intervention
-   Inflammatory arthropathy (rheumatoid arthritis, psoriatic arthritis, other seronegative spondyloarthropathies) with tricompartmental or severe bicompartmental involvement
-   Crystalline arthropathy (gout, calcium pyrophosphate deposition disease) with secondary joint destruction meeting radiographic criteria

**Section Two — Alternative Diagnoses with Joint Destruction**

Documented joint destruction or end-stage joint disease from conditions other than osteoarthritis:

-   Avascular necrosis (osteonecrosis) of the femoral condyle(s) or tibial plateau with subchondral collapse, joint surface irregularity, or secondary arthritis, when unicompartmental arthroplasty is not technically appropriate
-   Distal femur or proximal tibia fracture in the elderly with pre-existing severe arthritis where arthroplasty is preferable to open reduction internal fixation based on bone quality and patient-specific factors
-   Failed prior knee surgical procedures (high tibial osteotomy, prior arthroscopic debridement, unicompartmental arthroplasty) with progressive joint destruction meeting TKA criteria
-   Tumor or destructive bone lesion of the distal femur or proximal tibia requiring prosthetic reconstruction (oncologic TKA; documentation of tumor type and staging required)

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

The following documentation must be submitted with the prior authorization request. Requests that do not include required elements will not be approved without additional review.

**Imaging Requirements**

-   Standing weight-bearing plain radiographs of the affected knee, including:
    -   Standing anteroposterior (AP) view (bilateral preferred for comparison)
    -   Lateral view
    -   Sunrise or Merchant (patellofemoral) view
    -   Notch view (optional; useful when posterior condyle involvement is suspected)
-   Radiographs must be recent (generally within 12 months; within 6 months if clinical status has changed)
-   Kellgren-Lawrence grade must be specified or determinable from submitted images; grade 3 or higher (or documented bone-on-bone) is generally required
-   MRI is not routinely required for TKA authorization but may be submitted as supporting evidence when plain films are equivocal or when concurrent pathology (AVN, soft tissue derangement) is being characterized
-   If AVN is the indication, MRI or bone scan demonstrating osteonecrosis with staging is required

**Conservative Care Documentation**

-   Dated records demonstrating at least 3 months of conservative management, including:
    -   Physical therapy visit notes or documented home exercise program with initial instruction and follow-up records
    -   Medication history including NSAIDs, analgesics, or topical agents with dates of use and response
    -   Record of corticosteroid or hyaluronic acid injection(s) with dates, laterality, and clinical response, when performed
    -   Activity modification counseling with documented patient response
    -   Notation of contraindications to any standard conservative measure, with clinical rationale
-   Failure of conservative management must be documented: persistent pain and functional limitation despite completion of the prescribed program, or documented worsening during the trial

**Clinical Examination and History**

-   Orthopedic surgeon evaluation note documenting:
    -   Primary and secondary diagnoses
    -   Pain severity (e.g., numeric pain scale or descriptive documentation)
    -   Functional limitations affecting ADLs (ambulation, stair-climbing, transfers, work, recreational activities)
    -   Physical examination findings of the affected knee (range of motion in degrees, deformity, crepitus, stability, gait)
    -   Surgical plan and implant rationale
    -   Discussion of risks, benefits, and alternatives with the patient
-   BMI documentation (height and weight) is required; for patients with BMI above 40, documentation of weight management counseling or referral is expected

**Comorbidity and Clearance Documentation**

-   Relevant medical history including comorbidities that may increase surgical risk or affect eligibility (diabetes, cardiovascular disease, coagulation disorders, immunosuppression)
-   Documentation of absence of active local or systemic infection (including skin integrity assessment of the affected lower extremity)
-   Pre-surgical clearance by the patient's primary care or managing physician when significant comorbidities are present

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

TKA will not be authorized under the following circumstances. Relative contraindications should be individually evaluated by the clinical reviewer.

**Absolute Exclusions**

-   No conservative trial documented or conservative trial less than 3 months without documented contraindication to all conservative modalities
-   Absence of radiographic evidence of advanced joint disease (Kellgren-Lawrence grade 1 or less, or joint space narrowing less than 50% without other evidence of advanced disease)
-   Severe radiological disease without corresponding symptoms or functional impairment
-   Active local infection of the knee joint (septic arthritis) or periarticular soft tissue infection; active systemic infection or bacteremia
-   Active osteomyelitis involving the distal femur, proximal tibia, or patella
-   Skeletal immaturity (open physes); TKA is generally contraindicated until skeletal maturity is achieved
-   Non-functioning or absent extensor mechanism without a reconstructive plan
-   Severe neuropathic arthropathy (Charcot joint) without appropriate patient selection and surgical planning documentation
-   Insufficient bone stock precluding stable implant fixation without a documented plan for augmentation, bone grafting, or custom implant
-   Patient inability to participate in postoperative rehabilitation due to severe cognitive impairment or neurological deficit, unless goals of care are clearly documented

**Relative Contraindications (require case-by-case review and documentation)**

-   BMI above 40 without documented weight management efforts; patients with morbid obesity should ideally have documented weight-loss counseling or bariatric referral prior to elective TKA
-   Poorly controlled diabetes mellitus (HbA1c above 8.0%) — optimization of glycemic control is expected prior to elective TKA due to elevated infection risk
-   Active tobacco use — smoking cessation counseling is expected; active smokers have elevated complication and revision rates
-   Corticosteroid injection into the knee joint within 12 weeks prior to the planned surgical date; TKA should generally be deferred until at least 12 weeks after the last intra-articular injection
-   Significant medical comorbidities not yet optimized (uncontrolled heart failure, severe COPD, severe renal insufficiency) — medical clearance and optimization required
-   Severe peripheral vascular disease of the operative extremity without vascular surgery evaluation
-   Severe psychiatric or behavioral disorder that substantially impairs capacity for informed consent or postoperative compliance
-   Unrealistic patient expectations regarding outcomes or inability to understand surgical risks — documentation of shared decision-making is required

**Procedure-Specific Exclusions**

-   Isolated unicompartmental knee arthritis (single-compartment medial or lateral tibiofemoral disease, or isolated patellofemoral disease) may be more appropriately treated with unicompartmental knee arthroplasty (UKA/PKA); TKA for isolated compartmental disease requires documentation of contraindications to UKA or clinical rationale for tricompartmental resurfacing
-   Simultaneous bilateral TKA (staged or same-day) requires additional clinical justification and documentation of medical fitness for bilateral procedure
-   If preoperative records indicate either TKA or UKA may be performed based on intraoperative findings, separate authorization requests must be submitted for each procedure

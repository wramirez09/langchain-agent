COMMON REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION / CONSERVATIVE TREATMENT ELEMENTS

-   Submit documentation supporting medical necessity and a clear plan
    > of care.

    > Imaging must correlate with clinical findings.

    > Where conservative management is required, it generally must
    > include:

    -   Physical therapy, or a supervised home program with:

        -   patient-specific exercises

            > initial instruction by a qualified clinician

            > documented compliance

        > And at least 1 complementary conservative measure such as:

        -   anti-inflammatory or analgesic medication when not
            > contraindicated

            > adjunctive medication when appropriate

            > corticosteroid injection when appropriate

            > activity modification or a trial of rest

    > Clinical reevaluation should occur reasonably close to the date of
    > service.

    > Failure of conservative management generally requires:

    -   completion of the prescribed course

        > worsening or no meaningful improvement on reevaluation

        > consideration of a more invasive treatment

    > When conservative treatment is required, documentation of
    > compliance is expected. These common elements are described in the
    > knee arthroscopy, meniscal allograft, and osteochondral graft
    > sections

KNEE ARTHROSCOPY AND OPEN PROCEDURES Medical Necessity Criteria

-   Diagnostic arthroscopy

    -   Medically necessary for synovial biopsy or chondrocyte tissue
        > harvest, or when all of the following are met:

        -   At least 1 symptom:

            -   significant pain with functional limitation

                > instability such as giving way, catching, clicking, or
                > locking

                > limited range of motion

            > At least 1 exam finding:

            -   limited range of motion

                > joint swelling

                > inconclusive specific diagnostic maneuvers

                > local muscle weakness or atrophy

            > Inconclusive x-ray or advanced imaging

            > Failure of at least 6 weeks of conservative management

    > Removal of loose body

    -   Both required:

        -   knee pain with grinding, catching, or popping

            > radiographic evidence of a loose intra-articular foreign
            > body, implant, fracture fragment, or other distinct
            > structure

        > If another arthroscopy code is also authorized, the loose body
        > must be larger than 5 mm or require incision extension for
        > removal

    > Meniscal repair or meniscectomy: acute traumatic meniscal tear

    -   All required:

        -   knee injury within the last 3 months with new knee pain

            > moderate to severe pain causing functional limitation in
            > age-appropriate daily activities

            > catching, locking, or instability symptoms

            > physical exam consistent with meniscal pathology

            -   exception: if a concurrent ligament reconstruction is
                > planned and the meniscal tear is documented,
                > meniscal-specific exam findings are not required

            > imaging confirms acute meniscal tear features

        > Partial meniscectomy:

        -   for symptomatic tears not amenable to repair, especially if
            > the peripheral rim is intact

        > Meniscal repair:

        -   for symptomatic reducible peripheral tears that are
            > horizontal or longitudinal

    > Meniscal repair or meniscectomy: chronic degenerative tear

    -   All required:

        -   physical exam consistent with meniscal pathology

            > persistent or frequent mechanical symptoms, or failure of
            > conservative management for at least 12 weeks

            > imaging demonstrating a meniscal tear consistent with the
            > presentation

            > imaging showing no more than moderate osteoarthritis,
            > defined by at least 1 of:

            -   joint space preservation at least 50%

                > Kellgren-Lawrence grade 2 or less

                > modified Outerbridge grade 3 or less

    > Chondroplasty/debridement

    -   Requires focal cartilage lesion or unstable chondral flap
        > documented by MRI

        > And both:

        -   radiographic imaging with Kellgren-Lawrence grade 2 or less

            > at least 1 of:

            -   pain with mechanical symptoms

                > failure of at least 6 weeks of conservative management
                > when there is no chondral flap or mechanical symptoms

    > Posterolateral corner reconstruction

    -   All required:

        -   imaging confirmation of posterolateral structure injury

            > physical exam consistent with posterolateral corner injury

            > associated ligament injuries requiring treatment

    > Collateral or extra-articular ligament repair/reconstruction

    -   Either:

        -   diagnosis supported by advanced imaging showing a complete
            > tear, or exam findings showing instability due to the
            > ligament injury, with 6 weeks of conservative management

            > or associated ligament injury treatment

    > Lateral retinacular release for patellar compression syndrome

    -   All required:

        -   positive lateral patellar tilt on imaging

            > failure of at least 6 months of conservative management

            > Kellgren-Lawrence grade 2 or less patellofemoral
            > osteoarthritis

            > at least 1 of:

            -   pain with patellar compression and lateral facet
                > tenderness

                > inability to evert the lateral edge of the patella

                > positive patella glide test

                > positive patella tilt test

                > lateral trochlear or lateral patellar facet cartilage
                > lesion on recent imaging when symptoms match

    > Quadricepsplasty

    -   All required:

        -   knee extension contracture after prior femur or knee
            > fracture or surgery

            > knee flexion less than 90 degrees

            > failure of at least 12 weeks of conservative management

            > failure of arthroscopic lysis of adhesions

    > Distal realignment procedure for patellar instability

    -   In skeletally mature patients, all required:

        -   recurrent patellofemoral instability with pain limiting
            > function, or failure of at least 12 weeks of conservative
            > management including physical therapy

            > Kellgren-Lawrence grade 2 or less patellofemoral
            > osteoarthritis

            > at least 1 of:

            -   tibial tubercle-trochlear groove distance greater than
                > 20 mm

                > patella alta

    > Medial patellofemoral ligament reconstruction

    -   Either:

        -   done with distal realignment for patellofemoral instability

            > or all of the following:

            -   recurrent patellofemoral instability with pain limiting
                > function, or failure of at least 12 weeks of
                > conservative management

                > Kellgren-Lawrence grade 2 or less patellofemoral
                > osteoarthritis

                > tibial tubercle-trochlear groove distance less than 20
                > mm, normal trochlear morphology, and no patella alta

    > Plica resection

    -   At least 2 of the following 5:

        -   anteromedial knee joint line pain, especially at the medial
            > femoral condyle

            > audible clicking or snapping with painful arc from 30 to
            > 60 degrees

            > pain with stairs, squatting, rising from a chair, or
            > prolonged sitting

            > positive plica-specific test

            > visible or palpable tender plica

        > And both additional requirements:

        -   exclusion of other causes of anteromedial knee pain

            > failure of at least 12 weeks of conservative management

    > Excision of popliteal cyst

    -   Both required:

        -   posterior knee pain rated at least 3/10 for at least 12
            > weeks

            > imaging-confirmed popliteal cyst

    > Limited synovectomy

    -   All required:

        -   imaging confirmation of a localized synovial proliferative
            > process

            > exam findings and/or symptoms consistent with that process

            > documentation of at least 12 weeks of conservative
            > management

    > Major synovectomy

    -   Medically necessary for any of:

        -   rheumatoid arthritis or other chronic inflammatory
            > arthropathy with failure of medical management

            > hemophilic joint disease

            > diffuse synovial proliferative disorders such as pigmented
            > villonodular synovitis, synovial hemangioma, synovial
            > chondromatosis/osteochondromatosis, or infectious
            > synovitis

    > Osteochondritis dissecans

    -   Surgical treatment is medically necessary when either:

        -   failure of 12 weeks of conservative management

            > unstable lesion on advanced imaging or arthroscopy These
            > criteria are drawn from the knee arthroscopy section

Required Documentation

-   Imaging report from the past 12 months correlated with clinical
    > findings

    > Detailed x-ray description or radiology report

    > Radiologist report or summarized advanced imaging findings in the
    > clinical record

    > Documentation of conservative treatment when applicable

    > Documentation of compliance with therapy when applicable

    > Reevaluation near the anticipated procedure date

Limitations and Exclusions

-   In-office diagnostic arthroscopy is not medically necessary

    > Removal of loose body for Kellgren-Lawrence grade 4 osteoarthritis
    > is not medically necessary

    > Meniscal repair or meniscectomy is not medically necessary for:

    -   Kellgren-Lawrence grade 4 or diffuse modified Outerbridge grade
        > 4 changes with knee pain predating recent injury

        > partial meniscectomy for degenerative tears without mechanical
        > symptoms

    > Lateral retinacular release for medial tracking of the patella is
    > not medically necessary

    > Separate synovectomy for exposure/visualization or for
    > post-traumatic reactive synovitis is not medically necessary

    > Repair of subchondral bone defects using engineered calcium
    > phosphate or similar filler material is not medically necessary

    > Use of an implantable shock absorber is not medically necessary in
    > the osteochondritis dissecans context

    > Chondroplasty and meniscectomy performed together in the same knee
    > are considered part of the primary procedure rather than
    > separately necessary services

MENISCAL ALLOGRAFT TRANSPLANTATION OF THE KNEE Medical Necessity
Criteria

-   Medically necessary for significant partial loss of the meniscus
    > greater than 50% or complete loss of the meniscus, documented by
    > prior operative report, MRI, or diagnostic arthroscopy

    > And all of the following:

    -   age 55 or younger

        > skeletally mature

        > knee pain refractory to conservative treatment

        > ligamentous stability present before surgery or restored at
        > the same time

        > normal alignment without varus or valgus deformity

        > mild to moderate articular damage, defined as modified
        > Outerbridge grade 2 or less

    > Corrective procedures such as ligament/tendon repair, osteotomy
    > for realignment, or osteochondral treatment may be done at the
    > same time or beforehand

    > Not medically necessary for other meniscal implant types made with
    > collagen, polyurethane, or similar substitute materials

    > The listed G code corresponds to a collagen meniscus implant
    > procedure, but the policy specifically excludes
    > collagen/polyurethane meniscal implants from medical necessity
    > coverage under this guideline

OSTEOCHONDRAL GRAFTS Medical Necessity Criteria

-   General patient selection requirements

    -   All required:

        -   skeletal maturity documented by growth plate closure, or
            > lesion location not near the growth plate

            > disabling localized knee or ankle pain for at least 3
            > months

            > failure of at least 6 weeks of conservative treatment,
            > unless there is a symptomatic loose body

            > no local or systemic infection

            > no history of cancer involving bone, cartilage, fat, or
            > muscle in the affected limb

            > willingness and ability to comply with postoperative
            > weight-bearing restrictions and rehabilitation

        > And all lesion/joint characteristics:

        -   lesion is discrete and single, involving only one side of
            > the joint

            > lesion is largely contained with near-normal surrounding
            > cartilage

            > joint space is normal without inflammatory or degenerative
            > change

            > knee or ankle joint is stable with intact supporting
            > structures

            > normal alignment

        > Corrective procedures such as ligament/tendon repair,
        > osteotomy, meniscal transplant, or meniscal repair may be done
        > concurrently or beforehand

    > Osteochondral allograft transplantation for knee cartilage defects

    -   Both required:

        -   defect size at least 1.0 cm2 documented by MRI or
            > arthroscopy

            > focal full-thickness grade 3 or 4 isolated defect of the
            > weight-bearing medial femoral condyle, lateral femoral
            > condyle, trochlear region, or patella

    > Osteochondral autograft transplantation for knee cartilage defects

    -   All required:

        -   defect size between 1.0 and 2.5 cm2 documented by MRI or
            > arthroscopy

            > focal full-thickness grade 3 or 4 isolated defect
            > involving the weight-bearing medial or lateral femoral
            > condyle or trochlear region

            > no kissing lesion

    > Osteochondral autograft transplantation for talus cartilage
    > defects

    -   Either:

        -   large lesion greater than 1.0 cm2 or cystic lesion volume
            > greater than 3.0 cm3 without prior treatment

            > revision after failed marrow stimulation

    > Autologous chondrocyte implantation for knee/patella defects

    -   All required:

        -   primary chondral defect is present, or prior surgery failed

            > defect size at least 1.5 cm2 documented by MRI or
            > arthroscopy

            > focal full-thickness grade 3 or 4 isolated unipolar defect
            > involving the weight-bearing medial femoral condyle,
            > lateral femoral condyle, or patellofemoral region

            > defect limited to cartilage and not the subchondral bone,
            > except certain osteochondritis dissecans lesions with
            > small bony defects

            > minimal to absent surrounding degenerative change, with
            > modified Outerbridge grade 2 or less and normal-appearing
            > surrounding cartilage

            > normal knee biomechanics/alignment, with stability
            > restored if needed These criteria are described in the
            > osteochondral graft section

Limitations, Contraindications, and Exclusions

-   Contraindications for all procedures in this guideline include:

    -   allergy to gentamicin or other aminoglycosides

        > sensitivity to porcine or bovine culture products

        > severe knee osteoarthritis, Kellgren-Lawrence grade 3 or 4

        > inflammatory arthritis, inflammatory joint disease, or
        > uncorrected congenital coagulation disorder

        > knee surgery within the prior 6 months, except biopsy
        > procurement or concomitant preparation for implantation

        > inability to comply with prescribed postoperative
        > rehabilitation

    > Exclusions include:

    -   non-autologous mosaicplasty using resorbable synthetic bone
        > filler materials

        > minced articular cartilage products used to repair knee or
        > ankle osteochondral defects

        > particulated juvenile articular cartilage products

        > decellularized osteochondral allograft plugs or reduced
        > osteochondral allograft discs

        > autologous chondrocyte implantation in joints other than the
        > knee

        > allografts preserved by nonstandard tissue-bank methods

        > very large allograft reconstructions replacing half or more of
        > knee articular surfaces as an alternative to total joint
        > replacement for degenerative disease

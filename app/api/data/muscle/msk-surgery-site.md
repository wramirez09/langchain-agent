MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

General clinical appropriateness requirements

-   Before any intervention, the clinician should:

    -   Confirm the diagnosis or establish a reasonable pretest
        > likelihood

        > Perform a complete evaluation

        > Include history and physical examination

        > Review relevant laboratory studies when applicable

        > Review diagnostic testing when applicable

        > Review response to prior therapeutic intervention when
        > applicable

    > The expected benefit of the intervention should outweigh potential
    > harms, including harms related to delay or decreased access.

    > Current guidelines, clinical literature, or accepted standards of
    > practice should support the intervention as the best option among
    > alternatives.

    > The intervention should be reasonably likely to change management
    > or improve patient outcome.

Site-of-care medical necessity for hospital outpatient department

-   A hospital outpatient department is considered medically necessary
    > when the requested outpatient surgical service meets applicable
    > service criteria and ANY of the following is true:

1.  Need for ancillary resources not available outside the hospital
    > outpatient department

-   Expected surgical duration greater than 3 hours

    > Anticipated difficulty establishing or maintaining an airway based
    > on preoperative airway assessment, such as:

    -   Prior difficult intubation

        > Craniofacial abnormalities

        > Limited neck extension

    > Prior unexpected surgical or anesthetic complication causing
    > instability and requiring unplanned admission or extra care beyond
    > routine recovery, such as:

    -   Resuscitation

        > Management of hemodynamic instability

        > Prolonged observation

        > Transfusion

2\. Clinical comorbidities or complexities requiring higher-acuity
resources not available outside the hospital outpatient department

-   Age 18 or younger

    > Age 75 or older

    > Pregnancy

    > Increased bleeding risk

    > Intellectual disability or cognitive impairment

    > ASA class III or higher comorbidity, including examples such as:

    -   Body mass index 40 or greater

        > Treatment-resistant hypertension

        > Poorly controlled diabetes

        > End-stage renal disease

        > History of stroke or transient ischemic attack

        > History of myocardial infarction or acute coronary syndrome

        > Severe valvular heart disease

        > Sustained symptomatic arrhythmia despite treatment

        > NYHA class III or IV heart failure

        > Moderate or severe asthma

        > Moderate or severe COPD

        > Moderate or severe obstructive sleep apnea

        > Liver cirrhosis with MELD score greater than 8

3\. Risk of clinically significant delay in care

-   The procedure outside the hospital outpatient department would
    > reasonably be expected to create a clinically significant delay in
    > care.

4\. No geographically accessible non-hospital alternative

-   There is no geographically accessible non-hospital facility capable
    > of performing the requested procedure.

    > The exact meaning of inaccessibility may vary by health plan.

Defined subcriteria for key comorbidities

-   Moderate or severe asthma may be present when any of the following
    > apply:

    -   Daytime symptoms every day

        > Nocturnal symptoms at least once per week

        > Daily use of rescue inhaler

        > FEV1 less than 80 percent predicted despite medical management

    > Moderate or severe COPD:

    -   FEV1/FVC ratio 0.7 or less

        > FEV1 less than 80 percent predicted

    > Poorly controlled diabetes:

    -   Hemoglobin A1c greater than 9 for more than 1 year

        > Hospitalization for diabetic ketoacidosis within 6 months

        > Treatment for hypoglycemia within the past month

    > Treatment-resistant hypertension:

    -   Persistent hypertension while taking 3 or more antihypertensive
        > medications

    > Increased bleeding risk:

    -   Prior abnormal bleeding

        > Bleeding disorder or condition predisposing to bleeding

        > Prolonged INR despite treatment

        > Need to continue anticoagulant or antiplatelet therapy into
        > perioperative period

        > Anticipated need for transfusion, blood products, or other
        > bleeding-control support

    > Moderate or severe obstructive sleep apnea:

    -   Diagnosed by polysomnography or home sleep testing

        > AHI or RDI 15 or greater

    > End-stage renal disease:

    -   On regular hemodialysis

        > Or glomerular filtration rate less than 15 mL/min/1.73 m2

    > NYHA functional class III or IV heart failure qualifies under the
    > cardiac risk examples.

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

Core clinical documentation

-   History and physical examination

    > Relevant laboratory studies when applicable

    > Relevant diagnostic testing when applicable

    > Response to prior therapeutic intervention when applicable

    > Documentation sufficient to confirm the diagnosis or pretest
    > likelihood

    > Documentation supporting expected clinical benefit and need for
    > the requested setting

Request support documentation

-   Documentation must accurately reflect the patient's clinical status
    > at the time of the request.

    > Documentation must clearly show the ordering provider's clinical
    > intent.

    > Additional clinical documentation may be required to support the
    > request.

If requesting hospital outpatient department level of care, records
should support the applicable reason, such as:

-   Need for hospital-only ancillary resources

    > Procedure duration expectation over 3 hours

    > Airway risk findings from preoperative assessment

    > Prior unexpected surgical or anesthetic complication

    > Age-based risk

    > Pregnancy status

    > Bleeding risk details

    > Cognitive or intellectual impairment

    > Specific ASA III or higher comorbidity

    > Risk of delay in care if done elsewhere

    > Lack of accessible non-hospital facility

Potential additional review triggers

-   Peer-to-peer discussion may be needed when documentation does not
    > sufficiently establish appropriateness.

    > Multiple simultaneous diagnostic or therapeutic requests may
    > require further review.

    > Repeated testing or repeated intervention may require added
    > justification.

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

Scope limitations

-   The guideline addresses site of care only, not whether the
    > individual surgical procedure is itself medically necessary.

    > Some services may also need separate clinical appropriateness
    > review under another guideline.

    > It does not define provider qualifications or credentialing
    > requirements.

    > It does not define facility accreditation or equipment adequacy
    > standards.

Excluded procedures

-   Procedures that routinely require post-procedure hospital admission
    > are excluded from this guideline.

Operational limitations

-   Applies to a subset of outpatient procedures typically performed
    > outside the hospital.

    > Intended for cases with an expected same-day discharge plan,
    > including home care and pain control appropriate to the procedure.

    > Does not require all qualifying patients to be treated in a
    > hospital outpatient department; many may still safely receive care
    > in non-hospital settings.

Coverage and policy limitations

-   Does not address plan coverage, member benefits, or other
    > plan-specific rules.

    > Federal and state mandates may supersede the guideline.

    > Medicare-specific criteria may apply where established.

    > Authorization requirements may vary by health plan.

REPEAT TESTING / REPEAT INTERVENTION LIMITATIONS

-   Repeated testing of the same anatomic area for the same indication
    > is generally limited to:

    -   Post-intervention evaluation

        > Change in clinical status requiring new management decisions

        > Clarification of an earlier finding by a different technique
        > or protocol

    > Repeated testing may be subject to added review when due to:

    -   Technical issues at the same facility

        > Different facility request based on provider preference or
        > quality concern

        > Persistent symptoms without clinical change or treatment since
        > prior study

        > Same-area testing by multiple providers over a short period

    > Repeat therapeutic intervention in the same area is generally
    > appropriate only if the prior intervention helped and the expected
    > duration of relief has elapsed.

    > A repeat intervention before the expected duration of relief is
    > not appropriate unless the earlier intervention was never actually
    > performed.

BOTTOM LINE

-   Hospital outpatient department care is medically necessary for
    > certain outpatient surgical procedures when the patient needs
    > higher-acuity support, has significant comorbidity or complexity,
    > faces clinically meaningful delay outside the hospital setting, or
    > lacks access to an appropriate non-hospital alternative.

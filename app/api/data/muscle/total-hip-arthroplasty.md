MEDICAL NECESSITY CRITERIA

Total hip arthroplasty (THA) is the surgical removal and replacement of the femoral head and acetabular cartilage with prosthetic components. It is indicated when advanced hip joint disease causes persistent pain and functional limitation that is unresponsive to appropriate conservative management. There is no medical necessity to perform THA in individuals who have severe radiological disease but no symptoms, except in the case of acute femoral neck fracture or malignancy.

THA is considered medically necessary when the member meets the criteria of Section One OR Section Two below.

SECTION ONE — PRIMARY HIP ARTHRITIS OR STRUCTURAL HIP DISEASE

All of the following must be met:

-   Radiographic evidence of advanced hip joint disease on weight-bearing anteroposterior pelvis and lateral hip views, defined as at least one of:
    -   Kellgren-Lawrence grade 3 or 4 osteoarthritis (severe joint space narrowing, subchondral sclerosis, osteophyte formation, or bone-on-bone contact)
    -   Advanced avascular necrosis (osteonecrosis) of the femoral head with collapse, flattening, or subchondral fracture (ARCO/Steinberg stage III or higher)
    -   Post-traumatic arthritis of the hip secondary to prior acetabular or femoral fracture with joint space loss equivalent to Kellgren-Lawrence grade 3 or 4
    -   Hip dysplasia (developmental dysplasia of the hip) with secondary osteoarthritis at Kellgren-Lawrence grade 3 or 4
    -   Inflammatory arthritis of the hip (rheumatoid arthritis, psoriatic arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, or other inflammatory arthropathy) with radiographic evidence of advanced joint destruction

-   Persistent hip pain causing clinically significant functional impairment, affecting activities of daily living (ADLs) such as ambulation, stair climbing, rising from a chair, or personal care, despite completion of appropriate conservative management as specified below

-   Failure of at least 3 months of conservative management that must include all of the following unless individually contraindicated with documented rationale:
    -   Supervised physical therapy or a structured home exercise program with patient-specific hip strengthening and range-of-motion exercises, documented compliance, and initial instruction by a qualified clinician
    -   Anti-inflammatory or analgesic medication (oral NSAID, acetaminophen, or topical anti-inflammatory agent) unless contraindicated by comorbidity
    -   At least one additional conservative measure, such as:
        -   activity modification or relative rest
        -   use of an assistive device (cane, walker)
        -   intra-articular corticosteroid or viscosupplementation injection
        -   weight management counseling when BMI exceeds 35

-   Failure of conservative management is established by documented persistent or worsening pain and functional limitation on clinical reevaluation at or near the time of the surgical request

-   Clinical reevaluation within 90 days of the requested procedure date confirming continued symptoms and functional status

SECTION TWO — ACUTE OR URGENT INDICATIONS

THA or hemiarthroplasty is considered medically necessary, without a required trial of conservative management, for any of the following acute or urgent conditions:

-   Displaced femoral neck fracture (Garden type III or IV, or AO/OTA type 31-B2/B3) in a physiologically appropriate candidate in whom arthroplasty is preferred over internal fixation based on fracture pattern, bone quality, pre-injury activity level, and surgeon judgment
-   Non-displaced or valgus-impacted femoral neck fracture in an elderly or low-demand patient where arthroplasty is clinically preferred given fracture displacement risk and patient factors
-   Pathologic fracture of the proximal femur secondary to primary bone tumor or metastatic disease where arthroplasty provides superior mechanical reconstruction
-   Acute-on-chronic femoral head collapse due to avascular necrosis or inflammatory arthritis when conservative management is no longer feasible given the structural failure

INDICATIONS

THA addresses end-stage hip joint failure from structural or inflammatory disease. The most common indications are:

Primary osteoarthritis of the hip:
-   Idiopathic (primary) osteoarthritis, typically presenting in adults over age 50, with progressive loss of articular cartilage, joint space narrowing, subchondral sclerosis, and osteophyte formation
-   Diagnosis confirmed by weight-bearing anteroposterior pelvis radiographs; MRI is not routinely required for primary OA but may be used to evaluate concurrent soft-tissue pathology or early AVN

Avascular necrosis (osteonecrosis) of the femoral head:
-   AVN resulting from corticosteroid use, alcohol use disorder, sickle cell disease, prior hip trauma, radiation exposure, coagulopathy, or idiopathic causes
-   Advanced stages with femoral head collapse (ARCO stage III-IV; Steinberg stage IV-VI) not amenable to joint-preserving procedures
-   Earlier-stage AVN with large lesion size or failed prior core decompression where head preservation is no longer viable may also qualify with adequate clinical and radiographic documentation

Femoral neck fracture:
-   Displaced femoral neck fracture in older adults or those with poor bone quality where internal fixation carries high risk of non-union or avascular necrosis
-   Activity level, cognitive status, comorbidities, and contralateral hip status inform the choice between hemiarthroplasty and THA

Inflammatory arthritis:
-   Rheumatoid arthritis, psoriatic arthritis, ankylosing spondylitis, systemic lupus erythematosus, or other inflammatory arthropathy with advanced hip involvement and documented failure of disease-modifying anti-rheumatic drug (DMARD) therapy coordinated with the treating rheumatologist
-   Biologic DMARD agents should typically be held perioperatively per rheumatology guidance

Post-traumatic and secondary arthritis:
-   Post-traumatic hip arthritis following acetabular fracture, femoral head fracture, or prior proximal femur fracture with resultant joint incongruity and progressive arthritis
-   Secondary osteoarthritis from developmental dysplasia of the hip (DDH), Legg-Calve-Perthes disease, or slipped capital femoral epiphysis with advanced structural failure

REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

The following documentation must be submitted with the prior authorization request:

Imaging:
-   Weight-bearing anteroposterior pelvis radiograph and lateral hip radiograph from within the past 12 months, with a radiology report or treating physician's detailed description of findings including joint space measurement, Kellgren-Lawrence grade (when applicable), osteophyte formation, subchondral changes, and degree of deformity
-   For suspected avascular necrosis: MRI of the affected hip confirming the diagnosis, lesion classification (ARCO or Steinberg stage), and extent of femoral head involvement
-   For femoral neck fracture: acute plain radiographs and CT scan if fracture pattern is complex or if plain films are inconclusive

Clinical documentation:
-   History and physical examination note with documented hip pain localization, character, severity (numeric rating scale or equivalent), and duration
-   Functional status assessment documenting limitations in ADLs, ambulation distance, stair use, and occupational or recreational activities (Harris Hip Score, HOOS, or equivalent patient-reported outcome measure is preferred but not required)
-   Documentation of the type, duration, and compliance with each element of conservative management, including dates of physical therapy attendance or home program instruction, medication names and trial duration, and any injections with dates and clinical response
-   Clinical reevaluation note from within 90 days of the authorization request confirming continued symptoms and failure of conservative management
-   Operative indication note from the requesting surgeon describing the surgical plan, implant type (cemented, cementless, or hybrid), bearing surface selection, and approach
-   For inflammatory arthritis: rheumatology consultation or note documenting current DMARD/biologic regimen and perioperative management plan
-   For femoral neck fracture: emergency or hospital admission note, fracture classification, and documentation of pre-injury functional status

Comorbidity and risk documentation:
-   Cardiopulmonary clearance or risk stratification if clinically indicated given the member's medical history
-   Current BMI and documentation of weight management counseling or bariatric referral if BMI exceeds 40 (relative contraindication threshold may vary by payer)
-   Anesthesia consultation for ASA class III or higher patients

LIMITATIONS AND EXCLUSIONS

-   THA is not medically necessary for radiographically advanced hip disease in the absence of significant symptoms and functional impairment
-   THA is not medically necessary when an adequate trial of conservative management has not been completed or documented, unless an acute or urgent indication (Section Two) applies
-   THA is not medically necessary as a primary treatment for hip pain attributed to extra-articular sources (trochanteric bursitis, iliopsoas tendinopathy, piriformis syndrome, lumbar radiculopathy) that have not been adequately evaluated or treated
-   Unicompartmental (surface replacement) hip arthroplasty is subject to separate review criteria and is not covered under this guideline
-   Revision THA is subject to separate review criteria; the initial request should specify primary versus revision intent; if findings at time of surgery may require conversion from planned hemiarthroplasty to THA, separate requests must be submitted
-   Absolute contraindications that preclude authorization unless clinically resolved or addressed:
    -   Active local or systemic infection, including active periprosthetic joint infection of the same or contralateral hip, until adequately treated and cleared
    -   Absence of functional abductor musculature that cannot be reconstructed, making a stable prosthetic reconstruction anatomically infeasible
    -   Active malignancy involving the proximal femur or acetabulum when the surgical plan does not account for oncologic margins and reconstruction
    -   Severe cardiopulmonary disease that precludes safe administration of anesthesia (ASA class V or equivalent) without documented clearance
    -   Active intravenous drug use without enrollment in a supervised treatment program, unless an urgent fracture indication applies
-   Relative contraindications that require documented discussion, multidisciplinary input, or mitigation prior to authorization:
    -   BMI 40 or higher: documentation of weight management discussion and shared decision-making is required; some payers require documented weight loss attempts or bariatric surgery referral
    -   Uncontrolled diabetes mellitus (HbA1c greater than 8.0): documentation of optimization efforts and endocrinology input is expected
    -   Neuromuscular conditions affecting the hip (Parkinson disease, significant cerebral palsy spasticity) that substantially increase dislocation risk: documentation of surgeon's plan for constraint or soft-tissue management is required
    -   Dementia or other cognitive impairment: documentation of caregiver support plan and realistic rehabilitation expectations is required
    -   Any corticosteroid injection into the affected hip joint within 12 weeks of the planned surgery date; authorization may be deferred until the 12-week waiting period has elapsed given infection risk data
    -   Prior hip or pelvic radiation to the surgical field: documentation of radiation dose history and surgeon's implant/fixation strategy is required
-   THA performed solely for the purpose of leg-length equalization without underlying hip joint pathology is not medically necessary
-   Metal-on-metal bearing surface selection must comply with current FDA guidance; prior authorization for metal-on-metal THA requires documentation of specific clinical rationale given the FDA advisory regarding metal ion risks

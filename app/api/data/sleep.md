1\) General Required Documentation

\## Core documentation expected

\- Complete clinical history

\- Physical examination

\- Relevant prior laboratory studies, if applicable

\- Relevant prior diagnostic testing

\- Response to prior treatment, if applicable

\- Documentation that accurately reflects the clinical status at the
time of request

\- Documentation that clearly reflects the ordering provider's intent

\## Additional recurring documentation themes

\- Sleep study results with AHI/RDI/REI values when applicable

\- PAP compliance/adherence data when ongoing therapy is being requested

\- Treating provider attestation of clinical benefit when allowed
instead of device usage threshold

\- Prior treatment failure, intolerance, or contraindication when
escalation to another therapy is requested

\- Weight change documentation when repeat testing is requested due to
significant weight loss

\- For obesity hypoventilation syndrome (OHS), documentation of
hypoventilation by one of the required physiologic thresholds

\- For periodic limb movement disorder (PLMD) in the diagnostic section,
supporting clinical documentation is required

\# 2) Diagnostic Sleep Testing

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\### Home Sleep Apnea Testing (HSAT) for suspected obstructive sleep
apnea

HSAT is medically necessary when any of the following apply:

\- Observed apneas during sleep

\- A combination of at least 2 of the following 5 criteria:

\- Excessive daytime sleepiness

\- Habitual snoring or gasping/choking awakenings

\- Treatment-resistant hypertension

\- Obesity or large neck circumference

\- Craniofacial or upper-airway soft tissue abnormality, including
adenotonsillar hypertrophy, or neuromuscular disease

\- History of stroke more than 30 days earlier, transient ischemic
attack, coronary artery disease, or sustained supraventricular
tachycardic or bradycardic arrhythmias in a patient who also meets at
least 1 of the 5 criteria above

\- Conditions that may suggest OSA when the cause is unclear:

\- Right heart failure

\- Polycythemia

\- Sustained supraventricular or ventricular tachyarrhythmia occurring
only during sleep

\- Pulmonary hypertension

\### Follow-up HSAT for established OSA

Medically necessary when any of the following apply:

\- One-time follow-up after:

\- Upper-airway surgery performed to treat OSA and/or improve PAP
compliance

\- Initiation of an oral appliance

\- Reevaluation after significant weight loss (10% of body weight) to
determine whether OSA and need for CPAP persist

\- Prior to hypoglossal nerve stimulator implantation if no diagnostic
sleep study has been performed within the prior 18 months

\### In-lab sleep study in adults

Medically necessary for suspected OSA when the patient has a
contraindication to HSAT and meets any of the following:

\- Observed apneas during sleep

\- At least 2 of the same 5 OSA-related clinical criteria listed above

\- History of stroke more than 30 days earlier, TIA, CAD, or sustained
supraventricular tachycardic or bradycardic arrhythmias in a patient
meeting at least 1 of the 5 criteria

\- Conditions that may suggest OSA when the cause is unclear:

\- Right heart failure

\- Polycythemia

\- Sustained supraventricular or ventricular tachyarrhythmia only during
sleep

\- Pulmonary hypertension

\### Follow-up in-lab sleep study in adults with established OSA

Medically necessary when any of the following apply:

\- One-time follow-up after upper-airway surgery or oral appliance use
in a patient who has a contraindication to HSAT

\- Reevaluation after significant weight loss (10% body weight) in a
patient with HSAT contraindication

\- Prior to hypoglossal nerve stimulator implantation if no diagnostic
sleep study was done within the prior 18 months

\- One-time optimization after insertion of a hypoglossal or phrenic
nerve stimulator

\- CPAP/BPAP titration when:

\- The patient meets PAP criteria and has a contraindication to APAP, or

\- APAP titration attempt was unsuccessful, or

\- Split-night study did not adequately establish treatment settings

\- Re-titration when symptoms recur or worsen despite PAP adherence

\### In-lab sleep study in non-adults (17 years or younger)

Medically necessary for suspected sleep disorder when any of the
following apply:

\- Habitual snoring plus at least 1 of:

\- Restless/disturbed sleep

\- Behavioral disturbance or learning problems

\- Frequent awakenings

\- Enuresis

\- Growth retardation or failure to thrive

\- Excessive daytime somnolence or altered mental status not otherwise
explained

\- Polycythemia not otherwise explained

\- Cor pulmonale not otherwise explained

\- Witnessed apnea lasting more than 2 respiratory cycles

\- Labored breathing during sleep

\- Tonsillar/adenoidal hypertrophy when excluding OSA could avoid
surgery

\- Suspected congenital central alveolar hypoventilation syndrome or
sleep-related hypoventilation due to neuromuscular disease/chest wall
deformity

\- Apparent life-threatening event in an infant with evidence suggesting
a sleep-related breathing disorder

\- Exclusion of OSA more than 8 weeks after adenotonsillectomy for
suspected OSA

\- Initial study was inadequate, equivocal, or nondiagnostic and
home-observed breathing pattern differed from testing

\### Follow-up in-lab sleep study in non-adults

Medically necessary when any of the following apply:

\- Persistent snoring or other sleep-disordered breathing symptoms
despite PAP adherence

\- Adenotonsillectomy or other upper-airway surgery performed more than
8 weeks earlier for established OSA

\- Prior to hypoglossal nerve stimulator implantation if no diagnostic
study within the prior 18 months

\- Reevaluation after significant weight loss (10% of body weight)

\- CPAP/BPAP titration when the diagnostic study confirms PAP candidacy
and split-night study was not done or was inadequate

\- Change in clinical status or need to assess response after therapy
change when the original study showed a diagnosis other than OSA

\## Contraindications / Limitations

\### Contraindications to HSAT

\- Age 17 years or younger

\- Moderate or severe COPD

\- Moderate or severe congestive heart failure

\- CHF with ventricular fibrillation or sustained ventricular
tachycardia in a patient without an implanted defibrillator

\- Cognitive impairment preventing self-application of equipment when no
helper is available

\- Physical impairment preventing self-application of equipment when no
helper is available

\- Suspected or established:

\- Central sleep apnea

\- Narcolepsy

\- Idiopathic hypersomnia

\- Parasomnia other than bruxism and sleep talking

\- Nocturnal seizures

\- PLMD

\- Prior technically suboptimal HSAT under specified repeat-failure
scenarios

\- Prior 2-night HSAT that did not diagnose OSA despite ongoing
suspicion

\- Oxygen dependence

\- Stroke within the prior 30 days

\- Chronic opiate use when discontinuation is not possible

\- BMI greater than 33 kg/m2 plus serum bicarbonate greater than 28
mmol/L

\- Established OHS

\### Required documentation for suspected PLMD in this section

At least one of the following must be documented:

\- Pregnancy

\- Renal failure

\- Iron deficiency anemia

\- Peripheral neuropathy

\- Use of antidepressant or antipsychotic medication

\- Continued hypersomnia and clinical symptoms of PLMD after
sleep-disordered breathing has been ruled out by HSAT

\## Exclusions

\- Type IV HSAT is not medically necessary in all clinical scenarios

\# 3) MSLT / MWT

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\### Initial MSLT and/or MWT for suspected narcolepsy

Both are required:

\- Excessive daytime sleepiness for at least 8 weeks

\- At least 1 of:

\- Disrupted nocturnal sleep

\- Cataplexy

\- Hypnagogic or hypnopompic hallucinations

\- Sleep paralysis

\- Prior PSG since symptom onset, with symptoms persisting despite
adequate treatment of OSA if present

\### Repeat MSLT and/or MWT for suspected narcolepsy

Both are required:

\- Prior MSLT/MWT did not establish the diagnosis

\- Ongoing symptoms suggest narcolepsy

\### Repeat MWT for occupational safety evaluation

Both are required:

\- Established diagnosis of a sleep breathing disorder or narcolepsy

\- Testing is done while on current treatment to determine adequacy of
therapy

\### MSLT and/or MWT for idiopathic hypersomnia

Both are required:

\- Excessive daytime sleepiness for at least 8 weeks

\- At least 1 of:

\- Difficult morning awakening

\- Prolonged sleep during the primary sleep period

\- Sleep drunkenness

\- Frequent non-refreshing daytime naps

\- PSG or HSAT performed, with symptoms persisting despite adequate OSA
treatment if present

\## Required Documentation

\- Symptom duration

\- Clinical features supporting narcolepsy or idiopathic hypersomnia

\- Prior PSG or HSAT as required by the indication

\- Evidence that OSA has been treated when present

\- For repeat testing, prior nondiagnostic result plus persistent
symptoms

\- For occupational safety, diagnosis plus proof testing is on current
treatment

\## Limitations

\- MSLT/MWT should not be performed while the patient is under the
influence of:

\- Stimulants

\- Sedatives

\- REM-suppressing medications

\# 4) CPAP / APAP

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\### CPAP for adults

Both are required:

\- Home- or lab-based sleep study shows:

\- AHI 15 or greater, or

\- AHI 5-14 with at least one of:

\- Excessive daytime sleepiness

\- Impaired cognition

\- Mood disorders

\- Insomnia

\- Hypertension

\- Ischemic heart disease

\- History of stroke

\- Appropriate CPAP level has been determined

\### CPAP for non-adults

Both are required:

\- Lab-based sleep study with AHI of at least 1 and CPAP titration
performed

\- At least one of:

\- Adenotonsillectomy failed to cure OSA

\- Adenotonsillectomy not indicated due to minimal adenotonsillar tissue

\- OSA due to another cause such as craniofacial abnormality or morbid
obesity

\- Adenotonsillectomy contraindicated

\### APAP for adults

Both are required:

\- Home- or lab-based sleep study shows:

\- AHI 15 or greater, or

\- AHI 5-14 with at least one of:

\- Excessive daytime sleepiness

\- Impaired cognition

\- Mood disorders

\- Insomnia

\- Hypertension

\- Ischemic heart disease

\- History of stroke

\- No APAP contraindication

\### APAP for non-adults

All are required:

\- Lab-based sleep study with AHI of at least 1

\- Treating provider believes APAP is preferable to CPAP

\- At least one of:

\- Adenotonsillectomy failed

\- Adenotonsillectomy not indicated due to minimal adenotonsillar tissue

\- OSA due to another cause such as craniofacial abnormality or morbid
obesity

\- Adenotonsillectomy contraindicated

\### Ongoing APAP/CPAP

Medically necessary when compliance is shown:

\- Every 90 days during the first year

\- Annually thereafter

Compliance is met by either:

\- PAP use at least 4 hours per night on 70% of nights during a
consecutive 30-day period within the prior 90 days

\- Treating provider attests that the patient is receiving clinical
benefit at current usage levels

Non-adults do not require compliance documentation for continuation.

\## Required Documentation

\- Sleep study result with AHI threshold met

\- CPAP titration result when CPAP is requested

\- Pediatric surgical history/appropriateness of adenotonsillectomy when
relevant

\- APAP contraindication review

\- PAP adherence download or treating provider attestation for ongoing
therapy

\## Contraindications to APAP

\- Congestive heart failure

\- Moderate or severe COPD

\- Chronic opiate use

\- Supplemental oxygen used 24 hours per day

\- Central sleep apnea

\- Neuromuscular disorders

\- OHS

\### OHS documentation threshold

Any of the following:

\- PaCO2 or surrogate greater than 55 mmHg for at least 10 minutes

\- Increase in PaCO2 or surrogate by more than 10 mmHg during sleep to a
value above 50 mmHg for at least 10 minutes

\- Sleep oximetry showing oxygen saturation 88% or lower for at least 5
consecutive minutes during nocturnal recording of at least 2 hours while
on prescribed FiO2

\## Exclusions

\- Positive airway pressure modalities and add-on devices not addressed
in this policy are not medically necessary

\- Examples listed:

\- PapNap

\- Provent

\- Headstraps

\- Certain dental devices

\- Cloth mask accessories

\- Miscellaneous equipment code E1399 is specifically identified for
these excluded items

\# 5) BPAP

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\### BPAP without back-up rate

Medically necessary for:

\- OSA that has failed CPAP/APAP, or

\- Need for supplemental ventilatory support due to hypoventilation
syndrome

\### BPAP with or without back-up rate for established central sleep
apnea

Both are required:

\- CSA diagnosed by in-lab sleep study

\- OSA excluded or treated for at least 6 weeks

\- Titration study shows significant improvement of sleep-related
hypoventilation at the settings to be prescribed for home use while
breathing usual FiO2

\### Additional note for adaptive servo-ventilation mode

\- Appropriate only when left ventricular ejection fraction is greater
than 45%

\### BPAP with or without back-up rate for severe COPD

Either is sufficient:

\- Awake arterial blood gas PaCO2 52 mmHg or greater while breathing
usual FiO2

\- Sleep oximetry oxygen saturation 88% or less for at least 5
continuous minutes while breathing oxygen at 2 L/min or usual FiO2,
whichever is higher

\### BPAP with or without back-up rate for certain restrictive thoracic
disorders

Both are required:

\- Established diagnosis of progressive neuromuscular disease or severe
thoracic cage abnormality

\- At least one of:

\- Awake PaCO2 45 mmHg or greater while breathing usual FiO2

\- Sleep oximetry oxygen saturation 88% or less for at least 5
continuous minutes while breathing usual FiO2

\- Maximal inspiratory pressure less than 60 cm H2O

\- Forced vital capacity less than 50% predicted (progressive
neuromuscular disease only)

\### BPAP for obesity hypoventilation syndrome

\- The section includes OHS as an indication

\- Retrieved criteria define OHS as:

\- BMI greater than 30 kg/m2, and

\- Hypoventilation not solely attributable to pulmonary disease,
skeletal restriction, neuromuscular weakness, hypothyroidism, pleural
pathology, or medications

\- Supporting hypoventilation documentation follows the same physiologic
thresholds used elsewhere in the document:

\- PaCO2 or surrogate greater than 55 mmHg for at least 10 minutes, or

\- Rise in PaCO2 or surrogate greater than 10 mmHg during sleep to above
50 mmHg for at least 10 minutes, or

\- Sleep oximetry oxygen saturation 88% or less for at least 5
consecutive minutes during at least 2 hours of nocturnal recording on
prescribed FiO2

\### Ongoing BPAP for OSA

Adults must show compliance:

\- Every 90 days during first year

\- Annually thereafter

Compliance is met by either:

\- Device use at least 4 hours per night on 70% of nights during a
consecutive 30-day period within the prior 90 days

\- Treating provider attestation of ongoing clinical benefit

Compliance documentation is not required:

\- For non-adults

\- When BPAP is used for conditions other than OSA and CSA

\## Required Documentation

\- Prior CPAP/APAP failure if BPAP is requested for OSA

\- In-lab confirmation of CSA when applicable

\- OSA excluded or treated for at least 6 weeks for CSA requests

\- Titration study showing benefit at prescribed settings

\- ABG, sleep oximetry, inspiratory pressure, or FVC thresholds where
applicable

\- For ongoing therapy, adherence report or treating provider
attestation

addressed are not medically necessary

\- Provent

\# 6) Oral Appliances for OSA

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\### Device standards for mandibular repositioning appliances

An MRA must:

\- Have a fixed mechanical hinge at the sides, front, or palate

\- Allow mandibular advancement in increments of 1 mm or less

\- Protrude the mandible beyond the front teeth at maximum protrusion

\- Be adjustable by the patient in increments of 1 mm or less

\- Retain the adjustment setting when removed

\- Maintain mouth position during sleep to prevent dislodgement

\### Custom-fabricated oral appliance for severe OSA

All are required:

\- Age 16 years or older

\- Severe OSA with AHI greater than 30

\- Device is a tongue-retaining device or compliant mandibular
repositioning appliance

\- No periodontal disease

\- At least one of:

\- Not a candidate for PAP

\- PAP ineffective despite 45-day trial and PAP compliance program
participation

\- Tried CPAP but remained noncompliant despite 45-day trial and PAP
compliance program participation

\### Custom-fabricated oral appliance for mild or moderate OSA

All are required:

\- Age 16 years or older

\- Device is a tongue-retaining device or compliant mandibular
repositioning appliance

\- No periodontal disease

\- At least one of:

\- AHI 15-30

\- AHI 5-14 with one of:

\- Excessive daytime sleepiness

\- Impaired cognition

\- Mood disorders

\- Insomnia

\- Treatment-resistant hypertension

\- Ischemic heart disease

\- History of stroke

\- At least one of:

\- Not a candidate for PAP

\- PAP ineffective despite 45-day trial and PAP compliance program
participation

\- Tried CPAP but remained noncompliant despite 45-day trial and PAP
compliance program participation

\- Patient prefers oral appliance instead of PAP as initial therapy

\## Required Documentation

\- Age

\- AHI severity from sleep study

\- Type of oral appliance requested

\- Confirmation of no periodontal disease

\- PAP failure, PAP intolerance, PAP noncompliance, PAP
contraindication, or patient preference, depending on indication

\- Prescription from a medical provider for custom device

\## Limitations / Exclusions

\- Use caution with temporomandibular joint disease

\- Avoid in periodontal disease

\- Prefabricated oral appliances are not medically necessary in all
clinical scenarios

\# 7) Implanted Hypoglossal Nerve Stimulation

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\### Adults with OSA

All are required:

\- Age 18 years or older

\- AHI or RDI 15-65

\- Less than 25% central events

\- BMI 32 kg/m2 or less

\- Failed or intolerant of PAP therapy

\- Non-concentric retropalatal obstruction on drug-induced sleep
endoscopy

\### Adolescents and young adults with Down syndrome and OSA

All are required:

\- Age 13 to 21 years

\- AHI or RDI 10-50 with less than 25% central apneas

\- Prior adenotonsillectomy performed, or contraindicated

\- BMI at or below 95th percentile for age

\- Either:

\- Prior tracheotomy, or

\- CPAP ineffective because of noncompliance, discomfort, undesirable
side effects, persistent symptoms despite compliant use, or refusal to
use the device

\- Non-concentric retropalatal obstruction on drug-induced sleep
endoscopy

\## Required Documentation

\- Age

\- AHI/RDI and central event percentage

\- BMI

\- PAP failure or PAP intolerance details

\- Drug-induced sleep endoscopy result

\- For Down syndrome pathway:

\- Prior adenotonsillectomy or contraindication

\- Tracheotomy history or detailed CPAP nonuse/ineffectiveness rationale

\- Age-adjusted BMI percentile

\## Related Diagnostic Requirements

\- Diagnostic sleep study within the prior 18 months before implantation

\- Follow-up sleep testing may be used before implantation or for
one-time post-implant optimization

\# 8) Miscellaneous Devices for OSA and Restless Legs Syndrome

\## Medical Necessity Criteria

\- No positive medical-necessity criteria are provided for these devices

\- The section is exclusion-based

\## Exclusions

The following are not medically necessary in any clinical scenario:

\- Electronic positional therapy for OSA

\- Neuromuscular electrical training of the tongue musculature for OSA

\- Peroneal nerve stimulation for restless legs syndrome

\## Required Documentation

\- If requested, documentation would need to justify why the request
differs from the stated exclusions; otherwise these are excluded

\# 9) Key Limitations and Exclusions Summary

\## Diagnostic testing

\- Type IV HSAT excluded in all scenarios

\- HSAT inappropriate in many higher-risk or non-OSA scenarios,
including children, severe cardiopulmonary disease, neurologic/sleep
disorders other than OSA, oxygen dependence, chronic opiate use, and OHS

\## CPAP/APAP/BPAP

\- APAP cannot be used when contraindications are present

\- PAP devices/add-ons not specifically addressed are excluded

\- Ongoing adult PAP generally requires adherence proof or treating
provider attestation

\## Oral appliances

\- Prefabricated devices excluded

\- Custom devices require specific device features and appropriate
patient selection

\- Avoid with periodontal disease; caution with TMJ disease

\## Hypoglossal nerve stimulation

\- Requires strict age, BMI, AHI/RDI, central-event, PAP
failure/intolerance, and endoscopy criteria

\- Requires relatively recent sleep testing

\## Miscellaneous OSA/RLS devices

\- Positional therapy devices, tongue neuromuscular stimulation devices,
and peroneal nerve stimulation are not medically necessary under this
guideline

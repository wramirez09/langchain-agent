03Prior Authorization Required

Conditional

Commercial guidelines treat coronary artery calcium scoring as medically necessary only when used as an adjunct to global coronary or cardiovascular risk estimation over the next 10 years and when the result is expected to help guide management decisions. Prior authorization requirements vary by plan, but the request should be supported with risk calculation, risk enhancers, absence of known CAD, asymptomatic status, and a management-change rationale.

CAC testing provides a quantitative assessment of coronary artery calcium content in Agatston units, as an adjunct to the estimation of global risk for coronary or cardiovascular events over the next 10 years.
Atherosclerosis specificityNot documented
A CAC Score greater than 0 is a highly specific feature of coronary atherosclerosis.

Coronary artery calcium testing is considered medically necessary only to help with management decisions after global risk estimation for coronary or cardiovascular events over the next 10 years.

Include ASCVD 10-year risk calculation using the pooled cohort equation. The request should document the exact calculated 10-year ASCVD risk percentage.
Borderline ASCVD risk bandNot documented
For 10-year ASCVD risk 5% to less than 7.5%, coronary artery calcium scoring may be used when risk-enhancer information leaves uncertainty about whether to start statin therapy.
Intermediate ASCVD risk bandNot documented
For 10-year ASCVD risk 7.5% to less than 20%, coronary artery calcium scoring may be used when the decision about statin therapy remains uncertain after clinician-patient risk discussion.
Low-risk special circumstanceNot documented
For 10-year ASCVD risk less than 5%, coronary artery calcium scoring is generally not routine but may be considered in selected individuals when a strong risk enhancer, such as family history of premature ASCVD, materially changes risk discussion.
High-risk circumstanceNot documented
For 10-year ASCVD risk 20% or greater, coronary artery calcium scoring is less likely to change the need for intensive risk-reduction therapy; documentation should explain why CAC results are still necessary to change management.
No known CAD Not documented
Confirm no known CAD. The request should state that there is no prior myocardial infarction, coronary revascularization, known coronary stenosis, known coronary atherosclerosis, angina diagnosis, ischemic equivalent diagnosis, or established ASCVD that would make the test diagnostic rather than preventive / risk stratification.
Asymptomatic status Not documented
Confirm the member is asymptomatic. Coronary artery calcium scoring for risk stratification should not be substituted for diagnostic evaluation of active symptoms such as chest pain, pressure, dyspnea on exertion concerning for angina, syncope, acute coronary syndrome symptoms, or other symptoms requiring diagnostic cardiac imaging or functional testing.
Risk enhancers Not documented
Document risk enhancers, including family history of premature ASCVD, LDL levels, diabetes, smoking, chronic kidney disease, and inflammatory disorders.
Family history of premature ASCVDNot documented
Document whether there is family history of premature ASCVD and specify affected relative and age at event.
LDL levelsNot documented
Document lipid values, especially LDL cholesterol, and whether LDL cholesterol is 70 to 189 mg/dL when CAC is being used to refine statin decision-making in primary prevention.
DiabetesNot documented
Document whether diabetes is present. Diabetes may independently support statin therapy, and the request should explain why CAC results are needed to modify management if diabetes is present.
SmokingNot documented
Document current or prior smoking status because smoking changes ASCVD risk estimation and may affect interpretation of CAC = 0.
Chronic kidney diseaseNot documented
Document whether chronic kidney disease is present, including stage or estimated glomerular filtration rate when available.
Inflammatory disordersNot documented
Document whether chronic inflammatory disorders are present, such as rheumatoid arthritis, psoriasis, lupus, HIV, or other chronic inflammatory conditions.
How results will change management Not documented
The ordering clinician should document how results will change management, including whether CAC results will be used to start, intensify, or defer statin therapy and whether aspirin will be considered.
CAC = 0Not documented
If CAC = 0, statin therapy may be deferred or delayed when otherwise clinically appropriate, except when risk remains high because of circumstances such as diabetes, smoking, or strong family history of premature ASCVD.
CAC 1 to 99 Agatston unitsNot documented
If CAC is 1 to 99 Agatston units, statin therapy is generally favored, especially in individuals age 55 years or older.
CAC 100 Agatston units or greaterNot documented
If CAC is 100 Agatston units or greater, statin therapy is generally supported unless otherwise deferred after clinician-patient risk discussion.
CAC at or above the 75th percentileNot documented
If CAC is at or above the 75th percentile for age, sex, and ethnicity, statin therapy is generally supported unless otherwise deferred after clinician-patient risk discussion.
Aspirin considerationNot documented
If aspirin is being considered, document that CAC results will be used as part of individualized risk-benefit assessment, including bleeding risk; CAC 100 Agatston units or greater is commonly used as a threshold that may support net benefit in selected patients with low bleeding risk.
Expected benefit Not documented
Evidence should show that the requested test is expected to change management or improve outcomes and that expected benefits outweigh potential harms, including radiation exposure, incidental findings, downstream testing, and cost.

            07Limitations & Exclusions

            • CAC scoring is intended as an adjunct to global risk estimation for coronary or cardiovascular events over the next 10 years, not as a stand-alone screening test without ASCVD risk calculation.
            • A request filed simply as CAD without clarifying no known CAD may conflict with the preventive / risk-stratification purpose of CAC scoring.
            • CAC scoring is generally not the appropriate test for symptomatic patients or for evaluation of active chest pain or anginal equivalents.
            • CAC scoring is less likely to be supported when results will not change management, such as when the clinician has already decided to start or intensify therapy regardless of the result.
            • If the patient already has established ASCVD or known CAD, preventive CAC scoring is generally not necessary because the result is unlikely to change the indication for secondary prevention.
            • Radiation exposure, incidental findings, downstream diagnostic testing, and cost should be considered when documenting expected benefit.
